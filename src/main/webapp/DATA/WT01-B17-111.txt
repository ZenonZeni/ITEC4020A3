<BR><H3><font face="arial">Jack Lim's Fountain of Youth</CENTER></H3>
<H2 ALIGN="CENTER"><CENTER><font face="arial">Flowing with Your Original Energy</H2></TH></TR></TABLE></CENTER>
<P>
According to Australian Qigong teacher Jack Lim, we should
all live to be 150 and never lose our youthful appearance and vitality. The
secret to both longevity and rejuvenation, says Lim, is through tapping a
precious supply of &quot;original energy, or Qi&quot; in your body, and one way to
replenish this is through practicing a new set of Qigong exercises (called
Xing Ming Gong) recently revealed to the world by Chinese masters and now
taught, worldwide, by Lim. 
<P><B>Original Qi is like a bank balance; it's full
when you're born, but a penny drops out every day, so that by the time
you're 64, it's all used up. 
</B>
<P>

800-300-5663; fax: 818-242-1311.
For instructions for Xing Mong Gong and workshop schedules: Jack Lim, 458 Whitehorse

<HR WIDTH=85%>
<P>
With these exercises, which take about 40
minutes a day, done before sleeping, you can undo menopause, restore
fertility, regrow hair, make the skin youthful again, and, if the claims
are to be believed, pretty much start reversing a great deal of chronic,
degenerative disease. Lim says, &quot;As soon as your body gets its original Qi
energy back, it can start shedding sickness and rejuvenate. Every cell
starts moving into the vibrancy of youth again.&quot; Angina pain goes away,
chronic fatigue starts to lift, eyesight improves, skin cancers fall off,
and energy returns &quot;tenfold,&quot; report its practitioners.
