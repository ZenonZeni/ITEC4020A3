<H2>Non-Smokers' Rights Society </H2>
Box 27001<BR>
Vancouver  V5R 6A8<BR>
Fax: 540-2193<BR><BR>

Advocates for: Protection for non-smokers from smoking-related illness caused<BR>
 by second-hand smoke by banning smoking from public places; Preventing <BR>
children from starting to smoke through legislative controls limiting access,<BR>

 getting permanent funding for programs to help them remain smoke-free;<BR>
 Funded smoking cessation programs. Membership fee provides 3 newsletters/year,<BR>
 updates of Breathers' Dining Guide and access to research material.<BR><BR>

<H2>HANS (Health Action Network Society) 	435-0512</H2>
202-5262 Rumble Street<BR>
Burnaby  V5J 2B6<BR>
Fax: 435-1561<BR><BR>

A non-profit, consumer-health organization which networks with researchers<BR>
 'globally' to find information for members and the public 'locally'. An organization<BR>
 driven by consumers, with the consumers in mind. Although HANS honours all<BR>
 forms of health care, it focuses its research in the realm of natural medicine, including<BR>
 medical herbalism naturopathic medicine, registered massage therapy,<BR>
 chiropractic, orthomolecular medicine, etc.; and provides referrals for same.  The society<BR>
 attempts to answer the question: "What are my alternatives"? HANS also has <BR>
committees which research environmental considerations in health; such as, food contaminants,<BR>
 pesticides & herbicides, water contaminants, etc. Memberships are $25 per year which <BR>
entitle you to receive the society's publication Health Action, as well as other special benefits.<BR><BR>

Mailing Address: Box 3117<BR>
Vancouver  V6B 3X6<BR><BR>

Lifeforce is a Vancouver-based international ecology  organization. Lifeforce develops <BR>
educational campaigns to show people the interrelationship of human problems,<BR>
 animal problems, and environmental problems.<BR><BR>

<H2>Northeast Burnaby Organic Community Garden</H2>
Northeast Burnaby Community Association 	664-8845<BR>
Mailing Address: 2740 Beaverbrook Crescent <BR>
c/o Stoney Creek Community School<BR>
Burnaby<BR>
  V3J 7K4<BR><BR>

The organic gardens have been developed by volunteers from the community.<BR>
 The garden consists of 100 plots filled with top quality soil. Yearly fee is $20. 72<BR>
 garden plots are available to rent to community members. 16 are for classroom use<BR>
 in a nearby elementary school. The rest are for community club use, ie: Medicinal<BR>
 herb garden, girl guides, food bank etc. The focus is education about growing food<BR>
 without waste or chemicals. Information and support is available to any group<BR>
 wishing to start a community and/or school garden.<BR>

The BC Recycling Hotline is operated by the Recycling Council of BC under<BR>
