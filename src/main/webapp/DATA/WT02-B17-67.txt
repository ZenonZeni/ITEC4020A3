<H2>Appliance and Lighting <BR>Efficiency Standards Around the
Globe</H2>

<HR>
<IMG SRC="map.jpeg">
<HR>

<H3>Appliance
and Lighting Efficiency Standards in:</H3>

<P><B>Australia</B> - Due to
take effect in 1998, mandatory efficiency standards in Australia will
cover  refrigerators, freezers and water heaters.</P>
<P><B>Brazil</B> - Voluntary efficiency standards cover refrigerators
and freezers.</P>
<P><B>Canada</B> - Mandatory efficiency standards
cover all major appliances.</P>
<P><B>China</B> - Mandatory efficiency
standards cover  refrigerators, room air conditioning and machine
electricity use in clothes washers.</P>
<P><B>European Union</B> -
Union-wide efficiency standards covering refrigerators and freezers are
awaiting approval; standards for other major appliances have been
proposed.</P>
<P><B>Japan</B> - Voluntary efficiency standards cover
air conditioning.</P>
<P><B>South Korea</B> - Voluntary efficiency
standards cover refrigerators  and air conditioning.</P>
<P><B>Mexico</B>
- Mandatory efficiency standards cover refrigerators, freezers and room
air conditioners.</P>
<P><B>Philippines</B> - Mandatory efficiency
standards cover air conditioning.</P><P><B><A HREF="us_stds.html">United
States</A></B> - Mandatory efficiency standards cover all major
appliances as well as lighting products, plumbing products and small
motors.</P>
<P><FONT SIZE="2">Source: I.Turiel  "Present Status of
Residential Appliance Energy Efficiency Standards - An International
Review,"  Energy and Buildings (Elsevier: New York). 1996 (forthcoming).
</FONT></P>
