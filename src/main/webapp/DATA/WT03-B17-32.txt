<title> Gender</title>
<H1><center>Gender Roles and Identity</center></H1>
<p>
<p>
Gender roles and identity are issues determined and challenged by society's beliefs and standards.  In a positive sense, gender roles and identity help us establish a part of our self-identity.  In a negative sense, gender roles and identity sometimes restrict our choices in self-concept, professional roles, and societal roles.
<p>
Future information on this page will hopefully serve to help individuals better determine for themselves how to establish their own individual roles within gender and gender identity.
<p>

<center><h3>Support Groups</h3></center><p>
<u>Fridays at Three</U> is a support group for gay, lesbian, bisexual, and transgender students.  This is a <u>closed</u> support group for confidentiality purposes.  All potential participants must meet with Dr. Hellman for a screening interview prior to participation in the group.  The group is facilitated by Dr. Hellman and meets bi-weekly on Fridays at 3 p.m. Location of the group meeting is provided to participants after their screening interview.<font+2>Call ext. 3849 to set up an appointment for a screening interview!</font>
<p>
<u>Memphis Area Gay Youth (M.A.G.Y.)</u> provides support opportunities for gay, lesbian, bisexual, and transgender youth in the Memphis area.  Contact Jonathan Green at 725-7152.

<i><b>Out On Campus</b></i>  is accepting entries for inclusion in this anthology of students' stories about being gay on campus.  Editors Annie Stevens and Kim Howard are asking current or recent college students with the following experiences to send in stories for this collection:
<ul>
<li>students of color
<li>transgender students
<li>HIV+ students
<li>gay, lesbian, bisexual, or transgender students who are also athletes, international students, students with disabilities, students of religious faith, students at campuses in the South/mid-West, students in the Greek system, and/or students who turned to alcohol, suicide or eating disorders or who suffered harassment.
</ul>

For e-mail users, the American College Personnel Association has a Resource Clearinghouse covering a wide variety of gender and sexual orientation issues. For more information on the Clearinghouse's holdings, contact:  John Leppo, Resource Clearinghouse Coordinator, at jleppo@cabell.ucu.edu.

At a recent conference, I found the following information at an ACPA Standing Committee for Gay, Lesbian, and Biseuxal Awareness function.  See if these links interest you:
<ul>
<li><A HREF="http://www.ngltf.org">National Gay & Lesbian Task Force</A>.
<li><A HREF="http://www.hrcusa.org">Human Rights Campaign</A> makes e-mail activism easy with its Action e-mail feature.  Also provides links to information on Employment Non-discrimination Act, AIDS legislation, anti-gay legislation, and lesbian health issues and policy.
<li><A HREF="http://www.xq.com/cuav/index.html">Community United Against Violence</A> is the nation's oldest community project focused on anti-gay hate violence.

<li><A HREF="http://www.critpath.org/~russell/pflag/tg/">Transgender Family & Friends Support Network</A> organized by PFLAG to provide a support network for people who have friends or family members who are transgendered.
