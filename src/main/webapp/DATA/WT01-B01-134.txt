<p>
<B>Several European organizations regularly publish media monitoring
 reports.</B>
<!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http://www.demon.co.uk/iwpr/iwpr.htm/"><!-- List Tag --><UL>
<LI>The Institute for War and Peace Reporting (IWPR)</A> <BR>
is a conflict-monitoring and 
media support charity based in London which publishes the monthly 
journal War Report and the bimonthly Tribunal, a review of the Hague 
International War Crimes Tribunal.  In conjunction with Media Plan 
(MP), Bosnia's first independent media research institute with 
correspondents in six cities throughout Bosnia-Herzegovina, IWPR is 
publishing a <!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http://www.demon.co.uk/iwpr/monitor.html">weekly monitor of the media </A><BR>
<p>
<LI> <!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http://www.calvacom.fr/rsf/">Reportiers Sans Frontieres</A>, the Paris-based press freedom advocacy <BR>
organization, sent a fact-finding mission to Bosnia and Herzegovina in 
May 1996 and published their findings in this <!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http://www.calvacom.fr/rsf/ RSF_MAJ/RSFComm/Comm_VA/Doss_VA/Doss08A.html/">report </A>
<p>
<LI>  <!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http://www.intl-crisis-group.org/">   
The International Crisis Group</A>, a private multi-national organization 
devoted to preventing escalation of man-made crises, has a Bosnia 
Project, which is monitoring implementation of the Dayton Accords. 
They published a <!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http://www.intl-crisis-group.org/">report</A> <br>
on Aug. 14 
<p>
<LI><!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http://www.aut.ee.ethz.ch/~vesna/personal/medienhilfe/welcome.html/"> Medienhilfe Ex-Jugoslawien</A> was founded in December 1992 by a group of 
volunteer journalists and others committed to the struggle for 
independent media in former Yugoslavia. They publish a <!-- Link Tag --><A hREF="http://www.aut.ee.ethz.ch/~vesna/personal/medienhilfe/welcome.html/">weekly report </A>
<p>
<LI><!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="http:// http://www.dds.nl/~pressnow/index.htm/"> Press Now</A>, an NGO in Netherlands, supports independent media in former 
Yugoslavia.<BR>
</UL><p>
