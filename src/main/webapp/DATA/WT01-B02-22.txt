<P><H4><B>WHAT IS THE NATIONAL HOME EDUCATION RESEARCH INSTITUTE, AND WHAT CAN IT DO FOR YOU?</B></H4><P><HR><P>NHERI is the <B>National Home Education Research Institute</B>.  The institute has offices in Salem, Oregon and in Washington, DC. Dr. Brian Ray founded the institute in 1990 as a 501(c)3 non-profit research organization, and is the president of the institute. He lives in Salem, Oregon and heads up the West Coast Home Office of NHERI. Dr. Ray is responsible for doing and collecting research about home schooling, publishing the research journal called the <B>Home School Researcher</B>, and coordinating the day-to-day activities of the institute. William (Bill) Lloyd is a researcher and branch manager of NHERI's Washington DC office. Mr. Lloyd is also a statistician at the US Bureau of the Census in Suitland, MD; his duties with NHERI include working with the media on articles concerning home schooling, speaking to national and state legislators about home schooling research, and assisting Dr. Ray with his research duties.<P><B>Dr. Brian Ray</B> is perhaps the foremost expert in the country with regard to home schooling research. The institute he founded has hundreds of research works documented and catalogued on home schooling, many of which were done by NHERI. The Home School Legal Defense Association (HSLDA) was the sponsor for our 1990 study on home schooling students and their families, which is the single-most quoted research work of it's kind on this subject. We are now in the process of updating this nationwide study, and we should have a report available for public consumption by the end of 1996.<P><B>NHERI exists to do the following things</B>:<P><UL><LI>To research those who teach their children at home;<BR><LI>To publish the research journal called the <B>Home School Researcher</B>, which chronicles the current research being done about home schooling;<BR><LI>To speak to the media, legislators, and national organizations about the home schooling movement, showing factual research about those who teach their children at home;<BR><LI>To serve as conference speakers and teachers for groups interested in home schooling; and<BR><LI>To work with legislators on Capitol Hill, and with state/local legislators, on issues related to parental rights, the freedom we have to teach our children at home, and home schoolers coordinating with local school officials.</UL><P><B>Services we provide on a regular basis include</B>:<P><UL><LI>Speaking to home schooling and other types of conferences about home schooling research;<BR><LI>Giving seminars and lectures on how to teach your children at home;<BR><LI>Doing state- and national-level research about the home schooling community; and<BR><LI>Speaking to the media (television, radio, newspaper, etc.) about home schooling issues.</UL><P><BR><B>For more information about NHERI's services, to subscribe to the Home School Researcher,  to get started in your own home schooling, or if you would like to obtain information about home schooling research, you can contact the institute at the following addresses:</B><P><B>Salem, OR Home Office</B><P>Dr. Brian D.
