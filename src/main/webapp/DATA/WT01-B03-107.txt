<H2>Four Steps to the Successful <BR> Regulation of Tribal Gaming</H2></CENTER>
<H5>by <A HREF="TGCAcji.html">Charles J. Irwin</A>,General Counsel of
<A HREF="TGCA.html">Tribal Gaming Commission Administrators, Inc. (TGCA)</A></H5>
<H4>(1) Identify the need and define the scope of the system to be implemented,
by reviewing:</H4>
<UL>
<LI>&#183; IGRA and its regulations, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; your Tribal Ordinances, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; your Tribal-State compact, and 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; your management arrangements. 
</LI></UL>
<H4>Then, after that review, define in specific terms:</H4>
<UL>
<LI>&#183; the responsibilities of the various individuals &amp; groups
involved, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; the powers that each will be expected to exercise, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; the scope of the games, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; facility requirements, and 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; budgetary constraints. 
</LI></UL>
<H4>(2) Plan for the implementation process, by developing:</H4>
<UL>
<LI>&#183; Mission Statement &amp; Goals, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; Job Descriptions, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; Code of Conduct, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; Administrative Code &amp; Hearing Procedures, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; Gaming &amp; Licensing Regulations, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; Budget/Finance provisions. 
</LI></UL>
<H4>(3) Assemble &amp; train your Start-up Staff, to include:</H4>
<UL>
<LI><A HREF="TGCAStone.html">&#183; Gaming Commission Members</A>, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; commission administrative staff, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; key Compliance Personnel. 
</LI></UL>
<H4>(4) Develop &amp; install procedures to monitor gaming operations,
including:</H4>
<UL>
<LI>&#183; Internal Control Systems featuring dual-control and multiple checks
and balances, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; daily reporting systems, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; on-site enforcement personnel, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; paper trails and audit procedures, 
</LI>
<LI>&#183; continuing education of staff and compliance personnel. 
</LI></UL>
