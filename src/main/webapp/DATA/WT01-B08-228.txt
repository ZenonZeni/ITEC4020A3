<B>(WASHINGTON)</b></FONT><font size=3> -- The U.S. Department of Commerce and the Small Business Administration are co-sponsoring the last in a six-part series of defense conversion conferences designed to assist small and medium-sized companies improve businesses opportunities by using
technologies available from the federal laboratory system.  This
conference is scheduled for Wedneseday, December 11,  at the College
of Charleston in Charleston, S.C.<P>

Because technology developed for military purposes also has a
wide range of uses in the commercial sector, businesses need additional
information about available products and potential markets for those
products.   The conference has been designed to assist South Carolina
firms in their marketing, export control, and product diversification
strategies. There will be seminars on  technologies ready for licensing,
creative financing techniques, the patent and trademark process, and
targeting markets to take advantage of emerging technology
breakthroughs.<P> 

The cost for conference participants is $75 in advance, or $95 at
the door.  The conference runs from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.   Anyone
interested in attending or exhibiting may call 703/760-0762 or visit the
conference <a href="http://www.ideea.com/com/charleston"><I>web site</I></A> at  for additional information or to register.<P>
