<FONT  SIZE=+2><CENTER><B>California Medical Association</B></CENTER></FONT><P>

Resolution 711-94 (1994)<BR>
<B>COMPASSIONATE PRESCRIBING OF MARIJUANA</B><P>

RESOLVED: That the CMA supports efforts to expedite access to cannabinoids for therapeutic use as a Schedule II drug under the direction of a physician if controlled studies prove efficacy; and, be it further<P>

RESOLVED: That the California Delegation to the AMA be asked to introduce a similar resolution to the AMA House of Delegates in 1994.<P>

<B>ACTION: Adopted as amended.</B><P>

Assigned to: First Resolved: Council on Scientific Affairs; Second Resolved: California Delegation to the AMA.<P>

<CENTER>***</CENTER><P>

<FONT  SIZE=-1><B><I>Resolution 721-92 (1992)</I></B><P>

<B>PROTOCOLS FOR PRESCRIBING<BR>
CANNABINOIDS</B><P>

RESOLVED: That while opposing the recreational use<BR>
of marijuana, CMA believes therapeutic use of<BR>
cannabinoids under the direction of a physician may be<BR>
appropriate for certain conditions; and be it further<P>

RESOLVED: That CMA encourage a comprehensive<BR>
study to determine the appropriate protocols for the<BR>
prescriptive uses of cannabinoids.<P>

<I>ACTION: Adopted as amended.</I><P>

Assigned to: Council on Scientific Affairs.<P>
