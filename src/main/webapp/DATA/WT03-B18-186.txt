<p>
<b>Research Interests</b><p>

Experimental studies of electrostatic plasma 
turbulence in both the edge of tokamaks 
(<a href="http://fusion.gat.com">DIII-D</a>) and 
linear plasma devices 
(<a href="http://pisces.ucsd.edu/www/pisces/piscesa_desc.html">PISCES-A</a>).
  We have observed that the levels of electrostatic turbulence
and the turbulent particle transport across the magnetic field can be modified by changing the
strength and profile of the electric field in the plasma.<p>

In the next six months, we will be installing a temperature fluctuation diagnostic extension
to the midplane scanning probe on the DIII-D tokamak.  Correlated measurements of the fluctuations
in the electron temperature with fluctuations in the floating potential of the plasma will 
provide an estimate of the fluctuation of the plasma space potential.  Measuring this at two spatial
locations will allow for the fluctuating electric field and this data, correlated with the 
fluctuating plasma density, allows for an estimate of the cross field particle transport.<p>

For further information, consult my <a href="/~rlehmer/home.html">home page</a> or
contact <a href="mailto:rlehmer@ucsd.edu">Ron Lehmer</a>.<p>
