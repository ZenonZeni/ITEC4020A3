Let's look at this tax savings another way. The money you save will help toward a
child's education, buy
a major household appliance, or can be used as a downpayment on a car... all of
this, while you are
still providing for your retirement.
<P>
<STRONG>The Benefits of Tax-Deferred Accumulation</STRONG><BR>
Even if you cannot fully deduct your contributions, you may still be way ahead with
a MainStay IRA. The
secret is the potential for tax-deferred accumulation. Because you pay no income
tax on
the earnings of your
IRA contributions until you withdraw them at retirement, your account can grow much
faster than a
taxable investment, such as a traditional savings account.  Let's look at this
chart to see how this
works.

 <P>
This hypothetical example assumes an annual $2,000 IRA contribution earning 7.0%
compounded
monthly.  After 30 years, the IRA is worth $202,146.  This illustration does not
reflect the taxes due when
funds are withdrawn from the IRA.  However, a taxable investment, such as a
traditional savings
account, is worth only $140,537 for an individual in the 28% tax bracket.  As you
can see, the IRA is
worth $61,609 more than the savings account -- all because of tax-deferred
accumulation!
The potential for
tax-deferred accumulation is what makes an IRA such a powerful method of
accumulating
retirement assets. 
However, unlike a traditional savings account, the investment return and principal
value of the Fund will
fluctuate with changes in market conditions.  You should realize that this chart is
for illustrative
purposes only, and is not intended to predict nor guarantee the actual results that
will be realized from
any particular IRA investment.
