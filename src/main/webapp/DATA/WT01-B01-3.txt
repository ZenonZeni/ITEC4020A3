<p><h4> Biosolids compost is a clean, pathogen-free soil enhancer. The 

product produced by the March Creek Waste Water Treatment Plant meets all New 

York State and Federal standards. The material is sold throughout New York State as AllGro <tT><I>TM</I></TT>, &quot;an 

all purpose organic compost &amp; natural fertilizer.&quot;</p>

<p>

Compost is a foundation element of organic and biological agriculture. As mentioned earlier is is weed free making it an ideal enhancer. It is particularly beneficial for sub-standard earth. Building contractors will find it ideal for mixing with soils of high clay and sand content. Landscapers and nursery  professionals will find it ideal to prevent weed growth when used as mulch. Turf professionals may use it as a dressing for golf courses and cemeteries enhance growth and endurance.</P>

<p>

Geneva residents may pickup compost for non-commercial use. The product may be also purchased in bulk quaniities. Contact the Marsh Creek Wastewater Plant at (315) 789-8040 for details.</p>

<TABLE BORDER=10 CELLPADDING=10 CELLSPACING=10> 

<CAPTION ALIGN=TOP><B>Recommended Compost Application</B></CAPTION> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Turf Establishment</TD> <TD> Incorporate 1-3 inches of compost into the top 4-6 inches of soil. Increase  compost for less fertile or sandy spoil.</TD> 

</TR> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Turf Maintenance</TD> <TD>  Spread 1/3 inch of compost and rake in.</TD> 

</TR> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Flower Gardens</TD> <TD>  Spread 1 inch of compost and mix before planting.</TD> 

</TR> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Ornamentals</TD> <TD>  Spread 2-4 incches of compost two weeks before spring or fall planting.</TD> 

</TR> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Tree Planting</TD> <TD> Mix 1 to 4 ration of compost to soil and use for backfill.</TD> 

</TR> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Mulching</TD> <TD> Spread 2-3 inches over area to be covered.</TD> 

</TR> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Potting</TD> <TD> Mix equal mixtures of compost and potting materials. Water before planting.</TD> 

</TR> 

<TR> 

  <TD>Surface Cover</TD> <TD>  1 inch  of compost on 1000 square feet requires 3 cubic yards of compost</TD> 

</TR> 

</TABLE>
