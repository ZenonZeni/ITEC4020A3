<H2>Circuit Court Hears Challenge to Crypto Regulations</H2>
<FONT SIZE=-1>First Posted on January 9, 1997</FONT>
<P>
The U.S. Court of Appeals for the D.C. Circuit hears oral arguments Friday (Jan. 10) in a challenge to the encryption export control regulations. Plaintiff Phil Karn is arguing that the encryption licensing scheme -- which prohibits his export of encryption source code on a disk, but allows the export of identical source code when printed -- is an unconstitutional prior
restraint on free speech and is unlawfully arbitrary and capricious as applied. <P>

This is the first encryption challenge to be heard at the Circuit Court level. It comes on the heels of <A HREF="961218_Bernstein_ruling.html">a California federal court ruling</A> in a similar case that export controls <I>are</I> an unconstitutional prior restraint.
