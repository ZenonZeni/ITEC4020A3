<H3>4. Materials Improvements Are Cutting Vehicle Weight and Increasing 
Efficiency</H3>
<BR>
<BR>
Many automakers have argued that raising mileage levels through more 
stringent CAFE standards means cutting car weight, size, and safety. But 
improvements in aluminum and plastic composites promise lighter, 
higher-mileage cars that will be just as big, safe, and powerful as 
today's models.<P>
<p>     <B><I><A NAME="OakRidgeNationalLaboratory">Oak Ridge National Laboratory.</a></B></I>. The Oak Ridge National 
Laboratory in Oak Ridge, Tennessee coordinates a Lightweight Materials 
Program to develop cost-effective materials and technologies that will aid 
in automobile weight reduction while meeting or exceeding safety 
standards. The program also seeks to reduce the overall environmental 
impact of automobiles by minimizing process waste and increasing 
recyclability. A 25 percent weight reduction in current vehicles would 
save about 13 percent of total U.S. gasoline consumption, reducing carbon 
dioxide emissions by about 92 million metric tons per year.<a href="#268" name="r268"> [ 268 ]
</a>
