<h1><font COLOR="#0000FF">Beauty Salon Models</font> </h1>

<p><font SIZE=+1><b>Aero·Pure beauty salon units are electrically and
mechanically identical to other units:</b> </font></p>

<p>Beauty 100 is an ZN-15 in a salon cabinet. (ZNs are lower in price because
many more units are sold)</p>

<p>Beauty 200 is a Falcon 52 in a salon cabinet.</p>

<p>Beauty 300 is a Falcon 55 in a salon cabinet.</p>

<p>
<br></p>


<p><b><font SIZE=+1>Hair and nail salon environments are especially troublesome.
. . so there is a major need for these products!</font></b> </p>

<ol>
<li>More than one unit may be required depending on the size, layout: air
circulation and workload.</li>

<li>It is important to operate the unit or units as close to the ceiling
as possible.</li>

<li>Auxiliary fans (ceiling fans, blower or oscillating fans) are very
helpful and often essential.</li>

<li>A nightly ozone blast is essential. A timer should be installed (line
timers are available at hardware stores) so the unit(s) can be left on
high at night and turned off two to three hours before the first employee
arrives.</li>

<li>The ozone plates should be cleaned in ammonia and water and dried with
a hair dryer twice a month.</li>
</ol>
