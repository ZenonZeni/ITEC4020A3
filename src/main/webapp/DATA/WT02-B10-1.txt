<h1 align="center">Anipryl<sup><font size="4">&#174;</font></sup>
Information</h1>
<h1 align="center">for Canadian Veterinarians</h1>
<hr>
<h2>Bring old dogs back with Anipryl<sup><font size="2">&#174;</font></sup></h2>
<h2><font size="2"><em><strong>Approved by the Canadian Bureau of
Veterinary Medicine</strong></em></font></h2>
<ul>
    <li>First line therapy for canine Cushing's disease </li>
    <li>Proven safety and efficacy </li>
    <li>Convenient once-a-day-dosage </li>
    <li>Available exclusively through veterinarians </li>
</ul>
<h3>Indications</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Indicated for the treatment of uncomplicated pituitary
        dependent hyperadrenocorticism. </li>
</ul>
<h3><img src="images/anipactn.GIF" align="left" hspace="10"
width="155" height="199">Mode of action</h3>
<table >
    <tr>
        <td width="100%">
        <ul>
            <li>Research indicates that with aging, monoamine
                oxidase type B (MAOB), an enzyme which degrades
                dopamine, increases and thus, dopamine levels
                decrease. Anipryl<sup><font size="1">&#174;</font></sup>
                is an irreversible inhibitor of MAOB.<br>
                <br>
            </li>
            <li>Anipryl<sup><font size="1">&#174;</font></sup>
                therapy provides normalization of dopamine
                metabolism and HPA axis regulation. </li>
        </ul>
        </td>
    </tr>
</table>
