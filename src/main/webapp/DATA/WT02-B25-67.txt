<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<p align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Siblings &amp; Adult Children</b></font></p>
<hr>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Coping Tips </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - you cannot cure a mental disorder for a parent or sibling </p>
<p>2 - no one is to blame </p>
<p>3 - the mental illnesses are not on a continuum with mental health </p>
<p>4 - mental disorders affect more than the afflicted </p>
<p>5 - despite your best efforts, symptoms may get worse </p>
<p>6 - symptoms may improve </p>
<p>7 - if you feel extreme resentment, you are giving too much </p>
<p>8 - it is hard for all family members to accept the disorder </p>
<p>9 - acceptance of the disorder is helpful, but it is not necessary </p>
<p>10 - a delusion is not amenable to reason, so it needs no discussion </p>
<p>11 - separate the person from the disorder </p>
<p>12 - it is not OK for you to be neglected/you have emotional needs &amp; wants too </p>
<p>13 - the illness of a family member is nothing to be ashamed of </p>
<p>14 - you may encounter stigma from an apprehensive public, but you have 
nothing to be ashamed of or to hide </p>
<p>15 - you may have to revise your expectations </p>
<p>16 - you may have to renegotiate your emotional relationship </p>
<p>17 - acknowledge the courage your loved one has in dealing with his/her 
disorder </p>
<p>18 - those closest in sibling order tend to become the most emotionally enmeshed </p>
<p>19 - siblings grieve over what they had &amp; lost </p>
<p>20 - adult children grieve over what they never had </p>
<p>21 - acceptance should follow denial, sadness, &amp; anger </p>
<p>22 - understanding yields compassion </p>
<p>23 - addressing social complications might be helpful </p>
<p>24 - symptoms may change over time while the disorder remains the same </p>
<p>25 - request an explanation of the diagnosis from professionals </p>
<p>26 - mental health professionals have varied degrees of competence </p>
<p>27 - you have a right to ensure your personal safety </p>
<p>28 - don't take strange behavior personally/it's a symptom of the disorder </p>
<p>29 - don't be afraid to ask your loved one if he/she is contemplating suicide </p>
<p>30 - don't assume all responsibility for your mentally ill loved one </p>
<p>31 - you should not be assuming the role of parent or paid 
professional/caseworker </p>
<p>32 - the needs of the ill person do not necessarily always come first </p>
<p>33 - if you can't care for yourself, you can't care for another </p>
<p>34 - it is important to have boundaries &amp; to set clear limits </p>
<p>35 - don't expect nothing of your loved one just because he/she has limited 
capabilities </p>
<p>36 - it is natural to have all sorts of emotional reactions to your loved one's 
illness </p>
<p>37 - you should try hard to talk about your feelings </p>
<p>38 - you are not alone </p>
<p>39 - support groups can be very helpful </p>
</blockquote>
