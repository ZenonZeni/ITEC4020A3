<H3>Areas of interest</H3>
Computational Chemistry and Quantum Chemistry, with particular reference to:<P>
<UL>
        <LI>Accurate calculations of structures and harmonic frequencies with
            particular interest in compounds of the heavier main group elements
            such as Al, Ga, Ge, As etc.<P>
        <LI>Understanding, evaluating and improving simple qualitative bonding
            models by using accurate quantitative models.<P>
        <LI>Economical methods of modelling the results of <i>ab initio</i> 
	    calculations.
</UL>

This research is carried out on:<P>
<UL>
        <LI>an IBM RISC 6000 M2A unix workstation purchased by funds from
            the NTU Internal Research Grant.<P>
        <LI>a DEC Alpha 3000/800 unix workstation funded by a DEET 
	    Infrastructure C grant obtained by the Research group for
	    <A HREF="http://lacebark.ntu.edu.au/j_mitroy/mmpcs.html">
	    Mathematical Modelling of Physical and Chemical Systems</A>.<P>
        <LI>the ANU VPP330 Supercomputer using a grant of time from ANU.<P>
</UL>

I have a particular interest in:<P>
<UL>
       <LI>Teaching computational chemistry and physical chemistry making 
           use of computers in inovative ways. This is exemplified by the 
	   current <A HREF="http://www.chem.swin.edu.au/CAUT.html">CAUT 
	   Computational Chemistry project</A>. This has lead to the
     	   introduction in 1997 of a taught <A HREF="/msc/index.html">
	   Master of Science in Computational Chemistry</A> which is
	   being offered by the NTU, Swinburne University of Technology
	   and Monash University, taught by staff from these universities
	   plus Brian Yates from the University of Tasmania.<P>
       <LI>Improving teaching and learning in introductory and first year
           chemistry units.
</UL>
