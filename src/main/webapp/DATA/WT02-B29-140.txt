<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>Are You Retiring or Changing Jobs?</STRONG></FONT><P>

If you're thinking about changing jobs or retiring, you may be expecting a lump sum
distribution from
your employer-sponsored retirement plan.  The way you handle this distribution may
be one of the
most important financial decisions you make, because it could have a significant
impact on the taxes
you will pay.  The law now requires that participants be provided with a direct
rollover option and imposes
a mandatory 20% withholding on any "eligible rollover" distribution that is not
paid directly to another
eligible retirement plan or IRA.  This 20% withholding does not apply to
distributions from IRAs.
<P>
The direct rollover option is available in addition to the regular rollover
provisions of the Internal
Revenue Code.
<P>
You may generally elect to make a direct rollover of a portion of a distribution
and to receive a
distribution of the remaining portion, or divide an eligible rollover distribution
among several rollover
IRAs. Special rules apply to "direct rollovers" and you should consult your
employer and your
professional tax advisor on this issue.
<P>
By reinvesting your IRA assets in a MainStay IRA, you continue to defer taxes, and
your account will
benefit from any tax-deferred accumulation. Any amount that you do not rollover will be
taxable in the year you
receive it. Also, you cannot roll over any voluntary non-deductible contributions
you
made to your
employer-sponsored plan.
<P>
Generally, you can begin making withdrawals from your IRA without incurring a tax
penalty when you
reach age 59 1/2,
or if you become disabled. There are some exceptions to these guidelines, so it's
a good idea to talk
with your tax advisor.
<P>
When you begin to withdraw money from your IRA, it will be taxed as ordinary
income. However, many
people are in a lower tax bracket when they begin to withdraw, and therefore are
able to keep more of
their investment free from taxes.
<P>
By investing in a MainStay IRA, you may actually save twice on taxes - both when
you invest and when
you retire!
