<table cellpadding=15 cellspacing=15 width=100%>
<tr><td valign=top width=50%><h4><font color="#FF0000">What does the <em><i>Helpline</i></em> do?	</font></h4>
<h5>The NAMI <i>Helpline</i> is a toll-free 
information and referral service for people 
affected by mental illness. We offer 
support, information, and referral to local 
and state NAMI affiliates. The <i>Helpline</i> is 
accessible from all 50 states. There are four 
lines which we staff Monday through 
Friday between 10am and 5pm, EST. At 
other times, people may leave their name 
and address on the voice mail system, and 
we will send them information. </h5>
<h4><font color="#FF0000">Why do people call the <i>Helpline</i>?</font> </h4>
<h5>People call with a wide variety of 
situations and problems. Their questions 
may range from a simple &quot;please send me 
information&quot; to urgent requests for help in 
handling an emergency. Although we are 
not a &quot;crisis line,&quot; we sometimes receive 
calls from people experiencing urgent 
problems. Most calls fall somewhere in 
between. Callers come from all walks of 
life, with varying abilities to handle the 
complex difficulties that mental illness 
presents. </h5>
</td><td valign=top width=50%><h4><font color="#FF0000">What does the <em><i>Helpline</i></em> NOT do?</font> </h4>
<h5><strong><u>We do not make specific doctor referrals....</u></strong> </h5>
<ul>
<li><h5>because people's experience with any 
given doctor may vary greatly from 
person to person, and </h5>
</li>
<li><h5>to avoid organizational liability. </h5>
</li>
</ul>
<h5>If you are looking for a doctor, you may 
want to talk to the members of your local 
AMI, who can share their experiences and 
offer suggestions on what to look for. </h5>
<h5><strong><u>We also do not recommend specific 
treatment methods or give advice on 
medication use.</u></strong> While we may give basic 
information about medications, we 
absolutely refrain from communicating 
anything that could be construed as 
medical advice. We are lay people and are 
not trained in the use of medications or 
other treatments. We can encourage you to 
order the books NAMI has for sale on 
psychiatric medications as well as 
encourage you to seek a second opinion if 
you are not satisfied with a doctor's 
handling of your medication. </h5>
</td></tr>
</table>
</div>
<h4 align=center><font color="#FF0000">Is the <i>Helpline</i> a counseling service?</font> </h4>
<h5>Officially, the <i>Helpline</i> is not a counseling service, and no one who works on the <i>Helpline</i> is trained 
as a counselor. However, many of the volunteers are people who have been affected by mental illness 
and are naturally sympathetic to callers -- we listen and reassure. </h5>
