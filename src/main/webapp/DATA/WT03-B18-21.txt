<p><font size="3">The Istituto Automazione Navale (IAN) is one of
the hundreds of research centres constituting Italy's National Research
Council (</font><a href="http://www.cnr.it"><font size="3">CNR</font></a><font
size="3">), a publicly funded organization whose primary
objective is to further knowledge in a wide range of disciplines. </font></p>

<p><font size="3">IAN has conducted research into marine-maritime
subjects for over twenty years and has recently confirmed its prominent
role in several national and international projects in the field
of scientific engineering, many of which are funded by the </font><a
href="http://www.cec.lu/en/index.html"><font size="3">European
Commission</font></a><font size="3">. </font></p>

<p><font size="3">The focus has traditionally been on improving
efficiency and safety in </font><a href="/navig/basenavi.htm"><font
size="3">navigation</font></a><font size="3"> and on developing
systems for the protection of coasts and the </font><a
href="/ocean/baseocea.htm"><font size="3">marine environment</font></a><font
size="3">. More recently, following a natural evolution in its
objectives, IAN's research scope has expanded to encompass the
field of </font><a href="/robotics/Welcome.html"><font size="3">marine
robotics</font></a><font size="3"> and to pursue the development
of advanced technologies that can find an application in industry
and in marine sciences. </font></p>

<p><font size="3">IAN has a permanent staff of 12 research
scientists and 14 technical/administrative people. It is one of
the thirteen research centres encompassed within CNR&#146;s </font><a
href="http://www.ge.cnr.it"><font size="3">Genoa Research Area</font></a><font
size="3">, a logistic-organizational body with the role of rationalizing
services and scientific equipment. </font></p>
