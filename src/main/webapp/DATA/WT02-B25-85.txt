<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<h4 align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Schizo-Affective Disorder</b></font></h4>
<hr>
<h4 align=left><font color="#FF0000">What is Schizo-Affective Disorder?</font></h4>
<ul>
<li><p align=left>presence of psychotic symptoms in the absence of mood changes for at least two weeks in a patient who has 
a mood disorder.</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>relatively rare disorder</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>an appropriate diagnosis when the person does not fit diagnostic standard for either schizophrenia or 
&quot;affective&quot; (mood) disorders such as depression and manic depression.</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>often diagnosed as manic depression originally.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h4 align=left><font color="#FF0000">Symptoms</font></h4>
<ul>
<li><p align=left>characterized by delusions and hallucinations, catatonia, paranoia, bizarre behavior, and thought disorders 
that persist even after the mood has been stabilized. Example: A patient who is psychotic for three or four 
weeks while manic is not shizoaffective.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h4 align=left><font color="#FF0000">Treatment</font></h4>
<ul>
<li><p align=left>medication: treated with an anti-psychotic medication and lithium or with carbamazephine (an 
anticonvulsant medication) and lithium.</p>
</li>
</ul>
