<H2>Caatinga</H2>
</CENTER>
<P>
<BLOCKQUOTE><STRONG>Chief Threat:</STRONG> <I>Land conversion for agriculture and cattle pasture. </I>

<P>The 860,000-square-kilometer Caatinga of northeastern Brazil remains one of the least populated
and poorest major regions of the country.  Long cycles of severe drought periodically force local
populations to either migrate to urban slums or temporarily exhaust the region's natural resources.
CI's conservation solutions in the Caatinga include:

<UL>
<LI>working with local partner Funda&ccedil;ao Biodiversitas to conserve ecosystems in the Canudos region, home to many endangered species, including Lear's Macaw and the Brazilian three-banded armadillo -- the only armadillo species endemic to Brazil.
</UL></BLOCKQUOTE>
