<b><I>TEACHING Exceptional Children</I><br>
Volume 29 No. 2<br>
Nov/Dec 1996</b><p>

<b><i>Articles</b></i><p>
<ul>
<li>
<I>Sharing Ideas About Teaching Effectively: Using Technology to Collaborate--TEC Author Online Article</I> <br>by Pam Campbell and Jack Tierney <br>
This computer program enables teachers to create a database of effective teaching strategies and tactics that can be shared easily and efficiently.<p>
<li><I>Debut of TEC Author Online</I> by Judith Zorfass<br>
Log on to CEC's World Wide Web site and query Pam Campbell (author Sharing Ideas About Teaching Effectively).<P>
<li><I>What Successful Adults with Learning Disabilities Can Tell Us About Teaching Children</I><br> by Henry B. Reiff, Paul J. Gerber, and Rick Ginsberg <br>
Through the results of an ethnographic study of 71 successful adults with learning disabilities, teachers can get suggestions on how to work with children with learning disabilities.<p>

<li><I>Developing Job Clubs for Students in Transition</I><br> by Lauren E. Lindstrom, Michael R. Benz, and Michael D. Johnson<br>
Job clubs are not just about preparing for work--they also provide students with social and interpersonal skills.<p>

<li><I>Finding a "Voice": Primary Students with Developmental Disabilities Express Personal Meanings Through Writing</I><br> by Catherine E. Patzer and Barbara S. Pettegrew <br>
Learn how children with disabilities benefited academically, socially, and behaviorally from reading and writing activities that included a print-rich environment, a writing center, journals, pen-pal letters, a class post office, and reading-response notebooks.<p>

<li><I>The Hazeldean Project: Strategies for Improving Parent Information Sessions</I><br> by Linda McDonald, Gerard Kysela, Cathy Martin, and Susan Wheaton <br>
Strategies for improving parent information sessions based on parent feedback within a family-focused framework.<p>

<li><I>"PEPSI" A Screening and Programming Tool for Understanding the Whole Child</I><br> by J'Anne Ellsworth <br>
A tool that informs teachers and parents of children, from age 5 through adolescence, about students' needs and capabilities.<p>

<li><I>Multimedia Reading: Using Anchored Instruction and Video Technology in Vocabulary Lessons</I><br> by Candace Williams Glaser and Herbert Rieth <br>
Using a real event to serve as a reading context, teachers and 10 students with learning disabilities reviewed video segments on a videodisc to learn new vocabulary words and obtain information about the words.<p>

<li><I>Young Children at Their Best: Linking Play to Assessment and Intervention</I><br> by Linda J. Bufkin and Suzanne M. Bryde <br>
Help for the early childhood special educator with linking skills areas, objectives, and structured play activities with authentic assessment.<p>

<li><I>"In the Face of Predictable Crises" Developing A Comprehensive Treatment Plan for Students with Emotional or Behavioral Disorders</I><br> by Vernon F. Jones <br>
Key components of a comprehensive plan, with example, are provided to assist educators.<p>

<li><I>A Fun Alternative: Using Instructional Games to Foster Student Learning</I><br> by H. Timothy Blum and Dorothy Jean Yocom <br>
Teachers, parents, and other professionals learn how to design effective instructional games for all students.<p>

<li><I>Don't Buy a Lemon! Smart Shopping with Used Car Ads</I><br> by Rod Bertholf and Gregory J. Conderman <br>
Smart shopping with used car ads is a functional language arts activity designed for secondary learners with mild disabilities that integrates reading, writing, vocabulary, and study skills.<p>

<li><I>LEARN to Succeed</I><br> by Victoria P. Day and Gail S. Hackett <br>
A mnemonic learning strategy to assist students with or without disabilities in preparing for classroom tests.</ul><p>
