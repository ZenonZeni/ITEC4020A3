<h1><a name="RTFToC1">Introduction</a></h1>


<i><A HREF = "cover.HTM">Resources for VE Teachers</i></A> is designed to provide information about the
use of the varying exceptionalities model and to share ideas and practices that
teachers can use with their students.  <p>
<p>
The first section, <i><A HREF = "bees5.HTM">General Information,</A></i> includes the definition and
State Board of Education rules which apply to the model, as well as brief
descriptions on the history and rationale.<p>
<p>
The second section, <i><A HREF = "bees6.HTM">Uses of the Varying Exceptionalities Model,</A></i> provides
examples of guidelines and policies which have been developed in various
school districts for programs at the prekindergarten, elementary, middle, and
high school level.  Recommendations from previous task forces are summarized in
the description of "Best Practices."<p>
<p>
The third section, <i><A HREF = "bees7a.HTM">Effective Practices in Varying Exceptionalities Classes,
</i></A> is intended to share the practices that many teachers have found to be
effective in meeting the diversity of student needs.  This includes a brief
description of the characteristics of exceptional students,  and suggestions
for scheduling, grouping, and managing the educational environment.  In
response to the many concerns raised by VE teachers, information is also
included on managing behavior and using assistants in the classroom.<p>
<p>
The fourth section, <i><A HREF = "bees8.HTM">Staffing Considerations for Placement in Varying
Exceptionalities Classes,</i></A> is designed to guide the staffing process.  Key
questions and sources of information can be used by teachers to make sure that
all important factors are considered.<p>
<p>
The fifth section, <i><A HREF = "bees9.HTM">Support for Teachers,</i></A> is intended to help teachers
access support at both the state and district level.  The information for the
district level should be added to the appropriate spaces.<P>
