<h2>Sacramento Venues Can Host Major Events</h2>

<ul>
<li><a href=arco_arena.html>ARCO Arena</a><p>
The 17,000 seat facility is home to a variety of sports events including the
<a href=http://www.nba.com/kings/>Sacramento Kings</a>,
Sacramento Knights and
<a href=http://www.rollerhockey.com/riverrats.html>Sacramento River Rats</a>.
The ARCO Park master plan includes a 45,000 seat
<a href=images/arco_park_baseball.jpg>baseball</a> and/or
<a href=images/arco_park_football.jpg>football</a> stadium.

<p><li><a href=convention_center.html>Sacramento Convention Center</a><p>
The recently completed $85 million expansion features 140,000 square feet of
contiguous ground floor exhibit space, a hotel-style
<a href=convention_center_ballroom.html>grand ballroom,</a>
a rooftop garden terrace overlooking State Capitol Park and 31 convertible
meeting rooms. In addition to permanent seating for 2,452 in the
<a href=convention_center_theatre.html>performing arts theater,</a>
telescopic risers in the
<a href=convention_center_exhibit_hall.html>exhibit hall</a>
can accommodate a crowd of 6,500. The facility is host to the KAEPA Women's
Volleyball Championships as well as other sports events.

<p><li><a href=memorial_auditorium.html>Memorial Auditorium</a><p>
Currently undergoing a $12 million renovation of the 4,000 seat venue located
in mid-town Sacramento. The facility is a renowned sports venue for
professional boxing and tennis tournaments, high school basketball tournaments
as well as wrestling and gymnastics events. It will serve as the home for Cal
State Sacramento's Men's and Women's basketball beginning in November of 1997.

<p><li>Hughes Stadium</a><p>
The 22,000 seat venue has hosted all of Sacramento's major track and field
events, WAFL and CFL professional football as well as collegiate and high
school football. Hughes was also home to Double-A Minor League Baseball. The
facility recently completed a $175,000 track resurface and facility upgrade to
host the 1995 USA/Mobil Outdoor Track and Field Championships.

<p><li>Cal Expo</a><p>
The 350 acres facility with indoor/outdoor venues serving as the home of the
California State Fair as well as host to sports events, conventions, exhibit
shows and festivals throughout the year. Cal Expo buildings have accomodated
KAEPA Volleyball.

<p><li>CSUS Aquatic Center</a><p>
One of the three best rowing venues in the country as well as a practice
facility for national and international collegiate and Olympic rowing teams
throughout the year.

<p><li><a href=american_river.html>American River Parkway</a><p>
One of Northern California's most important preserves, the 26 mile parkway
lines the American River and is the site of many competitive running and cycling
competitions.

<p><li>CSU Sacramento</a><p>
Features the 21,000 seat Hornet Stadium with natural turf as a venue for
football and soccer; an all-purpose nine lane synthetic track; state-of-the-art
baseball facility; 2,000 seat gymnasium to accommodate basketball, and other
sports.

<p><li>Sacramento Softball Complex</a><p>
Elk Grove Softball Complex<p>
Both feature lighted fields, multiple diamonds and risers for seating
spectators. Both facilities have served as venues for all levels of national
softball championship competition including the recent U.S. Olympic Softball
Tour.

</ul>
