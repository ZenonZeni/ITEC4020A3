<p>
Sun Microsystems has announced the JAVA Cup International, a contest<br>
to promote the creation and public availability of small,<br>
platform-independent applications called JAVA applets. Software<br>
developers will compete to develop the most innovative and creative<br>
applets. The JAVA Cup International contest begins immediately and<br>
ends March 31, 1996.<br>
<p>
Who's Eligible: Developer community including business, academic, and<br>
individual programmers worldwide.<br>
<p>
Prizes: Prizes will total $1,000,000 in Sun equipment (U.S. list)<br>
distributed among winners. One grand prize and three prizes per<br>
category (two individual prizes and one team prize). The first 1,000<br>
to submit entries will receive a Java Coffee Cup &amp; T-Shirt.<br>
<p>
Categories: Productivity Tools, Internet/Web Agents, Educational,<br>
Developer Tools, Entertainment and Games, Unlimited.<br>
<p>
Judging Criteria: Best Graphics, System Friendliness, Clean Code,<br>
Security, Reliability, Interactivity, Innovation.<br>
<p>
Check out <a href="http://javacontest.sun.com">http://javacontest.sun.com</a> for complete details. <br>
