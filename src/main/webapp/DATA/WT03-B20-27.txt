<title>Wind Plants of the Netherlands</title>

<head><b><h1>Wind Plants of the Netherlands</h1></b></head><p>

<body>

For a nation known worldwide for its mastery of the wind in 

the eighteenth century, modern wind technology got off to a 

slow start in the Netherlands. Wind energy has finally begun 

to grow in the Netherlands after being stymied for nearly a 

decade by mis-guided government programs.<p>



Despite the highest population density outside Bangladesh, 

the Netherlands has one of the worlds' most ambitious wind 

programs. Officially the Netherlands is striving to install 

1,000 MW of wind generating capacity by the year 2000. 

Unfortunately only 200 MW will be installed by the end of 

1995 and it's likely the Dutch will miss their target..<p>

<h3><i>Goliath</i> near Eemshaven</h3></center><p>



The polder mill <i>Goliath</i> frames a modern wind turbine 

on land reclaimed from the sea near Eemshaven in the Dutch 

province of Groningen. The 26-meter diameter Micon (250 kW) 

turbine is the last in a string of 40 identical turbines on 

an interior dike near the gas-fired power station at Eemshaven.<p>
