<p><strong>Why <a href="http://www.oit.doe.gov">Industrial</a> Energy Use Matters</strong>
</center>
<ul>
<lh></lh>
<li>U.S. industry accounts for 25 percent of the nation's gross national product (GNP) and 
about 75 percent of our exports.  It provided jobs for 25 million workers in 1993.  
Technologies that improve the productivity of U.S. industries improve the entire 
economy.

<li>Industries consume 36 percent of the energy used in the U.S., at a cost of $100 
billion each year.  This level of energy use provides great opportunities for savings.

<li>One example:  Industry spends $30 billion annually to operate 40 million electric 
motors spread throughout industrial and manufacturing operations.  Electric motors consume 
70 percent of all energy in a typical manufacturing facility.  These costs can be cut in 
half with efficient motor systems.

<li>Industries spend $45 billion per year to meet emissions control regulations.  Energy 
efficiency and renewable energy technologies can prevent pollution before it occurs, 
freeing industrial capital for more productive purposes.
</ul>

<p><strong><a href="http://www.oit.doe.gov">DOE's Programs</a> . . . </strong>
<ul>
<li>Improve industrial productivity and <a href="http://www.oit.doe.gov/Access/nice3">competitiveness 
through energy efficiency</a>.

<li>Help industries prevent pollution and reduce pollution control costs.

<li>Will save $5.8 billion for industry by 2005.

<li>Will create at least 57,000 jobs.

<li>Like the <a href="http://www.motor.doe.gov">motor challenge</a>, will generate energy cost savings of $1.2 billion and 
electricity savings of 25 billion kWh in the year 2000.
</ul>
