<h1>The Drug Policy Debate </h1>

<h3>Legalization? Decriminalization? Prohibition? </h3>

<h2>The Opinion Page </h2>

<hr>

<p><img src="golddot1.gif" width="22" height="22"><a
href="http://turnpike.net/~jnr/consvtvs.htm">Conservatives on
Prohibition </a>-- For all those who thought that drug policy
reform was a liberal cause - here are numerous statements from
leading conservatives. This section is maintained by Jim
Rosenfield. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/booklist.htm">Recent Comments on the War on
Drugs</a> - 25 pages of extracts from current books on various
topics related to the War on Drugs. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/abby.htm">Letter from Dear Abby</a> Dear Abby
says she supports the Hoover Resolution. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/dole.htm">Letter from Bob Dole</a> saying he

<p><a href="../debate/open_letter4.htm">Dispatch from the Drug

<p><a href="../Misc/roe1.htm">Why we should legalize drugs </a>by
Benson B. Roe, MD, Professor Emeritus and former Chair of
Cardiothoracic Surgery at the University of California at San
Francisco. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/buck1.htm">The Effects of Legalization </a>From
&quot;Ain't Nobody's Business If You Do&quot;, by Peter
McWilliams. It is a discussion between William F. Buckley, Jr.
and Professor Gazzaniga (the Andrew W. Thompson Jr. Professor of
Psychiatry [Neuroscience] at Dartmouth Medical School) which was
originally published in the February 5, 1990, issue of the
National Review. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/media2.htm">Perspectives On Drugs </a>- How
Legalization Would Cut Crime The no-win 'drug war' keeps driving
up the price. Users commit crimes to cover the cost. The public
is the loser. By Steven B. Duke. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/knapp1.htm">Dethrone the Drug Czar</a> by
Whitman Knapp, Federal Judge. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/coughlin.htm">Testimony of NY State
Corrections Commissioner Thomas A. Coughlin</a> </p>

<p>This is the testimony of the New York State Commissioner of
Corrections before the New York State Legislature regarding the
Rockefeller Drug Laws. Simply put, he thinks the current drug
policy is a dumb idea. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/lp-rld.htm">Should We Re-Legalize Drugs? </a>A
statement by the Libertarian Party of America </p>

<p><a href="../LIBRARY/major.htm">Why Do We Make Major Problems
out of Minor Drugs of Abuse?</a>, by Frederick H. Meyers, M.D.,
Professor of Pharmacology, University of California, San
Francisco, CA </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/drugwars.htm">Drug wars: Menace to America</a>
by Tod Mikuriya, M.D. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/vduc.htm">Medicalization of Drug Abuse
Control: </a>Pharmacy Based Voluntary Drug Users Co-operatives A
Proposal by Tod H. Mikuriya. M.D. </p>

<p><a href="../Misc/haans1.htm">Why is There a 'War on Drugs? </a>by
Dave Haans </p>

<p><a href="../GOVPUBS/wrong1.htm">The Wrong Message Of
Legalizing Illicit Drugs </a></p>

<p>This is a pamphlet put out by the Partnership for a Drug-Free
America which basically states that it is wrong to talk about
drug policy reform because even talking about it encourages drug
use. Now even ordinary discussion has become dangerous. </p>
