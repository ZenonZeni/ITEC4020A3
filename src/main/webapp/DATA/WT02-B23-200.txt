<h1 align=center>Cultural/Linguistic Strand</h1>

<hr width="75%">

<h4>W15 (Double Session)<br>
WEDNESDAY 10:15 AM-11:15 AM</h4>

<h3>Language, Linguistic Diversity and Learning Disabilities:<br>
How to Distinguish Differences</h3>

<b>Alba Ortiz, Ph.D.</b>, University of Texas, Austin, TX

<p>This workshop will offer an in-depth focus on procedures, practices,
policies and assessment considerations in distinguishing between language
based learning disabilities and a second language speaker.

<hr width="75%">

<h4> W33<br>
WEDNESDAY 1:30 PM-2:30 PM</h4>

<h3>Native American Giving Ceremony</h3>
<b>John R. Fredericks</b> (Tsinijinee-Navajo), The Howard
School, Atlanta, GA
<p>The ceremony teaches participants about the Native American Culture and
the art of giving. They will learn how to use the ceremony, in their
classroom, to teach the students or introduce the students to Native
American cultures. Numerous artifacts and resources will be available at
the session.

<hr width="75%">

<h4>W38<br>
WEDNESDAY 2:45 PM-3:45 PM</h4>

<h3>Students with Limited English Proficiency and Learning Disabilities:
<br>
A Guide for the Monolingual Special Educator </h3>

<b>Rita Brusca-Vega</b>, Northeastern Illinois University, Chicago, IL

<p>An introduction to issues and concerns in bilingual/ESL special
education including language acquisition and proficiency, legal
obligations, accurate assessment, service delivery models, and
instructional strategies. This presentation is designed for special
educators with little or no background in bilingual education or teaching
English as a second language who are serving an increasing number of
culturally and linguistically diverse students.

<hr width="75%">

<h4>F6<br>
FRIDAY 10:15 AM-11:15 AM</h4>

<h3>The LD/Bilingual Confusion:<br>
Sorting out Fact from Fiction</h3>

<b>Joan Caton</b>, National-Louis University, Wheaton, IL<br>
<b>Barbara Hudgens, Ph.D. </b>, National-Louis University, Evanston,
IL<br>
<b>Barbara Leys</b>, National-Louis University, Evanston, IL

<p>The profiles of individuals with learning disabilities and those with
normal second language acquisition are contrasted and compared through a
dual overhead presentation and sample case studies. Highlighted are
differences in test scores, learning styles, background history and
overall profile between these two types of student learners. 


<hr width="75%">

<h4>F43<br>
FRIDAY 3:00 PM-4:00 PM</h4>

<h3>A Longitudinal Study of Academic Language Proficiency and
Crosslinguistlc Transfer in Hispanic Students in a Dual Language
Program</h3>

<b>Margaret M. Beeman, Ph.D.</b>, Northwestern University, Evanston, IL 

<p>This session will present the rationale, methodology and results of a
bilingual study which investigated developmental patterns in listening and
reading comprehension in Hispanic middle school students. The curriculum
based tasks were conducted in Spanish and English in the fall and spring
of the fifth grade year. 

<hr width="75%>

<h4> F56<br>
FRIDAY 4:15 PM-5:15 PM</h4>

<h3>Implementing a Dual Language Program in an Elementary School with a
Predominantly Latino Student Population: Beginning Stages </h3>

<b>Karen Beeman</b>, St. Procopius School, Chicago, IL<br> 
<b>Margaret Beeman, Ph.D. </b>, Northwestern University, Evanston, IL

<p>This session will present an overview of the implementation of a dual
language program in an elementary school with a predominantly Latino
student population. The program objective is to increase the academic
achievement of the students in both English and Spanish. Preliminary
assessment measures will be reviewed.

<hr width="75%>

<h4>S6<br>
SATURDAY 8:30 AM-10:00 AM</h4>

<h3>Tailgate Workshops</h3>
<b>Phyllis Siegel</b>, Deerfield, IL<br>
<b>Raquel Martinez</b>, School Psychologist, Vernon Hills, IL

<p>Brats, beer, burgers. No! ... but "Tailgate Workshops" are one
elementary school's innovative approach to increase and empower
ethnic/language minority parents in the school/special education process.
Philosophy and format of "Tailgate Workshops" will be discussed and
critiqued versus traditional parental involvement approach.
