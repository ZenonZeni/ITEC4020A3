<H1>
Victoria's competitive strengths</h1>
<p>
<I><b>
<blockquote>
<h3>Victoria is ...</H3></A>
<P>
The industrial heartland of Australia, providing 32% of Australia's
total manufacturing output.
<P>
The hub of Australia's transport system with Australasia's largest general
cargo port, and an international airport operating 24 hours a day served
by 42 international airlines.
<P>
At the centre of the south-eastern region of Australia, which also
includes the cities of Sydney, Adelaide and Hobart, where 70% of
Australias population lives.
<P>
The base for six of Australias ten largest corporations, most of
Australias world-class medical research institutes and more than 1,000 food processing companies.
<P>
Ideally located on the Pacific Rim, the worlds fastest growing region.

<hr>
<P>
</I></b>
<H3>Victoria has ...</H3>
<P>
<I><b>
A highly skilled and educated workforce that successfully embraces 
world best practice principles.
<P>
Australia's lowest energy prices for large industrial consumers.
<P>
Well-serviced land close to ports and airports available for lease or 
freehold purchase.
<P>
Abundant competitively priced raw materials to support the food 
processing and minerals processing industries.
<P>
A standard of living and lifestyle that makes everyday business a pleasure.
</blockquote>
<BR></I></b>
<HR>
<BR>
<I>
The Victorian Government's 1995 <A HREF="../AM/index.html">Advantage Melbourne</A> Study confirms Melbourne's location as a low cost business location.
<P>
The Study demonstrates Melbourne's continuing strengths in areas requiring:
<P>
<ul>
<li>Creative, well-educated labour
<li>Access to competitively priced raw materials
<li>High quality, reliable physical infrastructure
<li>Reliable institutuional infrastructure
</ul>
<p>
The data confirms the narrowing of the cost advantage traditionally associated with emerging Asian markets.'
<p>
KPMG Management Consulting 1995
</I>
