<UL>
<LI>Keyword text searching 
<LI>Article and issue retrieval
<LI>Headline scanning
</UL><BR>

<H3><I>Keyword Text Searching</I></H3>
	
To retrieve information from NewsNet that covers a topic of interest, perform a keyword text search.  Start by clicking on the <I>Text Search</I> icon from the <I>Main Commands and Featured Services</I> screen. <P>

<A HREF="screen2.html"><IMG BORDER=0 ALIGN=LEFT SRC="../smtour.gif">  Click here if you wish to display the Text Search screen</A><P>

Create your search strategy by completing the<I> [Text Search</I>] screen that is displayed, using the following steps:
<OL>
<LI><I>Define Search Type</I> --  Boolean or concept<P>

<LI><I>Construct Keyword Phrase</I><P><P>

<LI><I>Select Search Fields</I> -- NewsNet allows you to decide where in the article your keywords will appear...all text, headlines only, headlines and lead paragraph only<P>

<LI><I>Choose Database Codes</I> -- Identify which titles to search for your topic<P>

<LI><I>Select a Date or Date Range</I> --  Select the section of the NewsNet database you would like searched according to date or date range.<P>

<LI><I>Conduct the Search</I> --  Click the OK button and Baton will initiate a sign on (if you aren't already connected) and begin processing your search.<P>

<LI><I>Output Display</I> --  The way your search results are displayed will depend upon which <I>Search Type</I> you selected.  For a concept search, a bar graph will be displayed which illustrates the relevancy ranking of the retrieved articles.  With a Boolean search, relevancy ranking becomes less meaningful since all articles will contain your keywords, so the graph is replaced by a simple notification of the number of articles retrieved.  
</OL><BR>

<H3><I>Article and Issue Retrieval</I></H3>

Each story and article contained in the NewsNet database is assigned an accession number, which appears with the headlines that result from a <I>Text Search, NewsFlash hits</I> as well as a headline<I> Scan</I>.  These numbers enable you to quickly retrieve articles of interest by simply inputting the accession number in the proper box displayed after clicking on the<I> Retrieve Articles</I> icon from Baton's main command screen.<P>

To retrieve the full issue of any publication on NewsNet, click on the <I>Retrieve Full Issues</I> icon of the <I>Main Commands and Featured Services</I> screen.  <I>The Retrieve Full Issues</I> screen requires that you simply type in the title code and date of the issue requested.  Click the OK button and NewsNet will retrieve and display the full issue.<P><BR>

<H3><I>Scanning Headlines and Wires</I></H3>

Just like perusing the table of contents of a book or magazine, NewsNet allows you to scan issue headlines.  Click on the<I> Scan Headlines</I> icon from the<I> Main Commands and Featured Services</I> screen.  Type in the title code(s) of the publication(s) whose headlines you wish to scan and the date of the issue you wish to scan.  For assistance selecting the title code(s), click the <I>List Sources</I> button to display the <I>Database Codes Selectio</I>n screen and double click on your selected titles. Database codes for the Scan Headlines option can consist of any combination of one or more title, industry, source, or category codes. <P>

The<I> Scan Wires</I> feature lets you browse through information from individual newswires by displaying a sequentially numbered list of headlines from the one or more wire services that you specify. If you find an article of interest, click on the headline to receive the full text.</MENU>
