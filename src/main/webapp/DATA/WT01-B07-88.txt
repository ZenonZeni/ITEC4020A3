<TD ALIGN=left VALIGN=top width=72%><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><center><hr><H2><FONT COLOR=#008000>What is the <I><B>DAIS Corporation</B></I> ?</FONT></CENTER></H2></center>

<HR>

<P>

<FONT SIZE=3 FACE=ARIAL>The<I><B> DAIS Corporation</B></I>

was formed in April of 1993. The sole mission of the company

is to <I><B>develop new polymeric materials, and to use these new

materials to manufacture and market a line of low cost electrical

power sources</B></I>. DAIS is a business incubator company associated with <B>Rensselaer

Polytechnic Institute</B>, Troy, New York USA. Click <A HREF="http://www.rpi.edu/dept/incubator/homepage/" ><B>here</B></a> for more information on <B>Rensselaer's business Incubator program</B>.</FONT></A><P></P>

<HR>

<DL>

<DT></font>To date, <I><B><FONT FACE="ARIAL">DAIS</FONT></B></I> has: 

</DL>

<MENU>

<LI><FONT SIZE=2 FACE=ARIAL><B>Developed a new ion conducting hydrocarbon 

based PEM membrane material. This material is known as the DAIS 585 Ionomer.<P></P>

<LI>Developed an electrode structure specifically engineered to work

with the DAIS 585 ion conducting membrane. The Membrane/Electrode Assembly (MEA)

is known as the DAIS POG.

<P></P>

<LI>Created two low cost housing structures designed for the DAIS POG. One 

design uses a passive air arrangement while the other uses an active air

design. Both are currently in proto-type stage. <P></P>

<LI>Filed and received a US Patent, and filed international patents

on the polymeric material discoveries<P></P>

<LI>Filed a <I><B>Continuation in Part</B></I> to the initial US Patent

adding additional data, applications, and support for the initial DAIS 585

patent filing.<P></P>

<LI>Received encouragement and support of new fuel cell technology from

experts at<I> <B>Los Alamos National Laboratory, the New York State Energy

Research and Development Authority (NYSERDA), and Giner, Inc.</B></I><P></P> 

<LI>Identified, and executed a plan to improve the lifetime

of the new DAIS POG materials</B></FONT> 

</MENU>



<HR>



<P>

<EM><FONT SIZE=3 FACE=ARIAL><B>The firm's flagship product (the DAIS POG) has run in excess

of 2,560 hours in its stated operating environment. The environment is to use air, 

and run at ambient temperature and pressure.</B>

<BR>

</EM>

<P>

<EM><FONT SIZE=3 FACE=ARIAL><B>Current power density of the <B><FONT COLOR=#008000>DAIS POG</FONT></B> has

yielded in excess of 165mW/cm<SUP><B>2</B></SUP> (air, 40 degrees C,

ambient pressure). Power density, stability and lifetime are the

continued focus of the <B><FONT COLOR=#008000>DAIS</FONT></B>/Rensselaer

team. Development is well underway to meet the Corporation's established

operational parameters which are outlined below:</B></I></I></EM>

<MENU>

<B>

<LI><FONT COLOR=#008000 size=2 FACE=ARIAL >Ambient pressure on the Cathode

<LI>35 to 50 &#176;C operation

<LI>No additional hydration of the reactant gases

<LI>Active controlled air flow on the cathode

<LI>Consistent power level of no less than

200mW/cm<SUP>2</SUP>

<LI>Power density of .17 to .23 kW/liter and

.45 to .61 kW/kg

<LI>Lifetime minimum of 2,500 hours per cell</B></FONT>
</MENU>
