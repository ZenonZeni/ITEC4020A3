<H2>Pantanal</H2>
</CENTER>

<P>
<BLOCKQUOTE><STRONG>Threats:</STRONG> <I>Pollution; poaching; large-scale development.</I> 

<P><IMG SRC="../../../RESOURCE/SLIDES/BRAZIL/Elder.jpg" ALT="Elder.jpg - 7.8 K" WIDTH=127 HEIGHT=200 HSPACE=10 VSPACE=10 BORDER=0 ALIGN=right>
The Pantanal is the largest and most significant wetland in the Neotro../resource/icons.  Covering 140,000 square kilometers, the region is home to vast floral and faunal diversity. 

<P>
Though the Brazilian government has pledged to &quot;leave
the river as it is,&quot;  Hydrovia, the International Development
Bank's river diversion scheme in the Pantanal wetlands, is still
under consideration, threatening severe environmental consequences.
 In 1995, CI board and staff members worked together to persuade
leading businessmen that more benign economic options exist for
the region, the largest wetlands in Latin America. 

<P>
With the debut
of a new informational documentary in July 1995, &quot;Voices
of the Pantanal -- The Hydrovia Story,&quot;  CI's International
Communications Program launched a nonpartisan public awareness
campaign to explore the potential impact of Hydrovia.


<P>
Another new program got off the ground in 1995: Support System
for Conservationist Farmers.  The Support System helps Pantanal
ranchers to move to ecologically sustainable land-use practices.
CI is also helping private landowners establish official private
reserves on their land, and devising management plans for those
reserves.    

<P>
The &quot;Train the Trainers&quot; Ecotourism Workshops
started in the Pantanal in 1995 and proved tremendously popular.
 CI has conducted an additional 19  workshops in ten Brazilian
states. The Brazilian government  has requested 20 more ecotourism
 workshops in 1996. 
</BLOCKQUOTE>
