<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>403(b) Salary Reduction Plan Questions and
Answers</STRONG></FONT><P>



<STRONG>Q:	Who can establish 403(b) plans?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:	</STRONG>Employees of public schools, colleges, universities,
hospitals,
churches,
and other non-profit organizations as recognized under Section 501(c)(3) of the
IRS Code.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	What is the limit on contributions to the plan?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Individuals may defer up to $9,500 annually, depending upon
the
contribution
formula used.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	When must the plan be established?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:	</STRONG>Prior to the pay period in which the salary reduction is to
take place.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	When must contributions be made?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Each pay period as indicated in the salary deferral agreement
or within a
taxable year for which the contribution is to be counted.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	Is the employer a fiduciary?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	No, as long as individual participants exercise control over
the assets.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	Is plan administration required (e.g. annual reports to the
IRS)?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	No, unless employer contributions are made.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	What are the eligibility requirements for participants?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	All employees must be eligible if the 403(b) is a salary
reduction plan
only.  If
the plan allows for employer contributions, eligibility rules will be similar to
those for qualified plans.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	What are the vesting requirements?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:	</STRONG>There's immediate 100% vesting of employee deferrals.
	<P>
<STRONG>Q:	When can money come out of the plan?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Generally, at retirement, at termination of employment, in
case of death, in
case of disability.
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	How are distributions taxed?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	They're taxed as ordinary income.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	Are loans available?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Yes, if the employer's plan document allows for them. 
<P>
<STRONG>Q: 	Are annual contributions required?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	No.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	How are contributions/benefits determined?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:	</STRONG>Contributions are determined annually by the employee, subject
to certain
maximum amounts.
