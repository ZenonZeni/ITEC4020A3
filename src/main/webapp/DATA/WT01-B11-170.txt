<B>BURNING GUIDELINES
<P>
FACT SHEET</B></CENTER><P>

Changes to the Forest Fires Prevention Act will simplify the regulation and use of fire by the public, and reduce the number of permits required for burning.<P>

Changes to the Act are as follows:<P>

<B>A permit will not be required to burn piled wood, brush, or discarded wood by-products if:</B><P>

<UL>
<LI>the material to be burned is in a pile less than two metres high and less than two metres in diameter;
<LI>the fire is started no sooner than two hours before sunset and extinguished two hours after sunrise or earlier;
<LI>a responsible person is available to tend the fire, and the person tending the fire has tools or water to contain the fire;
<LI>the fire is two metres away from any flammable material.<P>
</UL>


<B>A permit will not be required to burn grass or leaves if:</B><P>
<UL>
<LI>the total area to be burned does not exceed one hectare;
<LI>the length of the flaming edge does not exceed 30 metres;
<LI>the fire is started no sooner than two hours before sunset and extinguished two hours after sunrise or earlier;
<LI> a responsible person is available to tend the fire, and the person tending the fire has tools or water to contain the fire.<P>
</UL>


<B>A permit will not be required for incinerators if they are:</B><P>
<UL>
<LI>closed devices;
<LI>at least five metres from any forest;
<LI>at least two metres from flammable material;
<LI> covered by a screen with mesh less than five millimetres in size;
<LI>tended by a responsible person until the fire is out.
</UL>

<B>A permit will not be required for:</B><P>
<UL>
<LI>fires established for cooking or to provide warmth;
<LI>travel within a restricted fire zone.
</UL>
<P>

 
<B>Permits will not be required for:</B><P>

Logging, mining, land clearing, dam construction, operating a mill or any other activity liable to cause the accumulation of slash or debris.  Information and operating conditions will be included in authorizations granted under other authorities, such as the Crown Forests Sustainability Act, Public Lands Act, and the Mining Act.<P>

<B>Permits will continue to be issued for the following:</B><P>
<UL>
<LI>all industrial slash pile burning operations;
<LI>prescribed burns for site preparation, ecological maintenance and rehabilitation or other similar purpose;
<LI>all other fires.
</UL><P><BR>
