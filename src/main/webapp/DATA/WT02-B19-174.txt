<TD ALIGN=LEFT WIDTH=315><H2> CEC History </H2> 
</TD> 
</TR> 
</TABLE>

<center><P>
<TABLE WIDTH=475 BORDER=0 CELLPADDING=0 CELLSPACING=0> 
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1922</td>
<td width=350 align=left><td>A group of administrators and supervisors attending the summer session at Teachers College,
      Columbia University and their faculty members organized the International Council for the Education of Exceptional Children.  Elizabeth E. Farrell was the Founder and first President, 1922-26.<br><BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125VALIGN=top>1923</td><td width=350 align=left><td>  The Council Newsletter was initiated and issued as news was acquired and as funds permitted.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1923</td><td width=350 align=left><td>      The first annual meeting of The International Council for the Education of Exceptional Children was held
 in Cleveland, Ohio.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1926 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>     M. LaVinia Warner was first Executive Secretary.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1933  </td><td width=350 align=left><td>   "The Education of" was eliminated from the name.  <U>The Council Newsletter</U> ended with the loss of the Council's funds in the bank crash of 1933.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1934</td><td width=350 align=left><td>      The Council appropriated its total cash balance of $150 to start a publication known as the <U>Council Review</U>.  This was abandoned after one year, and a contract was let for <U>The Journal of Exceptional Children</U>.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1941</td><td width=350align=left><td>      A merger of "ICEC" with the Special Education Department of the NEA resulted in the Council's becoming a department of The National Education Association.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1943 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>     Delegates approved bylaws amendments to give chapters representation in the Delegate Assembly.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1949-1961</td><td width=350 align=left><td>      Harley Z. Wooden served as CEC Executive Director.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1950 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>     A Council headquarters office in the NEA building was established.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1951  </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    "The Journal of" was dropped so that the journal became designated as <U>Exceptional Children</U>.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1953  </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    The Council's constitution was amended to provide for federations of local chapters--an expansion to the state or provincial level of organizations.  The constitution was revised to provide for divisions within CEC.  The first two divisions were CASE and TED.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1958</td><td width=350 align=left><td>    The word "international" was dropped from the CEC name.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1958 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>     CEC established the Committee on Canadian Affairs, later called the CEC Canadian Committee.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1961-1979 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>     William C. Geer served as Executive Director of CEC.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1961</td><td width=350  align=left><td>      CEC grew to an organization of approximately 14,000 members with 300 local chapters, 10 state or provincial branches and 28 federations.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1963 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>     The CEC Board of Governors adopted statements of purpose.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1963 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>     The Wallin Award was established to recognize a professional who had made an outstanding contribution to the education of children with exceptionalities.<p>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1968-1969</td><td width=350  align=left><td>      <i>TEACHING Exceptional Children</i> was initiated.  <U>Update</U> was first published.  <U>Special Education in Canada</i> was published for Canadian CEC members.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1969</td><td width=350 align=left><td>     CEC Headquarters was moved to Jefferson Plaza, Arlington, VA, from the NEA Building in Washington, DC.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1969 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>     The Council became incorporated under the laws of the District of Columbia pursuant of Articles of Incorporation filed on June 17, 1969.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1970  </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    The Delegate Assembly authorized a change in the CEC membership year to any 12 month period.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1971   </td><td width=350  align=left><td>   The Delegate Assembly approved two policy statements:  "Basic Commitments and Responsibilities to Exceptional Children" and "Policy Statement of Governmental Affairs."  The Foundation for Exceptional Children (FEC) was authorized by the Delegate assembly.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1972 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>     The Delegate Assembly approved sending both CEC publications to all CEC members.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1972  </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    The Articles of Incorporation and Bylaws were amended to allow high school students to be associate members of CEC.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1973 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>     The Delegate Assembly approved two policy statements:  "The Organization and Administration of Special Education" and "Education of the Gifted."<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1974 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>   CEC moved to its new headquarters building in Reston, VA.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1975 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    The Board of Governors established the CEC Award for Excellence program.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1978 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>     The Delegate Assembly approved two policy statements:  "Policy Statement on Career Education" and "Minorities Position Policy Statement."<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1979-1992 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    Jeptha V. Greer served as Executive Director of CEC.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1984</td><td width=350  align=left><td>      The Clarissa Hug Teacher of the Year Award was initiated by Delegate Assembly action.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1986 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>    Three unit awards were adopted for:  (1) Public Relations Achievement; (2) Leadership Training Achievement; (3) Programming Achievement.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1987 </td><td width=375 align=left><td>    CEC Standing Committees restructured to be implemented July 1, 1988.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1987</td><td width=350  align=left><td>    Full-time Canadian Office established.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1987 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>    Common currency of fees collected approved, beginning July 1, 1988.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1988 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    CEC divisions became open for international membership enrollments.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1988 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>    The Outstanding Contributor Award was approved by Delegate Assembly action.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1988</td><td width=350  align=left><td>     The U.S. Internal Revenue Service granted CEC 501(c)(3) tax exempt status.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1989</td><td width=350 align=left><td>    CEC Research Award approved by Delegate Assembly action, to be implemented in 1991.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1990 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    Delegates approved an amendment making both <i>Exceptional Children</i> and <i>TEACHING Exceptional Children</i> official journals.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1992 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>    Subdivisions become eligible to apply for CEC unit status.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1992</td><td width=350  align=left><td>     Associate membership category for parents and paraprofessionals established by the Delegate Assembly.  High School Student membership changed to separate category.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1992-1994</td><td width=350  align=left><td>     George E. Ayers served as Executive Director of CEC.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1993</td><td width=350  align=left><td>     A new Mission Statement was adopted by the Delegate Assembly in San Antonio.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1993 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    The Delegate Assembly in San Antonio approved the policies on Inclusion and Physical Intervention.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1994 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>   The Delegate Assembly in Denver passed the Resolution Regarding CEC's Support of Human Rights.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1994 </td><td width=350 align=left><td>    <i>CEC Today</i> newsletter premiered at the CEC Annual Convention in Denver.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1994</td><td width=350 align=left><td>    Nancy D. Safer appointed Interim Executive Director of CEC.<BR>
<BR>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1995 </td><td width=350  align=left><td>    The Delegate Assembly in Indianapolis approved changes in the CEC governance structure.<P>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1996</td><td width=350  align=left><td>  Nancy D. Safer appointed Executive Director of CEC.<P>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125  VALIGN=top>1996</td><td width=350  align=left><td> Policies on Discipline and the Use of Interpreters/Translators with Culturally/Linguistically Diverse Students Other Than Hearing Impaired adopted by the Delegate Assembly in Orlando.<P>
<TR><TD WIDTH=125 VALIGN=top>1996</td><td width=350  align=left><td> <I>CEC Today</I> was made an official CEC publication.<P>
</td></tr></table>
