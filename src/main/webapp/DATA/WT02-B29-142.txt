<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>Can I Deduct My IRA Contributions?</STRONG></FONT><P>



Many people can defer either all or a portion of their IRA contributions on their
tax return.  Deductibility
depends on you or your spouse's participation in certain employer-sponsored
retirement plans, such
as pension and profit sharing plans.  Generally, if your spouse is covered, you are
considered covered
for IRA deductibility purposes.  You can use the following chart to determine if
your IRA contributions can
be deducted.

<P>

If you qualify for a partial deduction, use the following worksheet to help determine
your actual
deduction.  Please consult your tax advisor before you make any investment
decisions with tax consequences.
<P>

<STRONG>IRA Deduction Worksheet</STRONG> - Example<BR>
<P>



<TABLE WIDTH=500 CELLPADDING=5>

<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	1. Enter $50,000 if you file jointly, or $35,000 if you are single
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	$50,000
	</TD>
</TR>
<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	2. Enter your adjusted gross income
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	$43,820
	</TD>
</TR>
<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	3. Subtract line 2 from line 1
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	$6,180
	</TD>
</TR>
<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	4. Multiply line 3 by .20
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	$1,236
	</TD>
</TR>
<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	5. Round up line 4 to the nearest $10
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	$1,240
	</TD>
</TR>
</TABLE>
<P>

If the number on line 4 or 5 is:<BR>

<UL>
<LI>$2,000 or more, your maximum tax-deductible contribution is $2,000, or 100%
of
your earned
income, whichever is less.

<LI>$200, your maximum tax-deductible contribution is $200. 

<LI>More than $200, but
less than $2,000, this
is the maximum tax-deductible contribution you are entitled to make.

</UL>
<P>
<FONT SIZE="-1">Note: For a Spousal IRA, to determine the total tax-deductible
amount that you and
your spouse may invest in your respective IRAs, simply recalculate lines 3-5, using
.225 on line
4.</FONT>
<P>

Remember, even if you can deduct only a portion of your IRA contribution, you
should still invest the
balance, up to the maximum limit of $2,000 of $2,250 for a Spousal IRA, as a
non-deductible
contribution.  This will allow you to maximize your retirement savings and benefit
from the potential of
tax-deferred growth.
