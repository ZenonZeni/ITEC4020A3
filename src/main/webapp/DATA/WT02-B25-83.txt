<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<p align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)</b></font></p>
<hr>
<p><font color="#FF0000">What is SAD? </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - a condition marked by seasonal depression </p>
<p>2 - an illness primarily affecting women </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Symptoms </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - periods of depression &amp; normal mood that accompany seasonal changes </p>
<p>2 - recurrent episodes of depression in certain months of the year (usually during 
the fall &amp; winter) </p>
<p>3 - usually surface in childhood or adolescence </p>
<p>4 - insomnia </p>
<p>5 - carbohydrate craving </p>
<p>6 - weight gain </p>
<p>7 - decreased sexual appetite </p>
<p>8 - lethargy </p>
<p>9 - hopelessness </p>
<p>10 - suicidal thoughts </p>
<p>11 - social withdrawal </p>
<p>12 - summer depression </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. decreased appetite </p>
<p>b. weight loss </p>
<p>c. constant agitation or anxiety </p>
</blockquote>
<p>13 - winter depression </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. between October/November &amp; March/April </p>
<p>b. depressions are usually mild to moderate (but can be severe) </p>
</blockquote>
<p>14 - strong reactions to changes in environmental light (react poorly to overcast 
days &amp; decreased indoor lighting) </p>
<p>15 - walking around the house turning on lights </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Treatment </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - SAD is often misdiagnosed as a viral infection </p>
<p>2 - light therapy </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. bright or fluorescent light reduces winter depression symptoms </p>
<p>b. reduces the level of the neurotransmitter melatonin normally 
present at night </p>
<p>c. 2 hours of treatment in the morning (light at night causes insomnia) </p>
<p>d. side effects may result (irritability, eye strain, headaches, mania) </p>
<p>e. schedule time outdoors in sunlight each day </p>
</blockquote>
<p>3 - antidepressant medications (Elavil &amp; Imipramine) </p>
<p>4 - monoamine exidase inhibitors (MAOIs) (Nardil &amp; Parnate) </p>
<p>5 - lithium </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">For more information </font></p>

and</u> <u>How to Overcome It</u>. Guilford Press, 1993.</p>
</blockquote>
