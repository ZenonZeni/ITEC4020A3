<h3>CALSTART</h3><p>

<blockquote>

<B>Project Title: Lifecycle Testing of Composite Flywheel Energy Storage Systems<p>
Performing Organization: U.S. Flywheel Systems, Inc.</B><p>

<P>Technical Contact: Mahesh Talwar  (805) 961-8922</P>

<P><B>Program Description</B></P>

<P>U.S. Flywheel Systems has developed a high-energy density composite rotor flywheel energy storage system, with a projected capability of enduring 10,000 charge/discharge cycles (the equivalent of approximately 27 years of normal operations).  This project will test a sample of four flywheel modules under controlled accelerated cycling regimes, to determine the actual durability and failure modes of these energy storage systems.</P>

<P>The flywheel modules developed by U.S. Flywheel Systems (USFS) have the following characteristics:</P>


<BLOCKQUOTE><P><TABLE CELLPADDING=2>
<TR><TD>Specific Power</TD><TD>2000 W/kg</TD></TR>
<TR><TD>Specific Energy</TD><TD>150 W-hr/kg</TD></TR>
<TR><TD>Energy Density</TD><TD>4.4 k Wh</TD></TR>
<TR><TD>Recharge Time</TD><TD>10-20 minutes</TD></TR>
</TABLE></P></BLOCKQUOTE>

<P>In this year-long program, USFS will use a proprietary state-of-the-art data acquisition system, using 125 sensors and data channels per flywheel, to measure 3-dimensional strain profiles under repeated cyclic testing, and to attempt to predict material yield prior to system failure.
</P>
<P><B>Milestones</B></P>

<P>The modules will be tested intensively until failure or five months of consecutive cycling are accomplished.</P>

<P><B>Participants</B></P>	

<P><UL>
<LI>Ashman Technology, Inc. — flywheel motors
<LI>AVCON, Inc. — magnetic bearings
<LI>Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency — co-funder
<LI>Power R&D — power electronics
<LI>U.S. Flywheel Systems — program manager</UL></P>
	
</blockquote>
