Joan Feigenbaum received a BA in Mathematics from Harvard and a PhD in<br>
Computer Science from Stanford.  She is currently a member of the<br>
Computing Principles Research Department of AT&amp;T Bell Laboratories in<br>
Murray Hill, NJ.  Her research interests are in computational<br>
complexity theory, cryptography and security, and graph theory and<br>
applications.  Within the security area, she is now working on a<br>
system for managing trust relationships in a large, heterogeneous<br>
electronic marketplace and on techniques for preserving the privacy of<br>
both owners and users of data in information retrieval services.  She<br>
is on the editorial boards of the SIAM Journal on Computing, the<br>
Journal of Algorithms, and the Journal of Cryptology.<br>
