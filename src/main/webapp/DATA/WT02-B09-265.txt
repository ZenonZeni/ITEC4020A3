<H2>Extention Education</H2>

<H2>Open Learning Agency	General Info: 431-3000</H2>
4355 Mathissi Place	Toll Free: 1-800-663-1663<BR> 
Burnaby  V5G 4S8<BR>
Course Info: 431-3300<BR>
Toll Free: 1-800-663-9711<BR>
Fax: 431-3333<BR><BR>

Funded by the provincial government, the Open Learning Agency offers high<BR>
 school completion, college and university courses and programs that lead to<BR>
 recognized certificates, diplomas and degrees. Courses are offered through distance<BR>
 education, so students can learn in their home. The Agency's Credit Bank allows<BR>
 students to transfer formal credit from other recognized educational institutions. <BR>
Challenge credit and experiential learning assessments are also available. <BR>
The Knowledge Network is the educational television service of the<BR>
 Open Learning Agency. The Knowledge Network broadcasts programs in<BR>
 support of some open learning courses, as well as general education<BR>
 programming for adults and children.<BR><BR>

<H2>SFU Women's Centre	291-3670</H2>
c/o Simon Fraser Student Society <BR>
Simon Fraser University<BR>
Burnaby  V5A 1S6<BR>
Fax: 291-5843<BR><BR>

A resource and drop-in centre for campus women (students, staff and faculty).<BR>
 Lounge is open 24 hours a day. Library/resource office is open, at a minimum, <BR>
Tuesday to Thursday. Library materials available for loan. <BR>
(Men may access the library via campus PIRG office at 291-4360).<BR>
 Resource office provides information and referrals. Also offers <BR>
discussion/support groups, workshops, special events and advocacy.<BR>
 The Centre is run by a collective: Campus women are welcome to join at any time.<BR><BR>

<H2>Women's Resources Centre	482-8585</H2>
UBC Centre for Continuing Education<BR> 
1-1144 Robson Street<BR>
Vancouver  V6E 1B2<BR>
Fax: 482-8591<BR><BR>

A free drop-in centre providing life and career planning for women and men<BR>
 who want information, discussion and support in assessing their current life situation <BR>
 and ways to change it if needed. A phone-in referral service.  A variety of career and<BR>
 personal development programs taught by professional counsellors and<BR>
 instructors include: vocational testing, resume consultation, life/work planning, <BR>
assertiveness training, building self-esteem, career change, communications skills, etc. Costs vary.<BR><BR>
