<dt><a href="http://www.enc.org/enctext.htm"><b>Eisenhower National Clearinghouse for Mathematics and Science Education</b></a> <b>(ENC)</b><br>
</dt>
</center></div>
<dd>Funded through a contract with the U. S. Department of Education to provide K-12 teachers with a central source of 
information on mathematics and science curriculum materials. ENC was established in 1992 at The Ohio State 
University and is located in Columbus, Ohio.<br>
<img src="id.gif" align=bottom width=14 height=14>The purpose of the Eisenhower National Clearinghouse is to encourage the adoption and use of K-12 curriculum 
materials and programs which support state and national efforts to improve teaching and learning in mathematics and 
science. It provides better access to resources by creating, maintaining, and cataloging a comprehensive, multi-media 
collection of materials and programs. <br>
<img src="id.gif" align=bottom width=14 height=14>The ENC catalog and other products are distributed nationally using both traditional formats and advanced 
computing and telecommunications technologies. Specifically, ENC provides the following products and services: 
<a href="http://www.ed.gov/programs.html#map">Educational Resources by State</a>. <br>
<img src="id.gif" align=bottom width=14 height=14>Many of the services and resources administered by the Department are best approached through state and regional 
contacts such as the Department's Regional Offices, Regional Educational Laboratories, and various technical 
assistance centers and resource centers. Use this U.S. map to click on a state to see what resources are available. <p align=center>(If your browser does not support image maps, or if you are looking for an area not pictured, such as one of the Trust 
Territories or Puerto Rico, try the following area list.)</p>
