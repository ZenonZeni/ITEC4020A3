<h1>Consumer Price Index-<font size="5">All Urban Consumers </font></h1>

<hr>

<p>Not Seasonally Adjusted ----- U.S. City Average All Items
----- Base Period : 1982-84=100</p>

<p>The Consumer Price Index is compiled by the Bureau of Labor
Statistics and is based upon a 1982 Base of 100. An Index of 158
indicates 58% inflation since 1982, the commonly quoted inflation
rate of say 3% is actually the change in the index from a year
earlier. By looking at the index we can see that what would have
cost an average of 9.9 cents in 1913 would cost us about $1.58 in
1996. </p>
