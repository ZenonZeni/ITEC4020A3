<p><a href="heroalc.htm">The Role of Ethanol Abuse</a> in the
Etiology of Heroin-Related Deaths --- This is a series of
excerpts from an article from the Center for Disease Control,
discussing the fact that many heroin &quot;overdoses&quot; are
actually the result of the use of alcohol and heroin at the same
time. Thus, many &quot;overdoses&quot; might be avoided simply by
telling people not to use the drugs together. </p>

<h2>Historical Articles on the Opiates </h2>

<p><a href="opichin1.htm">OPIUM IN CHINA (1700-1860)</a> </p>

<p><a href="lintse1.htm">Letter from Lin Tse-hsu to Queen
Victoria </a>On The Opium Trade, 1839 </p>

<p><a href="opiwar1.htm">A Short History of the Chinese Opium
Wars</a> </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/dequinc1.htm">Confessions of an English
Opium Eater</a> by Thomas de Quincey -- This is the famous essay
on opium from the early 1800's. </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/nytop2.txt">AN &quot;OPIUM JOINT&quot;
RAIDED</a>. The New York Times September 28, 1891, p. 2. A quaint
article about the opium problem in the 19th century. </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/opiapm.txt">Alcohol and Opium Compared: </a>Opinions
of over 100 Physicians on the use of Opium in China -- Compiled
by William Hector Park, M.D., American Presbyterian Mission
Press, Shanghai, 1899 </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/opihar.txt">What Shall They Do to Be
Saved?, </a>Harper's magazine 35, Aug. 1867, pp. 377-87. An
article about the evils of opium addiction. </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/opiny.txt">The Demon of the Orient </a>Allen
S. Williams, New York: &quot;Published by the Author,&quot; 1883 </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/opiout.txt">The Warfare Against
Opium-Smoking in America</a> Outdoor, Feb. 6, 1909, vol. 91, p275
</p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/opips.txt">How the Opium Habit is Acquired
</a>by Virgil G. Eaton, The Popular Science Monthly 33, Sept.
1888, 663-67 </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/nytop1a.txt">An Enterprising Turk</a>, The
New York Times July 1, 1890, p. 8 -- This is a story about how a
Turk decided to sell diluted opium and was quickly driven out of
business by public exposure. </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/nytop3.txt">IN THE LAND OF OPIUM </a>. -
THE IMMENSE AREAS GIVEN TO THE GROWTH OF THE POPPY The New York
Times March 29, 1896, p. 28 </p>

<p>The Drug's History Traced from the Plant to the Pipe--The
Scenes in the Indian Fields--Processes Through Which the Opium
Goes--The Monopoly of Finishing for Smoking in the Hands of
Chinese--View of a Lucknow Smoking Den. </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/rorop4.txt">The San Francisco Opium Joints
</a>-- The Review of Reviews 5 June (1892), p. 604 </p>

<p>A lurid description of the San Francisco opium dens. It is
worth noting that San Francisco had passed ordinances outlawing
opium smoking in opium dens some 17 years before this article was
written. </p>

<p><a href="../HISTORY/saop5.txt">THE OPIUM INDUSTRY IN AMERICA </a>by
C.F. HOLDER Scientific American 1898, p. 147 This is a review of
the opium industry as it existed in the late 19th century. </p>
