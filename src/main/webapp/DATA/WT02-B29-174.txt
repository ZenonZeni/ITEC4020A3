<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>Option 3</STRONG></FONT><BR>
<STRONG>Indirect Rollover IRA - Take the Money for Up To 60 Days, Then Deposit it Into an
IRA</STRONG><P>

If you intend to roll over your retirement savings plan's distribution, but receive a check
made payable to you, that is considered an "indirect" rollover. Any distribution done in this
manner will have 20% withheld automatically, so you'll only receive 80% of the money. (This
differs from the "direct" rollover. With a direct rollover, the check must be made out to the
custodian of a rollover IRA, not to you. Also, the direct rollover offers protection against
the automatic tax withholding, while the indirect rollover will not, unless you take the special
steps described
below.)<P>
To protect the entire amount of your indirect rollover from current taxes and a possible
penalty, you must come up with the withheld amount from another source to bring your IRA
deposit up to the full amount of your original distribution. If your distribution is large
(and hopefully it is), the shortfall created by this withheld amount may be difficult to
fund.<P>
If you deposit into your IRA only the amount you actually received, the 20% withheld is
taxable as current income, and may be subject to a 10% penalty as an early withdrawal as
well.<P>
You must also remember that you have only 60 calendar days from the day that you receive
your
check to deposit your distribution into your IRA. Otherwise, the entire amount may be taxable
as current income for the year in which you received it.
