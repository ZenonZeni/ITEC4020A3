<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>How Should You Receive Your Money When You Leave a Company?</STRONG></FONT><P>

Whether you are retiring, taking a job with another company, or leaving your company for some other reason, there are extremely important decisions for you to make.
<P>
One of the most important involves the retirement plan money that is yours to take when you leave your company.  This sum of money is called a distribution, and may be the single largest sum you will ever receive at one time.
<P>
The choices you make now regarding that money will affect not only your current and future income, but your income tax obligations as well.  What you do with this money may determine your financial well-being, now, and well into the future.
<P>
There are two major decisions you have to make regarding this distribution:<P>
<UI>
<li>How should you take your money from your employer?
<LI>What should you do with it then?
</ui>
<P>
Both of these decisions are very important and somewhat complex.  This section of the Web site was written to help you work through the first question so that you have information to help you make a decision that is right for you.  Of course you should also consult your tax advisor. Answers to the second question are explored in:  <EM><A HREF="prkric.html">Basic Investing Concepts</A></EM>.
