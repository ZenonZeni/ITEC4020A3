<h3><font color="#800000" face="Arial">16.000 Miles Medical
Rescue Mission In The South Atlantic</font></h3>

<p><font face="Arial"><strong>PORT STANLEY. (MP).-</strong> What
is believed to be the longest ever medical rescue mission took
place in the South Atlantic. </font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">An American nine seats, twin engined Lear
Jet made a 36 hours flight from Jacksonville, Florida to the
Falklands and back to pick up an 82 years old American who broke
his hip on a cruise ship.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">The &quot;<em>Society Explorer</em>&quot;
sailing for the South Georgia islands was surprised by a storm
and fierce seas in the middle of the South Atlantic leaving
several people injured and forced to divert to the Falklands.<strong><img
src="images/jacksonville.gif" align="right" hspace="0"
width="259" height="145"></strong></font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">The Lear Jet refueled in Rio do Janeiro
before landing at RAF's military base in Falklands and the
following morning made the 18 hours return trip to Jacksonville:
a total of 16.000 miles.</font></p>
