<h1>Demographic Profile</h1>

<h2>Location, Location, Location:</h2>

<ul>

<li>Sacramento is located in the heart of Northern California and is recognized
nationally as the fastest growing city of the 1990's.

<p><li>The metropolitan Sacramento area boasts a population of over 1.5 million
with projections indicating growth to nearly 4.6 million by the year 2000.

<p><li>With a low cost of living, Sacramentans have the second highest
spendable income in the U.S. with a strong federal, state and local
governmental workforce providing a substantial and continuous base for the
local economy.

<p><li>Sacramento's national profile has contributed to the successful location
of business to the market including
<a href=http://www.packardbell.com>Packard Bell</a>,
<a href=http://www.hp.com>Hewlett Packard</a> and
<a href=http://www.intel.com>Intel Corporation</a>.

<p><li>Centrally located with easy access within 90 minutes to nearby
recreational areas including Lake Tahoe, The Delta, the scenic Mother Lode,
Napa Valley and San Francisco Bay Area.

</ul>

<h2>Extensive Transportation:</h2>

<ul>

<li>Two major north-south freeways, Interstate 5 and Highway 99, cross paths
downtown with the major east-west routes: Interstate 80, the Business 80
offshoot and Highway 50.

<p><li>Regional Transit, the major public transit system, provides bus lines,
light rail lines and trains to over 23,000 commuters.

<p><li>Sacramento Metropolitan Airport, located 15 minutes from downtown
Sacramento and just 5 minutes from ARCO Arena.

<p><li>An extensive ground transportation network of limousines, courtesy
shuttles and private vehicles between venues and hotel facilities is available
to accommodate major sports events and conventions.

</ul>

<h2>Hotel Accommodations:</h2>

<ul>

<li>Over 28 hotels in the Sacramento area provide over 12,000 rooms to
accommodate sports events and conventions within a 10 mile radius and 5 to 15
minutes of downtown Sacramento.

<p><li>Over 2,000 dining establishments provide a generous sampling of cuisine
to suit sports event and convention patrons.

</ul>

<h2>An Ideal Sports Climate:</h2>

<ul>

<li>May - October: Warm, dry season

<p><li>November - April: Cool with a brief rainy season

</ul>

Low relative humidity: Approximately 25 percent throughout the year

<h2>19th Largest Nationally Ranked Media Market:</h2>

<ul>

<li>Network affiliates: NBC, ABC, CBS, FOX, Warner Bros. and Paramount
<p><li>Cable systems
<p><li>Over 25 AM/FM radio stations
<p><li>Print: McClatchy-owned Sacramento Bee and a network of over 30 regional
weekly and monthly publications

</ul>

<h2>Sacramento, The Adventure Capital:</h2>

Sacramento's history is well documented, creating the
focus for its attraction as a tourism destination site:

<ul>
<li><a href=http://www.oldsacramento.com>Historic Old Sacramento</a>,
A National Registered Landmark: Historic business,
residential, shopping and dining district along the Sacramento River.

<p><li>California State Capitol: The focus of a $70 million restoration in the
1980's, the building reflects the beauty and grandeur of the early 1900's.

<p><li><a href=http://www.csrmf.org/>
California State Railroad Museum</a>: The largest railroad museum in North
America featuring 21 restored locomotives and cars, 46 exhibits and rides on a
steam powered train.

<p><li>Downtown Plaza/K Street Mall: The fourteen block walking mall features
shopping, entertainment and nightlife, the Thursday Night Public Market and a
variety of special events.

<p><li>California State Fair: The late-summer event has been a tourism
tradition in Sacramento for decades, drawing over 750,000 patrons annually.

<p><li>A Multitude Of Other Tourism Attractions Include:
<a href=http://www.classicar.com/museums/toweford/toweford.htm>
Towe Ford Museum</a>,
Sutter's Fort and Indian Museum, Crocker Art Museum and the Governor's Mansion.

</ul>

<h2>Sacramento, The Sports Capital</h2>

Sacramento maintains the perfect combination of support for all major sports
events: The Sacramento Sports Commission, government and community business
leaders and grassroots volunteers.
