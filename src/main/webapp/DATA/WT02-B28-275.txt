<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>Simplified Employee Pension Plans
(SEP)</STRONG></FONT><P>


<STRONG>An Ideal Plan For Small And Medium Sized
Businesses</STRONG><BR>
A SEP is a simplified retirement plan, which allows employers to
make contributions
directly to their
employees' own Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs).  Employers
receive a tax
deduction for the full
amount of each contribution.
<P>
Because of its simplicity, the SEP is an excellent low-cost
alternative to a
pension or profit sharing plan,
allowing small and medium-sized businesses to control the cost of
providing a
retirement plan.
<P>
The IRS provides an approved document and there is no expensive,
time consuming
annual reporting
to either the IRS or the Department of Labor.  But what are the
specific benefits
to you?
<P>

<STRONG>Your Benefits With A SEP</STRONG><BR>
All contributions you make to your employees' SEP, up to the
lesser of 15% of each
employee's
compensation or $30,000<SUP><FONT SIZE="-1"><A
HREF="#1">1</A></FONT></SUP>, are
tax
deductible business expenses.
<P>
As an employee of your company, you are also entitled to set up a
SEP for your
benefit.
<P>
Each year, you determine the amount, if any, to be contributed to
the plan for that
year.
<P>
All earnings in a SEP accumulate tax-deferred and provide retirement
income in addition
to Social Security
benefits.
<P>
A SEP is an excellent incentive for attracting qualified
employees, reducing
turnover, and retaining
those
with proven ability and experience.
<P>
A SEP is easy to establish and understand, with minimal
recordkeeping and
government reporting.
<P>
<BR>
<FONT SIZE="-1"><SUP><FONT SIZE="-1"><A
NAME="1"><STRONG>1</STRONG></A></FONT></SUP> Only the first
$150,000 of compensation is considered, limiting the maximum
contribution to $22,500.</FONT>
<P>
<BR>

<P>
<STRONG>Fund Your SEP  With Mutual Funds</STRONG><BR>
Mutual funds can be excellent investments for your SEP. Many
mutual fund families
offer a wide
range of investment opportunities, designed to meet the financial
goals of
virtually every type of investor.
<P>
Since a SEP allows employees to select their investments, the
well-defined
investment objectives
and flexibility of mutual funds make them an ideal investment
option for a SEP. 
Moreover, mutual funds
are
professionally managed and provide you with diversified
investments.
<P>
<STRONG>SEP Highlights</STRONG><BR>
<P>
<STRONG>Employer Eligibility</STRONG><BR>
Incorporated or unincorporated businesses of self-employed
individuals can set up a
SEP.
<P>
<STRONG>Employee Eligibility</STRONG><BR>
Employees 21 years or older with any service in 3 of the
immediately preceding 5
years and who
earned
at least $400 (indexed annually).
<P>
<STRONG>Tax Deductible Contributions</STRONG><BR>
You can deduct up to the lesser of 15% of includible compensation
or $30,000<SUP><STRONG><A HREF="#1">1</A></STRONG></SUP> per
participant.
<P>
<STRONG>IRS Reporting Requirements</STRONG><BR>
There are none, providing you give all eligible employees certain
information.
<P>
<STRONG>Set Up, Initial Funding and Contribution
Deadline</STRONG><BR>
The employer tax filing due date (or later if granted an
extension for filing the
tax return).
<P>

$150,000 of compensation is considered, limiting the maximum
contribution to $22,500.</FONT>
