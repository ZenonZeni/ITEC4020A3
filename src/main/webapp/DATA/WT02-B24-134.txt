<h2 align=center>Table 7. Primary Disorder of Vigilance</h2>

<table>
<tr>
<td valign=top>A.
<td>A disorder of at least I year duration with symptoms from all five 
major symptom categories: 
</table>
<ol>
<li>Decreasing ability to sustain alertness, wakefulness, arousal, and 
watchfulness during continuous mental or other task) performance
<blockquote>
<table>
<tr>
<td>a.
<td>Complaints of tiredness, drowsiness, sleepiness, lethargy
<tr>
<td>b.
<td>Yawning, stretching, sleepy-eyed (glassy-eyed) appearance    
<tr>
<td>c.
<td>Falling asleep; excessive napping 
</table>
</blockquote>
<li>Decreasing attention to present activities
<blockquote>
<table>
<tr>
<td>a.
<td>Daydreaming    
<tr>
<td>b.
<td>Difficulty focusing attention; loses place in activities and 
conversation    

<tr>
<td>c.
<td>Poor performance    

<tr>
<td>d.
<td>Slow, delayed, or incomplete tasks
<tr>
<td>e.
<td>Disorganized 
</table>
</blockquote>
<li>Avoidance of structured or repetitive activities 
<blockquote>
<table>
<tr>
<td valign=top>a.
<td>Loss of interest in or complaint that structured activities are 
dull, boring, monotonous, tedious, uninteresting (or no longer 
interesting) 
<tr>
<td valign=top>b.
<td>Preference for shifting activities that have random or irregular 
changes in schedule or activity (orderly randomization) 
</TABLE>
</blockquote>
<li>Motor restlessness and behaviors to improve alertness  
<blockquote>
<table>
<tr>
<td>a.
<td>Fidgeting
<tr>
<td>b.
<td>Talkativeness

<tr>
<td>c.
<td>Moving about
<tr>
<td>d.
<td>Busyness
</table>
</blockquote>
<li>Caring, compassionate, affectionate, kind temperament 
</ol>
<table>
<tr>
<td valign=top>B.
<td>A major symptom category is accepted as positive when the symptom or 
one or more of its behaviors is  
identified in a semi-structured, closed-end interview of patient and 
primary caretakers (historians). 
</table>

<table>
<tr>
<td valign=top>C.
<td>This symptom complex must precede the onset of other medical 
disorders (including depression, narcolepsy, various medications, 
alcohol and drug abuse, hypothyroidism) that can cause secondary 
hypovigilance. 
</table>
<table>
<tr>
<td valign=top>D.
<td>Although symptomatology may be identified at any age, the disorder 
generally becomes more symptomatic with schooling and aging and can 
result in increasing maladaptation. 
</table>
<hr>
Adapted from Weinberg WA, Brumback RA Primary disorder of vigilance: A 
novel explanation of inattentiveness, daydreaming, boredom, 
restlessness. and sleepiness. <i>J Pediatr</i> 116:720-725, 1990 and 
Weinberg WA and Harper CR: Vigilance and its disorders. <i>Neurol Clin 
</i>11:59-78, 1993.
