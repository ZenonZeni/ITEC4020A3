<h3 align=left><font size=3><u>Press Advisory</u></font></h3>
<p align=center><font size=4><b>Wildman Becomes First Legislator to Use Electric Car For District Duties</b></font></p>
<p align=center><b>ASSEMBLYMAN RECEIVES CALSTART AWARD FOR</b> <b>&quot;NOT BLOWING SMOKE&quot;</b> </p>
<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>What</b></font><b>: Assemblyman Scott Wildman (D-Burbank, Glendale, L.A.) will become the first member of the California 
State Legislature to use a commercially produced electric vehicle, GM's EV1, as his official state car. <br>
</b></p>
<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Where</b></font><b>: Burbank City Hall, 275 E. Olive Avenue</b> </p>
<p><font size=2>(Parking will be available on Olive Avenue, in front of City Hall)<br>
</font></p>
<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>When</b></font><b>: Monday, February 10 10:00 a.m. </b></p>
<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Visuals</b></font><b>: A new EV1 electric car will be delivered to Wildman at his district offices in Burbank </b>at a news conference 
hosted by CALSTART.<b> </b>The vehicle will be available for press driving, and the assemblyman will receive an award for &quot;not 
blowing smoke&quot; in his new vehicle. </p>
<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Who</b></font><b>: Scott Wildman, </b>Assemblymember, 43<sup>rd</sup> District </p>
<p><b>Michael J. Gage, </b>President &amp; CEO, CALSTART </p>
<p><b>Enid Joffe, </b><a href="http:/www.calstart.org/edisonev">Edison EV</a> (charger provider) </p>
<p><b>Ron Stassi,</b> General Manager, Burbank Public Service Department </p>
<p><b>Tim Carmichael,</b> Coalition for Clean Air </p>
<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Background</b></font><b>: </b>The EV1 is a &quot;zero-emission vehicle&quot; produced in America by General Motors.<b> Assemblyman Scott 
Wildman becomes the first legislator to use an electric car in his job</b>. Wildman's commitment to electric vehicles as an 
environmentally sound means of personal transportation is especially important for the Burbank-Glendale area, which is 
considered by many to be the birthplace of the advanced transportation technologies industry. </p>
<p>Other participating organizations include Saturn/GM; <a href=http://www.calstart.org/edisonev/>Edison EV</a>; the City of Burbank (which will unveil a new public charger 
for use by electric vehicles); and the Coalition for Clean Air. Also attending will be the International Brotherhood of Electrical 
Workers, United Auto Workers, and several Burbank and Glendale businesspeople. </p>
<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Contact</b></font><b>: </b>Bill Van Amburg/Melanie Savage, CALSTART: (818) 565-5600</p>
<p>Ron Smith/Assemblymember Wildman's Office: 818/240-6330</p>
