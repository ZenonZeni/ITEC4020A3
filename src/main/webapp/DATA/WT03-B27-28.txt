<CENTER><B><H2>1995 Fred Meyer Challenge Children's Charities</B><BR></H2></CENTER>
<P>
<I>In the past nine years The Fred Meyer Challenge has donated more than $3.7 million to benefit local children's charities.  Each year, through <a href="sponsor.html">sponsor</a> and public support, we have collectively achieved our goal to enhance the quality of life for the children in our community.</I>
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1>
</PRE>
<a name="kerr"><B>Albertina Kerr Centers for Children</B><BR>
Chris Krenk, Chief Executive Officer<BR>
Marcia Director, Director of Development & Volunteer Services
<P>
The Albertina Kerr Centers has developed a technically sophisticated intervention process for the purpose of serving the community by enhancing the self-esteem, improving the physical and mental health, and promoting the self-responsibility of children and youth at risk, families in need, and individuals with disabilities.
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1 WIDTH=65%>
</PRE>
<a name="boys"><B>Boys & Girls Clubs of Portland Metropolitan Area</B><BR>
Dick Hanlin, Executive Director<BR>
Dana Gritzmacher, Development Director
<P>
Boys & Girls Clubs of Portland is a nonprofit youth development agency with a mission to improve the quality of life for young people in our community.  The organization operates facilities in Lents, Sellwood, Hillsboro, North Portland and Beaverton, as well as extension units in the Columbia Villa Housing project.
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1 WIDTH=65%>
</PRE>
<a name="doern"><B>Doernbecher Children's Hospital</B><BR>
J.S. May, Executive Director<BR>
Kathleen Shelton, Director
<P>
Doernbecher Children's Hospital provides a vital range of services for thousands of children including the treatment of cancer, heart and kidney disease, cystic fibrosis and other inherited disorders, organ transplants, trauma, as well as primary care.  The hospital takes care of virtually all children, regardless of the family's ability to pay.
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1 WIDTH=65%>
</PRE>
<a name="hearing"><B>Hearing and Speech Institute</B><BR>
Kristin Sorman, Director<BR>
<P>
This non-profit community agency receives some 25,000 visits a year from people of all ages with communication disorders.  Highly qualified speech-language pathologists and teachers use internationally recognized methods and materials pioneered by the Infant Hearing Resource program and guides children, teaching them games, songs and communication to prepare them for success in a hearing world in the Intensive Language program.
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1 WIDTH=65%>
</PRE>
<a name="emanuel"><B>Legacy Emanuel Children's Hospital</B><BR>
Claudine Larocque, Annual Gifts Officer<BR>
Michael Westby, Major Gifts Officer<BR>
<P>
With an 80-year tradition of caring and medical excellence, Legacy Emanuel is the most comprehensive private pediatric hospital in Oregon.  It has special teams of heatlhcare professionals to care for injuries related to children's burns, trauma, rehabilitation and development in a well-equipped neonatal special care unit; dedicated pediatric intensive care facility; Level I trauma center and Pediatric Critical Care Transport Program.
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1 WIDTH=65%>
</PRE>
<a name="march"><B>March of Dimes</B><BR>
Kathy Ellis, Executive Director<BR>
Dennis Gilsdorf, Communications Director<BR>
<P>
March of Dimes mission is the improve the health of babies by preventing birth defects and infant mortality through research, community service, education and advocacy.  Through programs such as the Children's Center and Elizabeth House, which supplies case management services for pregnant teenagers, the March of Dimes Campaign for Healthier Babies helps to ensure a healthy and happy future for tomorrow's babies.
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1 WIDTH=65%>
</PRE>
<a name="met"><B>Metropolitan Family Service</B><BR>
Gary Withers, Executive Director<BR>
<P>
Metropolitan Family Service is a private, nonprofit, nonsectarian, charitable family service agency that began operations in 1950 as a family counseling agency.  Serving the tri-county metropolitan area, the agency has developed a growing family of services in response to changing community needs.  The organization is committed to improving our community by preserving and strengthening all families and individuals.
<PRE>
<HR SIZE=1 WIDTH=65%>
</PRE>
<a name="prov"><B>Providence Child Center</B><BR>
Shoshana Grammer, Foundation Executive Director<BR>
Charlene Dahlen, Director of Annual Giving<BR>
<P>
Formerly known as Our Lady of Providence Nursery for adoptive babies when its doors first opened in 1945, the corridors of Providence Child Center are no longer a home for adoptive babies, but provide medical and educational services for disabled and non-disabled children in three very distinct programs:  The Providence Montessori School with an emphasis on individual development and personal attention through professional Montessori instruction; the Emily School for preschool children with delayed development in social, communication or motor skills; and the Children's Nursing Center for severely disabled infants and children.
