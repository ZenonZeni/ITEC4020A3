<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<p align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Panic Disorder (Anxiety, Phobias)</b></font></p>
<hr>
<p><font color="#FF0000">What is panic disorder? </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - when one has panic attacks, uncontrollable panic responses to ordinary 
nonthreatening situations </p>
<p>2 - when one experiences 4 or more panic attacks in a 4-week period or when 
one has recurrent fears of having panic attacks </p>
<p>3 - affects 2 to 5% of Americans </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Causes </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - no definitively known cause </p>
<p>2 - chemical or hormonal imbalances </p>
<p>3 - drug, caffeine, or alcohol abuse </p>
<p>4 - severe stress or other situational events </p>
<p>5 - genetic inheritability or predisposition </p>
<p>6 - generalized anxiety </p>
<p>7 - severe separation anxiety </p>
<p>8 - cognitive theorists believe &quot;wrong thinking&quot; causes anxiety &amp; triggers attacks </p>
<p>9 - physical defects in one's autonomic or automatic nervous system </p>
<p>10 - general hypersensitivity in the nervous system </p>
<p>11 - increased arousal </p>
<p>12 - sodium lactate when injected into those predisposed to attacks </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Symptoms </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - panic attacks (often mistaken for heart attacks, heart disease, or respiratory 
trouble) </p>
<p>2 - sweating </p>
<p>3 - hot or cold flashes </p>
<p>4 - choking or smothering sensations </p>
<p>5 - racing heart </p>
<p>6 - labored breathing </p>
<p>7 - trembling </p>
<p>8 - chest pains </p>
<p>9 - faintness </p>
<p>10 - disorientation </p>
<p>11 - feelings of dying, losing control, or losing one's mind </p>
<p>12 - anticipatory anxiety </p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>a. &quot;fear the fear&quot; </p>
<p>b. worry over when the next panic attack is coming </p>
<p>c. possibly debilitating </p>
<p>d. causes people to avoid that which they think will cause a panic 
attack thus limiting the things they do or the places they go </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>13 - usually appear in one's early twenties </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Phobias </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - chronic, distressing, restrictive anxiety disorders </p>
<p>2- irrational, involuntary, &amp; inappropriate fears of or responses to ordinary 
situations or things </p>
<p>3 - result in panic attacks when one must face that which one is phobic about </p>
<p>4 - may lead to depression/over half of those with panic disorders suffer 
depression </p>
<p>5 - may lead to alcoholism, loss of productivity, shame, secrecy, &amp; low 
self-esteem </p>
<p>6 - may lead to a pathologic dependency/one is unable to function without the 
assistance of people they trust </p>
<p>7 - single/simple phobia: an unreasonable fear of specific circumstances or 
objects </p>
<p>8 - social phobia: extreme fear of making a spectacle of oneself in public thus 
forcing one to avoid public occasions or areas </p>
<p>9 - agoraphobia: fear of the marketplace/an intense fear about feeling trapped in 
any situation, particularly in public places, combined with an overwhelming fear 
of having a panic attack in unfamiliar surroundings </p>
<p>10 - affect between 5.1 &amp; 12.5% of Americans at some point in their lives </p>
<p>11 - the leading psychiatric disorder among women of all ages </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Treatment </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - recovery is most successful with a combination of treatments </p>
<p>2 - 75 to 90% of those treated show significant improvement </p>
<p>3 - only 1/4 of sufferers ever seek appropriate treatment </p>
<p>4 - cognitive/behavioral therapy </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. helps people to think &amp; behave appropriately </p>
<p>b. supported exposure &amp; hierarchical desensitization (being exposed 
to &amp; slowly getting used to the threatening object or situation) </p>
<p>c. family members &amp; friends must provide support, assistance, &amp; 
encouragement </p>
</blockquote>
<p>5 - medication </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. most effective when part of a more comprehensive treatment plan 
that involves supportive therapy </p>
<p>b. antidepressants like imipramine &amp; phenelzine </p>
<p>c. antianxiety agents like alprazolam </p>
<p>d. beta blockers which limit neuron activity in the brain help social 
phobias </p>
</blockquote>
<p>6 - psychotherapy </p>
<p>7 - healthy living habits (ie. exercise, balanced diet, moderate use of caffeine or 
alcohol, reduced stress) </p>
<p>8 - peer support </p>
</blockquote>
