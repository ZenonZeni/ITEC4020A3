<H1>"Near-Real-Time" Sea Surface Height</H1>
<p>
<TABLE><TR><TD COLSTART="1"><img src="images/ssh.gif" alt="Near-Real-Time Sea Surface Height"> <ALIGN="BOTTOM"></TD><TD COLSTART="2">
<p>
<h3>After influence of gravity is taken into account, the "dynamic ocean topography" - or hills and valleys - is mainly caused by ocean circulation or currents. Dynamic ocean topography is used to calculate the currents in a similar way to how meteorlogists use atmospheric pressure maps to determine wind speed.
<p>
Around the globe, the overall change in sea surface height caused by currents is about 2 meters (which is up to 75 times <i>less</i> than the influence of gravity!) The highest sea surface height is shown as read and lowest as blue.
<p>
</H3></TD></TR></TABLE> 
<h3>  
<p>
<a href=http://www-ccar.Colorado.EDU/~leben/datahost/>Click here</a> to access the overall directory "Global Near-Real-Time Altimeter Sea Surface Height Page."
<p>
The <a href=http://www-ccar.colorado.edu/~leben/datahost/sshpic.html>"Global Near Real-Time Altimeter Sea Surface Height Visualization"</a> has interactive software program that lets you generate sea surface height along the satellites' "ground tracks" for<i>a single day.</i>
<p>
The <a href=http://shaman.colorado.edu/~leben/datahost/ssh.html>"Global Near Real-Time Altimeter Along-Track Data Host"</a> gives the following data in ASCII format:</h3>
<h4><ul>
<li>Calendar date (month/day/year)<br></li>
<li>Time of day (hour:min:sec)<br></li>
<li>Seconds past midnight 1-Jan-1996<br></li>
<li>Latitude (degrees)<br></li>
<li>East longitude (degrees)<br></li>
<li>Sea Surface Height (millimeters)<br></li>
</ul></h4>
<p>  
<h3>
In addition to sea surface height is how the shape of the ocean changes over months or seasons. Maps of <a href=nrt_ssa.html>sea surface anomaly</a> - also known as sea surface variability - are used to study changes in the ocean over months or seasons. 
</h3>
