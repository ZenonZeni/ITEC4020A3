<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>Are Pension Plans Too Complicated For Small
Businesses?</STRONG></FONT><P>
<STRONG>Simplified Employee Pension Plan Ideas From The Company You Keep</STRONG>
<BR>

Owners of small businesses agree that a retirement plan can do a lot for a growing company.
Finding
one with features that your business needs, such as tax advantages, low cost, and minimal
paperwork,
can be challenging. Look no further than a Simplified Employee Pension plan (SEP).<P>
A SEP plan is an easy way to make contributions toward your
employees' retirement income - and your own.<P>
Through a SEP, you take a tax deduction for contributions you make directly to your
employees' IRAs.
Meanwhile, these contributions are excluded from employees' gross incomes. Since employees
handle their own IRAs, you pay benefits directly into their accounts instead of operating a
pension plan
of your own. Your contributions are also completely discretionary, and you can change them
from year to
year. 
<P>
Even if you've missed the year-end deadline that applies to most qualified plans, you can
still establish
a SEP for last year - and get the tax deduction.<P>
Thanks to the tax deductibility of contributions, the net cost of installing and running a
SEP can be zero
or may even create a positive cash flow for many business owners. 
<P>
<STRONG>The Simplified Employee Pension Plan (SEP): Tax-Free, Form-Free, Hassle-Free...Completely Stress-Free </STRONG><BR>
You may find a SEP most attractive for what it doesn't have:<P>
<UL>
<LI><EM>No</EM> annual IRS or Department of Labor filings;
<LI><EM>No</EM> funding until your company's tax filing date;
<LI><EM>No</EM> income taxes on contributions;
<LI><EM>No</EM> required contributions from year to year;
<LI><EM>No</EM> complicated plan documentation;
<LI><EM>No</EM> complex record keeping or expensive administration.
</UL>
<P>
A SEP means no longer trying to accommodate an expensive, labor-intensive pension plan designed 
for a large company. A SEP is a cost-effective and flexible program, packed with employee
advantages
and the flexibility a small company needs. You can:<P>

<UL>
<LI><EM>Choose</EM> from a wide variety of investment options;
<LI><EM>Offer</EM> a companion plan (a Salary Reduction SEP [SARSEP]) that enables employees
to make
pre-tax contributions, just like a 401(k);
<LI><EM>Design</EM> eligibility criteria that make the most sense for your company.
</UL>
