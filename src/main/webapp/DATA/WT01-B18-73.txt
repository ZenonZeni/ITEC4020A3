<P><FONT SIZE=5 COLOR="#AF0000"><A NAME="collect"></A>Leaf Collection
on City Streets</FONT></P>

<P>Corvallis Disposal's annual leaf collection program has begun, and
will continue through January 3.</P>

<P><B>Corvallis:</B> During this time, residents within the city
limits of Corvallis can place their leaves in curbed streets for
pick-up each week on garbage collection day.</P>

<P>In order to prevent blockage of bike lanes, we plan to collect
leaves every week-day from those streets in the central part of the
city that have major bike paths. Please try to avoid putting your
leaves directly in the bike lanes.</P>

<P><B>Philomath:</B> In Philomath, leaves will be collected from all
streets within the city limits on one day only - Thursday, December
5. Please have your leaves in the street by 7:00 in the morning.</P>

<P><B>Tangent &amp; Adair Village:</B> For those of you who live in
Tangent or Adair Village, remember to use your yard-debris cart or
composter for leaf disposal. This service is provided free with your
weekly garbage service. Please give us a call if you don't have one
yet.</P>

<P><B>Other Areas:</B> Many of you who live outside of the above
areas will likely use your leaves for mulching, composting or
burning. If you want to though, you can take them to the Processing
and Recovery Center. They charge two dollars per cubic yard of leaves
that you drop off.</P>

<P>Please give them a call at 745-2018 if you have any questions.

<P><FONT SIZE=5 COLOR="#AF0000"><A NAME="compost"></A>Your Leaves
Help Make Compost</FONT></P>

<P>The leaves and yard debris collected by Corvallis Disposal are
eventually returned to area gardens as nutrient-rich compost.</P>

<P>CDC drivers deliver truck loads of leaves and yard debris to the
Processing and Recovery Center (PRC) located off Highway 99 about ten
miles north of Corvallis.</P>

<P>Here, the leaves are stacked in piles and left untouched for about
four months. The mixed yard debris is spread out in long rows, then
covered with tarps to reduce leaching of nutrients by rainwater.</P>

<P>Next spring, the leaves from this fall will be mixed together with
fresh yard debris and run through a shredder. The mixed ratio is 20%
leaves to 80% green yard debris.</P>

<P>This shredded mixture is put in piles and turned twice weekly.
After about 100 days - with the help of nature's decomposers
(bacteria, fungi, insects, etc.) - the pile turns into a rich
compost.</P>

<P>It is high in carbon which provides energy for organisms, and in
nitrogen which is necessary for cell development. Compost also helps
loosen clay soils, aides in moisture retention, and generally
provides a healthy growing environment for plants.</P>

<P>The PRC's compost can be purchased for $8 per cubic yard.
(745-2018) <FONT COLOR="#AF0000"><A HREF="#top">TOP</A></FONT></P>

<P><HR WIDTH="10%"><B><FONT SIZE=5 COLOR="#AF0000">Holiday
Recycling</FONT></B></P>

<P>Wrapping paper &amp; cards can be recycled with your paperboard
&amp; household mail mix <B>(NO foil, plastic or tissue paper
please).</B></P>

<P>Christmas trees will be picked up on your garbage day from
December 30 through January 10. If your tree is clean of all
flocking, tinsel, etc. and you put it at the curb, we will recycle
it. Otherwise, we will throw it away as garbage free of charge.</P>
