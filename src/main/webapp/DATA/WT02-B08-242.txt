<h3>Scott Polar Research Institute</h3>

<h2>OSTROVA DE-LONGA ('De Long Islands')</h2>



<hr>
<p>


Compiled by R. K. Headland<br>
<br>
Five  small isolated <a href="perisib.htm">Arctic Ocean islands</a> in the East Siberian Sea, part of  the  
Novosibiriskiye Ostrova ('New  Siberian Islands').  They are situated to the 
north-east of Ostrov Anzhu (main  part  of the archipelago), the closest is approximately 
60 km and farthest 200 km distant.<p>

<dl><b>Ostrov Zhanetta ('Jeanette')</b> 76&#183;83&#176;N : 158&#183;08&#176;E 
[85&#183;37&#176;N : 175&#183;64&#176;E] 
<dd>Discovered 16 May 1881 by William Dunbar aboard <i>Jeanette</i>.
<dd>Area 0&#183;2 km2, elevation about 250 m, 30% glacierized.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Genriyetta ('Henrietta')</b> 77&#183;08&#176;N : 156&#183;50&#176;E 
[85&#183;64&#176;N : 173&#183;89&#176;E] 
<dd>Discovered 20 May 1881 by Thomas Washington De Long aboard <i>Jeanette</i>. 
Polar Station established 1937, closed 1963.
<dd>Area 7 km2, elevation 340 m, 40% glacierized.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Bennett</b> 76&#183;67&#176;N : 149&#183;08&#176;E 
[85&#183;19&#176;N : 165&#183;64&#176;E] 
<dd>Discovered 15 July 1881 by Aneguin aboard <i>Jeanette</i>.
<dd>Area 75 km2, elevation 448 m, 60% glacierized.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Vil'kitskogo [formerly Ostrov General Vil'kitskogo]</b>  
75&#183;73&#176;N : 155&#183;55&#176;E [84&#183;14&#176;N : 
172&#183;83&#176;E] 
<dd>Discovered 20 August 1913 by Aleksey Nikolayevich Zhokhov aboard <i>Taymyr</i>.
<dd>Area about 2 km2, elevation about 70 m, unglacierized.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Zhokhova [formerly Ostrov Novopashennogo]</b>  
76&#183;17&#176;N : 152&#183;83&#176;E [84&#183;63&#176;N : 
169&#183;81&#176;E] 
<dd>Discovered 27 August 1914 by Per Alekseyevich Novopashennyy aboard 
<i>Vaygach</i>.
<dd>Polar Station established 1955.
<dd>Area about 40 km2, elevation about 120 m, unglacierized.</dl>
