<CENTER><H2>FACT SHEET</H2><P><BR>

<B>
Chronology of Public Consultation on
Temagami Land Use Planning


</B></CENTER><P><BR>

Over the past decade, land use and resource management issues in the Temagami area have been the subject of considerable public debate -- and a great deal of public consultation -- in an attempt to resolve the issues.  This fact sheet offers a chronology of those public consultation efforts, including the significant groups and decisions involved, from 1987 to the present.<P>

<TABLE BORDER="1" WIDTH="100%" CELLPADDING=6>
<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>December 1987:</B></TD><TD>	The Temagami Area Working Group (TAWG) is established by then Natural Resources Minister Vincent Kerrio to review and recommend solutions to land and resource use conflicts on the Crown land area around Lady Evelyn-Smoothwater Provincial Park.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>January to 
March 1988:</B></TD><TD>		TAWG hosts public meetings, receives submissions from interested groups and delivers its report to the Minister.  Its recommendations include making Temagami a model district in the management of its forestry, recreation, tourism and earth and life science features.  It also recommended the creation of a citizens' committee to advise the government on Temagami land use and resource management issues.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>July 1988:</B></TD><TD>		The Temagami Advisory Council (TAC) is established to advise the government on land and resource management matters in the area, including advice on the construction of an extension to the Red Squirrel Road.
</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>August 1989:</B></TD><TD>		Then Natural Resources Minister Lyn McLeod announces the Temagami Area Comprehensive Planning Program (CPP), and sets a completion date of March 31, 1992.  TAC is asked to seek public participation and develop recommendations for a comprehensive land use and resource management plan.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>January 1990:</B></TD><TD>	TAC meets with more than 25 organizations to gather comments on draft objectives it has designed to guide the comprehensive planning program.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>August to
September 1990:</B></TD><TD>	TAC holds open houses in seven communities to discuss the background information that will be used to prepare its comprehensive land use and resource management plan, and invites comments on a revised set of planning objectives.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">

<B> May 1991:</B></TD><TD>		Then Natural Resources Minister Bud Wildman replaces TAC with the Comprehensive Planning Council (CPC) to strengthen the role of local communities in the management of natural resources in the Temagami area, and extends the deadline for the comprehensive plan to March 31, 1994.  The Ontario government and the Teme-Augama Anishnabai jointly establish the Wendaban Stewardship Authority (WSA) to develop a Forest Stewardship Plan for four townships at the centre of the Temagami area.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>July 1992:</B></TD><TD>		The WSA hosts a meeting with local individuals and interested groups to discuss methods for public participation in the development of its Forest Stewardship Plan.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>September 1992
to September 1993:</B></TD><TD>	The CPC conducts public consultations on behalf of the province and the Teme-Augama Anishnabai as part of the negotiations for a Treaty of Co-existence.  As a result of the negotiations, the Teme-Augama Anishnabai join the CPC on an interim basis.  The deadline for the comprehensive plan is again extended, to March 31, 1996.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>October 1993:</B></TD><TD>	After two more public meetings to identify problems and outstanding issues, and receive comments on goals and objectives, the WSA hosts a final public review and critique of the planning options and zoning decisions in its Forest Stewardship Plan.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>May to
August 1994:</B></TD><TD>		The CPC releases three alternative scenarios for the Temagami Comprehensive Planning Area (excluding the area covered by the WSA's Forest Stewardship Plan).  The scenarios reflect a range of potential land use alternatives, degrees of commercial development and resource protection.  The CPC seeks public reaction by holding open houses and workshops in four communities, and through 14 meetings with interested organizations.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>February 1995:</B></TD><TD>	Then Natural Resources Minister Howard Hampton announces Ontario's intention to protect the Obabika Lake North old-growth site, a significant provincial example of old-growth pine forest.  At the same time, the province removes all or parts of nine townships located northeast of Lady Evelyn-Smoothwater Provincial Park from the Temagami Comprehensive Planning Area.<P>
 The WSA officially submits its Forest Stewardship Plan, and the government directs the CPC to incorporate it into the comprehensive plan.<P>

The Teme-Augama Anishnabai withdraw from the CPC, pending the resolution of outstanding concerns with the government.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>June 1995:</B></TD><TD>		The CPC releases an update on its comprehensive plan, and circulates a single, comprehensive land use map that attempts to show how the many competing demands on the Temagami-area resources can be accommodated.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>November 1995:</B></TD><TD>	Chris Hodgson, Minister of Natural Resources and Minister of Northern Development and Mines, directs the CPC to complete and submit recommendations on land use in Temagami by March 1, 1996.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>December 1995
to January 1996:</B></TD><TD>	The CPC releases a draft land use proposal, hosts two open houses in Temagami, and four issue-resolution sessions with representatives of local, regional and provincial organizations.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>April 1996:</B></TD><TD>		After a delay due to the public service strike, the CPC submits its report on land use to Minister Hodgson.</TD></TR>

<TR VALIGN="TOP">
<TD WIDTH="25%">
<B>June 1996:</B></TD><TD>		Minister Hodgson announces the province's decision on land use in the Temagami area.</TD></TR>
</TABLE>
