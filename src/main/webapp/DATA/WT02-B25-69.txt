<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<p align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Statistical Prevalence of Mental Illness</b></font></p>
<hr>
<p><font color="#FF0000">What is the Epidemiologic Catchment Area (ECA) Study/Survey? </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - research conducted &amp; supported by the National Institute of Mental Health 
(NIMH) </p>
<p>2 - a survey conducted between 1980 &amp; 1984 </p>
<p>3 - intended to help improve the diagnosis, treatment, &amp; prevention of mental 
disorders </p>
<p>4 - provides detailed information of the toll taken by various mental disorders </p>
<p>5 - aimed to help clinical researchers to design rigorous studies of mental 
disorders </p>
<p>6 - intended to contribute to the development of better mental health services </p>
<p>7 - offers a table showing the prevalence of various mental disorders among 
adults during a 1-month period &amp; during a year </p>
<p>8 - the # of affected adults is based on estimates of the U.S. resident population 
from the 1990 census of 182.6 million persons aged 18 &amp; over </p>
<p>9 - as some people have more than one mental disorder, the #s for each type of 
disorder, if added together, will be more than the total # of individuals with 
mental disorders </p>
<p>10 - the survey covered schizophrenia/schizophreniform disorders, bipolar 
disorder, major depression, dysthymia, phobia, panic disorder, 
obsessive-compulsive disorder, somatization disorder, antisocial personality 
disorder, severe cognitive impairment, &amp; substance use disorders </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Statistics from the Epidemiologic Catchment Area Study/Survey </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - prevalence of mental disorders among adults during a 1-month period </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. 15.7% will suffer from any mental disorder or substance use 
disorder covered in the survey </p>
<p>b. 13% will suffer from any mental disorder except substance use 
disorders covered in the survey </p>
<p>c. 0.7% will suffer from schizophrenia/schizophreniform disorders </p>
<p>d. 5.2% will suffer from depressive (affective) disorders </p>
<p>e. 0.6% will suffer from bipolar disorder (manic depression) </p>
<p>f. 1.8% will suffer from major depression </p>
<p>g. 3.3% will suffer from dysthymia </p>
<p>h. 7.3% will suffer from anxiety disorders </p>
<p>I. 6.3% will suffer from phobia </p>
<p>j. 0.5% will suffer from panic disorder </p>
<p>k. 1.3% will suffer from obsessive-compulsive disorder </p>
<p>l. 0.1% will suffer from somatization disorder (a chronic 
psychiatric condition characterized by multiple physical complaints 
for which there is no apparent physical cause) </p>
<p>m. 0.5% will suffer from antisocial personality disorder </p>
<p>n. 1.7% will suffer from severe cognitive impairment </p>
<p>o. 3.8% will suffer from substance use disorders </p>
<p>p. 2.8% will suffer from alcohol abuse/dependence </p>
<p>q. 1.3% will suffer from drug abuse/dependence </p>
</blockquote>
<p>2 - prevalence of mental disorders among adults during a year </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. 28.1% will suffer from any mental disorder &amp; substance use 
disorder covered in the survey </p>
<p>b. 22.1% will suffer from any mental disorder except substance use 
disorders covered in the survey </p>
<p>c. 1.1% will suffer from schizophrenia/schizophreniform disorders </p>
<p>d. 9.5% will suffer from depressive (affective) disorders </p>

<p>k. 2.1% will suffer from obsessive-compulsive disorder </p>
<p>l. 0.2% will suffer from somatization disorder (a chronic 
psychiatric condition characterized by multiple physical complaints
