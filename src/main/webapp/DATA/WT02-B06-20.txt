<B>President Signs Executive Order, Appoints &quot;Special Envoy for 
Cryptography&quot;; First Steps
Towards Administration Key Recovery Plan</B> <FONT SIZE=-1>[updated November 18, 1996]</FONT>

<P>
Taking the first steps towards implementing its new key recovery encryption
policy, the President signed an <A HREF="clipper311exo.html">Executive 
Order</A> on November 15 transferring jurisdiction over encryption exports 
to the Commerce Department.  The White House also announced the appointment of Ambassador David L. Aaron as &quot;Special Envoy for Cryptography&quot; to promote 
U.S. encryption policies abroad.

<B>Members of Congress Raise Questions about Clipper 3.1.1</B> <FONT SIZE=-1>[updated October 22, 1996]</FONT>
<P>
Twenty-one Senators and Representatives, including Senator Ron Wyden (D-OR) and Senator Conrad Burns (R-MT) signed a letter opposing the latest Administration proposal to ease export controls on products using strong encryption.  In the bipartisan letter sent to Department of Commerce Secretary Mickey Kantor and dated October 15, 1996, the members write, &quot;Unfortunately, the Administration's most recent encryption initiative shortchanges both U.S. business and law enforcement interests.&quot;  They ask the Administration to work with them, industry, and other interested groups to modify the proposal.
