<TITLE>Melanoma</TITLE>
</HEAD><BODY><P>Skin cancer is the most common type of cancer. There are
several types of which the melanoma is by far the worst. Melanoma is often
found in young and middle aged adults and it can be deadly if not removed
early. It is becomming more common every year. It is most common in white
people.
<P>Some risk factors are:
<P>1. Family history of malignant melanoma.
<P>2. Blond or red hair.
<P>3. Marked freckling of the upper back.
<P>4. History of blistering sunburns as a child.
<P>5. History of working outdoors for three or more years as a teenager.
<P>What does it look like:
<P>A. Asymmetry- one half of the lesion does not look like the other half.
<P>B. Border irregularity- The edges of the mole are not sharp.
<P>C. Color varied- The lesion has more than one color. Tan, brown, black,
and sometimes red, white, or blue.
<P>D. Diameter- The diameter of the lesion is usually over 6 millimeters.
<P>Skin self examinations should be done monthly after a bath or a shower.
If you find any change in size, color, or shape of any previously seen mole,
see your physician.
