<td align="left" width="90%" valign="top"><center><font size=6><b>Tank Manager&#174</b></font></center>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Technical Specifications</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tank Manager&#174 was designed to help those who are responsible for maintaining or inspecting aboveground storage tanks (AST) or underground storage tanks (UST).  With Tank Manager&#174 , you can schedule inspections and maintenance, track inspection results, record inventory levels and provide detailed or summary reports on the information entered.</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Regulatory Compliance</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are currently a wide variety of regulations, codes, and standards dealing with aboveground and underground storage tanks.  Regulations exist on the national, state and, in some locations, local levels.  Tank Manager&#174 is designed to help you stay in compliance with these regulations.  The program assists you to ensure required inspections are performed on time, and inspection results are recorded and available for review and analysis.  Tank Manager&#174  will also track the inspection histories for each aboveground storage tank, documenting and recordkeeping requirements of current regulations.</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Inventory Management</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tank Manager&#174 will track all of your aboveground and underground storage tanks as well as the product and inventory levels in each of these tanks.  You will always have an accurate accounting for your storage tanks including:  locations, models, serial number, construction types, and much more.  In addition, you can customize field names for you own particular tracking needs.</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Predictive Maintenance</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>Tank Manager&#174 includes fields to allow you to enter specific inspection results including engineering tests and thickness readings.  Keeping track of wall thickness, bottom plate thickness, and settlement information can help you determine corrosion and deterioration rates, which is extremely useful in predicting possible failures.  By taking a proactive approach to predictive maintenance, you can prevent unexpected failures before they happen, thus reducing downtime, product loss, and associated cleanup costs of leakage.</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>General Tank Information</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tank Manager&#174 will track information on all of your aboveground and underground storage tanks, including the following:
<ul>
<li>Owner of tank, e.g. Company, Division, Region, etc.</li>
<li>Location of tank, e.g. Tank Farm 1, Tank Farm 2, etc.</li>
<li>Type of tank, e.g. Aboveground Storage (AST) or Underground Storage (UST)</li>
<li>Product contained in each tank</li>
<li>Specific gravity of the product</li>
<li>Tank Manufacturer, Year Manufactured, Manufacturer's Code, and Type of Construction</li>
<li>Serial Number and State Identification number</li>
<li>Tank Capacity and Dimensions</li>
<li>Manufacturer, Type and Installation Date of Cathodic Protection System</li>
</ul>
</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Aboveground Storage Tanks (AST)</b></center></font>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For aboveground storage tanks, the following information can be tracked:
<ul>
<li>Number and sizes of each course (this can be modified as needed)</li>
<li>Design Level of each tank</li>
<li>Yield, Tensile strength and Joint efficiency on each tank</li>
<li>Roof and Bottom types</li>
<li>Vapor Detection Equipment Manufacturer and Installation date</li>
<li>Last dates tested and strapped</li>
<li>Frequency for performing external, internal, primary seal, and secondary seal inspections</li>
</ul>
</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Underground Storage Tanks (UST)</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For underground storage tanks, the following information can be tracked:
<ul>
<li>Construction Material for each tank</li>
<li>Wall Type, and whether it is vaulted</li>
<li>Monitoring System Manufacturer and Installation date</li>
<li>Leak Detector Manufacturer, Location and Installation date</li>
</ul>
</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Custom Fields</b></font></center>
<P><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In addition, there are over 20 additional fields for recording your own custom data.  You can use these fields to record additional data, such as:
<ul>
<li>Roof Drain Type</li>
<li>Internal Heaters</li>
<li>Types of coils</li>
<li>Insulation Type</li>
<li>Gaging Type</li>
<li>Foundation Type</li>
<li>External Casing</li>
<li>Pipe Inspection information</li>
<li>Primary Seal Type</li>
<li>Pump information</li>
</ul>
These fields can be customized as text, date, numeric or Yes/No fields.
</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Recording Inspections</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whenever inspections are performed, you can enter the information to record:
<ul>
<li>Company, Location, and Tank identification number</li>
<li>Inspection date, inspection number, type of inspection, and the reason for the inspection</li>
<li>Who performed the inspection</li>
<li>Notes on the results of the inspection</li>
</ul>
</font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Review Tools</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;You can examine many tank records at once through a simple spreadsheet.</font</p>
<br><br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Reports</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A number of reports are available to help you manage your tank farms and schedule inspections effectively, including:
<ul>
<li>Inspections Due</li>
<li>Tank Summary</li>
<li>Tank Detail</li>
<li>Inspections by Date</li>
<li>Inspections by Tank</li>
<li>Thickness Readings</li>
<li>Inventory Readings</li>
</ul>
The reports can be customized to include only specific companies, locations or tanks.</p>
<p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Extensive inspection checklists are also available for your in-service and out-service inspections to ensure your inspections cover the most important regulatory issues.  These inspection checklists are available for Microsoft Word and Word Perfect. Tank Manager&#174 also has extensive reference material that can point you to the definitive source for all of your regulatory questions <b>including state agencies for all 50 states!</b></font></p>
<br>
<center><img src="senhr.gif" height="5" width="536"></center>
<br>
<center><font size=5><b>Computer System Requirements</b></font></center>
<p><font size=4>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To use Tank Manager&#174 , your system must meet the following requirements:
<ul>
<li>Personal computer with 386DX/25 or higher (486 or higher recommended)</li>
<li>Microsoft Windows 95, Windows NT Workstation 3.51 or later, or Windows 3.1 (requires MS-DOS 5.0 or later) operating system</li>
<li>6 MB of memory (8 MB or more recommended) if using Windows 95 or Windows 3.1, 16 MB if using Windows NT Workstation</li>
<li>9 MB available hard-disk space</li>
<li>VGA or higher resolution monitor</li>
<li>Microsoft Mouse or other pointing device</li>
<li>Microsoft Word 2.0 (or higher) or WordPerfect for Windows 6.0 (or higher) are required for the inspection checklists.</li>
</ul>
