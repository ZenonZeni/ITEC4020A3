<U><B>Disclaimer:</B></U>  The following links are provided for your information only.  We do not specifically endorse any service or information provided on the internet beyond that information that is specifically published by our Center.  Self-help resources can be great to accompany work you are doing in therapy or in a support group.  We do not necessarily recommend self-treatment as a sole course of treatment. If you are interested in talking with a counselor at Rhodes, return to our <A HREF="http://hellman.stuaffairs.rhodes.edu/default.html">homepage</A> for more information about our counseling services.<p>
Netscape can lead you to some good resources on self-help groups, information about mental and physcial health issues, and wellness materials.  Here are some links you might find useful:
<P>
<UL>
<LI><A HREF="http://www.gasou.edu/psychweb/resource/selfhelp.htm">PsychWeb</A> has some wonderful resources on most any topic related to mental wellness.
<li><A HREF="http://www.apa.org/">PsychNET</A> is the site for the American Psychological Association and offers downloadable mental health information and other Web links.
<li><A HREF="http://www.psychology.com/">Cyber-Psych Link</A> provides access to psychology journals, newsgroups and more.
<li><A HREF="http://www.coil.com/~grohol/">Psych Central</A> is Dr. John Grohol's site where he features articles on mental health and hundreds of links!
<LI><A HREF="http://www.selfhelp.com/bootstraps.html">Bootstraps</A> is a weekly column on self-help issues.  They also keep an archive of previous columns.
<li><A HREF="http://www.well.com/user/selfhelp/artindex.htm">Self Help and Psychology Magazine</A> indexes self-help articles.
<li><A HREF="http://www.intac.com/~woy/drwoititz/source.htm">ACOA OnLine</A> has some great information and links regarding alcohol issues.  A good site for ACOAs and also for others interested in alcohol and other issues.
<li>It's not Frasier Crane, but<A HREF="http://www.onramp.net/cyberpsych/althome.html">Cyber Psychologist</A> has some good information on a variety of mental health issues.  Keep in mind that cyber-help should be an adjunct service to real-life counseling.
<li>The <A HREF="http://www.users.interport.net/~mpulier/psyjumps.html">Mental Health Resource Index</A> has a load of information on numerous mental health issues.  Good place to check for links to other sites, get information, and research issues.
<li> Although listed as a panic and anxiety page, good <A HREF="http://frank.mtsu.edu/~sward/anxiety/relax.html">relaxation and stress reduction</A> tips can be found at this location.
<li><I>More to come...keep checking here!</I>
</UL><P>
