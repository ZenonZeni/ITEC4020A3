<h2>Silicon</h2>
<p><strong>Semicrystalline Silicon</strong>

<p>Semicrystalline silicon can be produced in a variety of ways. The most popular commercial methods 
involve a casting process in which molten silicon is directly cast into a mold and allowed to solidify
into an ingot. Generally, the mold is square, producing an ingot that can be cut and <a href="slicing.html">
sliced</a> into square cells to fit more compactly into a PV module. (Round cells leave space between
them, where square cells can fit together with a minimum of wasted space).

<p><font color="#F79D00">The most popular method for making commercial semicrystalline silicon is casting, in which molten 
silicon is poured directly into a mold and allowed to solidify into an ingot.</font>
