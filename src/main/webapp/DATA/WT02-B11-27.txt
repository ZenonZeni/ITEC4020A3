<H1>The Northern Miner - How To Use It</H1>
<h2>What you need to access the Northern Miner WWW Product</H2>
<table>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>A Windows or Windows 95 PC, a Macintosh (Power PC or 68000) or Unix computer running the X Window system. 8 megabytes of memory or more is recommended.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>A dedicated or dial up TCP/IP Internet connection (a shell account will not work).</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>A graphical WWW browser such as Netscape 2.0 (recommended) or Microsoft Internet Explorer that can display tables and graphics. The Northern Miner Home page contains a link to the Netscape down load site which allows you to obtain the most recent version of Netscape.</td>
 </tr>
</table>
<h2>How to use the Northern Miner WWW Product</H2>
<table>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>Contact The Northern Miner circulation department as described in the WWW
<A HREF="home.html"><b>product announcement</b></A> and the newspaper insert to order the service.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>You will be assigned a <b>User Name</b> and <b>Password</b> that you need to access the service. These are for your use only. Do not give this information to other people.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>Point your WWW browser at the 
<A HREF="http://www.northernminer.com/">Northern Miner Home page URL</A> and click on the <b>Subscription WWW Service</b> link.
</td>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>You will be prompted to enter the <b>User Name</b> and <b>Password</b> that you obtained from the Northern Miner circulation department. Enter these into the dialog box and click on the <b>OK</b> button.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>You are now in the <b>Subscription WWW Service</b> home page. The page consists of the following:
  <table>
   <tr>
    <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
    <td>The date and time that the page was last updated.</td>
   </tr>
   <tr>
    <td><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
    <td>A link back to the Northern Miner Home page.</td>
   </tr>
   <tr>
    <td><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
    <td valign=top>A link to a page containing a links to all the issues.</td>
   </tr>
   <tr>
    <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
    <td>Short cut links directly to the current and back issues.</td>
   </tr>
   <tr>
    <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
    <td>A link to the Company Index page for each letter of the alphabet. Each company has a page that contains links to all the articles that the company is referenced in. For example, to find all the articles that reference Noranda, click on the <b>n</b> link.</td>
   </tr>
  </table>
  </td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>All <b>Subscription WWW Service</b> pages have the same general structure. 
  All pages contain back links to all the lower levels, right back to the Northern Miner 
  home page, so you can navigate back to any point without having to visit the intervening pages. 
  The WWW browser navigation buttons work as well.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>Each issue consists of a page with a list of all the headlines in the issue.
  Each headline is a link to the text of the article.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>Each article page contains the headline and full text of the newspaper article.
  As well, you can navigate directly to the <b>Previous Article</b> and <b>Next Article</b>.
  Each reference to a company in an article is a link back to the company page, 
  which contains links to all the articles that the company has been referenced in.
  </td>
 </tr>
</table>
<h2>What to do when you can't access the Northern Miner WWW Product</H2>
<table>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>Verify that your Internet connection to your ISP is working. This is by far the most common cause of problems. Verify that your modem and phone line are working. Even if your Internet connection is working, problems in other parts of the Internet may prevent you from reaching the Northern Miner WWW site. Contact your ISP help desk to find out what the current status of their network and their link to the Internet is.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>If the <b>User Name</b> and <b>Password</b> that you enter into the dialog box are not accepted, first verify that they are correct. Most <b>User Names</b> and <b>Passwords</b> assigned by the Northern Miner are in lower case, so if you entered them in UPPER case, then try again using lower case. If still not accepted then contact the Northern Miner circulation department to verify the <b>User Name</b> and <b>Password</b>.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>If the <b>Subscription WWW Service</b> home page is the same as last week and the new issue
  has been published (that is it's after 12:00 EST on Friday) then (this example for Netscape only)
  select the <b>Options</b> menu item and then the <b>Network Preferences</b> menu item.
  Then select <b>Cache</b> and click on the <b>Clear Memory Cache Now</b> and 
  <b>Clear Disk Cache Now</b> buttons. In general, you should configure you WWW browser to cache pages for less than 7 days.</td>
 </tr>
</table>

<h2>I'm new to this Internet stuff. What do I need to get Internet Access?</H2>
<table>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>A Windows or Windows 95 PC, a Macintosh (Power PC or 68000) or Unix computer running the X Window system. 8 megabytes of memory or more is recommended.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>For dial up service you need a 14.4 or 28.8 modem.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>An Internet Access software package:
   <table>
    <tr>
     <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
     <td>For Windows 3.1 we suggest Internet in a Box, available at most computer stores.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
     <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
     <td>Windows 95 Internet access is available using the Remote Access (RAS) product.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
     <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/blueb.gif"></td>
     <td>For the Macintosh we suggest <a href="http://www.mcp.com/hayden/iskm/mac.html">The Internet Starter Kit</a>, by Adam C. Engst., published by Hayden Books.</td>
    </tr>
  </table>
 </td>
 <tr>
  <td valign=top><IMG SRC="/icons/northernminer/redb.gif"></td>
  <td>You need an Internet Service Provider (ISP) to provide Internet access. There are several national organizations such as Compuserve or America Online that provide this service. There are also local ISPs in most areas.</td>
 </tr>
</table>
