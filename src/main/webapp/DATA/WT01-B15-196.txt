<H1>Publications</H1>

<HR>
The U.S. Department of Energy's Office of Civilian Radioactive Waste Management (OCRWM) offers publications, free of charge, to all interested parties through the OCRWM National Information Center. You may order the following publications by calling 1-800-225-6972 (202-488-6720 in the Washington, D.C., area), or by <A HREF="/comments/compage1.htm">sending us a message</A>.


<H3>Program-Wide Information (listed alphabetically)</H3>


<CITE>Analysis of the Total System Life Cycle Cost of the Civilian Radioactive Waste Management Program</CITE> (DOE/RW-0479)  Documents the total system life cycle cost estimate of one concept for the Civilian Radioactive Waste Management System.  The concept examined was a surrogate single repository system using multi-purpose canisters without interim storage.   (9/95, 67 pages)<P>
<P>

<CITE>Draft Civilian Radioactive Waste Management Program Plan, Revision 1</CITE> ( DOE/RW-0458, Rev. 1)  Supersedes the Civilian Radioactive Waste Management Program Plan dated December 19, 1994.  The revised Plan reflects Congressional direction incorporated in the fiscal year 1996 appropriations and the policy direction provided in the Administration's fiscal year 1997 budget request. (5/96, 112 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>International Nuclear Fuel Cycle Fact Book</CITE> (DOE/RW-0431) Provides a ready source of information concerning foreign fuel cycle programs, facilities, and personnel. (9/92, 260 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Locations of Spent Nuclear Fuel and High-Level Radioactive Waste Ultimately Destined for Geologic Disposal </CITE>(DOE/RW-0447) Lists U.S. locations of commercial reactors, non-DOE research reactors, Naval reactor fuel, and DOE-owned spent nuclear fuel and high-level radioactive waste. (9/94, 8 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>1994 Acceptance Priority Ranking and Annual Capacity Report</CITE> (DOE/RW-0457) Establishes the order in which the Department of Energy allocates the projected spent nuclear acceptance capacity of the Civilian Radioactive Waste Management System and projects the Department of Energy's annual receiving capacity of spent nuclear fuel and high-level radioactive waste.  (3/95, 125 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Notice of Inquiry on Waste Acceptance Issues: Response Summary</CITE> (DOE/RW-0462) Summarizes comments received in response to the Department of Energy's Notice of Inquiry on Waste Acceptance Issues.  (3/95, 110 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Nuclear Waste Fund Fee Adequacy: An Assessment</CITE> (DOE/RW-0490) Presents the Department of Energy's analysis of the adequacy of the 1.0-mil-per-kWh fee being paid by the nuclear utilities for the permanent disposal of their spent nuclear fuel. (10/96, 11 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Nuclear Waste Policy Act as Amended, with Appropriations Acts Appended</CITE> (DOE/RW-0438, Rev.1) Comprises a reprint of Public Law 97-425 and the following amendments: Title V of Public Law 100-203, Public Law 100-507, and the Energy Policy Act of 1992 (Public Law 102-486).  Energy and Water Development Appropriations Acts from 1984 through 1995 are appended.  (2/95, 129 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>OCRWM's Transportation Report</CITE> (DOE/RW-0473)  Provides background information on OCRWM's Transportation Program.  (6/95, 52 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Office of Civilian Radioactive Waste Management: Fiscal Year 1994 Annual Report to Congress</CITE> (DOE/RW-0464) Contains details of the Office of Civilian Radioactive Waste Management's activities and expenditures during fiscal year 1994 (October 1, 1993, through September 30, 1994), including audited financial statements. (5/95, 100 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Office of Civilian Radioactive Waste Management: Fiscal Year 1995 Annual Report to Congress</CITE> ( DOE/RW-0487) Contains details of the Office of Civilian Radioactive Waste Management's activities and expenditures during fiscal year 1995 (October 1, 1994, through September 30, 1995), including audited financial statements. (5/96, 95 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Spent Fuel Storage Requirements 1994-2042</CITE> (DOE/RW-0431, Rev. 1) Supplies information on light-water reactor spent fuel storage requirements.  (6/95, 139 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Strategy for OCRWM To Provide Training Assistance to State, Tribal, and Local Governments</CITE> (DOE/RW-0374P) Outlines the process OCRWM will follow to provide funding and technical assistance to States and Indian Tribes as required by Section 180(c) of the Nuclear Waste Policy Act of 1982, as amended. (11/92, 30 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Technical Career Opportunities in High-Level Radioactive Waste Management</CITE> (DOE/RW-0411)  Discusses careers available in the waste-management field and the type of education needed to begin those careers. (1993, 15 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>The Nuclear Waste Primer: A Handbook for Citizens</CITE> (1993 Revised Edition) Provides information about nuclear waste in the United States--what it is, where it comes from, how it has been managed, and what we can do with it in the future.  (1993, 170 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>The OCRWM Enterprise</CITE> (semiannual newsletter) Replaces the quarterly OCRWM Bulletin/Of Mountains and Science newsletter.  Details activities of the OCRWM Program and provides a calendar listing of OCRWM-related events.<P>
<P>

<H3>Yucca-Mountain-Specific Information</H3>

<CITE>DOE's Yucca Mountain Studies </CITE>(DOE/RW-0345P) Explains why Yucca Mountain, Nevada, is being studied as a potential geologic repository site. (12/92, 91 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Nevada and the Yucca Mountain Project</CITE> (Folder, includes 5 fact sheets) Contains public-outreach information on the Yucca Mountain Project.  (6/94)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Site Characterization Progress Report: Yucca Mountain, Nevada, Number 14 </CITE>(DOE/RW-0488)  Covers the progress of site-characterization activities performed at the Yucca Mountain, Nevada, site during the reporting period October 1, 1995, through March 31, 1996.  (8/96, 576 pages) Note: Site Characterization Progress Reports Numbers 9 through 13 are also available.  <P>
<P>
<CITE>Why Are Scientists Studying Yucca Mountain? </CITE>(Folder, includes 18 fact sheets) Contains information related to the site-characterization activities being conducted at Yucca Mountain, including environmental studies, earth sciences, project management, the cultural resources program, transportation, and radiation safety.<P>


<H3>Multi-Purpose Canister Information (Historical Documents)</H3>

<CITE>Conceptual Design for a Waste-Management System That Uses Multi-Purpose Canisters</CITE> (DOE/RW-0432)  Summarizes how multi-purpose canisters would be integrated into the design of the Nation's civilian radioactive waste-management system.  (1/94, 14 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Implementation Plan for the Environmental Impact Statement for a Multi-Purpose Canister System for Management of Civilian and Naval Spent Nuclear Fuel</CITE> (DOE/RW -0475) Serves as the Department of Energy's proposal to develop a multi-purpose canister for storage, transportation, and disposal of spent nuclear fuel from commercial and naval reactors (8/95, 104 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Multi-Purpose Canister Evaluation: A Systems Engineering Approach</CITE> (DOE/RW-0445) Details the Department of Energy's efforts to investigate various container systems for handling, transporting, storing, and disposing of spent nuclear fuel assemblies.  (9/94, 131 pages)<P>
<P>
<CITE>Multi-Purpose Canister System Environmental Impact Statement Information Set</CITE>  Includes a variety of fact sheets related to the OCRWM Program.  Topics include safety, spent nuclear fuel, transportation, Environmental Impact Statements, radiation, and more.<P>
