<H1>Utility &amp; Consumer Incentives:  Demand-Side Management
Programs</H1>
<H3>Overview</H3>

<P><B>Demand-side management (DSM)</B>
programs are explicit promotional activities by utilities to modify
customers' demands for energy services. Examples of utility DSM programs
include:</P>

<UL>
<LI>General information campaigns to increase awareness;</LI>
<LI>Technical audits of facilities to make specific recommendations for
improvements;</LI>
<LI>Financial assistance (either in the form of loans, direct payments,
or free installation) for the adoption of specific energy efficient
measures;</LI>
<LI>Financial payments or discounts as compensation for the
installation of utility controlled load shedding devices on customer
equipment (called direct load control); and</LI>
<LI>Innovative tariffs (such as time of day pricing).</LI></UL>
