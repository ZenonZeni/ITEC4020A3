<B><I><FONT SIZE=5>Energy and Business Productivity</FONT></I></B><HR>
Energy is second in cost only to personnel in many government and business operations.  Reducing energy use goes straight to the bottom line. If  employee productivity is increased simultaneously, the benefits can more than double. At the U.S. Post Office in Reno, Nevada, improvements in lighting reduced energy costs and, more significantly, directly improved mail sorting efficiency by 6 percent. This is one of numerous examples of increased productivity as a direct result of energy efficiency.</body><HR>
