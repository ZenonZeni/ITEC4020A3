<H1> Rotary Combustion Engine Data </H1>

<P> Categories are: <A HREF = "#NSU">NSU</A>,
<A HREF = "#Mazda">Mazda</A>,
<A HREF = "#Curtiss-Wright">Curtiss-Wright</A>,
<A HREF = "#Daimler-Benz">Daimler-Benz</A>,
<A HREF = "#FichtelSachs">Fichtel &#38; Sachs</A>,
<A HREF = "#Rolls-Royce">Rolls-Royce</A>,
<A HREF = "#Norton">Norton</A>, and
<A HREF = "#Other">Other</A>.

<IMG SRC = "../Images/engine-hist.gif" WIDTH = 504 HEIGHT = 214>

<P> Power figures are SAE net unless noted SAE gross, DIN, or JIS.
Early (1960 and before) German horsepower figures are almost certainly
gross even if not marked as such.  American horsepower appears to have
gone to net about 1970. Multiplying JIS by .80 or .85 gives SAE net.

<P> Engine capacities are quoted as single chamber capacity.  Various
bodies de-rate the RCE by applying coefficients of 1.7, 1.8, 2.0 or
even 3.0 to boost the apparent size.
