<h3>Southern Coalition for Advanced Transportation (SCAT)</h3><p>

<blockquote>
<B>Project Title:  Fast Charger / Charging Station<p>
Performing Organization:  Electronic Power Technology, Westinghouse; Georgia Power; Georgia Tech, Blue Bird; GNE</B><p>

<P>POC: John Wilson, Telephone (404) 526­7228</P>

<P><B>Program Summary: </B></P> 



<P>A rapid charger and charging station will be developed with a bus recharge of 112 hour (see 93 SCAT
Project # 12 GA). The station will be installed at Georgia Tech.</P>

<P><B>Benefit: </B></P><P>Extended EV range. increase battery life, and reduce battery maintenance. </P>

<P><B>Features of the Hybrid Electric HMMWV:</B></P>
<P><UL>
<LI>     All Electric or Full Hybrid Operation 
<LI>     High Vehicle Performance / Low Observable Signatures 
<LI>     300 Miles Range in Hybrid /20 Miles Range in Electric Mode 
<LI>     Up to 360 Horsepower available at the wheel drive shaft 
<LI>     Electronic Transmission (Not Mechanical) 80 MPH Top Speed 
<LI>     0 ­ 60 MPH in 11 Seconds with 3400 pound payload 
<LI>     Regenerative Braking 
<LI>     Power Steering and Brakes 
<LI>     90 Amp 24 volt electrical system 
<LI>     Internal and External Diagnostics 
<LI>     Mobile Energy source with 250 Kwatts of Peak Power Available 
<LI>     Drivetrain Concepts Scaleable/Transportable to a wide variety of Vehicles and applications 
<LI>     Vehicle operation is transparent to Hybrid Energy Source which can be gasoline, diesel, natural gas or advanced power sources such as fuel cells, free pistons, rotary engines can be used and even interchanged. 
<LI>     The integrated battery tray and its batteries can be changed in less than 10 minutes to a new pack or a new type of battery to adapt to new applications or requirements</UL></P>

<P><B>DELIVERABLES: </B></P><P>One fully modified Hybrid Electric HMMWV Prototype Vehicle One Off­board
Maintenance Unit 
</P>
<P><B>SCHEDULE: </B></P><P>Delivery to the U.S. Army by December 1996 
</P>
</blockquote>
