<H2>Energy Related WWW Sites</H2>

This is a sampling of 'entry points' for renewable and efficienct energy 
information on the World Wide Web. This list is in no way comprehensive,
and will change periodically.

<ul>

<p>
<li> <B>The US DOE Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy Network</B><br>
       EE/RE news from the DOE's national labs. Broad and complete. 
       <br><a href="http://www.eren.doe.gov/"><b>http://www.eren.doe.gov/</B></a>

<p>
<li> <B>Association of Energy Services Professionals</B><br>
       The Association of Energy Services Professionals (formerly the Association of Demand-Side Management Professionals) is an association of
nearly 2,000 utility executives, consultants, manufacturers, researchers, government regulators, and others concerned with promoting energy efficiency and value. 
       <br><a href="http://www.dnai.com:80/AESP/"><b>http://www.dnai.com:80/AESP/</b></a>

<p>
<li> <B>California Energy Commission Home Page</B><br>
       Programs of the California Energy Commission, including policy, case studies, and export program. Limited gopher site. 
       <br><a href="http://resources.agency.ca.gov/cechomepage.html"><b>http://resources.agency.ca.gov/cechomepage.html</b></a>

<p>
<li> <B>Electric Vehicle Sites on the We</B>b 
       A comprehensive listing of EV info online, including academic, commercial, and government sites. Sponsored by the GreenWheels electric vehicle company. 
       <br><a href="http://northshore.shore.net/~kester/websites.html"><b>http://northshore.shore.net/~kester/websites.html</b></a>

<p>
<li> <B>Critical Mass Energy Project</B><br>
       Fantastic resource for sustainable energy activists, containing background literature, congressional testimony, fact sheets, and publication lists for a variety of energy related topics, as well as chronicles of current energy-related legislative events. 
       <br><a href="http://www.essential.org/CMEP/"><b>http://www.essential.org/CMEP/</b></a>

<p>
<li> <B>Energy Yellow Pages</B><br>
       A comprehensive directory of renewable energy companies, trade associations, research organizations, educational programs and publications. Standard listings are available at no charge, expanded ones available for a fee. 
       <br><a href="http://www.ccnet.com/~nep/yellow.htm"><b>http://www.ccnet.com/~nep/yellow.htm</b></a>

<p>
<li> <B>Sun At Work In Europe</B><br>
       Sun At Work In Europe (SAWIE) is a quarterly publication which aims to provide an overview of the development and applications of renewable energy technology, particularly solar energy, in a European context. 
       <br><a href="http://sun1.bham.ac.uk/thorntme/sawie.html"><b>http://sun1.bham.ac.uk/thorntme/sawie.html</b></a>

<p>
<li> <B>"the Source" for Renewable Energy</B><br>
       Comprehensive directory of businesses who manufacture, distribute or service renewable energy products, organized by geographic location, by business type, and by product type. <br>
       <a href="http://www.rmii.com/theSource/renewableEnergy/"><b>http://www.rmii.com/theSource/renewableEnergy/</b></a>

<p>
<li><B>EnergyOnline</B><br>
       Business and Regulatory Information on the Energy Industry, with an emphasis on Electric
       Industry Restructuring. Effectively structured, with lots of annotated legislation, reports,
       etc., including a discussion forum. <br>
       <a href="http://www.energyonline.com"><b>http://www.energyonline.com</b></a>
<p>
<li><B>The Electronic Lobbyist</b><br>
Automated form for sending E-Mail in support of wind,
biomass, solar, and hydro energy to your Congressman/woman<br>
<a href="http://www.netcom.com/~stevie2/budget.html"><b>http://www.netcom.com/~stevie2/budget.html</b></a>
</ul>
