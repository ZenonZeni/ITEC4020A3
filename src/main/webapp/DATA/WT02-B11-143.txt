I recevied you questions. Following are my answers<br>
<p>
1. STT is a secure protocol. There are now only prototype products. We plan<br>
to test early implementation of the protocols early next year. We hope there<br>
will be production products by mid 1996.<br>
<p>
2. I believe you can look at the cryptogrphy used in STT. You see that we<br>
have even specified how to generate random numbers. Of course, after the<br>
specification is implemented, there will more opportunity to see the "entire<br>
process".<br>
<p>
3. Why do you think there should be more than one root key? I expect that<br>
there will be others who develop root keys, but we see no need for that if<br>
the root key is managed securely. <br>
<p>
4. I will have to talk to our technical people regarding the processing time<br>
for a 2k key. I do believe that there was analysis done that took into<br>
account the tradeoffs between size and performance, but will have to check.<br>
