<H1>County Forecasts</H1>
<HR>
<P>In response to the sharply increased interest in subregional forecasts,
Cambridge Econometrics now provides a comprehensive range of forecasting
services at the subregional level.</P>
<H2>Medium and Long Term County Analysis and Forecasts</H2>
<UL>
<LI>annual forecasts for output, employment, consumer spending and population
to the year 2010 for the 66 English and Welsh counties and Scottish regions</LI>
<LI>forecasts produced twice a year in February and July, consistent with
Cambridge Econometrics' report <I>Regional Economic Prospects</I> (published at
the same time)</LI>
<LI>methodology and data made transparent; CE will discuss further if required;
excellence and rigour of methodology a special feature of service</LI>
<LI>integrated national-regional-county model, disaggregated at the level of 49
sectors, providing consistent forecasts for counties adding up to regional and
national results</LI>
<LI>computerised county databank of historical data and forecasts</LI>
<LI>use of latest data from ONS</LI>
<LI>forecasts available for a single county or any number of counties</LI></UL>
<H2>The GB County Databank</H2>
<UL>
<LI>historical data from 1981</LI>
<LI>annual forecasts for output and employment to the year 2010 for the 66
English and Welsh counties and Scottish regions</LI>
<LI>annual forecasts to the year 2010 for consumer spending and population</LI>
<LI>data and forecasts provided as printed summary tables and in Lotus 1-2-3 or
ASCII format</LI>
<LI>the level of industrial disaggregation for output and employment is set at
9 sectors:</LI></UL>
