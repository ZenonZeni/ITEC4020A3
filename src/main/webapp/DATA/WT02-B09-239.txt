<H2>Protecting  Business  Ideas</H2>
<H2>Copyright</H2>
<H2>Supply & Services Canada, Printing & Publishing Division </H2>
270 Albert Street<BR>
Ottawa, Ont.<BR>
  K1P 5G8<BR><BR>
Provides protection from others reproducing or copying your work and exists<BR>
 for the duration of your life plus 50 years following your death. Subject matter<BR>
 protected by copyright includes any written material, musical or dramatic<BR>
 compositions, maps, works of art, photographs, drawings of a scientific or technical<BR>
 nature, motion pictures, sound recordings and computer programs. In Canada, copyright<BR>
 protection is automatic upon creation of an original work. For further information write to above address.<BR><BR>

<H2>Patents</H2>
<H2>Patent Act & Patent Rules-Supply & Services Canada</H2>
Ottawa, Ont.<BR>
  K1A 0A5<BR><BR>

A patent is a government grant giving a legal right to the ownership of an invention.<BR>
 No one is permitted to make use of, or sell the invention during the 17 year life of the<BR>
 patent. It is not renewable and is available to anyone who wants to make use of the<BR>
 invention after that time. Patents can be expensive to obtain as the services of a lawyer<BR>
 are often required and the process may take as long as two and a half years to obtain.<BR>
 For further information write to above address.<BR><BR>

<H2>Trademarks</H2>
<H2>Protecting Business Ideas </H2>
Registrar of Trademarks<BR>
Ottawa, Ont.<BR>
 K1A 0E1<BR><BR>

A word, logo, symbol, design or picture may identify a product from others in<BR>
 the marketplace. To register a trademark, a search of previous and pending<BR>
 registrations must be conducted to ensure the trademark is not already in use.<BR>
 A completed application for trademark registration must be sent to the above address. <BR>
Once the application is received, it is published in the Trade Mark Journal to determine<BR>
 if anyone opposes the registration. The process takes one year and is valid for 15 years.<BR>
 May be renewed.<BR><BR>
