<H2>What about Oxygen Toxicity ?</H2>
<UL><P>As a certified air diver, you were taught how to monitor your nitrogen uptake by using a set of dive tables.  The deeper the depth, the shorter the amount of allowable no-decompression time available due to nitrogen accumulation.  The same principle applies to oxygen at greater depths.  The greater the depth, the shorter the bottom time.  Oxygen toxicity ( for air or EANx ) usually comes into play deeper than at the standard recreational depths of 40-130 ft. EANx dive tables provide a reliable, conservative, and easy way to dive between 40-130 ft, while taking advantage of EANx benefits.</UL>
