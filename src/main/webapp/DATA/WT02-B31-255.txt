    <td><font size=+1><a target="reference" href="http://www.willamette.edu/~dlabar/firewall.html">
        Packet Filtering in Internet Firewalls</a></font><br>
        by <a target="reference" href="http://www.willamette.edu/~dlabar">Derek LaBar</a></br><p></
p>
        <font size=-1><b>Abstract</b><br>
        Firewalls are becoming a popular method of protecting private networks
        that wish to connect to the Internet. Many firewalls feature screening
        routers with packet filtering measures as an important security 
        mechanism. <br><br>

        This paper briefly states the basic ideas behind security policies
        followed by firewalls. It then summarizes why firewalls are justified,
        what they protect, and what they do not protect. The discussion proceeds
        to emphasize packet filtering implementations, particularly filtering by
        IP addresses, TCP ports, and protocols.  Specific examples of filtering
        services such as FTP, X11, DNS, Telnet, and NTP are examined. Then
        popular firewall architectures involving screening routers are
        discussed pointing out the advantages and disadvantages of each type.
        Finally, future filtering strategies such as dynamic filtering and
        non-linear filtering methods are addressed. </font>
