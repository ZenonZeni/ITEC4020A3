<H2>ESL By Phone Program	299-4808</H2>
Burnaby Multicultural Society <BR>
2nd Floor, 250 Willingdon Avenue<BR>
Burnaby  V5C 5E9<BR>
Fax: 299-4020<BR><BR>

Students are paired with a tutor and two half-hour sessions are carried out every<BR>
 week from their home. It offers an opportunity to improve conversational English in a<BR>
 non-threatening environment and also gives the student someone to turn to for answers to <BR>
questions and concerns about their new home. The program runs for 8 weeks.<BR>
 Call regarding fees for service. Volunteer tutors are always needed.<BR><BR>

<H2>Settlement Section 	684-7498 </H2> 
530 Drake Street,<BR> 
Vancouver V6B 2H3<BR>
Fax: 684-5683<BR><BR>
 
Information, service and skills centre for immigrants, refugees and non-English<BR>
 speaking B.C. residents. Assists in their settlement and encourages their integration<BR>
 into the community. Sponsors English conversation classes; cultural orientation classes;<BR>
 family, individual and job counselling; escort-interpretation and information and<BR>
 referral services. Operates the "Welcome House", a temporary reception centre for<BR>
 government sponsored refugees.Specially funded programs focus on English<BR>
 language training, vocational skills training and employment orientation. Fee-paying<BR>
 courses for English and computer skills development are also offered. Non-profit society.<BR><BR>

c/o Lower Mainland Purpose Society<BR> 
40 Begbie Street<BR>
New Westminster  V3M 3L9<BR>
Fax: 526-6546<BR><BR>

Promotes awareness and appreciation of the multicultural nature of the community<BR>

 securing gainful employment.<BR><BR>

<H2>Career and Employment Preparation	527-5120</H2>

<H2>Douglas College </H2>
PO Box 2503-700 Royal Avenue 	TTY: 527-5450<BR>
New Westminster  V3L 5B2<BR>
Fax: 527-5095<BR><BR>

The Career & Employment Preparation Program is a full-time course, <BR>
