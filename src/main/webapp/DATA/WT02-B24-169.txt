<H3 ALIGN="center"><FONT FACE="Bodoni BdCn BT">PLANNING TO BOND, BUILD, REMODEL OR RENOVATE YOUR SCHOOL FACILITY?</FONT></H3>
<H3 ALIGN="center"><FONT FACE="Bodoni BdCn BT">IF YOU ARE, THIS IS THE CONFERENCE FOR YOU!</FONT></H3>
<P ALIGN="center"><B>THURSDAY, FEBRUARY 6, 1997 - 6 P.M. - 10 P.M.</B></P>
<P ALIGN="center"><B>FRIDAY, FEBRUARY 7, 1997 - 8 A.M. - 3:15 P.M.</B></P>
<P ALIGN="center"><B>HOLIDAY INN-STEVENS POINT</B></P>
<P>Referendums are passing at an ever increasing rate as school districts cope with revenue caps through the bond referendum process.  If you are planning facility improvements, you will want to attend the First Annual School Facilities Conference to learn the following:</P>
<UL>
<LI> Build new or remodel:  Here is help answering the age-old question.</LI>
<LI> Elevators, lifts and ramps:  Renovating to meet handicap accessibility rules.</LI>
<LI> Underground storage tanks:  Removal of old tanks or installation of new.  What is the latest in the state requirements?</LI>
<LI> Air Quality &quot;headaches.&quot;  How to deal with indoor air quality management in new/old schools..</LI>
<LI>Environmental control systems:  Performance contracting can save you money.</LI>
<LI> Considering a bond referendum?  How and when to select a financing team.</LI>
<LI>Roof problems?  Technical presentation on various roof designs.</LI>
<LI> Playground dangers:  New or modified playground systems.</LI>
<LI> Flooring systems:  Wood, synthetic, carpet, help on decisions from installation to finishes.</LI>
<LI> The Big Picture:  Creating a facility master plan for your school district.</LI>
<LI> Technology:  Designing the networked school of the future.</LI>
<LI> Computer aided facility management:  Using technology to coordinate facility management.</LI>
<LI> Innovative building designs--planning a school of the future.</LI>
<LI> Passing a bond referendum--how other schools have done it.</LI>
<LI> Asbestos removal--renovating or remodeling opens up new problems in asbestos.</LI>
<LI> The perils of site selection.  Learn to consider zoning environmental issues and dealing with local politics.</LI>
<LI> Athletic field design.</LI>
<LI> Landscape design--it's more than planting a few trees.</LI>
<LI> Visit with a room full of vendors specializing in school facilities needs.</LI>
</UL>





















<P><B>WHO SHOULD ATTEND THIS SEMINAR?</B>  Superintendents, business officials, school board members and other interested school employees.</P>
