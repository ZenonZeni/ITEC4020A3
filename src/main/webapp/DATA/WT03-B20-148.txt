<H1>Laboratories</H1>
<HR SIZE=1 NOSHADE>



<P>RSSL&#39;s multidisciplinary laboratories are amongst the best equipped in Europe. Continuing investment in new technologies and methodologies ensures that RSSL is always able to provide the most up to date analytical techniques to its customers, and can address virtually any analytical problem. </P>

<P>Our facilities are divided across a number of broad disciplines</P>

<HR>

<P><IMG ALIGN=RIGHT SRC="nmr.gif" alt="nmr" align=bottom><H2>Analytical chemistry</H2></P>
<P>Gas chromatography, HPLC, SFC, ion chromatography, ATD, mass spectrometry (GC-MS, Py-MS, selective ion monitoring, fast atom bombardment, high resolution, ICP-MS, isotope MS), NMR (400 MHz), FT-IR, UV spectrophotometry, AA spectroscopy. Many of these techniques are used in authenticity studies, investigations of taints and off-flavours, and pesticide residue analysis.</P>
<BR><BR><BR><BR>

<HR>

<P><H2>Physical chemistry</H2></P>
<P>Rheology, tensile and compression testing, particle sizing, viscometry, melting point, tristimulus colorimetry, Differential Scanning Calorimetry, surface tension, moisture content, density, particle size reduction.</P>

<HR>

<P><IMG ALIGN=RIGHT SRC="microscopy200.gif" alt="microscopy" align=bottom><H2>Microscopy & image analysis</H2></P>
<P>Light microscopy, fluorescence, SEM, TEM, X-ray microanalysis, FT-IR, surface interferometry. Microscopy is often the first step for the ERS in addressing product contamination incidents and identifying foreign bodies.</P>
<BR><BR><BR><BR><BR><BR>

<HR>
<P><IMG ALIGN=RIGHT SRC="microbiology200.gif" alt="microbiology" align=bottom><H2>Microbiology</H2></P>
<P>Rapid microbiological methods include conductance, ATP bioluminescence, DEFT, flow cytometry, ELISA methods for Listeria and Salmonella, toxicology kits.</P>
<BR><BR><BR><BR><BR><BR><BR>

<HR>

<P><IMG ALIGN=RIGHT SRC="booth200.gif" alt="test booth" align=bottom><H2>Sensory evaluation</H2></P>
<P>Dedicated taste test laboratories equipped with PC's running RSSL's bespoke software TASTE. In addition to its role in formulating products, sensory evaluation is often the first stage in taint and off-flavour investigations.</P>
