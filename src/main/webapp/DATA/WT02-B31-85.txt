<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>Fixed Deferred Annuities</STRONG></FONT><P>

	Fixed annuities are viewed as secure investments by many people, since the insurance
company that offers the annuity guarantees the current cash value plus a future guaranteed
interest rate.  Today, they often offer interest rates that are comparable to (or higher
than) CDs, along with the added benefit of deferring taxes on the earnings until they
are withdrawn.  Unlike annuities, CDs are FDIC insured.



<P>
	Fixed annuities, unlike many other fixed interest investments, contain guarantees of
minimum future interest rates.  All fixed annuities have a minimum rate guarantee that
specifies the lowest rate the insurer can offer.  Some contracts also offer a "bailout"
provision that allows the owner to withdraw money from the annuity without surrender
charges
if the crediting rate falls by more than a specific amount.
