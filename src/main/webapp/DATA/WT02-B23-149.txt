<p><em><strong>Title</strong></em><strong>. </strong>Instantaneous Velocity and Pressure Features in a Turbulent Boundary Layer</p>
<p><em><strong>Abstract</strong></em><strong>. </strong>The velocity in a turbulent boundary layer was measured with Particle Image Velocimetry simultaneously with the 
wall pressure using a 13 x 3 array of microphones at Re<sub><font size=2>theta</font></sub><font size=3> = 1520, 3950 and 6790. Velocity measurements spanned two 
boundary layer thickness, 2</font><font size=4><em> </em></font><font size=3><em>delta</em></font><font size=4>, </font><font size=3>in the streamwise direction and from the buffer layer to nearly <em>delta</em>, in the wall normal 
direction. The wall pressure spanned 1 <em>delta</em> in the streamwise direction. The instantaneous velocity fields show organized 
motions also observed in data bases of Direct Numerical Simulations performed at lower Reynolds numbers. The shear 
layer structure is present in many of the realizations and the slope of the interface that divides low and high streamwise 
momentum flow ranges from 20&#186; when the shear layer is confined to the near wall region (<em>y</em></font><sup><font size=2><em>+</em></font></sup><font size=3> &lt; 100) to 45&#186; when spanning 
several hundred <em>y</em></font><sup><font size=2><em>+</em></font></sup><font size=3>. The shear layer interface is characterized by multiple vortex-like structures. Negative total vorticity, 
typically on the order of <em>omega</em></font><sub><font size=2><em>z</em></font></sub><font size=3> </font><sup><font size=2>+</font></sup><font size=3>&lt; -0.07, is distributed along the interface; however, only localized areas exist where there 
is significant levels of negative Reynolds Stress product, <em>u'v' </em>&lt; -3 <em>utau</em></font><sup><font size=2>2 </font></sup><font size=2>. </font><font size=3>The characteristic wall pressure signature of a 
shear layer consists of consecutive negative and positive pressures composing a local adverse <em>dp/dx</em>. It is estimated that the 
structures associated with this wall-pressure signature convects at a speed of 0.5 <em>U</em></font><sub><font size=2><em>inf</em></font></sub><font size=3> (12<em> utau</em>) with the positive pressure 
peak preceding the negative pressure peak by some eight viscous time units (<em>t</em></font><sup><font size=2><em>+</em></font></sup><font size=3> = 8). Tracking some of these structures 
shows that they may convect large distances, on the order of <em>delta</em>.</font></p>
