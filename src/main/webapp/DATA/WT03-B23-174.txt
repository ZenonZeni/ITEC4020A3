<FONT  SIZE=+1><STRONG>Kathleen Camilli</STRONG></FONT>, Tucker Anthony's Director of Economic Research, is responsible for providing daily input to both the fixed income and equity sides of the firm on the outlook for the U.S. economy and interest rates. Ms. Camilli brings over fifteen years of research and analytical experience to Tucker Anthony. She was formerly the Chief Economist at Maria Fiorini Ramirez, Inc.
<P>
Ms. Camilli began her career on Wall Street as a money market economist at Drexel Burnham Lambert.  Prior to joining Drexel, she was a Fedwatcher at Chase Manhattan Bank. She was trained as a practicing economist at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, where she was responsible for forecasting reserves for the Open Market Desk. She is the author of several articles, including “Reserves Forecasting for Open Market Operations” published in the FRBNY Quarterly Review in 1986.
<P>
Ms. Camilli is widely quoted in the financial press including <EM>The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, USA Today</EM>, and various financial news wires. She has been a frequent guest on CNN, CNBC, Nightly Business Report, Dow Jones Investor Network, and Bloomberg Business News. Ms. Camilli was recently chosen to contribute financial forecasts for the Blue Chip Financial Forecasts. 
<P>
Ms. Camilli received a B.A. in Economics and French from Rutgers University, a M.A. in French from New York University, and an MBA in Finance from New York University.
