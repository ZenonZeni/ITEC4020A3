<h3 align=center><font color="#FF0000">OBSESSIVE-COMPULSIVE DISORDER</font></h3>
<p align=left>Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is an anxiety disorder that is potentially disabling 
and can persist throughout a person&#146;s life.</p>
<p align=left>A person with OCD becomes trapped in a pattern of repetitive thoughts and behaviors that 
are senseless and distressing, but extremely difficult to overcome.</p>
<p align=left>OCD occurs in a spectrum from mild to severe; but if severe and left untreated, it can destroy 
a person&#146;s capacity to function at work, school or in the home.</p>
<p align=left>OCD can be effectively treated.</p>
<p align=left>Studies indicate that at least one-third of OCD cases in adults began in childhood.</p>
<p align=left>OCD affects more than 2 percent of the population. It is more common than such severe 
mental illnesses as schizophrenia and manic-depressive illness, and males and females are 
equally affected.</p>
<p align=left>Some obsessions and compulsions experienced by people with OCD are:</p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li><p align=left>concerns with contamination</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>overconcern with keeping objects in perfect order</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>images of death or horrible events</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>personally unacceptable religious or sexual thoughts</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>excessive or ritualized washing, cleaning or grooming</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>checking lights or stoves continually</p>
</li>
<li><p align=left>counting, arranging, collecting, repeating or examining </p>
</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
