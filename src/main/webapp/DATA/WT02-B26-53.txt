<H2>Bipolar Disorder And Multiple Relapses</H2>
 <HR>

	Patients with bipolar disorder have a high risk of relapsing even with aggressive treatment.  In The American Journal of Psychiatry (November 1995), Gitlin et al. reported a study of 82 outpatients with bipolar disorder for an average of more than four years.  There was a 73 percent probability that over five years a patient with bipolar disorder would relapse into an episode of mania or depression, and very likely more than once.  Even subjects who did not relapse reported a considerable amount of depressive symptoms.  Poor psychosocial functioning, particularly unemployment, accompanied poor syndromal course and predicted a shorter time to relapse.
