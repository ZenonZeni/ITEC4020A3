<h1>The LaGuardia Report - Sociological Study </h1>

<h1>Conclusions</h1>

<hr>

<p>From the foregoing study the following conclusions are drawn: </p>

<ol>
    <li>Marihuana is used extensively in the Borough of Manhattan
        but the problem is not as acute as it is reported to be
        in other sections of the United States. </li>
    <li>The introduction of marihuana into this area is recent as
        compared to other localities. </li>
    <li>The cost of marihuana is low and therefore within the
        purchasing power of most persons. </li>
    <li>The distribution and use of marihuana is centered in
        Harlem. </li>
    <li>The majority of marihuana smokers are Negroes and
        Latin-Americans. </li>
    <li>The consensus among marihuana smokers is that the use of
        the drug creates a definite feeling of adequacy. </li>
    <li>The practice of smoking marihuana does not lead to
        addiction in the medical sense of the word. </li>
    <li>The sale and distribution of marihuana is not under the
        control of any single organized group. </li>
    <li>The use of marihuana does not lead to morphine or heroin
        or cocaine addiction and no effort is made to create a
        market for these narcotics by stimulating the practice of
        marihuana smoking. </li>
    <li>Marihuana is not the determining factor in the commission
        of major crimes. </li>
    <li>Marihuana smoking is not widespread among school
        children. </li>
    <li>Juvenile delinquency is not associated with the practice
        of smoking marihuana. </li>
    <li>The publicity concerning the catastrophic effects of
        marihuana smoking in New York City is unfounded. </li>
</ol>
