<meta name="keywords" content="NAMI,depression"><meta name="GENERATOR" content="Microsoft FrontPage 1.1"></head><body background="/graphics/doc.gif" bgcolor="#FFFFFF" text="#000000" link="#000080" vlink="#0000FF" alink="#0000FF"><h3 align=center><img src="/graphics/blulogo3.gif" align=middle> <a href="/awareweek/aware.htm">Mental Illness Awareness Week</a></h3><h3 align=center><font color="#FF0000">DEPRESSION</font></h3><p align=left><font color="#000000">Major depression, or unipolar depression, is manifested by a combination of symptoms that interfere with the ability to work, sleep, eat and enjoy once pleasurable activities. It is more than a passing case of the &quot;blues.&quot; A person with depression cannot just &quot;pull themselves together.&quot;</font></p><p align=left><font color="#000000">Approximately 50 to 60 percent of individuals with major depression can be expected to experience a second episode. Individuals who have had two episodes have a 70 percent chance of having a third episode, and individuals who have had three episodes have a 90 percent chance of having a fourth episode.</font></p><p align=left><font color="#000000">In any given year, more than 17 million American adults have some form of an affective disorder, and roughly 5 percent of Americans suffer from major depression.</font></p><p align=left><font color="#000000">Women are twice as likely as men to experience major depression; one in four women, as opposed to one in eight men, are likely to experience a mood disorder at some point in their lives.</font></p><p align=left><font color="#000000">It takes an average of eight years to get a proper diagnosis of major depression.</font></p><p align=left><font color="#000000">Depression is more widespread than coronary heart disease (7 million), cancer (6 million), and AIDS (200,000).</font></p><p align=left><font color="#000000">Signs and Symptoms of Major Depression:</font></p><blockquote><ul><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Persistent sad or &quot;empty&quot; moods</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Feelings of hopelessness, pessimism</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Feelings of guilt, worthlessness, helplessness</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Loss of interest or pleasure in hobbies and activities once enjoyed, including sex</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Insomnia, early-morning awakening, or oversleeping</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Appetite and/or weight loss or overeating and weight gain</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Decreased energy, fatigue, being &quot;slowed down&quot;</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Thoughts of death or suicide, suicide attempts</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Restlessness, irritability</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Difficulty concentrating, remembering, making decisions</font></p></li><li><p align=left><font color="#000000">Persistent physical symptoms that do not respond to treatment, such as headaches, digestive disorders and chronic pain.</font></p></li></ul></blockquote><hr><p align=center><a href="mailto:barbara@nami.org,namiofc@aol.com">COMMENTS</a> | <a href="/faq.htm">CONTACT</a> | <a href="/joinform.htm">MEMBERSHIP</a></p></body></html>
