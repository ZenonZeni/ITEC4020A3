<B>Course Characteristics</B><BR>
<UL>
<LI>Yardage
<UL>
<LI>Jacobsen  --  7,034
<LI>Championship  --  6,558
<LI>Member  --  6,046  
<LI>Forward  --  5,345
</UL>
<P>
<LI>Course Terrain:  Rolling Hills
<LI>Soil Condition
<UL>
<LI>Fairways & Rough:  Clay Loam
<LI>Greens & Tees:  Constructed to USGA specifications
</UL>
<LI>Grass Varities
<UL>
<LI>Greens:  Pennlinks Creeping Bentgrass
<LI>Tees:  Pennlinks Creeping Bentgrass
<LI>Fairways:  Bardot Colonial Bentgrass
<LI>Rough:  Champion Perennial Ryegrass
</UL>
<LI>Average Green:  6,200 square feet
<LI>Average Tee:  1,360 square feet
<LI>Fairway Acreage:  34
<LI>Rough Acreage:  150
<LI>Sand Bunkers:  76
<LI>Water Hazards
<UL>
<LI>2 streams
<LI>2 ponds
<LI>1 lake
</UL>
<LI>Green Speed:  Stimpmeter readings average 9.5
<LI>Rounds per Year:  20,000
</UL>
<B>Course Maintenance</B>
<UL>
<LI>Aerification Schedule
<UL>
<LI>Greens & Tees:  Late Spring & early Fall
<LI>Fairways:  Late Spring & early Fall (Verti-Drain November-March)
</UL>
<LI>Top Dressing Program:  Greens & Tees every 2-3 weeks, March-November
<LI>Drainage Conditions:  Excellent
<LI>Irrigation System:  Toro Network 8000
<LI>Source of Water:  Willamette River
<LI>Mowing Height
<UL>
<LI>Greens:  1/8"
<LI>Tees:  1/4"
<LI>Fairways: 7/16"
<LI>Rough:  1 1/2"
</UL></UL>
<B>Environmental Practices</B>
<UL>
<LI>Management of wildlife food and cover
<LI>Integrated pest management program (limited use of pesticides and fertilizers)
<LI>Monitoring of water resources for water quality and wildlife potential
<LI>Water conservation measures
<LI>Encourage member and public involvement to help achieve environmental goals
</UL>
<B>Wildlife on Golf Course</B>
<UL>
Blue Heron, Ringneck Pheasant, Osprey Eagle, Bald Eagle, Golden Eagle, Great Horned Owl, Turkey Vulture, Pileated Woodpecker, Redtail Hawk, Coyote, Raccoon, White and Black-Tail Deer, Mountain Beaver, Elk, Mountain Lion
</UL>
<B>Superintendent Information</B>
<UL>
<LI>Course Superintendent:  John Anderson
<LI>Years at The Oregon Golf Club:  4
</UL>
<B>Course History/Interesting Facts</B>
<UL>
<LI>Founded July 4, 1992
<LI>The golf course offers some of the Portland area's most dramatic views of Mt. Hood, Mt. Adams and Mt. St. Helens
<LI>Twentieth golf course in the United States to become fully certified in the Audubon Cooperative Sanctuary Program for Golf Courses, sponsored by the USGA and the Audubon Society of New York State.
<LI>Annual site of The Fred Meyer Challenge, hosted by Peter Jacobsen
<LI>MOre than 3,000 roses are planted on the course
<LI>Recieved national recognition for winning the grand prize -- commercial division in the American Association of Rose Society's "Landscape with Roses" contest.
</UL>
<B>Other Key Course Personnel</B>
<UL>
<LI>Course Architects:  Peter Jacobsen & Ken Kavanaugh
<LI>General Manager:  Rick Mant
<LI>Director of Golf:  Peter Jacobsen
<LI>Head Golf Professional:  Craig Griswold
<LI>Assistant Superintendent:  Bruce Brower, GCSAA
</UL>
