<FONT COLOR="#FF0000">
<h3>Food Safety Act 1990</h3>
</FONT>
<h4>Definition of "Food"</h4>

The Act defines food <i>(Section 1)</i>:<p>

1.(1) In this Act "food" includes -<br>
(a) drink;<br>
(b) articles and substances of no nutritional value which are used for human consumption;<br>
(c) chewing gum and other product of a like nature and use; and<br>
(d) articles and substances used as ingredients in the preparation of food or anything falling within this subsection.<p>

(2) In this Act "food" does not include -<br>
(a) live animals or birds, or live fish which are not used for human consumption while they are alive;<br>
(b) fodder or feeding stuffs for animals, birds or fish;<br>
(c) controlled drugs within the meaning of the Misuse of Drugs Act 1971; or<br>
(d) subject to such exceptions as may be specified in an order made by the Ministers -<br>
	(i) medicinal products within the meaning of the Medicines Act 1968 in respect of which product licences within the meaning of that Act are for the time being in force; or<br>
	(ii) other articles or substance in respect of which such licences are for the time being in force in pursuance of orders under section 104 or 105 of that Act (application of Act to other articles and substances).<p>

<h4>Main Offences</h4>

The Act makes it an offence to
<ul>

<li>render food injurious to health <i>(Section 7)</i><p>

<li>sell food which fails to comply with food safety requirements <i>(Section 8).</i><p>  
<ul>
<li>if it has been rendered injurious to health<br>
<li>if it is unfit for human consumption<br>
<li>if it is so contaminated that it would not be reasonable to expect it to be used for human consumption in that state<br>
</ul>
<p>
<li>sell any food which is not of the nature or substance or quality demanded by the purchaser <i>(Section 14)</i>
<p>
<li>display food for sale with a label which:<br>
falsely describes the food, or<br>
is likely to mislead as to the nature or substance or quality of the food <i>(Section 15)</i><p>
</ul>
<h4>Enforcement Powers</h4>

Inspection and seizure of suspected food <i>(Section 9)</i><br>
Provides inspectors with powers to inspect for food which fails to meet food safety requirements or appears likely to cause food poisoning.
<p>
Improvement Notices <i>(Section 10)</i><br>
When an officer believes that regulations are not being met, he may specify required measures to be taken in a specified period of at least 14 days.
<p>
Prohibition Orders.<i>(Section 11)</i><br>
Imposed by Courts at time of conviction. <br>
a) If use of equipment or building involves risk of injury to health, courts may impose prohibitions on the use of the equipment or building;<br>
b) If the court thinks it proper, the convicted person may be prohibited from participating in the management of any food business<p>

Emergency Prohibition Notices and Orders. <i>(Section 12)</i>
If use of equipment or building involves imminent risk of injury to health, officers  may impose emergency prohibitions on the use of the equipment or building.  They must, within 3 days, apply to the courts for an Emergency Prohibition Order if the prohibition is to be extended.<p>

<h4>Defences</h4>

Offences due to fault of another person <i>(Section 20)</i>
<p>
Defence of due diligence <i>(Section 21)</i><br>
... it shall be a defence for the person charged to prove that he took all reasonable precautions  and exercised all due diligence to avoid the commission of the offence by himself or a person under his control.
<p>
<h4>Administration</h4>

Food authorities <i>(Section 5)</i>  Include:<br>
<ul>
<li>England:  the council of London boroughs, districts or non-metropolitan counties;
<li>Wales: the council of county or county boroughs;
<li>Scotland:  district councils
</ul>
<p>


Appointment of public analysts <i>(Section 27)</i><br>
("Analysis" includes microbiological assay and any technique for establishing the composition of food - Section 53)<p>

Provision of facilities for examinations <i>(Section 28)</i><br>
("Examination" means a microbiological examination)<p>

Procurement of samples <i>(Section 29)</i>
<p>
Analysis (and examination) of samples <i>(Section 30)</i>
<p>
Power to issue codes of practice <i>(Section 40)</i><br>
(1) For the guidance of food authorities, the Ministers or the Minister may issue codes of recommended practice as regards the execution and enforcement of this Act and of regulations and orders made under it<br>
(2) In the exercise of the functions conferred on them by or under this Act, every food authority - (a) shall have regard to any relevant provision of any such code; and (b) shall comply with any direction which is given by the Ministers or the Minister and requires them to take any specified steps in order to comply with such code.<br>


<h4>Regulations</h4>

The Act provides enabling powers to issue Regulations for:<br>
<ul>
<li>food safety and consumer protection <i>(Section 16 and Schedule 1)</i>
<li>enforcement of Community provisions <i>(Section 17)</i>
<li>special provision for particular foods <i>(Sections 18)</i>
<li>registration and licensing of food premises <i>(Section 19)</i>
</ul>

<h4>Penalties </h4>

Obstruction - Fine at Level 5 (£5,000) and/or imprisonment up to 3 months.
<p>
Other offences<br>
a) Conviction on indictment - unlimited fine and/or imprisonment up to 2 years<br>
b) Summary conviction<br>
- for offences under Sections 7, 8 or 14, fine of £20,000 and/or imprisonment up to 6 months.<br>
- for offences under other sections,  fine of £5,000 and/or imprisonment up to 6 months<p>
