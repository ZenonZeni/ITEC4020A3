<b>JAMES MacKENZIE </b>

<P>

Hydrogen Technical Advisory Panel member James MacKenzie has been a 

senior associate at the World Resources Institute for the past 10 years 
and is currently 

a member of the Climate, Energy, and Pollution Program there. While 
working at the 

institute, he completed studies in conservation, nuclear power, solar 
energy, oil 

resources, acid rain, air pollution, and climate change. Recently he has 
specialized in 

transportation issues; he is co-author of Car Trouble and wrote Keys to 
the Car, Electric 

and Hydrogen Vehicles for the 21st Century.  

<P>

MacKenzie received a Ph.D. in physics from the University of Minnesota. 
After 

completing graduate work at Argonne National Laboratory and the 
Massachusetts 

Institute of Technology, he began his career by focusing on public 
policy and energy 

issues. He was head of the energy staff at the Council of Environmental 
Quality during 

the Carter Administration.
