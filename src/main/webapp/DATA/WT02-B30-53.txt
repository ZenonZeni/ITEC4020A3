<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>Getting Information on Total Return</STRONG></FONT><P>



<STRONG>Total return information is available from a number of sources</STRONG><BR>

<UL TYPE=DISC>
	<LI>Your Registered Representative 
	<LI>Your mutual fund company
	<LI>The fund prospectus and annual report
	<LI>Daily newspapers and other financial publications
	<LI>Independent mutual fund performance monitors
</UL>
<P>
When you review total return data, it helps to read the fine print.  It explains the factors used
in
calculating performance and may help you make more meaningful comparisons among
different
funds.
<P>
Many daily newspapers publish performance data for mutual funds, including current Net
Asset Values,
changes in NAVs, and year-to-date total returns. Note that this data typically is not adjusted to include sales charges.
<P>

<STRONG>What total returns can tell you</STRONG><BR>
<UL TYPE=DISC>
	<LI>How a fund performed over different time periods
	<LI>How volatile a fund's performance has been year by year
	<LI>How sales charges may have affected a fund's performance
</UL>
<P>

<STRONG>What total returns can't tell you</STRONG><BR>
<UL TYPE=DISC>
	<LI>How the markets, economy, or fund will fare in the future
	<LI>Whether the fund's objectives are consistent with your own
	<LI>What risks the managers took to achieve the reported returns
</UL>
<P>

<STRONG>The value of your Registered Representative</STRONG><BR>
Just like in baseball, statistics tell only part of the story.  To look at the whole picture, your
Registered
Representative is your most valuable resource.  He or she understands your objectives, risk
tolerance,
and other factors which may affect your investment decisions.  The personal service your
Registered
Representative delivers can help you select the best course of action for your situation.
<P>

Total returns include the reinvestment of any dividends and capital gains
distributions.  If you take either
in cash, your actual returns may differ. 
<P>
SEC returns include the maximum applicable sales charges.  If you're eligible for lower
sales charges,
your returns may be higher than those reported.
