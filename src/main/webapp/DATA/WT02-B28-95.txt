<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>Board of Directors</STRONG></FONT><P>


<STRONG>Robert M. Baylis</STRONG> was elected a director in January 1996.  He is a former vice
chairman of CS First Boston, Inc.  Mr. Baylis serves on the Investment Committee.
<P>
<STRONG>William G. Burns</STRONG> was elected a director in 1986.  He is a former vice chairman
of NYNEX Corporation.  Mr. Burns is a member of the Audit and Investment Committees.
<P>
<STRONG>Patricia T. Carbine</STRONG> was elected a director in 1976.  She is a co-founder and the
president of the Ms. Foundation for Education and Communication, Inc.  Ms. Carbine is chairwoman of
the Audit Committee and a member of both the Corporate Organization &amp; Compensation and
Investment Committees.
<P>
<STRONG>Paul W. Douglas</STRONG> was elected a director in 1987.  He is a retired chairman and
chief executive officer of The Pittston Company.  Mr. Douglas is a member of the Audit, Corporate
Organization &amp; Compensation, and Executive &amp; Insurance Committees.
<P>
<STRONG>Kent B. Foster</STRONG> was elected a director in 1995.  He is the president of GTE
Corporation.  Mr. Foster is a member of the Executive &amp; Insurance Committee.
<P>
<STRONG>Lee M. Gammill, Jr.</STRONG> was elected to the Board in 1992.  He is currently vice
chairman of the Board of New York Life.  Mr. Gammill is a member of the Executive &amp; Insurance
Committee.
<P>
<STRONG>Richard L. Gelb</STRONG> was elected a director in 1981.  He is chairman emeritus of
Bristol-Myers Squibb Company.  Mr. Gelb serves as chairman of the Corporate Organization &amp;
Compensation Committee and is a member of the Investment Committee.
<P>
<STRONG>William R. Grant</STRONG> was elected a director in 1985.  He is chairman of Galen
Associates.  Mr. Grant is a member of both the Executive &amp; Insurance and Investment Committees.
<P>
<STRONG>Conrad Harper</STRONG> rejoined the Board in 1996 after a three-year absence during which he served as a legal
adviser to the Department of State and as a member of the Permanent Court of Arbitration at the Hague.  He
is a partner at the law firm of Simpson Thacher & Bartlett. Mr. Harper is a member of the Executive & Insurance Committee.<P>

<STRONG>Harry G. Hohn</STRONG> was elected a director in 1985.  He is chairman of the Board and
chief executive officer of New York Life.  Mr. Hohn is chairman of both the Executive &amp; Insurance and
Investment Committees.
<P>
<STRONG>Thomas W. Langfitt, M.D.</STRONG>, was elected to the Board in 1989.  He is chairman
and chief executive officer of The Glenmede Corporation.  Dr. Langfitt is a member of the Audit and
Executive &amp; Insurance Committees.
<P>
<STRONG>Leslie G. McCraw</STRONG> was elected a director in 1995.   He is the chairman and
chief executive officer of Fluor Corporation.  Mr. McCraw serves on the Investment Committee.
<P>
<STRONG>David W. Mitchell</STRONG> was elected to the Board in 1973.  He is a retired chairman
and chief executive officer of Avon Products, Inc.  Mr. Mitchell is vice chairman of the Audit Committee
and a member of the Investment Committee.
<P>
<STRONG>Richard R. Pivirotto</STRONG> has been a member of the Board since 1974.  He is a
retired chairman of Associated Dry Goods Corporation.   Mr. Pivirotto is vice chairman of the Corporate
Organization &amp; Compensation Committee, and a member of the Executive &amp; Insurance and Investment
Committees.
<P>
<STRONG>Robert J. Richardson, Ph.D.</STRONG>, was elected a director in 1984.  He is a retired
president of Bell Canada Enterprises Inc.  Dr. Richardson serves on the Audit and Corporate
Organization &amp; Compensation Committees.
<P>

<STRONG>Seymour Sternberg</STRONG> was elected to the Board in 1995.  He is the president and
chief operating officer of New York Life.  Mr. Sternberg is vice chairman of both the Executive &amp;
Insurance and Investment Committees.
