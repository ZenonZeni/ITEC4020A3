<CENTER><H2><A NAME="TOP">Symposium Offers Solutions to the <BR>Challenges of Educating Students from <BR>Diverse Cultural Backgrounds</CENTER></H2></CENTER>
The Council for Exceptional Children’s (CEC’s) 1997 Symposium on Culturally and Linguistically Diverse Exceptional Learners  provides new strategies for educating students with disabilities and/or gifts and talents who are from diverse cultures. The Symposium will be held January 8-10, 1997, at the The Raddison Hotel New Orleans.<P>
	Over the course of three days, renowned leaders in the field will address the most compelling and critical issues involving students with special needs of diverse backgrounds, giving insights into current problems as well as practical tools and solutions. The Symposium focuses on the following areas:<P>
<UL>
<LI>Overrepresentation of Minorities in Special Education
<LI>Social/Behavior Management for Students of Diverse Cultures
<LI>Effective Instruction for Language Minority Students with Disabilities
<LI>Creating Environments for Learning in a Multicultural Classroom
<LI>The Negative Impact of Grade Point Averages and Test Results on the Achievement of Students from Diverse Cultures
<LI>Gifted Education for Students from Diverse Cultures
</UL>

	Key presenters include:<P>
	<UL>
<LI>Margaret Wang, Principal Investigator, The National Center on Education in the 	Inner Cities 
<LI>	Carmen Arrega-Mayer, Researcher, Juniper Gardens Children’s Project
<LI>	Russell Gersten, Researcher, Eugene Research Institute                   
<LI>	Beth Harry, Professor, Professor, University of Miami 
<LI>	Sharon Ishii-Jordan, Professor, University of Nebraska 
<LI>	Esther Leung, Professor, Eastern Kentucky University 
<LI>	Cathy Kea, Professor, North Carolina A&T State University 
<LI>	Robert Rueda, Professor, University of Southern California
</UL>

	
	The Symposium is co-sponsored by CEC’s Division for Culturally and Linguistically Diverse Exceptional Learners (DDEL).<P>
