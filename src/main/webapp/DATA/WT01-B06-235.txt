<h3>Search Engine Behavior</h3>
<ol>
<li>Searches are performed across several fields:
<ol type=A>
<li>company name
<li>city
<li>state (two-letter postal abbreviation)
<li>ZIP Code
</ol>
<li>The engine can take as many keywords as you can fit into the box.  Separate keywords
with a space.
<li>If a match of any of your keywords occurs with any field in the master database, the engine will display the match.
<li>Matches are not restricted to whole-word matches.  For example, if you use
"<strong>wis</strong>" as your keyword, you'll pull up matches with "<strong>Wis</strong>dom"
and "Le<strong>wis</strong>."
<li>The engine is not case-sensitive.
</ol>
<h3>Tips for Searching</h3>
<ol>
<li>Try to use one or two keywords that are unique to the company you are searching for.
<li>"Less is More"&#151 The fewer characters you use in the search, the more matches you'll get.
<li>If you want to find only those companies in your ZIP code, simply type
in your ZIP code.  Do likewise for area code, city and state.  However, searching by these
methods, especially by the two-letter state abbreviation, will bring up other matches that
really shouldn't be included.  For example, a search for "<strong>CA</strong>" will bring up every company that has the letters "C" and "A" in its name.
</ol>
