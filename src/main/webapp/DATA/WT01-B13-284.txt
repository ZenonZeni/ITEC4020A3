<B>Why Kemper Asian Growth Fund makes sense</B>
<UL TYPE=CIRCLE>
<LI>Ability to invest in developing or emerging countries
<LI>Experienced management
<LI>Potential to increase returns
</UL>

<P>

Fund Objective: Long-term capital growth, by investing in a diversified portfolio consisting primarily of equity securities of Asian companies.

<P>

<B>Ability to invest in developing or emerging countries</B><BR>
The fund invests primarily in Asian equity securities believed to have potential for capital growth. The fund invests primarily in developing or "emerging" economies.

<P>

For example:
<UL TYPE=CIRCLE>
<LI>China
<LI>Indonesia
<LI>Korea
<LI>Malaysia
<LI>Philippines
<LI>Thailand
<LI>Taiwan
</UL>

As a policy, the fund will invest in Japan only when economic conditions warrant and then only on a limited basis. However, the fund may invest without limit in developed countries such as Hong Kong and Singapore.

<P>

Portfolio Manager: Andrew Mason, director of Asian Equities. Prior to joining Zurich Kemper Investments, Mr. Mason was portfolio manager of the Asian and Japanese portfolios for a United Kingdom pension fund and has 14 years of international investment experience..

<P>

Kemper has been managing international equities since 1981 -- longer than 94% of the companies managing international equities.*<BR>
