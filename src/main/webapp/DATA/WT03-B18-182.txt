<h2>
After reaching the milestone of completing the Beryllium Safety System, the
PISCES Lab at UC-San Diego had an open house in mid-March 1996.
</h2>

<img src="/www/pisces/openhouse/john1.jpg"><P>
Some members of the Beryllium Safety System construction team, including
technicans from sub-contractor General Atomics, stand with lab manager John
Latchem (center) in front of the Beryllium Safety System.<p>

<img src="/www/pisces/openhouse/piscesb1.jpg"><P>
Visitors examine the control console area of PISCES-B, a plasma-materials test
facility enclosed in the Beryllium Safety System.<P>

<img src="/www/pisces/openhouse/charlie1.jpg"><P>
Dr. Charles Baker (left), head of the ITER US Home Team located at UC-San
Diego, chats with a visitor in the PISCES-A area of the PISCES Lab.<P>

<img src="/www/pisces/openhouse/russ1.jpg"><P>
Dr. Russell Doerner (left) of the PISCES Lab research staff, explains the
operation of PISCES-A, a linear plasma device used for both non-hazardous
plasma-material interaction studies and basic plasma physics experiments.<P>

<img src="/www/pisces/openhouse/russ2.jpg"><P>
Dr. Russell Doerner, in front of the PISCES-A device.<P>
