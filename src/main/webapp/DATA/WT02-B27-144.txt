Euthanasia - recommended reading.</H1>

<H3><I>See also: <A HREF="quotes.html">Journal articles.</A></I></H3>

<H3>A selected list of some key euthanasia-related books, compiled by<BR>
the Scottish Voluntary Euthanasia Society</H3>

<P><BR>
<BR>
</P>

<LI>Albery, N <I>The Natural Death Handbook.</I> Pbk 224pp &pound;9.99GBP
Published by Virgin Books 1993. Excellent coverage of various topics surrounding
death, handled in a constructive and original manner. Good source references.
<I>Also <A HREF="http://www.newciv.org/GIB/natdeath/ndh0.html">on-line.</A></I>
<BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Albery, N &amp; Mezey, M (eds). <I>Re-Inventing Society: A Bumper Book
of Best Ideas, Schemes and Speculations.</I> Pbk 280pp &pound;14.85GBP
Institute for Social Inventions 1994. Includes many creative and thought-provoking
ideas about dying. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Ari&eacute;s, P <I>Western Attitudes toward Death from the Middle Ages
to the Present.</I> Pbk 111pp Marion Boyars Publishers 1994. Four essays
on the changing attitudes to death in Western civilisation under the influence
of industrialisation, mass culture and economic materialism. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Barnard, C <I>Good Life, Good Death - a Doctor's Case for Euthanasia
and Suicide.</I> Hbk 146pp Prentice-Hall 1980. Bold and provocative work
by the famous heart surgeon. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Barr, A, Biggar, J, Dalgleish, A &amp; Stevens, H <I>Drafting Wills
in Scotland.</I> Hbk 519pp &pound;95GBP Butterworths/The Law Society of
Scotland. Standard Scottish reference work for lawyers. Includes the VESS
Living Will. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Battin, M <I>The Least Worst Death.</I> Hbk 305pp Oxford University
Press. Collection of essays by a well-known ethicist. Contains some errors
relating to the right-to-die movement, but otherwise very sound. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Beauchamp T, Childress J <I>Principles of Biomedical Ethics (4th Edition).</I>
Pbk 546pp &pound;16.95GBP Oxford University Press 1994. One of the modern
&quot;classics&quot; of bioethics, explaining methods of analysing ethical
dilemmas according to the &quot;four principles&quot; of autonomy, beneficence,
non-maleficence and justice. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Brazier M. <I>Medicine, Patients &amp; the Law.</I> Pbk 696pp &pound;9.99GBP
Penguin Books 2nd edition 1992. Excellent basic textbook on medical law.
<A NAME="bma4"></A><BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>British Medical Association <I><A HREF="http://www.bmj.com/bmj/bmjpubs/bmapubs.htm">Medical
Ethics Today.</A></I> Pbk 374pp BMJ 1993. Very readable account of the
practice of medical ethics. Supportive of living wills. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>British Medical Association <I><A HREF="http://www.bmj.com/bmj/bmjpubs/bmapubs.htm">Advance
Statements about Medical Treatment.</A></I> Pbk 40pp+insert (16pp) &pound;4.95GBP
BMJ Publishing Group 1995. The BMA's excellent Code of Practice on Living
Wills. Also available from VESS. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Carmichael, K <I>Ceremony of Innocence - Tears, Power &amp; Protest.</I>
Pbk 203pp Powerful and inspiring examination of attitudes to grief and
dying, written by a leading broadcaster who is also the Vice-Convener of
the VESS Executive Committee. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Docker C <I>Collected Living Wills.</I> Pbk. First international collection
of living will documents. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Docker C, Smith C <A HREF="dd.html">Departing Drugs</A> Pbk 68pp. First
international scientifically researched manual on self-deliverance. Includes
a personalised &quot;Do Not Resuscitate&quot; note, an extensive glossary,
and a country-by-country listing. Available to established members of a
right-to-die organsiation only. Available from VESS. Also available in
German. Other languages in press. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Docker C, Smith C, Hofsess J, Dunn B <A HREF="bfe.html">Beyond Final
Exit</A> Pbk 115pp 1995. Collection of essays examining the evidence on
various methods of self-deliverance. Available from VESS. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Downie, R <I><A HREF="downie.gif">The Healing Arts:</A> An Oxford Illustrated
Anthology.</I> Hbk 334pp &pound;18.99GBP Oxford University Press 1994.
A celebration of the part played by medicine in the development of the
arts. A wonderful fireside book or gift. Copiously illustrated. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Downie R &amp; Calman K. <I>Healthy Respect - Ethics in Health Care.</I>
Pbk 291pp &pound;13.95GBP Oxford University Press 2nd edition 1994. Brillian
book on practical ethics for doctors and health care workers. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Downie R &amp; Charlton B. <I>The Making of a Doctor.</I> Hbk 204pp
&pound;24.50 Oxford University Press 1992. Highly recommended. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Fletcher N, Holt J, Brazier M, Harris J. <I>Ethics, Law &amp; Nursing.</I>
Pbk 185pp &pound;14.99 Manchester University Press 1995. Competent, multidisciplinary
guide. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Gillon R. <I>Philosophical Medical Ethics.</I> Pbk 190pp &pound;16.50GBP
John Wiley &amp; Sons 1985. Very straightforward, authoritative and readable
introduction to health care ethics. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Glover, J <I>Causing Death and Saving Lives.</I> Pbk 328pp Penguin.
Moral philosopher examines a range of issues including euthanasia. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Kennedy, I. <I>Treat Me Right - Essays in Medical Law &amp; Ethics.</I>
Pbk 428pp &pound;17.95GBP Clarendon Press (OUP) 1994. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Kennedy, I &amp; Grubb, A <I>Medical Law - Text With Materials (2nd
Edition).</I> Hbk 1423pp &pound;38.95GBP Butterworths 1994. Includes an
analytical overview of all the major right-to-die cases in Britain, with
large sections from the proceedings. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Kennedy, L <I>Euthanasia - the Good Death.</I> Pbk 56pp &pound;3.99GBP
Chatto &amp; Windus 1990. Highly readable case for euthanasia from this
well-known broadcaster in the Chatto Counterblast series. <A NAME="<kevorkian&quot;"></A><BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Kevorkian, J <I>Prescription: Medicine.</I> Hbk 268pp Prometheus Books
1991. Inventor of the controversial &quot;suicide machine&quot;, Dr. Kevorkian
outlines his views on rational self-deliverance. Also includes much interesting
historical detail on methods of capital punishment, related with colour
and flair. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Kilner J <I>Who Lives? Who Dies? - Ethical Criteria in Patient Selection.</I>
Hbk 359pp Yale University Press. An outstanding in-depth analysis of the
ethics of health rationing. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Kuhse, H <I>The Sanctity-of-Life Doctrine in Medicine.</I> Hbk 236pp
Oxford University Press 1987. Comprehensive critique from a bioethicist.
<BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Kuhse, H (ed) <I>Willing to Listen - Wanting to Die.</I> Pbk 277pp
AU$16.95 Penguin 1994. Collection of very different perspectives on death
and dignity. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>The Law Society &amp; the British Medical Association. <I>Assessment
of Mental Capacity - Guidance for Doctors &amp; Lawyers.</I> Pbk 152pp
British Medical Association 1995. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Little, S <I>And Who Should Forbid Them!</I> Pbk 28pp Published by
VESS 1989. Moving collection of real cases compiled from her experience
as Chairman before her own death. Available from VESS. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Logue, B <I>Last Rights - Death Control and the Elderly in America.</I>
Hbk 372pp Maxwell MacMillan 1993. Well-researched overview written by a
demographer. One of the few extensive coverages by a social scientist of
the right to die question. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>van der Maas, P, van Delden, J, Pijnenborg, L <I>Euthanasia and other
Medical Decisions Concerning the End of Life.</I> Hbk 262pp &middot;120GBP
(approx) Elsevier 1992. Probably the best book published on such facts
and statistics in the Netherlands. <A NAME="ddl"></A><BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>McLean, S (ed.) <I><A HREF="ddl.html">Death, Dying &amp; the Law.</A></I>
Hbk &pound;37.50GBP Dartmouth 1996. Some outstanding essays on various
aspects of how progress may realistically be achieved. <A NAME="cgdlw"></A><BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>McLean, S (ed.) <I><A HREF="ci.html">Contemporary Issues in Law, Medicine
and Ethics.</A></I> Hbk &pound;42.50GBP Dartmouth 1996. Covers living wills,
PVS, organ transplants and various other issues. Fairly detailed analysis
of the history, theory, legalities and future potential (and limitations)
of living wills (by Chris Docker). <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>McLean, S, Britton, A <I><A HREF="ssv.html">Sometimes a Small Victory</A>
. </I>Pbk &pound;9.99 GBP inc UK postage. Institute of Law &amp; Ethics
in Medicine, Glasgow University 1996. Results of a year-long investigation
into the feasibility of legal reform to allow physician assisted suicide,
including multi-disciplinary analysis, parliamentary debates, surveys,
draft bill (legislative template). Distributed by VESS.<BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Noll, P <I>In the Face of Death.</I> Pbk 254pp &pound;5.99GBP Penguin
1990. Courageous autobiography of a lawyer with cancer who decided to forego
life-sustaining treatment. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Nuland, S <I>How We Die.</I> Hbk 278pp &pound;15.99GBP Chatto &amp;
Windus. Graphic descriptions of exactly what happens during the dying phase
of comon killer diseases. <A NAME="ogden"></A><BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Ogden, R <I>Euthanasia, Assisted Suicide &amp; AIDS.</I> Pbk 135pp
Perreault Goedman Publishing 1994. Outstanding thesis on assisted suicide
between AIDS patients and their &quot;buddies.&quot; Ogden was faced with
a court subpoena to give evidence on his anonymised case studies but he
refused to name names. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Rachels, J <I>The End of Life: Euthanasia and Morality.</I> Pbk 204pp
Oxford University Press 1986. An examination of the ideas and assumptions
behind the proscription on killing. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Randall, F, &amp; Downie, R. <I>Palliative Care Ethics - A Good Companion</I>
Pbk, 202pp Oxford University Press 1996. Remarkable co-authorship between
a palliative care specialist (who is strongly opposed to the &quot;right
to die&quot;) and a moral philosopher (who supports it). The book doesn't
examine their differences, it concentrates on areas in which consensus
could be (and has been) achieved. The section on living wills, for instance,
is impressive for its lack of bias. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Seguin, M <I>A Gentle Death.</I> Pbk 249pp Key Porter Books 1994. Moving
work by a registered nurse who networks with professionals in specific
cases to ensure dying with dignity. Ms Seguin is the Executive Director
of Canada's Dying In Dignity Organisation. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Shavelson L. <I>A Chosen Death - The Dying Confront Assisted Suicide.</I>
Hbk 240pp Simon &amp; Schuster 1995. <BR>
<BR>
</LI>

<LI>Singer, P <I>Rethinking Life &amp; Death - The Collapse of our Traditional
Ethics.</I> Pbk, 256pp &pound;7.99GBP Oxford University Press 1995. Utilitarian
approach with intersting examination of many case histories. 
<HR>Enquiries on particular areas or books are welcome. VESS does not retail
or distribute any titles except where indicated.
