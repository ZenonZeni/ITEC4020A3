<p align=center><font color="#FF0000">Outreach to Public Libraries: Suggested Activities</font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">Books continue to be one of the best sources of public information about mental illnesses. Mental Illness 
Awareness Week is a good time to make sure your library is carrying the latest, most up-to-date books available 
on mental illnesses and their treatments. NAMI's Literature Committee has recommended these books this year 
for a Basic Library on Mental Illness. </font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">Steps for Building Your Public Library's Collection</font></p>
<blockquote>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">1.	Adopt the goal of adding books on the &quot;Basic Library List&quot; to your public library.</font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">2.	Look through the card catalog and on the library shelves to see what kind of books the library 
already has on subjects related to mental illness.</font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">3.	Talk to the librarian in charge of recommending books for purchase. Bring the &quot;Basic Library 
List,&quot; and explain that the books listed have been evaluated and recommended by NAMI.</font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">4.	Suggest which books on the list are a higher priority because they are interesting to read and 
contain current, useful information.</font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">5.	Follow up by writing a &quot;thank you&quot; note after you meet with the librarian, or after the books you 
recommended are on the library shelves.</font></p>
</blockquote>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">Library Exhibit</font></p>
<blockquote>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">1.	Explain MIAW 1996 to your local librarian or the person in charge of special programs at the 
library.</font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">2.	Suggest books from the &quot;Basic Library List&quot; to exhibit.</font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">3.	Offer to help select books and plan the display. You may wish to use the MIAW 1995 poster, 
&quot;Treatment Works.&quot;</font></p>
</blockquote>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">Educational Program</font></p>
<blockquote>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">1.	Explain MIAW 1996 to your local librarian or the person in charge of special programs at the 
library. </font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">2.	Suggest an educational program at the library in which family members, consumers, and mental 
health professionals would speak. Use the theme &quot;Treatment Works&quot; focusing on the designated 
illness for the day. Resources on different illnesses are listed elsewhere in this year's MIAW kit. </font></p>
<p align=left><font color="#000000">3.	Offer to help get speakers and publicize the event (e.g., flyers at the library, notices in your local 
newspaper and affiliate newsletter)</font></p>
</blockquote>
