<h4 align="center">Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>

<p align="center"><font color="#000080"><b>Sleep Disorders</b></font></p>

<hr>

<p><font color="#FF0000">General Facts about Insomnia </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - the inability to get the amount of sleep needed to
    function efficiently during the day</p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
    <p>2 - not a disease, but a symptom to be investigated</p>
    <p>3 - may be associated with organic disease (ie. arthritis,
    heart disorder) </p>
    <p>4 - may be a symptom of depression</p>
    <p>5 - may be caused by a person&#146;s lifestyle</p>
    <p>6 - can be temporarily abolished by administration of a
    drug</p>
    <p>7 - bad to take medication for relief without
    investigating the underlying cause</p>
    <p>8 - associated with increased mortality, poor career
    performance, over-eating, &amp; increased hospitalization</p>
    <p>9 - up to 35% of Americans complain of difficulty sleeping</p>
    <p>10 - up to 18% of Americans complain of serious sleep
    problems</p>
    <p>11 - approximately 4% of Americans receive prescription to
    help them sleep </p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000">Transient Insomnia </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - can be caused by stress, jet lag, or physical
    environment</p>
    <p>2 - may last several days</p>
    <p>3 - can generally be relieved by exercise, a hot bath,
    warm milk, daytime napping, changing bedroom environment</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000">Short-Term Insomnia </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - result of stress, illness, or bereavement </p>
    <p>2 - may linger for up to three weeks</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000">Long-Term Insomnia </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - lasts for more than three weeks </p>
    <p>2 - calls for an extensive diagnostic examination by a
    sleep disorder specialist</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000">Insomnia Related to Another Mental
Disorder </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - more than 1/2 of insomnia cases are related to
    depression, anxiety, or psychological stress</p>
    <p>2 - early morning wakefulness can be a sign of depression</p>
    <p>3 - sudden, dramatic decrease in sleep may be a sign of
    mania or schizophrenia</p>
    <p>4 - poor sleep may suggest obsessive-compulsive disorder</p>
    <p>5 - panic episodes during sleep suggest panic disorder</p>
    <p>6 - alcohol causes numerous awakenings during the night
    for long periods of time</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000">Insomnia in Children </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - usually from stress</p>
    <p>2 - problem resides within the sleep-wake circuits of the
    brain stem or within neurochemical balances impinging on
    these circuits</p>
    <p>3 - children with insomnia have often been poor sleepers
    since birth</p>
    <p>4 - medication is not recommended for most children</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000">Insomnia in the Elderly </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - often the result of changes in the body&#146;s
    &quot;internal clock&quot;</p>
    <p>2 - the elderly often experience depression or anxiety
    disorders which can disturb sleeping patterns</p>
    <p>3 - the elderly often suffer from Alzheimer&#146;s disease
    which can affect sleeping</p>
    <p>4 - the elderly are prone to become confused &amp;
    restless at night in the dark</p>
    <p>5 - the elderly are often more sensitive than others to
    medication due to a less functioning liver &amp; a more
    sensitive nervous system</p>
    <p>6 - medication tends to linger for a long time in the
    bodies of elderly patients</p>
    <p>7 - careful assessment is necessary when prescribing
    sleeping pills for the elderly</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000">Treatments </font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - Benzodiazepines</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. flurazepam (Dalmane)/long-lasting &amp; may cause
        drowsiness</p>
        <p>b. temazepam (Restoril)/intermediate acting agent</p>
        <p>c. triazolam (Halcion)/short-acting with no sedation</p>
        <p>d. affect memory (learning new information) when taken
        in large doses</p>
        <p>e. should not be used on a long-term basis/decrease in
        efficacy over time</p>
    </blockquote>
    <p>2 - tricyclic antidepressants</p>
    <p>3 - talk therapy</p>
    <p>4 - cognitive therapy &amp;/or stress management</p>
    <p>5 - relaxation therapies</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. abdominal breathing, progressive muscle relaxation,
        meditation, imaging,  hypnosis</p>
        <p>b. biofeedback, stimulus control, sleep curtailment,
        light therapy</p>
        <p>c. exercise, hot bath, warm milk, daytime napping,
        changing bedroom  environment</p>
        <p>d. may be used in combination with medication</p>
    </blockquote>
    <p>6 - sometimes several treatments are necessary</p>
</blockquote>
