<CENTER><B><I> Defense Diversification and Resource Matching Programs</I></B></CENTER><P></FONT><FONT SIZE=3><img src="blueball.gif"> -- <B>The competitiveness of U.S. firms and the continued viability of the U.S. defense industrial base is of critical importance to the economic security of the United States.  The Department of Commerce's Bureau of Export Administration has developed a comprehensive Resource Matching Program to assist U.S. firms develop new business opportunities in growing domestic and international markets to reduce their reliance upon defense procurement. These Resource Matching Programs include:<UL><LI TYPE=disc><B><I> International Marketing Assistance  Workshop:</I> </font><font size=2>International sales offer tremendous opportunities for firms seeking to expand their markets; your strongest domestic and foreign  competitors already know this.  This program describes  new Federal initiatives on trade advocacy, defense export assistance, and the variety of world-wide promotional services available to facilitate both commercial and defense exports. This half-day workshop is conducted by trade specialists from the Departments of Commerce and Defense and local and state trade organizations. Select <a href="ddsem.htm"><I>Matching Programs</I></A> to get information on the locations, dates, and fees for the Defense Diversification/Resource Matching seminars. <p></B></font><font size=3><LI TYPE=disc><B><I> Intellectual Property Rights, Patents and Trade Secrets Workshop:</I></B></font><font size=2>  <B>As an inventor, do you have exclusive rights to your technology?  Have you secured patents for your products?  Is your technology protected by secrecy agreements?  This session discusses these issues as well as the significant changes to intellectual property rights recently negotiated in the Uruguay Round of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT). In this half-day workshop, experts from the Federal Government and the private sector explain the patent process and discuss requirements for obtaining intellectual property protection.  Select <a href="ddsem.htm"><I>Matching Programs</I></A> to get information on the locations, dates, and fees for the Defense Diversification/Resource Matching seminars. <P></B></font><font size=3><LI TYPE=disc><B><I>Workshop on Regulatory Requirements for Defense Exports: </I></B></font><font size=2> -- <B>Defense export sales in 1997 are forecast to reach $14  billion.  This workshop is designed to assist your company in entering and/or expanding your presence in this market.  This full day workshop provides participants with an overview of recent trade policy initiatives impacting defense exports, and a detailed discussion of the licensing requirements for the export of defense articles and services.  This "hands-on" session focuses on understanding, and complying with, the International Traffic in Arms Regulations administered by the Department of State and is led by experts from the Department of State's Office of Defense Trade Controls and the U.S. Customs Service.  Select <a href="ddsem.htm"><I>Matching Programs</I></A> to get information on the locations, dates, and fees for the Defense Diversification/Resource Matching seminars. </B><P></B></font><font size=3><LI TYPE=disc><B><I> The International Standard: ISO 9000 Workshop:</I></B>	</font><font size=2>-- <B> More than ever, dual-use products are being designed to meet ISO 9000 standards.  Even the U. S. Department of Defense and NATO are increasing their purchases of products designed to this standard.  If you are not using  these standards, you may find yourself at a competitive disadvantage. In this half-day workshop you will learn how ISO 9000 contributes to a company's quality system.  Representatives of the U.S. Department of Commerce will explain changing trends in defense and global market standards and specifications so your company can identify differences between your existing quality system and the requirements of ISO 9000.   Select <a href="ddsem.htm"><I>Matching Programs</I></A> to get information on the locations, dates, and fees for the Defense Diversification/Resource Matching seminars.</B> <P>

</font><font size=3><LI TYPE=disc><B><I> International Fraud and Industrial Espionage Workshop:</I></B></font><font size=2> -- <B>Unfortunately, international fraud and industrial espionage have become growth industries.  Learn preventive measures to identify the threat posed by international scam operators and how you can effectively deal with the most common fraudulent practices.  Also learn how your competitors, as well as certain foreign governments, are actively seeking access to proprietary technology that is the lifeblood of your business. In this half-day workshop representatives from the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the Department of Commerce, and the Defense Technology Security Administration identify specific and generic threats, provide tips on how to safeguard your technology, and outline the laws that regulate the exchange or export of proprietary information.  Select <a href="ddsem.htm"><I>Matching Programs</I></A> to get information on the locations, dates, and fees for the Defense Diversification/Resource Matching seminars. <P></font><font size=3><LI TYPE=disc><B><I>Workshop on Marketing to the U.S. and State Governments: </I></B> </font><font size=2> -- <B> While the defense procurement budget has declined dramatically, other Federal agencies and state governments still procure billions of dollars of products and services annually. You don't have to be a prime contractor to enter this lucrative market.  This workshop provides specific guidance on bidding procedures, set aside contracts for minority and disadvantaged firms, and "tricks of the trade" that will help you successfully do business with Federal and State governments. This half-day workshop features procurement specialists from the Department of Defense, Department of Commerce, the U.S. Agency for International Development, state agencies, and the Office of Small and Disadvantaged Businesses.  Select <a href="ddsem.htm"><I>Matching Programs</I></A> to get information on the locations, dates, and fees for the Defense Diversification/Resource Matching seminars. <P>

</B></font><font size=3><LI TYPE=disc><B><I> Federal, State and Private Sector Financial Assistance:</I></B> </font><font size=2> -- <B>Federal and state agencies offer grants and matching funds for a variety of uses.  Hundreds of millions of dollars have been awarded through the Technology Reinvestment and Advanced Technology Programs to commercialize or develop new technologies. Other organizations offer financing, loan guarantees, and insurance to facilitate exports. This session will describe how you can obtain this financial assistance. This half-day workshop features financial experts from public and private sector organizations who will identify sources of financial assistance, define eligibility requirements, and provide assistance in the application process.  Venture capitalists will also be available to describe alternative funding opportunities.  Venture capitalists will also be available to describe alternative funding opportunities. Select <a href="ddsem.htm"><I>Matching Programs</I></A> to get information on the locations, dates, and fees for the Defense Diversification/Resource Matching seminars. <P>

</B></font><font size=3><LI TYPE=disc><B><I> Technology Transfer: How to Access and Commercialize Federal R & D:</I></B> </font><font size=2>-- <B>  Technologies and processes developed by our national laboratories are available to individual firms for a wide range of commercial applications.  This session will describe the myriad of technology transfer programs that can enable your company to: tap into valuable Federal R&D; diversify your product lines; improve your manufacturing processes; and strengthen your overall competitiveness. This half-day workshop will feature speakers from our national laboratories, the U.S. Department of Commerce and private sector executives who have developed commercially viable products.
