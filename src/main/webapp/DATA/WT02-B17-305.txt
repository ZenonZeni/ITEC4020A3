<h3><b>Recent HTS Highlights</b></h3>
<P>
Researchers in DOE national laboratories and in private industry have made a number of 
advances in HTS technology in recent years. The advances include progress in developing 
HTS wire technologies and power applications. 
<P>
<I>Note: For help deciphering abbreviations, click on <a href="abbreviations.html">
Abbreviations and Units of Measure</a>.</I>

<p><STRONG><I>Progress in Wire Technology</I></STRONG>
<P>
<STRONG>YBCO Wire</STRONG> 
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1.2 micron thick YBCO on Ni substrate with textured YSZ buffer
layer yields 1,200,000 A/cm<SUP>2</SUP> at 75 K and 0 T and 200,000
A/cm<SUP>2</SUP> at 75 K and 1 T (LANL)
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG>BSCCO Wire</STRONG> 
<P>
<UL>
<LI>Rolled, multifilamentary Bi-2223 wire yields 44,000 A/cm<SUP>2</SUP>
at 77 K and 0 T (ASC/WDG)
<LI>Short, pressed Bi-2223 wire yields 74,000 A/cm<SUP>2</SUP>
at 77 K and 0 T (ANL)
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG>TBCCO Wire</STRONG> 
<P>
<UL>
<LI>Electrodeposited wire made from Tl-1223 on Ag foil yields
J<SUB>c</SUB> of 68,000 A/cm<SUP>2</SUP> at 77 K and 0 T (NREL)
<LI>Spray pyrolyzed Tl-1223 on ceramic yields J<SUB>c</SUB>=325,000
A/cm<SUP>2</SUP> at 77 K and 0 T (GE/ORNL)
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG><I>Progress in Systems Technology</I></STRONG>
<P>
<STRONG>Long-Length Wire</STRONG> 
<UL>
<LI>1180 meters, Bi-2223/Ag powder-in-tube wire manufactured by
ASC with J<SUB>c</SUB>=12,500 A/cm<SUP>2</SUP> (WDG)
<LI>1260 meters, Bi-2223/Ag powder-in-tube wire manufactured by
IGC with J<SUB>c</SUB>=12,000 A/cm<SUP>2</SUP> (ANL)
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG>Coils</STRONG> 
<P>
ASC: Bi-2223 coils
<P>
<UL>
<LI>At 4.2 K, B (field) = 3.3 T
<LI>At 30 K, B = 2.6 T
<LI>At 77 K, B = 0.6 T
</UL>
<P>
IGC: Bi-2223 coils
<P>
<UL>
<LI>At 4.2 K, B = 3.2 T
<LI>At 27 K, B = 2.2 T
<LI>At 77 K, B = 0.38 T
</UL>
<P>
Oxford: Bi-2212 coils
<P>
<UL>
<LI>At 4.2 K, B = 1.1 T
<LI>At 15 K, B = 1.0 T
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG>Systems and Components</STRONG> 
<UL>
<LI>Recently began 2-year, $900,000 Southwire-led transmission
cable project. Initial 1-meter cable yielded I<SUB>c</SUB> of
800 A at 77 K at self-field (ORNL)
<LI>Announced HTS transformer project with team members Waukesha
Electric and Rochester Gas & Electric (ORNL)
<LI>Demonstrated cryocooled HTS magnetic system that produces 0.82 T at 20 K and 0.15 T at
72 K with magnet I<sub>c</sub> of 21 A at 77 K (IGC)
<LI>Completed and tested 3.3-T HTS magnet for magnetic separator system
<LI>Flywheel energy storage: extremely low coefficient of friction demonstrated in prototype
units suitable for >90% round trip efficiency (Commonwealth Research/ANL)
<LI>Current leads: Demonstrated 1500-A HTS leads for Superconductivity, Inc. (ANL)
<LI>Current leads: Designed and built components for 16-kA current lead system for Babcock
and Wilcox (ASC/ANL)
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG><I>Superconductivity Partnership Initiative</I></STRONG>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>Lockheed Martin successfully tested a 2.4-kV HTS fault-current limiter at a Southern
California Edison substation
<LI>125-hp HTS motor and 100-MVA generator coil subset testing will be completed in early
1996
<LI>115-kV HTS transmission cable project is on track for successful test in 1998
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG><I>Technology Partnership Initiative</I></STRONG>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>Signed a total of 97 Superconductivity Agreements with 38 active agreements including 9
CRADAS
<LI>Granted 4 licenses for use of national laboratory technologies
<LI>Issued 3 Technology Transfer reports to Congress
<LI>Funded 4 Superconductivity Partnership Initiative projects worth over $10 million
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG><I>Commercial Products Derived from Program Discoveries</I></STRONG>
<P>
<UL>

<LI>HTS powders via aerosol pyrolysis system (Superconductive Components, Inc. and
ASC/ORNL)
<LI>Cryogenic dewar coolant level sensor (Illinois Superconductor/ANL)
<LI>Non-contact rapid screening system (Lake Shore Cryotronics/LANL)
<LI>Sputtering Targets (Consultec Scientific/ORNL)
<LI>Automated multilayer deposition system (Neocera/ORNL)
</UL>
