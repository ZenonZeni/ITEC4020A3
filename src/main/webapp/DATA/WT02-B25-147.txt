<h3 align=center><font color="#FF0000">Suggested Agenda</font></h3>
<p>Below is a brief explanation of the themes assigned to each day of the week. For further information, a list of 
resources available to support your efforts is listed under the description. Please feel free to take events in your 
own direction, and continue to use these materials and conduct events all year long.</p>
<blockquote>
<p><em><strong>Sunday	</strong></em><em>&quot;Open Your Mind&quot; Candlelight Vigil</em></p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li>Nationwide Candlelight Vigil (<a href="/awareweek/vigilkit.htm">please see event outline, suggestions and press 
materials</a>)</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p><em><strong>Monday</strong></em><em>	Manic-Depressive Illness (</em><a href="/awareweek/bipolar.htm"><em>bipolar disorder</em></a><em>)</em></p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li>Bipolar disorder is a combination of excessive &quot;highs&quot; or periods of mania 
followed by dramatic &quot;lows&quot; or plunges into depression. In any given year, 2.2 
million Americans suffer from bipolar disorder.*</li>
<li>Available resources: Understanding Manic-Depressive Illness NAMI; Let&#146;s Talk 
Facts About Manic-Depressive Illness, APA; About Manic-Depressive Illness 
(#1026) NDMDA; Bi-polar Disorder (NIH 93-3679) NIMH; Bipolar Disorder: 
Self-Portrait of an Illness (outcome study) NDMDA; Everyone Needs a Hand to 
Hold On To (video and discussion guide) NDMDA; Guide Book to 
Manic-Depressive Illness for Patients and Families NDMDA</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p><em><strong>Tuesday	</strong></em><a href="/awareweek/schizo.htm"><em>Schizophrenia</em></a></p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li>This no-fault, biological brain disorder impairs a person&#146;s ability to think clearly, 
manage his or her emotions, make decisions and relate to others. It is often 
accompanied by delusions and voices that make it impossible for the person to 
distinguish reality from fantasy. There are 2 million Americans suffering from 
schizophrenia in any given year.*</li>
<li>Available resources: Understanding Schizophrenia, NAMI; Let&#146;s Talk Facts About 
Schizophrenia, APA; Schizophrenia: Questions and Answers ADM 90-1457 (also 
in Spanish) NIMH</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p><em><strong>Wednesday </strong></em><a href="/research.htm"><em>Research on Brain Disorders</em></a></p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li>Mental illnesses are brain disorders. In fact, 90% of what scientists know about 
the brain has been discovered in the past 10 years. Through CAT scans and MRIs, 
researchers have developed photography illustrating the difference between an 
affected brain and a healthy brain. Research brings us newer medication with less 
side effects, better treatments, and insight into the origin of these disorders.</li>
<li>Available resources: Brain Slides; Effecicacy of Schizophrenia; </li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p><em><strong>Thursday</strong></em><em> </em><a href="/awareweek/depress.htm"><em>Depressive Illness</em></a></p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li>Major depression involves periodic disturbances in mood, concentration, sleep, 
activity, appetite and social behavior. If left untreated, it can lead to suicide. 
However, between 80 percent and 90 percent of those suffering from depression 
can be effectively treated. Major depression strikes 9.3 million Americans in any 
given year.*</li>
<li>Available resources: Understanding Major Depression, NAMI; Answers to 
Questions about Clinical Depression, NMHA; Depressive Illness: the Medical 
Facts, The Human Challenge (#2009) NDMDA; Depressive Illness: a Guide for 
Patients and Families NDMDA; Everyone Needs a Hand to Hold On To (video 
and discussion guide) NDMDA; Depressive Illness: Treatment Brings New Hope 
NIH 93-3612 NIMH; Plain Talk About Depression NIH 94-3561 NIMH; Helpful 
Facts About Depression NIH 94-3875 (also in Spanish) NIMH</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p><em><strong>Friday</strong></em><em> </em><a href="/awareweek/ocd.htm"><em>Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder </em></a><em>/ </em><a href="/awareweek/panic.htm"><em>Panic Disorder</em></a></p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li>Obsessions are intrusive, irrational thoughts and unwanted ideas or impulses. 
Compulsions are repetitive rituals a victim feels he or she must do or something 
bad will happen. Sufferers experience &quot;pathological doubt&quot; compounded by 
obsessive, negative thoughts and repetitive rituals. OCD strikes 3.9 million 
Americans in a year.* The 2.4 million Americans* who suffer from panic disorder 
have uncontrollable panic responses to ordinary, nonthreatening situations more 
than four times in a four-week period.</li>
<li>Available resources: Let&#146;s Talk Facts About Obsessive Compulsive Disorder 
APA, Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder NAMI; Let&#146;s Talk Facts About Panic 
Disorder, APA; Getting Treatment for Panic Disorder NIH 94-3641 NIMH; 
Understanding Panic Disorder NIH 93-3509 NIMH; Obsessive-Compulsive 
Disorder NIH 94-3755 NIMH</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p><em><strong>Saturday</strong></em><em> </em><a href="/research/resear2.htm#child"><em>Children&#146;s Disorders</em></a></p>
<blockquote>
<ul>
<li>Many mental disorders are common to both children and adults. In fact, 
schizophrenia and manic-depression are known to strike adolescents and 
teenagers. Illnesses include Attention Deficit Disorder (ADD), hyperactivity, 
Tourette&#146;s syndrome, anxiety and eating disorders. Please see Glossary enclosed 
in this packet for more details on each disorder.</li>
<li>Available resources: Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder in Children NAMI; 
Depressive Disorders in Children and Adolescents NAMI; Facts for Families 
Series (enclosed in kit) AACAP; Glossary of Illnesses (enclosed in kit) AACAP; 
Adolescent Depression NMHA; Troubled and Troubling Children NMHA; 
Learning Disabilities NIH 93-3611 NIMH; Let&#146;s Talk About Depression ADM 
91-1695 NIMH; Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder NIH 94-3572 NIMH; 
Eating Disorders NIH 94-3477 NIMH; What to do When a Friend is Depressed: a 
Guide for Students NIH 94-3824 NIMH; Let&#146;s Talk Facts About Childhood 
Disorders APA</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p>* figures based on Epidemiologic Catchment Area (ECA) study conducted by the National Institute 
for Mental Health.</p>
</blockquote>
