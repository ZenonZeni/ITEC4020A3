<P>
<h2>Oil-Heated Federal Facilities Get Priority for SAVEnergy Audits Due to Projected Higher Heating Oil Prices</h2>

In its <I>Short Term Energy Outlook</I>, DOE's Energy Information
Administration (EIA) projects that heating oil prices are expected
to be about 9 cents more per gallon (10 percent higher) than last
winter, even under normal weather conditions.  Higher crude oil
costs account for 5 cents of the expected increase.  The relatively
tight supply situation accounts for the remainder of the increase.
 Colder-than-normal weather this winter would cause tighter markets
and push prices even higher.  Because of these expected price
increases, FEMP's SAVEnergy Audit Program will give highest
priority to those Federal facilities currently using fuel oil
when scheduling audits.  
<P>
<I>Facility managers should contact FEMP liaison Karen Thomas
of the National Renewable Energy Laboratory at 202-651-7536 for
more information. Copies of the Outlook, 4th Quarter 1996, can
be obtained through the EIA Home Page at <a href="http://www.eia.doe.gov">http://www.eia.doe.gov</a>,
by calling EIA's National Energy Information Center at 202-586-8800,
or by sending an email to <a href="mailto:infoctr@eia.doe.gov">infoctr@eia.doe.gov</a>.</I>
