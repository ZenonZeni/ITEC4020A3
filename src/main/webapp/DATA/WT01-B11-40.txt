<H1> Recent and Current RCE Developments </H1>

<UL>

<LI> May 10, 1996: <STRONG> <A HREF = "independent.html">Wallace Vows to
Build Mazda</A></STRONG> "It is not a strategy (to let Mazda disappear).
It is a strategy to build Mazda".  Yearly earnings are to be released
next week. Mazda will have a quarterly meeting at the end of the month.

<LI> April 12, 1996: <STRONG> <A HREF = "ford.html">Ford Tightens
Embrace of Mazda</A></STRONG>.  Ford increased its ownership of <A HREF
= "mazda.html">Mazda</A> from 23 % to 33 %.  Henry Wallace will become
president.  Previously he was president of Ford Venezuela and has been
executive vice president of Mazda since 1994.

<LI> April 3, 1996: <STRONG> <A HREF =
"http://www.carmag.co.jp/news/9604/news_9604_002.html">Simulation
Technology for Mazda's Rotary Engine Wins Award"</A> </STRONG> from the
Japan Society of Mechanical Engineers.  Yuji Hamai of Mazda's Technical
Research Center did research titled "Numerical Study of the Flow Field
Inside Rotary Engines".  Some of the airflow in a rotary engine can
become supersonic, making simulation difficult.

<LI> April: There will be no 1997 RX-7 for sale in North America.

<LI> Feb. 20: <STRONG><A HREF = "groundless.html">Mazda Denies Demise of
Rotary</A></STRONG>.  Ronald Leicht (from Ford), Mazda's senior managing
director, said the engine's fate will depend on customer needs.  A Mazda
spokesman made clarifying remarks pointing to the <A HREF =
"rx-01.html">RX-01</A> prototype and the <A HREF =
"hydrogen.html">hydrogen</A> rotary being use tested.

<LI> Jan. 1, 1996: <STRONG> <A HREF =
"http://www.carmag.co.jp/news/9512/news_9512_o_01.html">Mazda Creates
New Marketing Division</A> </STRONG> Kenichi Yamamoto, currently
director and general manager of Corporate Planning will become director
and GM of Marketing, reporting to R.J.  Leicht (Ford), senior managing
director of Marketing.

<LI> 1996 <A NAME = "Moller"> <A HREF =
"http://bozemanlegg.com/~mi/">Moller International</A> - Home of the
RotaPower Engine is a very interesting company which is now producing a
modern rotary engine.  Their slogan "Making new concepts in personal
transportation a reality" refers to their powered lift vehicle in
development, with eight Moller engines.  There is also an Aerobot.
Prospects look good and the Air Force has contracted production of 100
engines in Davis, California.

<LI> Aug. 1995: Mazda began a <A HREF = "hydrogen.html#test">two year
trial</A> of it's ultra low emissions hydrogen fuelled rotary engine.  A
Capella cargo van will be driven at least 20,000 km.

</UL>
