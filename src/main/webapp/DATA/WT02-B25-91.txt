<h4 align="center"><font color="#000080"><b>Lithium--Treatment
for </b></font><a href="bipolar.htm"><font color="#000080"><b>Manic
Depression</b></font></a></h4>

<hr>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Risks or Side Effects</b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - nausea, loss of appetite, &amp; mild diarrhea
    disappear after a few weeks</p>
    <p>2 - dizziness &amp; tremors are controlled by decreasing
    the dosage or taking medications like Cogentin or Inderal
    (Inderal may lower blood pressure &amp; heart rate or cause
    anxiety after discontinuation)</p>
    <p>3 - increased urination (reduced by taking Midamor or
    taking lithium at bedtime)</p>
    <p>4 - excessive thirst</p>
    <p>5 - dangerous for those with kidney disease</p>
    <p>6 - weight gain</p>
    <p>7 - hypothyroidism</p>
    <p>8 - increased white blood cell count</p>
    <p>9 - skin rashes</p>
    <p>10 - birth defects </p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. during first trimester of pregnancy</p>
        <p>b. risk of Ebstein&#146;s anomaly (a rare cardiac
        malformation)</p>
        <p>c. high birthweight</p>
    </blockquote>
    <p>11 - monitor blood level so as not to give too much
    lithium which will cause a toxic reaction </p>
    <p>12 - consult a doctor before taking certain medications</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. ibuprofen (Advil)</p>
        <p>b. acetazolamide</p>
        <p>c. antihypertensives</p>
        <p>d. anti-inflammatory drugs</p>
        <p>e. calcium channel blockers</p>
        <p>f. carbamazepine</p>
        <p>g. diuretics</p>
        <p>h. hydroxyzine</p>
        <p>I. inderal</p>
        <p>j. procardia</p>
        <p>k. marijuana</p>
        <p>l. muscle relaxants</p>
        <p>m. neuroleptics</p>
        <p>n. table salt</p>
        <p>o. baking powder</p>
        <p>p. tetracycline</p>
        <p>q. tricyclic antidepressants</p>
        <p>r. MOA inhibitors</p>
        <p>s. caffeine</p>
    </blockquote>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="2"><strong>&nbsp;</strong></font><font
color="#FF0000"><strong>What is Lithium?</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - a salt</p>
    <p>2 - introduced to the US in the 1960s</p>
    <p>3 - the most commonly used medication to treat bipolar
    disorder</p>
    <p>4 - there are a variety of different brands that come in
    tablets, capsules, &amp; liquids/Lithotabs, Lithane,
    Lithonate, Eskatlith, Cibalith, Lithobid</p>
    <p>5 - helpful in 70 to 80% of bipolar patients</p>
    <p>6 - one of the most effective psychiatric medications
    available</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>What/Whom does Lithium Treat?</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - both manic episodes in persons with bipolar disorder
    &amp; major depression</p>
    <p>2 - those who do not respond well to tricyclic
    antidepressants</p>
    <p>3 - often given along with antidepressants</p>
    <p>4 - treats schizophrenia in cases where there is a
    schizophrenic thought disorder  accompanied by a change in
    mood that mimics either mania or depression</p>
    <p>5 - affective disorders/experiencing of strong emotions
    not related to what is happening in the environment</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Dosage</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - requires 2 to 3 weeks for maximum effectiveness</p>
    <p>2 - take antipsychotics like Haldol until the Lithium
    takes affect (presents a danger of toxic reaction)</p>
    <p>3 - inadequate when depressions occur</p>
</blockquote>
