<P><img width=21 height=13 src="image/book.gif"> The Hugo Awards were named after Hugo Gernsback (1884 - 1967), an early science fiction writer, editor and publisher. It was largely through Gernsback's various pulp magazines (such as <B>Amazing Stories</B>) that science fiction took hold in the United States. The awards were first given at the 1953 World SF Convention. The awards were dropped the next year. However, since 1955 they have been given annually in various categories (only book-related awards are included below). The Hugo is thought of as a fan award, as voting for the distinction is by those individuals attending the annual WorldCon convention.
