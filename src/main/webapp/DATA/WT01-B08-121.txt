<A NAME="note"></A><H3>A Note from the President</H3>
<B><P>I admit it. I enjoy mowing the lawn. In fact, I enjoy it so much that I even mow my neighbor's yard every week.  It sounds strange as I write this but I seem to get lost when I am pushing the mower back and forth. It must go back to my Midwest roots and the long summers of lawn mowing.
My first real business in high school was mowing lawns for all of our neighbors. In my old neighborhood there are no fences. It is just a collection of shared lawns with houses in the middle. It was not uncommon to start mowing in a straight line at one home and turn around four houses later.
We rarely see lawns of this magnitude in the West. Like most of my neighbors, we've taken out most of the existing lawn and put in flower beds. Even as good as the flower beds look, without the lawn in the middle the yard would look incomplete.
</P>
Grow your own!<BR>
John D. Graham<BR>
</B>
