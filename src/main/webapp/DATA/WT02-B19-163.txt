<TR><TD ALIGN=LEFT WIDTH=60><td align=left width=500 VALIGN=TOP>
CEC's specialized divisions provide additional focus and depth of information to a particular exceptionality or interest area in special education.  Each publishes journals, newsletters, books, and monographs; sponsors conferences and other professional development programs; and provides unique networking opportunities you won't find available elsewhere.  Only members of The Council for Exceptional Children can join the divisions. </td> </tr></TABLE> <p>

<TABLE WIDTH=600 BORDER=0 CELLPADDING=0 CELLSPACING=0>
<tr><TD ALIGN=left width=60><td align=left WIDTH=500><b><i><A HREF="http://curry.edschool.virginia.edu/~sjs5d/dld/">Division for Learning Disabilities (DLD)</A></b></i><br>DLD promotes improved services, research, and legislation for individuals with learning disabilities.  Members receive <i> Learning Disabilities Research and Practice</I> quarterly and the <i> DLD Times</I> newsletter three times a year.<P>     

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"     ><b><i>Council for Children with Behavioral Disorders (CCBD)</b></i></A><br>CCBD is committed to promoting and facilitating the education and general welfare of children and youth with behavioral and emotional disorders.  Members receive <i>Behavioral Disorders</i> and the <i> CCBD Newsletter</I> each quarterly, and <i> Beyond Behavior </I> magazine three times a year.<P>

<A HREF="http://www.soe.uwm.edu/dec/dec.html"><b><I>Division for Early Childhood (DEC)</b></i></A><br> DEC is the group for individuals who work with or on behalf of children with special needs, birth through age 8, and their families.  Members receive the <i> Journal of Early Intervention,</I> the <I> DEC Communicator</I> newsletter, and discounts on the DEC annual conference and other DEC publications. <P>

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><b><i>Division on Mental Retardation and Developmental Disabilities (MRDD)</b></i></A><br>MRDD promotes the education and general welfare of individuals with mental retardation, developmental disabilities, and intellectual challenges, and those who serve them.  Members receive <i>Education</i> and <i> Training in Mental Retardation and Developmental Disabilities </I> quarterly and the <i> MRDD Express</i> newsletter three times a year.<P>

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><b><i>Council of Administrators of Special Education (CASE)</b></i></A><br>CASE promotes professional leadership and provides special education administrators with opportunities for personal and professional advancement.  Members receive <i>CASE IN POINT</i> twice a year, and five issues of the <i> CASE Newsletter.</I><P>   
   
<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><b><i>Teacher Education Division (TED)</B></i></A><br>TED promotes the preparation and continuing professional development of effective professionals in special education and related service fields.  Members receive <i>Teacher Education and Special Education</I> quarterly and the <i>TED Newsletter</I> three times a year.<P>     

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><b><I>Division for Children's Communication Development (DCCD)</B></i></A><br> DCCD is dedicated to improving the education of children with communication delays and disorders and hearing loss.  Members receive the <i><A HREF="http://vax1.mankato.msus.edu/~brice/jccd.html"><STRONG>Journal of Childhood Communication Disorders</STRONG></A> </I> and the <I> DCCD Newsletter, </i> each twice yearly.<P>
      
<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><b><i>The Association for the Gifted (TAG)</B></I></A><br>  TAG focuses on the delivery of information to professionals as well as parents about gifted and talented children and their needs.  Members receive the <i>Journal for the Gifted </I> and the <I>TAG Update </I> newsletter, each quarterly.<P>     

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><b><I>Council for Educational Diagnostic Services (CEDS)</b></i></A><br>CEDS promotes the highest quality of diagnostic and prescriptive procedures involved in the education of individuals who are gifted.  Members receive <i> Diagnostique</i> and the <i> CEDS Communique</I> newsletter, each quarterly.<P>      
<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><b><I>Division for Physical and Health Disabilities (DPHD)</b></i></A><br>DPHD advocates for quality education for all individuals with physical disabilities, multiple disabilities, and special health care needs served in schools, hospitals, or home settings.  Members receive <I>Physical Disabilities-Education and Related Services</I> once a year and the <I>DPHD Newsletter</I> quarterly.<P>

<A HREF="http://www.kumc.edu/dcdt/"><b><i>Division on Career Development and Transition (DCDT)</B></i></A><br>DCDT focuses on the career development of individuals with disabilities and/or who are gifted and their transition from school to adult life.  Members receive <I>Career Development for Exceptional Individuals</I> twice yearly and the <I>DCDT Network</i> newsletter quarterly.<P>      

<A HREF="http://www.ed.sc.edu/tam/tam.html"><B><I>Technology and Media Division (TAM)</B></I></A><BR>TAM promotes the availability and effective use of technology and media for individuals with disabilities and/or who are gifted. Members receive the <I>Journal of Special Education Technology</I> quarterly and the <I>TAM Newsletter</i> five times a year.<P>    

<AHREF="/dv/dv-cecdr.htm"     ><B><I>Division for Research (CEC-DR)</B></I></A><br> CEC-DR is devoted to the advancement of research related to the education of individuals with disabilities and/or who are gifted.  Members receive <I>Exceptionality</I> quarterly and CEC-DR's <I>Focus on Research</i> newsletter three times a year.<P>     

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><B><I>Division for Culturally and Linguistically Diverse Exceptional Learners (DDEL)</B></I></A><BR>DDEL is dedicated to advancing and improving educational opportunities for culturally and linguistically diverse learners with disabilities and/or who are gifted, their families, and the professionals who serve them.  Members receive a yearly monograph and three issues of the <I>DDEL Newsletter.</I><P>

     
<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"> <B><I>Division on Visual Impairments (DVI)</B></I></A><BR> DVI advances the education of children and youth who have visual impairments that impede their educational progress.  Members receive the <I>DVI Quarterly Newsletter.</I><P>     

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html"><B><I>Division of International Special Education and Services (DISES)</B></I></A><BR> DISES focuses on special education programs and services in other countries.  DISES activities cut across categorical lines and cover all age groups, from infants through adults.  Members receive a yearly monograph and three issues of the <I>DISES Newsletter.</I><P>    

<AHREF="ab-div-menu.html" ><B><I>CEC Pioneers Division (CEC-PD)</B></I></A><BR> CEC-PD promotes activities and programs to increase awareness of the educational needs of children with disabilities and/or who are gifted, and the services available to them.  The division supports CEC's programs and activities at all levels.  Members receive three issues of the<I>Pioneers Press</I> newsletter.  Join CEC-PD if you are a CEC life member, retired life member, retired regular member, or have been a member for 20 years or more.<P>
