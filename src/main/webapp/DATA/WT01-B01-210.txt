<P>Located just a few blocks from the White House, Lincoln Memorial 
and the Kennedy Center, the Washington Institute of Surgical Endoscopy (WISE) is 
an innovative facility dedicated to education and research in minimally invasive surgery.
  Our mission is to train surgeons and other health care professionals, accumulate 
information in the rapidly evolving field of MIS, and provide a fertile research 
enviromnent for more advanced procedures and technology.</p>
<p>WISE houses five operating stations, each equipped with dual monitors, three chip
 video enhancement, and fully tiltable operating tables.  In addition, 
slide-making, hard copy still photography, and VCR recording capabilities are available.  A 
flexible tip thoracoscope, laparoscopic choledochoscope, flexible gastrointestinal 
endoscopes, five Harmonic Scalpels and a robotic arm are available.</p>
<p> WISE is under the direction of <b>Jonathan M. Sackier</b>, M.D., F.R.C.S, F.A.C.S., 
Associate Professor of Surgery at The George Washington University Medical Center.</P>

<h2>Laparoscopic Anti-Reflux and Hiatal Hernia Surgery</h2>
<h3>Course Description</h3>
<p>Gastroesophageal reflux is common, affecting 40% of the population.  The vast majority 
of patients are controlled with simple measures, but some require more intensive treatment.  Proir
to the introduction of laparoscopy, few patients were referred for surgical management.  However
, the development of minimally invasive fundoplocation represents a meaningful alternativce for many.</p>

<h3>Course Objectives</h3>
<UL>
<LI>Participants will appreciate the indication for surgery in gastroesopheal reflux disease.
<LI>Participants will understand the utility of preoperative evaluation.
<LI>Participants will comprehend the techniques of laparoscopic operations for anti-reflux disease.
<LI>Participants will appreciate the range of complications and how to avoid them.
<LI>Participants will comprehend the presentation and laparoscopic management of paraesophageal hernia.
</UL>

<h2>Laparoscopic Management of the Common Bile Duct and Difficult Cholecystectomy</h2>
<h3>Course Description</h3>
<p>Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is now widely practiced for elective gallbladder surgery.
  However, there are a number of situations that can compromise the successful 
completion of the minimally invasive operation.  Theses include dealing with common 
duct stones, acute cholecystitis, bleeding, dropped stone, as well as general problems 
related to laparoscopy such as difficulty in gaining access.</p>

<h3>Course Objectives</h3>
<UL>
<LI>Participants will appreciate the range of clinical problems related to the CBD.
<LI>Participants will understand techniques of CBD imaging.
<LI>Participants will be exposed to management techniques of CBD stones.
<LI>Participants will appreciate techniques to avoid CBD injury and how to manage it should such injury occur.
<LI>Participants will understand how to manage the range of clinical problems associatd with complicated gallbladder disease.
</UL>
<h3>Dates Offered</h3>

<h2>Laparoscopic Colon and Rectal Surgery</h2>
<h3>Course Description</h3>
<p>The Laparoscopic revolution, engendered by the advent of laparoscopic cholecystectomy,
 has now made a wide range of colon and rectal procedures feasible by the laparoscopic 
route.  This course evaluates these procedures, exposing the participant to the 
indications, contraindications, techniques and complications of each operation.</p>

<h3>Course Objectives</h3>
<UL>
<LI>Improve awareness of utility of laparoscopy as a diagnostic tool.
<LI>Expose participants to principles of laparoscopic suturing.
<LI>Identify suitable patients for laparoscopic colon and rectal procedures.
<LI>Comprend skills requred to perform colon and rectal operations.
<LI>Participants will understand range of possible complications.
</UL>
<h3>Dates Offered</h3>
<UL>
<LI>March 10-11
<LI>June 20-21
<LI>September 15-16
<LI>December 4-5
