<h1>Drug Laws in South America </h1>

<hr>

<p><b>Laws on possessing marijuana for personal use in South
America:</b> </p>

<p><b>ARGENTINA: </b>No jail or fine for possession; users
usually must undergo court-supervised rehabilitation. </p>

<p><b>BOLIVIA:</b> Possession illegal. No move to decriminalize. </p>

<p><b>BRAZIL:</b> Possession illegal. Attorney general proposes
to end jail and fines, substituting mandatory rehabilitation for
users. </p>

<p><b>CHILE: </b>Possession illegal. Weak support for
decriminalizing, but no formal proposal to change law. </p>

<p><b>COLOMBIA</b>: Possession of small quantities of all drugs
legal; permitted ``personal dose'' for marijuana is 20 grams. </p>

<p><b>ECUADOR: </b>Possession illegal. No move to decriminalize. </p>

<p><b>PARAGUAY: </b>Possession illegal. No move to decriminalize.
</p>

<p><b>PERU:</b> Possession of small amounts legal; judges decide
what constitutes ``personal'' amount. </p>

<p><b>URUGUAY: </b>Possession for personal use not penalized; law
does not specify quantity for ``personal'' amount. </p>

<p><b>VENEZUELA: </b>Possession of up to 20 grams not punished;
users must go through drug treatment program. <br>
</p>
