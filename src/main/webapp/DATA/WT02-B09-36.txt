  <A NAME="AOL"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      AMERICA ONLINE
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.cloud9.net/~jegelhof/aol/aol-lwst/lwst-main.html">HAGEN
    v. AMERICA ONLINE, INC</A>. Class action for breach of contract and deceit
    based on alleged overbilling practices of AOL, filed in the Superior Court
    of California in and for the City and County of San Francisco on July 14,
    1995. <A NAME="ASBESTOS"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      ASBESTOS CASES
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.nacubo.org/website/asbtslaw.html">CENTRAL WESLEYAN COLLEGE
    v. W.R. GRACE &amp; CO</A>. Class action on behalf of public and private
    colleges and universities in the United States seeking compensatory and punitive
    damages, as well as injunctive relief, based on alleged liability for
    asbestos-containing products. Filed in federal court in Charleston, South
    Carolina on July 17, 1987. (Notice dated April 2, 1996: Pendency of Action;
    Proposed Settlement with certain Defendants; and Opportunity for Intervention)
    <A NAME="BALLY'S"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      BALLY'S - DALLAS HEALTH CLUBS
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.texlaw.com/jackson.htm">HEALTH &amp; TENNIS CORP. OF
    AMERICA v. KEITH JACKSON, et al</A>. Class action against Bally's health
    club alleging violations of the DTPA and the Consumer Credit Code. The plaintiffs
    claim that Bally's charged an excessive time-price differential in the form
    of monthly dues. Judge John Specia of Bexar County's 225th District signed
    an order certifying the plaintiffs' class on Dec. 11, 1995. Class certification
    was affirmed by the 4th Court of Appeals on June 5, 1996.
    <A NAME="BAUSCH-LOMB"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      BAUSCH &amp; LOMB CONTACT LENS LITIGATION
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://seamless.com/alexanderlaw/txt/article/lenseset.shtml">In
    re BAUSCH &amp; LOMB CONTACT LENS LITIGATION</A>, U.S. District Court for
    the Northern District of Alabama, Civil Action No. 94-C-1144-W. Preliminary
    approval of a class settlement on behalf of consumer purchasers of Bausch
    &amp; Lomb's Medalist lenses from January 1, 1991 through December 31, 1995,
    Optima FW lenses from November 1, 1990 through December 31, 1995, and Criterion
    Ultra FW lenses from November 1, 1990 through April 30, 1996. The Court has
    scheduled a fairness hearing on November 26, 1996. <A NAME="BIOGEN"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      BIOGEN, INC.
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="classactions/biogen.html">In re: Biogen Securities Litigation</A>,
    United States District Court for the District of Massachusetts, Civ. No.
    94-12177-PBS. Securities class action against Biogen and its CEO. Plaintiffs
    are seeking to communicate with persons who purchased Biogen common stock
    between January 11, 1994 and March 14, 1994.
  <DT>
    <H3>
      BOSTON TECHNOLOGY
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.bostontechnology.com/newsuit.htm">Shareholders' class
    action suit</A> filed against Boston Technology, Inc. and certain of its
    current and former officers and directors in the United States District Court
    for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania. <A NAME="CALIFORNIA"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      CALIFORNIA SMALL BUSINESSES
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.dgs.ca.gov/osmb/lglnonly.htm">LIDO FINANCIAL SERVICES,
    Inc. v. STATE OF CALIFORNIA</A>, Case No. BC 081 255 in the Superior Court
    for the State of California, County of Los Angeles, Central District. Class
    action on behalf of small businesses who may be entitled to interest on amounts
    which the State of California and its agencies paid late during the 63-day
    budget crisis of 1992. On March 21, 1996, the class was certified. Claims
    form is available online. <A NAME="CINCINNATI"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      CINCINNATI COMPUTER CONNECTION BBS
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://w3.one.net/~smadigan/free/text/classact.htm">GUEST v. LEIS</A>.
    Class action on behalf of subscribers to the Cincinnati Computer Connection
    electronic bulletin board system seeking to redress alleged violation of
    their civil rights by Hamilton County Sheriff, filed in U.S. District Court
    for the Southern District Of Ohio, Western Division on August 7, 1995.
    (Complaint) <A NAME="CISCO-TELEBIT"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      CISCO SYSTEMS &amp; TELEBIT
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.telebit.com/NewS/lawstpr.html">Cisco Systems, Inc. and
    Telebit Corp.</A> have been named in a securities class action filed in the
    Middlesex County, Massachusetts, Superior Court. <A NAME="censorship"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      COMMUNICATIONS DECENCY ACT OF
      1996<IMG SRC="images/rib_trn_tiny_plain.gif" WIDTH="18" HEIGHT="30" ALIGN=ABSMIDDLE
	  NATURALSIZEFLAG="3">
    </H3>
  <DD>
    Sections 502 and 507 of the
    <A HREF="http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/query/z?c104:s.652.enr:">Telecommunications
    Act of 1996</A> criminalize the online expression of "offensive" information
    and ideas. Three lawsuits seek to invalidate the statute:<BR>
    <BR>
    <UL TYPE=circle>
      <LI>
	<A HREF="http://www.eff.org/pub/Legal/Cases/CRLP_v_DoJ/">SANGER v. RENO</A>.
	Planned Parenthood of New York City and other abortion rights groups challenge
	the constitutionality of the Act in a <STRONG>class action</STRONG> lawsuit
	filed in U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of New York on February
	7, 1996.
      <LI>
	<A HREF="http://www.aclu.org/issues/cyber/arcl.html">AMERICAN CIVIL LIBERTIES
	UNION v. RENO</A>. Twenty organizations and individuals attempt to stop
	enforcement of the Act in a lawsuit filed in U.S. District Court for the
	Eastern District of Pennsylvania on February 8, 1996.
      <LI>
	<A HREF="http://www.cdt.org/ciec/index.html">CIEC v. U.S. DEPARTMENT OF
	JUSTICE</A>. Citizens Internet Empowerment Coalition's 35 members, including
	the American Library Association, America Online, Compuserve, Prodigy, Microsoft,
	NETCOM, Commercial Internet eXchange, Newspaper Association of America, Wired
	Magazine, Hotwired, and Families Against Internet Censorhip, seek to overturn
	the Act as violative of the rights of ALL Internet users in a lawsuit filed
	in U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania on February
	26, 1996. (Complaint)
    </UL>
    <P>
    <A NAME="CONSTELLATION"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      CONSTELLATION BANCORP
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.hunt.com/legal/ca06.htm">MEILKE v. CONSTELLATION
    BANCORP</A>. Securities class action on behalf of purchasers of common stock
    of Constellation Bancorp between February 6, 1989 and January 24, 1990, filed
    in U.S. District Court in New York City. Notice of proposed settlement. Hearing
    on April 22, 1996. (Notice of Pendency and Proposed Settlement)
    <A NAME="CRANE CANADA"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      CRANE CANADA
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.singleton.com/crane.html">Crane Canada</A>. The B.C.
    Supreme Court certified a class action lawsuit against Crane Canada Inc.
    for damages arising from the failure of thousands of toilet tanks manufactured
    by Crane. <A NAME="DETROIT EDISON"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      DETROIT EDISON
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.detnews.com/menu/stories/50011.htm">Detroit Edison</A>
    is facing a class action lawsuit from older and African-American workers
    who allege they were systematically discriminated against by corporate
    downsizing. <A NAME="EQUITABLE"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      EQUITABLE LIFE ASSURANCE SOCIETY
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://pciweb.baka.com/web/adelman/index.html">MULTI-STATE CLASS
    ACTIONS</A> have been filed against the Equitable Life Assurance Society
    alleging that premiums were improperly raised. Substantial refunds are being
    sought. Policyholders who are or were insured under any Equitable Lifetime
    Major Medical Policy should check this out. <A NAME="FEDEX"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      FEDERAL EXPRESS CORPORATION
    </H3>
  <DD>
    Three class actions have been filed alleging that Federal Express impermissibly
    collected a 6.25% federal excise tax on items shipped by air after the tax
    expired on December 31, 1995. Lawsuits were filed:<BR>
    <BR>
    <UL TYPE=disc>
      <LI>
	On April 30, 1996 in the
	<A HREF="http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/230211/0000950103-96-000920.txt">Superior
	Court of the State of California</A>
      <LI>
	On May 14, 1996 in the
	<A HREF="http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/230211/0000950103-96-000920.txt">United
	States District Court for the District of Minnesota</A>
      <LI>
	On June 7, 1996 in the
	<A HREF="http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/230211/0000950103-96-000947.txt">District
	Court of the State of Minnesota</A>.
    </UL>

  <DT>
    <H3>
      GRAND CASINOS
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.grandcasinos.com/press/LAWSUIT.html">Grand Casinos</A>
    (NYSE:GND) has been named by shareholders of Stratosphere Corporation as
    a defendant in securities class action lawsuits. <A NAME="HEMOPHILIA"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      HEMOPHILIA HIV COMPENSATION LITIGATION
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.web-depot.com/hemophilia/">Proposed $640-million
    settlement</A> with U.S. hemophiliacs who became infected with HIV from
    contaminated blood-clotting medications. <A NAME="HOME SIDING"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      HOME SIDING
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.hunt.com/lpsiding.htm">LOUISIANA-PACIFIC INNER-SEAL&reg;
    SIDING LITIGATION</A>. Class action alleging that siding may prematurely
    rot, buckle, crack, or deteriorate filed in U.S. District Court in Portland,
    Oregon. Proposed settlement establishes a compensation program. (Proposed
    Settlement - "Opt Out" Deadline of March 15, 1996)
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.kinsella.com/masonite.html">NAEF v. MASONITE CORP.</A>
    Nationwide class action on behalf of property owners where exterior Masonite
    Hardboard Siding, manufactured since 1980, has been installed. Certified
    as a class action by the Circuit Court of Mobile County, Alabama, with notice
    to potential class members dated March 7, 1996. Seeks damages and replacement
    of allegedly defective siding. (Notice of Class Action, Order Certifying
    Plaintiff Class)
    <P>
    <A NAME="INTEL"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      INTEL
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://pentium.intel.com/procs/support/pentium/fdiv/index.htm">In
    re INTEL PENTIUM(TM) PROCESSOR LITIGATION</A>. Class action on behalf of
    users of computers containing the Intel Pentium(R) processor, filed in Superior
    Court of California for the County of Santa Clara. Settlement in 1995 provides
    for replacement of processors. <A NAME="LENDCO"></A>
  <DT>
    <H3>
      LENDCO ACCEPTANCE CORPORATION
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.sddt.com/files/notices/misc/pn95080750102.html">In re
    CHRISTINE M. SPRENG v. LENDCO ACCEPTANCE CORPORATION LIQUIDATING TRUST, et
    al.</A> Class action seeking monetary damages for alleged inadequate disclosure
    of lease terms and imposition of unreasonable early termination penalties
    in connection with consumer automobile leases. (Notice of Settlement Hearing
    on September 18, 1995) <A NAME="MEDAPHIS"></A>
  <DT>

      MEDAPHIS CORP
    </H3>
  <DD>
    <A HREF="http://www.net-sales.com/carrtabbandpope/medaphis.htm">MEDAPHIS

    District of Georgia (Case No. 1 :96-CV-2088) on behalf of persons who purchased
