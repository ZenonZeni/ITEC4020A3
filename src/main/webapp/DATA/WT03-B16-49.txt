<h2>Background</H2>

<A HREF="pic/crs_ant.jpg"><i>CRISP's remote sensing satellite ground station</i></a> 
is one of the first multi-mission
ground stations built around the open system concept, with high level
of flexibility to accommodate future satellite missions.
It is able to acquire images of the 
earth's surface within a radius of about 3000km; covering the following countries:

<dl><dl>
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> Brunei
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> <A HREF="http://mawar.inn.bppt.go.id/">Indonesia</a>
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> Kampuchea
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> Laos
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> <A HREF="http://mimos.my/">Malaysia</a>
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> Myanmar
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> <A HREF="http://www.mozcom.com/SCF/SCF.html">Philippines</a>
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> <A HREF="http://www.ncb.gov.sg/sog/sog.html">Singapore</a>
<dt><img src="pic/redball.gif"> <A HREF="http://suif.stanford.edu/~lanka/sri_lanka.html">Sri Lanka</A><BR>

In the first year of operation (1996), <i>CRISP</i> will receive and process
data from <b>SPOT</b>, <b>ERS</b> and <b>Radarsat</b> satellites.<p>

<h2>Antenna System</h2>

<i>CRISP's ground station</i> is equipped with a <A HREF="pic/crs_ant.jpg">
13-meter solid reflector, 3 axis, X band antenna<i>(32k)</i></A>
 for reception of satellite signal. 
The 13-meter antenna is one of the largest of its kind in Asia. 
Compared to smaller antenna system, the 13-meter antenna has the
following advantages:
<dl>

    <dt><h3><img src="pic/redball.gif">  Superior G/T</h3> 
    <dl> 
    <dt>The 35 db/K obtainable from this antenna provides
        a wide performance margin above the requirements of all current
        satellite systems.<p>
     </dl>

    <dt><h3><img src="pic/redball.gif">  Superior Quality of Received Signal</h3> 
    <dl>
     <dt>The higher G/T will result
        in better quality of received signal, e.g. lower noise level and
        reduced occurence of drop lines.<p>
     </dl>
</dl>
The 7 degree tilt third axis of the antenna allows overhead tracking of
satellites at an altitude as low as 400 km, without overdriving the
antenna servo system.<p>

The whole antenna system is controlled by a sophisticated Ground
Station Computer, which enable automatic tracking of
satellites.<p>


<h2>Data Processing System</h2>

The data processing system are based on standard Silicon Graphics
Computers.  The host is <i>a 4 CPU Silicon Graphics Challenge</i>,
capable of delivering up to 96 Mega-flops processing power.  <i>Four
Silicon Graphics Indigo2</i> workstations augment the Challenge host to provide all
the functions of the data processing system.<p>

Except for frame synchronisation, all function essential for processing
of the satellite signal are implement with software.  This gives
the system a high degree of reliability, flexibility and
maintainability.<p>
