<H2>PM announces Australia's new Science Ministry</H2>
<H3><HR></H3>
<BR>
Prime Minister John Howard announced his new front bench on Friday 8 March,
reducing in size both the Cabinet and Ministry. <BR>
The new Science Ministers are: 
<BLOCKQUOTE>The Hon John Moore (Lib), Minister for Industry, Science and
Tourism, and Vice President of the Executive Council.<BR>
<BR>
Mr Peter McGauran (Nat), Minister for Science and Technology, also Deputy
Leader of the House.<BR>
<BR>
Mrs Bronwyn Bishop (Lib), Minister for Defence Industry, Science and Personnel.</BLOCKQUOTE>
<B>Mr Moore</B> has held the Queensland seat of Ryan since 1975. President
of the Queensland Liberal Party from 1973&shyp;1976 and again from 1984&shyp;1990,
Mr Moore served as Federal Minister for Business and Consumer Affairs from
1980&shyp;82. In opposition, he worked on several parliamentary committees,
with numerous shadow appointments: Finance; Communications; Local Government
and Northern Development; Transport and Aviation; Business and Consumer
Affairs; and, to 1990, Business, Privatisation and Consumer Affairs. <BR>
<BR>
<B>Mr McGauran</B> has been the member for Gippsland since 1983. A former
barrister and solicitor, Mr McGauran has served on parliamentary committees
including: Transport Safety; Legal and Constitutional Affairs; Public Accounts;
National Crime Authority; ASIO; and Telecommunications Interception. <BR>
<BR>
<B>Mrs Bishop</B>, also a former solicitor, was first elected to the Senate
in 1987. She held the Shadow Ministry for Public Administration, Federal
Affairs &amp; Local Government from 1989&shyp;1990 and, following her election
to the House of Representatives in 1994, held various Shadow Ministries.
She has served on several parliamentary finance and other committees.<BR>
Minister Moore and the Minister for Small Business and Consumer Affairs,
Geoff Prosser, met representatives of industry bodies on 11 April to discuss
the impact of proposed modifications to the Tariff Concession System. <BR>
