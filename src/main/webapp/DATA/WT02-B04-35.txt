<BIG><STRONG>UPDATE</STRONG></BIG> <FONT SIZE=-1>[September 26, 1996]</FONT>
<P>
On <B>Wednesday September 25</B>, the House Judiciary Committee held a hearing on H.R. 3011.  In addition to being the first hearing on the bill, it was the first time this session that a House committee considered changes to current encryption policy. 
<P>
Witnesses at the hearing included:
<UL>
<LI>Congressman Robert Goodlatte (R-VA), a sponsor of H.R. 3011
<P>
<LI>Representatives from the Administration, including:
     <UL>
     <LI>Deputy Attorney General Jamie Gorelick, representing the Department of Justice
     <LI>William Crowell, Deputy Director of the National Security Agency (NSA)
     </UL>
<P>
<LI>Industry Representatives, including:
     <UL>
     <LI>Roberta Katz, VP and General Counsel, Netscape Communications Corp.
     <LI>Melinda Brown, VP and General Counsel, Lotus Development Corp.
     <LI>Patricia Ripley, Managing Director, Bear Stearns and Co. (a Wall Street investment firm)
     <LI>Dr. Charles Deneka, Senior VP and CTO of Corning, Inc. (on behalf of the National Association of Manufacturers)
     </UL>
</UL>
While Administration witnesses reaffirmed their commitment to key escrow and export controls, Rep. Goodlatte and industry witnesses questioned the Administration's policy.

<BIG><STRONG>Background on H.R. 3011/S.1587</STRONG></BIG>
<P>
S. 1587 [sponsored by Sen. Pat Leahy (D-VT) and Sen. Conrad Burns (R-MT)] and H.R. 3011 [sponsored by Rep. Robert Goodlatte (R-VA), Rep. Anna Eshoo (D-CA), and others] were the first bills introduced in the 104th Congress to roll back restrictive export controls on encryption products.
<P>
S.1587 preceded <A HREF="/crypto/pro_CODE.html">S. 1726, the &quot;Pro-CODE&quot; bill</A>, also sponsored by Sens. Burns and Leahy.
