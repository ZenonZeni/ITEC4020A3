Interest rates are really low, we are paying off our national debts, the stock market is booming and the unemployment rate is 8 to 11 percent!<p>For the past month Canadian market sectors dominated my International Index List. In the 12 sectors that make up the top quartile of the list, only the Paris Bourse and the Dow Jones Industrial Average are present among the Canuck stuff. Top indices were Real Estate (!!), TSE Financials (mostly the banks), the blue chip TSE 35 and the Oil and Gas index. Hardly anything Canadian is in the bottom half of the table. The group that is getting the most attention these days is the banking sector. Roaring ever higher as rates fall and profitability rises, the bank bashers are emerging in force. Wow, a billion dollar profit?! I wonder what that means as Return On Equity??? In percentage terms, it's probably nowhere near as much as we'd all like from an investment.<p>While I remain bullish on Canada, There is a definite sense of optimism among investors and that should be a caution to you all. This happy little game will not go on forever. Go read<i> "Contrarion Investment Strategy"</i>  by David Dreman. Published about 20 years ago, and generally available in the public library system, Dreman's book shows the wisdom of not following the crowd. He has a perfect little chart that shows that <i>extreme</i> levels of optimism occour at market tops. Those figures are gleaned from weekly sentiment surveys of market pro's opinions.
