<P><B>Welcome to the web pages of the KFB Electric and Hybrid vehicle program</B>. Through these pages, we present a general overview of our assignment, our current activities and monthly updated (minimum) report on the status of the different project fleets. </P>



<!-- Link Tag --><A HREF="../index.html">KFB</A> is responsible for a national program (SEK 120 Million, USD 17 Million total ) for <B>research, development and demonstration</B> of electric and hybrid vehicles. The program will continue until the end of 1999. The main goals are to assess the possibility and environmental potential for a large scale introduction of electric and hybrid vehicles, and to take the first steps towards emission-free motoring. Focusing on demonstration, between 200 and 400 vehicles, including passenger cars, buses and light and heavy trucks run or will run in city project fleets within the program. Others are included for evaluation through linked projects. All demonstration activities will be evaluated regarding vehicle proficiency, user acceptance, and economy, including a systems analysis and a concluding systems study to assess decisive areas.

<P><B>KFB, the Swedish Transport and Communications Research Board,</B> is a government authority with planning, initiating,coordinating and supporting functions in Swedish transport and communications research. KFB`s activities encompass transportation, traffic, postal services and telecommunications,as well as the impact of tranports and communications on the environment, traffic safety and regional development. KFB is also responsible for information and documentation wthin its areas of responsibility.</P>
