<title> Operations Research</title>
<h1> Operations Research</h1>
<img align=middle src="/icons/line.blue.gif" alt="">
<p>
This research area, due to its significant mathematical nature, is primarily supported by the departmental extensive 
computing facilities. An additional targeted-research facility in this area is the Healthcare Productivity Laboratory (Prof. 
M.W. Carter).<p>

<a href="../../staff/profiles/carter.html"> <b> Professor M.W. Carter </b></a>
<br>
Course and examination timetabling. Healthcare 
applications. Production scheduling. Lagrangian 
relaxation, integer programming, and steel scheduling.<p>

<a href="../../staff/profiles/posner.html"> <b> Professor M.J.M. Posner </b></a>
<br>
Stochastic models in operational research. Analysis of 
complex queuing models. Queuing, production, and 
communication networks. Modular repair systems.<p>

<a href="../../staff/profiles/rogers.html"> <b> Professor J.S. Rogers </b></a>
<br>
 Strategic planning models: hierarchical optimization. 
Applied linear and nonlinear programming; equilibrium 
algorithms. Economic	design of energy and tele-
communications.<p>

<a href="../../staff/profiles/sris.html"> <b> Professor C. Sriskandarajah </b></a>
<br>
Machine scheduling theory, computational complexity. 
design and analysis of algorithms for combinatorial 
problems.<p>
