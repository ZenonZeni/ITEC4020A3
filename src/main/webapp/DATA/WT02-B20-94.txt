<center><h2>HOW TO JOIN A LISTSERV</h2></center><P>

E-mail lists, often known as listservs, are interactive computer-based communications. 
E-mail lists can be manually administered by humans, or they can be automated by
servers such as listserv, or majordomo.<P>

<B><I>Subscribing to a Manually Administered List</B></I><P>

To subscribe to (join) a manually administered list, send a regular e-mail message to
the administrator or owner of the list.<P>

<B><I>Subscribing to an Automated List</B></I><P>

Automated lists have at least two accounts--one for the server (the list ADDRESS) and
one for the actual list (the list NAME).  To subscribe to (join) an automated list, send an
e-mail message to the list ADDRESS, not the list NAME, for the server to recognize the
command.  Examples are given for two of the more common types of automated
lists listserv and majordomo. <P>

<b>For listserv lists:</B><BR>
<ul><LI>Leave the Subject: line blank
<LI> In the body of the message, type only the following line:<BR>

          <i>subscribe {listname} {yourfirstname} {yourlastname}</I><br>
     ex:  subscribe l-hcap Happy Golucky</ul><P>
       
<b>For majordomo lists:</B>  Majordomo lists do not require {yourfirstname} and
{yourlastname} in the message.<br>
<ul><LI>Leave the Subject: line blank
<LI> In the body of the message, type only the following line:<BR>

      <i>subscribe {listname}</I><BR>
      subscribe spedtalk</UL><P>
       
<B><I>Posting Messages to a List</B></I><P>

To post a message to the list, send it to the NAME of the list in the TO: field.<P>

<table width=475>
<tr><td width=200 valign=top><b>Example:</b></td><td width=300><b> TO:  spedtalk@virginia.edu</b></td></tr></table><P>

<b><i>To Find a List of Lists</b></i><P>
A quick way to find a list of (the thousands of) lists is as follows:<P>

<table width=475>
<tr><td width=200 valign=top>Send message to:</td><td width=300>listserv@listserv.net</td></tr>
<tr><td width=200 valign=top>With command in body: </td><td width=300>lists global</td></tr>
<tr><td width=200 valign=top> Or: </td> <td width=300>         lists global/[keyword]</td></tr></table>
where [keyword] is the topic of your choice (e.g., disability).<P>
