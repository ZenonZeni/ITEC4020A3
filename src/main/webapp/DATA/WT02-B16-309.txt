<H3>Electrochemical Processes</H3>

<P>

Fuel cells are electrochemical devices in which hydrogen and oxygen 

(typically) are combined to produce a flow of electrons between 
electrodes, 

generating a current. If oxygen is used, water is produced as the only 
emission.  

Research on fuel cells is focused on improving efficiency and reducing 
costs.
