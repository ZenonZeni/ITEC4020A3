<h2 align="center"><a name="History"><font color="#0000FF">SKB</font></a><font
color="#0000FF"> Environmental - Compliance and Beyond </font></h2>
<p><font color="#0000FF"><i>&quot;Pioneer&quot;- &quot;Cutting
edge&quot; - &quot;Forefront,&quot;</i> these are our hallmarks
as a company of <i>&quot;firsts.&quot;</i></font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF">A wholly owned subsidiary of Carl
Bolander &amp; Sons Company, Minnesota's premier specialists in
site preparation and demolition, <b>SKB Environmental</b> was
formed in 1983, as one of the first privately held demolition
waste disposal companies to serve the construction industry in
Minnesota. In those early years, the company functioned as a
complement to Bolander's demolition business, arranging
cost-effective disposal services for construction and demolition
(C&amp;D) debris. </font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF">In 1989, we opened Minnesota's first
state-of-the-art landfill for disposal of C&amp;D waste. Typical
of the company's commitment to responsible practices, the Rich
Valley Landfill is the first clay lined C&amp;D landfill with
leachate collection in Minnesota. In fact, it is one of a handful
of C&amp;D facilities in the nation that combine such rigorous
design features with stringent operating practices and
groundwater monitoring &#151; becoming a model facility for the
C&amp;D industry. Environmental data from the Rich Valley
facility was relied upon heavily in EPA's recent development of
RCRA standards for disposal of C&amp;D materials. Information
about potential impacts from C&amp;D facilities is available in a
white paper and background studies prepared for the </font><a
href="related.htm#NADC"><font color="#0000FF">National
Association of Demolition Contractors</font></a><font
color="#0000FF">.</font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF">Our commitment to environmental
protection is also reflected in <b>SKB Environmental</b>'s focus
on recycling economically recoverable concrete,brick, block,
ferrous and non-ferrous metals, corrugated cardboard and wood
waste in its operations. The first large-scale state-of-the-art
C&amp;D processing facility/transfer station in Minnesota's Twin
Cities Area is being developed at our facility in central
Minneapolis.</font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF">Expanding our services to include yard
waste management &#151; composting of leaves and grass and
production of landscape mulch from brush and wood wastes, we have
become the largest yard waste processor in Minnesota, currently
operating four facilities, with total processed quantities
exceeding 60,000 tons in 1995.</font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF">At the company's Empire Township site,
another Minnesota <i>&quot;first&quot;</i> has opened &#151;
Minnesota's first composting facility for source separated
compostable materials. The facility will blend wastes like food
processing residues with yard waste and compost the materials
using innovative containment technology.</font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF"><b>SKB Environmental</b> has an
excellent reputation: </font></p>
<ul>
    <li><font color="#0000FF">for doing the correct thing
        environmentally; </font></li>
    <li><font color="#0000FF">for customer satisfaction, service,
        and value; </font></li>
    <li><font color="#0000FF">for the rapport and high regard the
        company has earned with regulatory and permitting
        agencies; </font></li>
    <li><font color="#0000FF">and for its performance record of
        never receiving a citation or notice of violation for any
        of its operations.</font></li>
</ul>
