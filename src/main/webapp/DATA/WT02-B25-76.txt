<h3 align="center">Helpline Fact Sheet </h3>

<h4 align="center"><font color="#000080"><b>Borderline
Personality Disorder [BPD] </b></font></h4>

<table border="0" cellpadding="15" cellspacing="15" width="100%">
    <tr>
        <td valign="top" width="50%"><font color="#FF0000"><b>Symptoms
        of BPD</b></font><p>1 - mood swings</p>
        <p>2 - periods of intense depression, irritability,
        &amp;/or anxiety lasting from a few hours to a few days</p>
        <p>3 - inappropriate, intense, or uncontrolled anger</p>
        <p>4 - impulsiveness in spending, sex, substance use,
        shoplifting, reckless driving, or binge eating</p>
        <p>5 - recurring suicidal threats or self-injurious
        behavior</p>
        <p>6 - unstable, intense personal relationships</p>
        <p>7 - extreme black &amp; white views of people &amp;
        experiences, sometimes alternating between &quot;all
        good&quot; idealization &amp; &quot;all bad&quot; devaluation</p>
        <p>8 - persistent uncertainty about self-image, long term
        goals, friendships, &amp; values</p>
        <p>9 - chronic boredom or feelings of emptiness</p>
        <p>10 - frantic efforts to avoid real or imagined
        abandonment</p>
        <p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Treatment for BPD</b></font></p>
        <p>1 - medication</p>
        <blockquote>
            <p>a. reduces anxiety, depression, &amp; impulsivity </p>
            <p>b. helps one deal with harmful patterns of
            thinking &amp; interacting</p>
            <p>c. fails to correct ingrained character
            difficulties</p>
            <p>d. antidepressants</p>
            <p>e. anticonvulsants</p>
            <p>f. neuroleptics</p>
        </blockquote>
        <p>2 - psychotherapy</p>
        <p>3 - short-term hospitalization when necessary during
        times of extreme stress, impulsivity, or substance abuse</p>
        <p>4 - generally difficult &amp; long term</p>
        <p>5 - BPD symptoms often interfere with therapy</p>
        <p>6- usually effective</p>
        <p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>What is BPD?</strong></font></p>
        <p>1 - fairly common biologically based disorder</p>
        <p>2 - characterized by impulsivity &amp; instability in
        mood, self-image, &amp; personal relationships</p>
        <p>3 - diagnosed more often in females than in males</p>
        <p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Causes of BPD</strong></font></p>
        <p>1 - unclear, but psychological &amp; biological factors
        may be involved</p>
        <p>2 - originally thought to border on schizophrenia</p>
        <p>3 - related to serious depressive illness</p>
        <p>4 - associated with neurological &amp; attention deficit
        disorders</p>
        <p>5 - difficulties in psychological development during
        childhood due to such things as abuse or neglect may
        create identity &amp; personality problems</p>
        <p>6 - more research is needed</p>
        <p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Simultaneously present
        disorders</strong></font></p>
        <p>1 - serious depressive illness, including bipolar
        disorder</p>
        <p>2 - eating disorders</p>
        <p>3 - alcohol/drug abuse</p>
        <p>4 - sleep disorder</p>
