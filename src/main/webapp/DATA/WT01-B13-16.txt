<BLOCKQUOTE>

<IMG SRC="gifs/miss_prospectus_head.gif" ALT="What You Might Miss If You
Don't Read The Prospectus">
<BR>

Every mutual fund shareholder has been provided with a prospectus at one
time or another. Ah, but have you read the prospectus?
<P>

Mutual fund prospectuses may look drearily alike but each fund company
uses the prospectus to describe its own investment policies and
practices. Most practices are reasonable and easy to understand - even
so, you'll be better off knowing them sooner rather than later. 
<P>

Here's a short list of what you might miss if you don't read the
prospectus:
<P>

<UL>
<LI> Some fund companies assess a fee every time you reinvest your
dividends.
<LI> Some fund companies assess a fee if you redeem your shares before a
prescribed time period.
<LI> Some fund companies impose a 30-day or longer waiting period before
they'll exchange your shares from one fund into another.
<LI> Some funds insist on the right to pay your redemption proceeds in
"marketable securities" instead of cash in the case of large
redemptions. 
<P>
</UL>
