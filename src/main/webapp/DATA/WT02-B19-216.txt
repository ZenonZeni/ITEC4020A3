CEC invites all educators, administrators and support staff involved in teaching students with
disabilities to our institute dedicated to the ideas, philosophy, and practice of including students with
exceptionalities in the general content area with highest possible expectations. Nine unique, but
related <A HREF="#sessions">sessions</A> will address key components of an inclusive education program: student learning
needs (curriculum), teaching skills(instruction), and cooperation among educators (collaboration). <P>

Attending these sessions will give participants a comprehensive view of the critical features of a
quality inclusive program with specific learner outcomes such as:

     <UL>
<LI>Identify elements of successful collaborative teaching experiences and plan activities to  improve existing collaborative efforts.
<LI>     Learn the concept and application of multi-level teaching.
<LI>     Examine the application of performance based assessments.
<LI>     Integrate instruction with classroom and behavior management activities.
<LI>     Practice conflict resolution and mediation strategies concerning special education.
<LI>     Develop a structure to design curriculum that is accessible to students of differing    achievement levels.
<LI>      Learn to effectively increase reading abilities with problem readers.
</UL>

<H3><A NAME="1">Increasing Reading Power</A></H3>
<STRONG>Presenter: Kathy Jungjohann, University of Oregon, Eugene, Oregon</STRONG><P>

<STRONG>Important skills.</STRONG> Increase your student  competence in reading and comprehension in this  highly
interactive workshop.   Learn to select appropriate reading material and develop strategies for  
increasing student rate, accuracy, and comprehension while developing systems to monitor and
document  progress in this critical content area.<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>
<H3> <A NAME="2">Using Big Ideas To Include All Kids</A></H3>
<STRONG>Presenter: Kathy Jungjohann, University of Oregon, Eugene, Oregon</STRONG><P>
<STRONG>Inclusive Strategies.</STRONG> Use collaborative teaching relationships to identify the "big ideas" that will
support low achieving students in content areas.  Focus on concepts  and principals rather  than
restrictive, isolated facts.  This session will provide a framework and resources to  identify important
concepts that will provide the curricular supports to teach all students.
<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>

                <H3> <A NAME="3">Assessing Kids Using Big Ideas</A></H3>
<STRONG>Presenter: Kathy Jungjohann, University of Oregon, Eugene, Oregon</STRONG><P>
<STRONG>Effective Instruction.</STRONG> A follow-up to "Using Big Ideas to Include All Kids", this session will
provide educators  the tools to assess student mastery of key concepts in content curricula through
performance  assessments.  Learn the framework for developing assessment tasks that requires
higher order thinking skills and utilizing scoring guides to assess mastery of big ideas  in content
curricula.<P>

 
<EM><STRONG>Who Should Attend:</STRONG> Elementary School Teachers, Reading Specialists, Program Leaders</EM>

<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>




       <H3> <A NAME="4">Making the Curriculum Accessible to All Learners</A>
</H3>
<STRONG>Presenter: Barbara Hoskins, Consultant, Pasadena, California</STRONG><P>
                                

                                
<STRONG>Improved curriculum.</STRONG> Meet the needs of students will varying abilities through solid assessment
and a flexible curriculum that focus on teacher collaboration.  Learn how effective curriculum design
enables students with physical, social, and cognitive disabilities access to general content.  Uses of
appropriate modifications and adaptations will be presented.

<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>
           <H3> <A NAME="5">Designing Your Role in Inclusive Schools</A></H3>
  <STRONG>Presenter: Barbara Hoskins, Consultant, Pasadena, California</STRONG><P>                              
<STRONG>Evolutionary.</STRONG> Keep current with the changing special educator role.   Clarify  the collaborative and
consultative relationships special educators face in the inclusive school.  Learn the influences   that
are shaping special education to be  more inclusive and with higher expectations.  Participants will
develop a framework for planning effective strategies in the inclusive school and learn how effective
curriculum design is a critical feature in this process. 
                                
<P>
<EM>
<STRONG>Who Should Attend:</STRONG> All Educators, School Administrators, and Program Leaders</EM>



 <P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>


  <H3><A NAME="6">Collaboration: A Priority In A Successful, Inclusive School</A></H3>
                                
<STRONG>Presenter: Jane Oates, Temple University, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</STRONG><P>
<STRONG>Teamwork.</STRONG> Develop strategies for an inclusive classroom that help  educators to "work on the same
page". Learn to recognize critical features of the collaborative relationships in a  successful program.
Address the traditional barriers that prevent  teachers from working as a  productive team and create
the truly inclusive environment for all students.

<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>

          <H3>W<A NAME="7">orking to Promote Success for All Students</A></H3>
<STRONG>Presenter: Jane Oates, Temple University, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania</STRONG><P>
<STRONG>Teach to Abilities.</STRONG>  Explore the concept of multi-level teaching.  Plan effective instruction for  
students at differing ability levels by making the curriculum accessible to their needs. Use of 
positive classroom structures, thorough assessment, and curriculum adjustment that focus the 
individual needs of the students will be presented.  <P>
<EM><STRONG>
Who Should Attend:</STRONG> All educators and School Administrators</EM>
<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>


         <H3> <A NAME="8"> A Positive Approach to Behavior Management</A></H3>
<STRONG>Presenter: Craig Darch, Auburn University, Auburn, Alabama</STRONG><P>

<STRONG>Create a productive classroom.</STRONG> Learn strategies for teaching and managing social behavior in the 
 classroom that will minimize disruption in the inclusive classroom.  Integrate instruction with 20
classroom management activities through lessons that emphasize the learning of positive and 
appropriate behaviors rather than isolated, disconnected punishment procedures.

<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>
    <A NAME="9"><H3>Application of Specific Classroom Management Strategies</H3></A>
<STRONG>Presenter: Craig Darch, Auburn University, Auburn, Alabama</STRONG><P>
<STRONG>Make a plan.</STRONG> Learn specific applications of Instructional Classroom Management that will provide
a temporal framework in the form of a 180-day Management plan.  Strategies for linking
reinforcement to instruction will be presented as will a model for using  punishment as a  transitional
tool.<P>
<EM>

<STRONG>Who Should Attend:</STRONG> All Educators/School Administrators, Behavioral Specialists, School
Psychologists
</EM>
<P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>



<H3>        <A NAME="10">       Public Policy and Unified Schools</A></H3>
<STRONG> Presenter: TBA</STRONG><P>

<STRONG>Leadership. </STRONG> Explore how the proposed revisions to IDEA may affect inclusionary practices in the
unified school system.  Learn how program administrators can prepare teachers, parents, and
students for these potential changes and discuss whether the local, state, or federal governments will
take the lead in directing the inclusion of students with exceptionalities in the general education
classroom.<P>
<EM><STRONG>Who Should Attend:</STRONG> Special and General Education Administrators, Program Leaders</EM>

                 <P>

<A HREF="#top">Top of Page
</A><hr>    
                                
        <H3><A NAME="11">Administrators and Inclusion-Help or Hindrance?</A></H3>
<STRONG> Presenter: TBA</STRONG><P>
<STRONG>Direction.</STRONG> What beliefs, principles, and vision must a district adopt to develop effective inclusionary
practices.  What role does the site-based administrator play?  Can special education administrators
and site-based administrator collaborate productively?  Discover the answers to these questions and
more in this enlightening session.  Learn the policies boards of education must adopt to promote a
unified system of education.<P>
<EM><STRONG>Who Should Attend:</STRONG> Special and General Education Administrators, Program Leaders</EM>
