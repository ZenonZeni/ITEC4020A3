<h2>How It All Works</h2>
<p><strong>Making n and p type Material</strong>
<p>The most common way of making p-type or n-type silicon material is to add an element that has
an extra electron or is lacking an electron.  In silicon, we use a process called "doping."  

<p>We'll use silicon as an example because crystalline silicon was the semiconductor material used
in the earliest successful PV devices, it's still the most widely used PV material, and, although
other PV materials and designs exploit the PV effect in slightly different ways, knowing how the
effect works in crystalline silicon gives us a basic understanding of how it works in all devices.
