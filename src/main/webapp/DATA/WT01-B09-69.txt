<P>
<B> Project Summary:</B>
<P>
                     Integration of five different advanced battery
technologies into the US Electricar S10 pickup trucks powered
by the GM Delco Dolphin 50 &#153; drive system. Five technologies
include GNB Lead Acid, Optima Spiral Wound Lead Acid, Electrosource
Horizon Lead Acid, SAFT NiCad, and Powercell Zinc Bromine. Vehicles
will have rapid charging capability with 100 kW Electronic Power
Technology, Inc. Charger.
<P>
Structural analysis and 30 mph frontal impact crash analysis to
be performed for each battery type. Working DYNA3D computer model
to be supplied to the University of Hawaii at Manoa. (UH)
<P>
                     Installation of full vehicle dynamometer
and battery testing equipment in Hawaii. Battery testing and data
collection from onboard Detection Limit Technology acquisition
systems to be reported to Electric &amp; Hybrid Vehicle National
Data Center at UH. On road testing for 12 months.
<P>
<B>Project Deliverables:</B>
<P><UL>

<LI> Four S10 pickup trucks equipped with GNB batteries.
<P>
<LI> Four S10 pickup trucks equipped with Optima batteries.
<P>
<LI> Three S10 pickup trucks equipped with Electrosource batteries.
<P>
<LI> Three S10 pickup trucks equipped with SAFT batteries.
<P>
<LI> Three S10 pickup trucks equipped with Powercell batteries.
. Dynamometer
<P>
<LI> Battery Test Equipment
<P>
<LI> Structural and crash analysis reports
<P>
<LI> Working DYNA3D model simulating 30 mph frontal crash
<P>

</UL>
<B> Project Schedule:</B>
<P>
            Project start date 10/1/95
<P>
            Project duration 18 months
