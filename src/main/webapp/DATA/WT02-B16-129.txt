<p><strong>Why <a href="http://www.ott.doe.gov">Transportation</a> Energy Matters</strong>
<ul>
<lh></lh>
<li>Each day, Americans use 4 million more barrels of oil for transportation than we 
produce.  We are importing more oil than ever before, and the trend is upward.  The gap 
between what we use and what we produce is projected to rise to 8 million barrels per day 
by the year 2010.

<li>Oil imports contribute significantly to the nation's foreign trade deficit, and make 
our economy vulnerable to price swings and supply disruptions.

<li>Emissions from the nation's nearly 200 million cars and trucks account for about half 
of all air pollution in the U.S. and more than 80 percent of air pollution in cities.  The 
American Lung Association estimates that America spends more than $60 billion each year on 
health care as a direct result of air pollution.

<li>America's cars are more efficient than ever, but our rate of fuel use continues to 
grow.  Since 1980, the <a href="/feguide">miles-per-gallon rating of passenger cars</a>
has improved 39 percent, yet fuel consumption is up 19 percent.  A key reason:  we are driving 
50 percent more miles.

<li>Only 13 percent of the energy used by today's vehicles actually propels them forward; 
much of the rest could be saved with better equipment technology.

<li>The development and use of <a href="http://afdc3.nrel.gov">alternative fuels</a> would create new U.S. industries, while 
reducing oil imports, the trade deficit and air pollution.
</ul>

<p><strong><a href="http://www.ott.doe.gov/program/program.html">DOE's Programs</a> . . . </strong>
<ul>
<lh></lh>
<li>Partner with industries to produce 
<a href="http://www.ott.doe.gov/program/program.html#hev">more efficient vehicle technologies</a>.

<li>Work with industry, government, and fleet managers to encourage the use of cleaner, 
<a href="http://afdc3.nrel.gov">alternative fuels</a> and 
<a href="http://www.ott.doe.gov/program/oaat/afv.html">alternative fuel vehicles</a>.

<li>Will help government and automakers produce a car three times as efficient as today's 
models, without sacrificing performance or safety.

<li>Will produce annual energy savings of $47 million by 2000 and $676 million by 2010.

<li>Will create nearly 830,000 jobs by 2010.
</ul>
