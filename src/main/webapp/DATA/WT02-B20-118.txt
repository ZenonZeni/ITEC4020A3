<H3>The 1997 CEC Business, Agency, and Community Award</H3>


<STRONG>Deadline:</STRONG> Nominations must be postmarked by November 15, 1996.<P>

<STRONG>Background:</STRONG>This award was established in 1992 by the Board of Governors to recognize a business, agency, or community that has promoted and provided for the enhancement, awareness, and employment of individuals with exceptionalities* in order to promote and support their full participation within the community.<P>

<STRONG>Candidate(s):</STRONG> Nominees may be private or public businesses or agencies.
<P>


<STRONG>Nominator:</STRONG> Nominations can be accepted from individuals or CEC units.<P>

*Individuals with exceptionalities includes individuals with disabilities as well as individuals who are gifted and talented.<P>

Print out nomination form below, or request forms and information sheets from CEC Headquarters: Office of the Executive Director, 1920 Association Drive, Reston, VA  20191-1589.

<H4>Information Needed to Complete Nomination</H4>

1.	All information requested on the nomination form.<P>

2.	Description of the business or agency nominated, including its purpose and history.<P>

3.	Description of the efforts of the business or agency to meet at least three (3) of the following criteria:<P>
<DL><DT>
	<DD>(a)	Demonstrated a particular accommodation for a person with a disability, including innovative individualized accommodations or more general building-wide accommodations.

	<DD>(b)	Demonstrated an active hiring campaign to encourage the employment of individuals with disabilities.

	<DD>(c)	Demonstrated physical modification of the environment to accommodate individuals with disabilities.

	<DD>(d)	Maintained an ongoing, long-term hiring/accommodation practice.

	<DD>(e)	Demonstrated a formal relationship with schools for the sponsorship of work-study sites, community-based training experiences, and transitioning opportunities for individuals with exceptionalities.

	<DD>(f)	Sponsored a mentoring program for students with exceptionalities.

	<DD>(g)	Supported a career development program for students with exceptionalities.

	<DD>(h)	Provided a scholarship program for individual students with exceptionalities.

	<DD>(i)	Offered onsite specific skill training programs for students with exceptionalities.

	<DD>(j)	Sponsored public awareness activities showing the contributions to society of individuals with exceptionalities.

	<DD>(k)	Demonstrated an active public relations campaign which enhances awareness about the needs of individuals with exceptionalities.
</DT></DL>
4.	Three to five letters of recommendation documenting the nominee's efforts in meeting the criteria for the award.<P>
