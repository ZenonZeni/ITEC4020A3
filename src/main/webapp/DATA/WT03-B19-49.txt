<H2>TeraScan &reg; Earth Remote Sensing System</H2>

<P>
The SeaSpace TeraScan &reg; system offers real-time data reception and in-depth analysis for the broad spectrum of polar orbiting and geostationary weather satellites.  Satellites covered include 
the NOAA <A HREF="glossary.html#TIROS">TIROS-N</A>, <A HREF="glossary.html#DMSP">DMSP</A>, <A HREF="glossary.html#GOES">GOES</A>, <A HREF="glossary.html#METEOSAT">
METEOSAT</A>, <A HREF="glossary.html#GMS">GMS</A>, FY and <A HREF="glossary.html#SeaWiFS">SeaStar (SeaWiFS</A>).  TeraScan &reg; systems are used worldwide in both land-based and shipboard applications.  In 
addition, the portability of the system has enabled it to be quickly transported and utilized in a wide variety of field applications.
</P>
<P>
TeraScan &reg; systems are located in universities, laboratories, and other research insititutions in the U.S., Europe, the Far East, and even the Antarctic.  
</P>
<BR>
<BR>
<H2>Applications of TeraScan</H2>
<UL> <STRONG>Atmosphere</STRONG>
<LI> cloud cover type, extent, and temperature
<LI> weather fronts, tropical storm monitoring
<LI> agricultural frost warnings
<LI> temperature and moisture soundings
<LI> access to ARGOS Automatic Weather Station data
<LI> weather predictions
</UL>
<UL> <STRONG>Ocean</STRONG>
<LI> sea surface temperatures
<LI> surface current patters
<LI> ARGOS drift buoy tracking
<LI> fisheries, ocean survey and research applications
<LI> ice cover
<LI> search and rescue
</UL>
<UL> <STRONG>Land</STRONG>
<LI> vegetation/desertification
<LI> snow cover
<LI> hydrology
<LI> day/night temperatures
<LI> forest fire detection and monitoring
<LI> ARGOS animal tracking
</UL>

<H2>Product Description</H2>

<HR>

<H3>TeraScan Antenna Server for <A HREF="glossary.html#polar_orbit">Polar Orbiting Satellites</A></H3>

<P>
The TeraScan &reg; HRPT Antenna Server acquires, archives, processes, and displays telemetry data from the TIROS-N polar orbiting weather satellite sensors, including:
</P>

<P>
This system features a remarkably small (1.0 meter) tracking reflector antenna, coupled with the most powerful and cost-effective desktop workstation computer available today.  
The result is a compact unit, appropriate for both permanent installation and temporary deployment out in the field.  <P>
</P>
<DL>
<DT><A HREF="glossary.html#HRPT">HRPT</A>
<DD>1 km resolution multispectral imagery
<DT><A HREF="glossary.html#OLS">DMSP OLS</A>
<DD>0.5 km resolution infrared and visible imagery
<DT><A HREF="glossary.html#SSMI">DMSP SSMI</A>
<DD>Microwave Images for ocean surface wind speed, ice area, ice age, ice edge, precipitation over land, cloud liquid water, water vapor, precipitation, and soil moisture
<DT><A HREF="glossary.html#SSMT1">DMSP SSMT/1</A>
<DD>Atmospheric temperature soundings
<DT><A HREF="glossary.html#SSMT2">DMSP SSMT/2</A>
<DD>Atmospheric water vapor profiles 
<DT><A HREF="glossary.html#TOVS">TOVS</A>
<DD>Vertical sounding of atmospheric temperature and moisture
<DT><A HREF="glossary.html#ARGOS">ARGOS</A>
<DD>earth location and data relay from small transmitters mounted on buoys, balloons, wildlife, etc.
</DL>

<HR>

<H3>TeraScan Antenna Server for <A HREF="glossary.html#geostationary_orbit">Geostationary Satellites</A></H3>

<P>
The TeraScan &reg; Geostationary Antenna Server features a (3.8 meter) steerable antenna, coupled with the most powerful and cost-effective desktop workstation computer available today.  
The result is a compact unit, appropriate for both permanent installation and temporary deployment out in the field.  <P>
</P>
