<H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Links to Resources for Mathematics Teachers</H2>
<FONT SIZE=-1>Developed by <A HREF="../Students#CS">Corinne Schaeffer</A></FONT><BR>
<HR> 

<A HREF="http://forum.swarthmore.edu/">The Math Forum</A>.  This is a must see resource.  If what you are looking for isn't here, <A HREF="http://forum.swarthmore.edu/~steve/">Steve's Dump</A> will help you find it.<BR>
<BR>

<A HREF="http://www.maa.org/t_and_l/">MAA Online:  Teaching and Learning</A>.  A portion of the Mathematical Association of America's home page devoted to providing information on both research and practice.<BR> 
<BR>

<A HREF="http://www.stolaf.edu/stolaf/other/extend/">EXTEND:  Perspectives on Mathematics Education</A>.  This is a national Internet forum on mathematics education intended to involve new constituencies, to engage new voices, and to examine new perspectives.  The "Resources" link is very good.<BR>
<BR>

<A HREF="http://www.learner.org/k12/">The Annenberg/CPB K-12 Math and Science Project</A>.  A variety of opportunities to learn about reform in math and science education.<BR>
<BR>
	
<A HREF="http://www.paston.co.uk/users/mathsnet/intro.html">MathsNet</A>.  A growing collection of pages about mathematics education, information technology, and the Internet.  Pages on logo, spreadsheets and graphs, users of specific educational software like
WinLogo, Excel and Omnigraph, animated graphs, puzzles, articles, other good links. <BR>
<BR>

<A HREF="http://labnet.terc.edu/labnet/labnet.html">LabNet Home Page</A>.  An electronic meeting place for science and math teachers to share resources and experiences.<BR>
<BR>

<A HREF="http://www.enc.org/">Eisenhower National Clearinghouse</A>.  Here you will find curriculum resources, publications, and links to other sites.  You may want to check out the <i>Lessons and Activities</i> for mathematics.<BR>
<BR>

<A HREF="http://www.glenbrook.k12.il.us/gbsmat/glazer/network.html">Networking projects</A>.  You will find lessons, activities, data sets, and reference materials in this portion of teacher Evan Glazer's Home Page. <BR>
<BR>

<A HREF="http://www.ed.gov/NCES/timss">Pursuing Excellence:  Initial Findings from the Third International Mathematics and 	Science Study</A>. <BR>
<BR>

<A HREF="http://www.ed.gov/index.html">U.S. Department of Education</A>.  There are several links to a variety of educational resources that may be of interest.<BR>
<BR>
