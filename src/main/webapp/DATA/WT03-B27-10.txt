<p align="center"><a name="Mkt Rvw"></a><font color="#008000"><b>MARKET
REVIEW</b></font></p>

<p><font size="3">For the last couple of months, while the
large-cap stocks that make up the Dow Jones Industrial Average and
S&amp;P500 have been leading the market to all time highs, the
small-cap stocks have been severely lagging. Although the Nasdaq
composite has also gained substantially, most of this return has
come from large companies like Microsoft and Intel.</font></p>

<p><font size="3">So far in 1996, the smaller stock oriented
Russell 2000 index has grown only 11.77%. The stocks recommended
in this newsletter closely resemble those in the Russell 2000.
Since July 1, 1996, the Russell 2000 has grown only by 1.84%. But aren&#146;t
small stocks supposed to outperform their larger brethren? Over the
long-term the answer is an emphatic YES! In the shorter term
however, markets are sometimes dominated by perceptions or
changes in investor sentiment.</font></p>

<p><font size="3">The word on the Street is that institutional
money managers have been bombarded with new cash flowing into
mutual funds and they remain cautious as the bull market
continues. Looking for somewhere to put the new funds, they have
sought shelter in the safer blue-chips. The subsequent increase
in demand for these stocks has driven the large-caps higher and
left the small-caps behind.</font></p>

<p><font size="3">Despite their recent lackluster performance,
the longer-term performance of small-cap is far superior to the large-caps.
As things stand today, the small-cap stocks are now better
relative values. The cyclical extremes of Wall Street sometimes
defy rationality, but over a period of time these disparities
normally fall back in line. Sooner or later, the correlation of
returns between small-caps and large-caps will return to reasonable levels.</font></p>

<p><font size="3">For those investors who have the patience and
the stomach to weather the short-term volatile fluctuations of this
sector, small-cap growth stocks continue to offer an opportunity
to achieve superior long-term returns.</font></p>
