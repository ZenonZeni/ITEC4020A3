<B>About Telematics Trust</B>
<P>
The Telematics Course Development Fund Trust (Telematics Trust)
is a public charitable trust established with the purpose of promoting
and supporting the application of appropriate technology, particularly
new information technologies with a view to expanding the range
and forms of education delivery through telecommunications.  Furthermore,
gifts and contributions to the Trust are sought from corporations,
trusts, various institutions and from the public who are based
in Victoria, Australia.
<P>
Initially funded in 1987 by a $3 million grant by the Victorian
State Government, the Telematics Trust was established to fund
and assist the development and promotion of appropriate technologies
in the following key areas:
<UL>
<LI>Work education and training within Victoria.
<LI>Marketing, sale and delivery by telematic means of projects
for education and training, and
<LI>New information technologies in the area of &quot;distance
education&quot; complementary with existing educational and training
institutions.
</UL>

<P>

<P>
<B>Assistance From the Trust</B>
<P>
The Trust assists enterprise through the provision of grants,
debt or equity investments in projects that the Trustees believe
will promote new telecommunication-based education and training
in Victoria Australia.<BR>

<P>
<B>How to Apply to the Trust</B>
<P>
Victorian based organisations are assisted through the provision
of grants, equity of debt investments.  In addition, the Secretariat
provides a comprehensive range of project development, advice
and support to potential applicants.
<P>
<U><B>Selection Criteria</B></U>
<P>
Projects assisted by the Trust will be:
<UL>
<LI>related to appropriate information and communications technology
<LI>commercially viable
<LI>primarily based in Victoria
<LI>funded by a loan, grant or equity investment, and
<LI>legally bound by a formal agreement.
</UL>

<P>

<P>
<U><B>The Submission</B></U>
<P>
The final submission should be articulated through a Memorandum
of Information, written in consultation with a Secretary to the
Trustees.  The proposal should include details of the following:
<UL>
<LI>the telematic project
<LI>the management and organisation of the project
<LI>the expertise of key personnel
<LI>the potential market, advertising and promotion
<LI>the budget - expenditure and income, and
<LI>the financial background of the organisation.
</UL>

<P>

<P>
<B>The Secretariat and Its Role</B>
<P>
The Telematics Trust operates through a Secretariat which acts
on behalf of the Trust.
<P>
Under the terms of the Deed of the Trust, the Secretariat acts
as a management and investment adviser to the Trust.
