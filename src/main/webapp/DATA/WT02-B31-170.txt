<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>What Are the Obstacles?</STRONG></FONT><P>


	The kinds of investments that are right for you will depend largely on how you feel
about risk and
reward.  While most people want low risk and high returns, that combination may be difficult
to find.<P>

<STRONG>Growth of $1 in different investments 1925-1994  <FONT SIZE=-1></FONT><SUP><A
HREF="#1">1</A></SUP></STRONG><BR>

<IMG SRC="/nyl/images/pfpitpobim.gif" ALT="Growth  - 1925-1995" ALIGN=TOP WIDTH="472"
HEIGHT="236" BORDER="0">
<P>

	As you can see from this chart, over time, common stocks have substantially
outperformed
long-term
government bonds, as well as Treasury bills, and outpaced inflation.  The chart also shows
that over
short periods, stocks have had more ups and downs than fixed income securities, subjecting
investors
to greater risk.  While bonds and Treasury bills tend to provide relatively stable income, their
returns
also may vary with changes in interest rates.  As a general rule, investments with a higher
return
potential tend to involve more risk, and low-risk investments tend to provide lower returns. 
However,
past performance is not a guarantee of future results.
