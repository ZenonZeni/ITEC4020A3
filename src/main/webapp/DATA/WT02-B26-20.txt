<H2>Drugs and the Brain </H2>
<H4>by Solomon Snyder
</H4></font>
<hr>



	Dr. Snyder has published a revised edition of his original 
1986 work, Drugs and the Brain.  In his book he describes what 
scientists have discovered so far about the mechanisms by which 
drugs affect the brain.  The text covers not only the brain 
disorders commonly refereed to as mental illnesses, but also other 
brain disorders such as Parkinson's and Alzheimer's diseases.  
Well-done diagrams and illustrations supplement the text, which can 
sometimes be challenging to those without a scientific bent.  Dr. 
Snyder, who has been a leader in the field of brain research and in 
the use of drugs in psychiatry for a quarter of a century, is 
director of the Department of Neuroscience at Johns Hopkins 
University. 
<P>
	Drugs and the Brain by Solomon Snyder can be ordered or bought 
through your local bookstore.  Scientific American Library, 1996. 
228 pp.  $19.95.
