<P>
<B><I><FONT  SIZE=+1>1882 - 1922</I></FONT></B>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1882 --- Thomas A. Edison's Pearl Street steam powered central
station begins to supply electricity to New York City.</LI>
</UL>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1892 ---  William A. Tucker and S. Reed Anthony (a direct descendant
of both Miles Standish and John Alden) begin their partnership
on May 2. They opened their office at 50 State Street in the center
of Boston's financial district and,  with a staff of five, started
a banking and brokerage business named Tucker Anthony &amp; Co.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1898 --- Spanish-American War starts as Spain declares war on
the United States in April. The war ends by the end of the year.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1899 --- Tucker Anthony &amp; Co. closely associates itself
with the use of the new electrical technology and becomes identified
with the public utility industry and emerges as a leader in financing
and organizing electric street railways.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1901 --- The United States Steel Corporation is incorporated
in New Jersey and is the first billion-dollar corporation.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1903 --- Orville and Wilbur Wright make the first of four successful
flights in a heavier-than-air machine at Kitty Hawk in North Carolina.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1908 --- Tucker Anthony &amp; Co. opens a branch in New York
in the financial district at 24 Broad Street. Henry Ford introduces
his famous mass-produced Model T at an expensive $850. By 1926,
the price has gone down to $310 and is within the reach of almost
everyone.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1912 --- The steamship<I> Titanic </I>strikes an iceberg as
it steams at full speed through icy waters.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1914 --- S. Reed Anthony dies on March 10, at the age of 50.
The Panama Canal is completed, and later in the year, World War
I begins in Europe.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI> 1916 --- The Dow Jones Industrial Average hits 100.
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1918 --- World War I ends when Germany signs the armistice treaty
at five in the morning in a railroad dining car in the forest
of Compiegne.</LI><BR>
</UL>

<P>

<P>
<B><I><FONT  SIZE=+1>1923- 1933</FONT></I></B>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1923 --- The longest bull market begins with the Dow Jones Industrial
Average at 85.76.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1924 --- William A. Tucker retires from the firm he has founded
and led for the past 33 years. Calvin Coolidge is elected
President of the United States in the first election in which
women could vote. George Gershwin's <I>Rhapsody in Blue </I>receives
its first performance.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1927 --- Five years of careful underwriting and tight management
have earned Tucker Anthony great respect in the financial community.
The firm is now known to distribute only high-quality issues and
has a reputation for holding important accounts. Charles Lindbergh
flies his monoplane, <I>The Spirit of St. Louis,</I> from
New York to Paris.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1929 --- Tucker Anthony's 11 partners are conducting business
from the firm's two principal offices in Boston and New York and
through 10 branch offices with a staff of more than 300.
The longest bull market ends in September with the Dow at 381.17.After a steady decline since a market peak, the speculation balloon
bursts in October. Some 13,000,000 shares are sold on October
24, giving this day the label &quot;Black Thursday&quot; and 16,000,000
 shares change hands on October 29 on what becomes known
as &quot;Black Tuesday.&quot;</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1930 --- Five months after the market crash, the Great Depression
begins in earnest. That year 1,350 banks failed, and unemployment
begins to climb and peaks two years later with 13 million Americans
out of work. Those who still had jobs were receiving wages 60% lower than in 1929.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1933 --- FDR is inaugurated as the 32nd President of the United
States and implements the New Deal. Investors get a boost with
the passage of the Securities Exchange Act of 1933 -- creating
the Securities and Exchange Commission.</LI><BR>
</UL>

<P>

<P>
<B><I><FONT  SIZE=+1>1934- 1945</FONT></I></B>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1941 --- While Tucker Anthony is busy redefining its business
since the Great Depression, the world becomes more violent. The
United States enters World War II after Japan's surprise attack
on the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor on December 7.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1942 --- Tucker Anthony celebrates 50 years of doing business.
The Allies morale gets a tremendous boost when 16 U.S bombers,
led by Major General James Doolittle, take off from the carrier<I>
Hornet </I>and raid Tokyo and three other Japanese cities.
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1945 --- World War II ends as Germany surrenders to the Allies
on May 7 and Japan surrenders on September 2 aboard the USS <I>Missouri</I>.</LI><BR>
</UL>

<P>

<P>
<B><I><FONT  SIZE=+1>1946- 1955</FONT></I></B>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1949 --- The postwar market rise begins with a slow ascent from
a Dow low of 161 points and continues for 16 years. General Motors
drops automobile prices for the first time since World War II.
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1950 --- The Korean War begins as the North Korean army launches
a full-scale invasion of  South Korea. The war continues until
July 1953, when the Chinese agree to a  cease-fire.</LI><BR>
</UL>

<P>

<P>

<B><FONT  SIZE=+1><I>1956 - 1971</I></FONT></B>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1956 --- Tucker Anthony &amp; Co. merges with R.L. Day &amp;
Co., a respected  New England investment house, and becomes Tucker
Anthony &amp; R.L.Day. The Salk polio vaccine, developed by Dr.
Jonas Salk, is made available to the public.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1962 --- Astronaut John Glenn is the first American to orbit
the earth. America's towns,  schools and factories are working
at near capacity to serve a massive population of children and
teenagers of the postwar baby boom. In October, the world gets
a scare from &quot;The Cuban Missile Crisis&quot;  when a U-2
plane flying a routine reconnaissance mission over Cuba discovers
the existence of a fully equipped missile base.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1964 --- After two U.S. destroyers are attacked off the coast
of Vietnam by North Vietnamese P.T. boats, the U.S. Congress passes
the Gulf of Tonkin Resolution which gives President Johnson the
power &quot;to take all necessary measures to repel any armed
attack against the forces of the United States....&quot;</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1968 --- A rapid rise in market volume causes a &quot;paper
crunch&quot; on Wall Street as many firms fail to keep up with
the work. They misplace accounts and money. Unlike other firms,
Tucker Anthony is able to keep its back-office business current
but is also able to take on work for other firms. The U.S. Marines
come under attack at <I>Khe Sanh </I>in Vietnam in January followed
by a major communist offensive in February on the eve of Tet,
the lunar New Year.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1969 --- On July 20 at 10:56 PM (EDT), Neil Armstrong steps
onto the lunar surface, the first man to walk on the moon.</LI><BR>
</UL>

<P>

<P>
<B><I><FONT  SIZE=+1>1972 - Present</FONT></I></B>
<P>
<UL>
<LI>1972 --- The United States ends its involvement in the bitter
Vietnam War as the last units of U.S. ground forces withdraw from
Vietnam in August. The official cease-fire doesn't take place
until January 1973 when Henry Kissinger signs a four-party pact
in Paris.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1973 --- The market reaches a top of 1051 in January and then
plunges for the next two years. The Dow Jones Industrials lose
more than 400. The NYSE seat that had sold  for
$515,000 in 1969 was, five years later, going for the bargain
price of $70,000.</LI>
</UL>
<P>

 <UL>
<LI>1974 --- President Nixon resigns on national television on August 8.
</UL>

</OL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1975 --- In order to keep up with a changing American society,
Tucker Anthony changes from a partnership to a corporation. The
year is observed around the world as the International Women's
Year. For the first time the U.S. military academies open their
doors to females.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1982 --- Tucker Anthony &amp; R. L. Day is acquired by John
Hancock Mutual Life Insurance Company. In the agreement, Tucker
Anthony retains its name as well as its management structure,
and a full measure of autonomy is granted.</LI> 
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1987 --- The worst day in Wall Street history ends with the
Dow Jones Industrial Average losing 508 points, or 22.6%
of its total. The collapse is blamed on computerized trading and
panic reaction.</LI> 
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1990 --- Tucker Anthony Incorporated is one of only three firms
to be profitable for the year. The second longest bull market
begins on October 11 with the Dow at 2365.10.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1991 --- The Dow sets a new record by closing above 3000 for
the first time.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1992 --- When the red flag flying over the Kremlin is officially
hauled down and replaced by the blue-red-white flag of Russia
on December 25, 1991, the year begins with the end of the cold
war and Tucker Anthony Incorporated celebrating its 100th anniversary.</LI>
</UL>

<P>

<UL>
<LI>1995 --- The Dow closes above 4000 for the first time in February
then sets another record by closing above 5000 on November 20.</LI>
</UL>
<UL>
<LI>
1996 --- On Columbus Day, October 14,  the Dow closes at a all-time high of 6010. Management and a group of employees from Freedom Securities Corporation, together with two equity partners, Thomas H. Lee Company and SCP Private Equity Partners, L.P., complete the purchase of the firm from John Hancock Mutual Life Insurance Company on November 29. Freedom Securities is the holding company for Tucker Anthony, Sutro & Co. and Freedom Capital Management Corporation. The Dow finishes the year at 6448.
<P>
