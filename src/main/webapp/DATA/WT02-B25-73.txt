<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<p align=center><font color="#000080"><b>What To Do When An Employee Is Depressed --</b></font></p>
<p align=center><font color="#000080"><b>A Guide For Supervisors</b></font></p>
<hr>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Depression is more than the Blues </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - intense emotions for long periods of times </p>
<p>2 - a physical condition requiring treatment </p>
<p>3 - affects the total person - body, feelings, thoughts, behaviors </p>
<p>4 - comes in various forms - single bouts, recurrent episodes, mood swings </p>
<p>5 - each year, depression affects at least 10 million people between the ages of 
25 &amp; 44 </p>
<p>6 - untreated clinical depression may become a chronic condition that disrupts 
work, family, &amp; personal life </p>
<p>7 - more than 80% of depressed people can be treated quickly &amp; effectively, 
generally without missing much time from work or needing costly 
hospitalization </p>
<p>8 - 2 out of 3 depressed people do not receive the treatment they need </p>
<p>9 - symptoms </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. persistent sad or &quot;empty&quot; mood </p>
<p>b. loss of interest or pleasure in ordinary activities, including sex </p>
<p>c. decreased energy, fatigue, &quot;slowed down&quot; </p>
<p>d. sleep disturbances </p>
<p>e. eating disturbances </p>
<p>f. difficulty concentrating, remembering, making decisions </p>
<p>g. feelings of hopelessness/pessimism </p>
<p>h. feelings of guilt, worthlessness, helplessness </p>
<p>I. suicidal thoughts </p>
<p>j. irritability </p>
<p>k. excessive crying </p>
<p>l. chronic aches &amp; pains that don't respond to treatment </p>
<p>m. manic symptoms (inappropriate elation, irritability, decreased 
need for sleep, increased energy/activity, increased 
talking/moving/sexual activity, racing thoughts, disturbed ability to 
make decisions, grandiose notions, easily distracted) </p>
<p>n. symptoms in the workplace (decreased productivity, morale 
problems, lack of cooperation, safety risks/accidents, absenteeism, 
frequent statements about being tired, complaints about unexplained 
aches &amp; pains, alcohol/drug abuse)</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Depression Affects the Workplace </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - depressed employees may seem less productive &amp; reliable than usual </p>
<p>2 - depressed employees may often call in sick, arrive late to work, have more 
accidents, or just seem less interested in work </p>
<p>3 - depression results in more days in bed than many other ailments </p>
<p>4 - at any one time, 1 employee in 20 is experiencing depression </p>
<p>5 - in 1989, depression cost the Nation about $27 billion, of which more than 
$17 billion was due to time lost from work </p>
<p>6 - depression affects workers' productivity, judgment, ability to work with 
others, &amp; overall job performance </p>
<p>7 - individuals with depression are more likely to abuse alcohol &amp; drugs, 
resulting in other problems both on &amp; off the job</p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">How Supervisors Can Help Employees With Depression </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - provide training on depression &amp; other mental disorders for supervisors, 
employee assistance, &amp; occupational health personnel </p>
<p>2 - make appropriate treatment available through employee assistance programs 
&amp; through company-sponsored health benefits which will ultimately result in 
reductions in lost time &amp; in job-related accidents as well as in increases in 
productivity </p>
<p>3 - be open &amp; communicate </p>
<p>4 - learn about depression &amp; the sources of help </p>
<p>5 - recognize when an employee shows signs of a problem affecting 
performance </p>
<p>6 - note changes in work performance &amp; listen to employee concerns </p>
<p>7 - ask a counselor how best to approach an employee you suspect suffers from 
depression </p>
<p>8 - do not try to diagnose the problem yourself </p>
<p>9 - refer the employee to a professional </p>
<p>10 - be flexible about work schedule </p>
<p>11 - take suicidal comments seriously &amp; report them to a professional </p>
</blockquote>
