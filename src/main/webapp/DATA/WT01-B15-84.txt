At the other end of the performance tables are Communications, Real Estate, Banking and Pipelines.<p>Part of the rally seems to come from lower Canadian interest rates creating rising Canadian bond prices. The converse situation in the US-the perception of a stronger economy pushing up international prices for raw materials, seems to help our natural resources investments.<p>I believe that we have begun an intermediate-term rally. However, I don't see strong evidence for a continued move well into 1997 because of US considerations. Add to your positions, but this is probably not the time for <i>outright highly leveraged</i> speculation. The natural resources component of the TSE should give the Canadian markets more power than the US at this late stage of the bull market.<p>Last month, I reported good economic news from various bank think-tanks. This month The Fraser Institute gave a failing grade to the Canadian government on its ability to control fiscal policy and to meet past budget predictions. While the Liberals have made some positive gains, the Institute ranks the Federal government tenth out of eleven for the combined Federal and Provincial governments for both 1995-96 and 1996-7. That's not a pretty picture.
