<I>The Automobile at the Cross-Roads (ACROSS)
Slutrapport<BR>
<BR>

</I>



<B>Farv&auml;l till bensinbilen</B>






<P>Kanehira Maruo, Avdelningen f&ouml;r humanteknologi, Institutionen f&ouml;r tv&auml;rvetenskapliga studier av m&auml;nniskans villkor, G&ouml;teborgs Universitet
</P>


 

<B>Referat (syfte, metod, resultat)</B><BR>

Syftet med den h&auml;r rapporten &auml;r att beskriva och j&auml;mf&ouml;ra de tv&aring; milj&ouml;drivna teknikf&ouml;r&auml;ndringar som har haft och som kan komma att ha stor betydelse f&ouml;r bilteknologins utveckling, n&auml;mligen katalysatorteknikens inf&ouml;rande och elbilsteknologins genombrott.<BR>




<P>Metod: Intervjuer och litteraturstudie.
</P>




<P>Resultat: Elbilen har idag blivit det hetaste alternativet till bensinbilen. Reformulerad bensin, etanol, metanol, naturgas och biogas verkar endast partiellt eller kortsiktigt kunna l&ouml;sa den globala energif&ouml;rs&ouml;rjningen och de globala milj&ouml;-problemen. Den s k "technology forcing"-strategin fungerade v&auml;l n&auml;r man  inf&ouml;rde  katalysatortekniken p&aring; 70-talet, och samma strategi har hittills fungerat v&auml;l n&auml;r man f&ouml;rs&ouml;ker utveckla elbilsteknologin. Det viktigaste &auml;r att "technology forcing"-strategin och andra strategier, t ex ekonomiska styrmedel, till&auml;mpas samtidigt. </P>



<B>Abstract (aim, method, results)</B><BR>

The aim of this report is to describe and compare those two environmentally motivated technical changes that have been and may continue to be of great importance for development of the automotive technology, namely the introduction of catalytic converter technology, and the breakthrough of electric-vehicle technology.<BR>




<P>Methods: Interviews and textual studies.
</P>




<P>Results: The electric vehicle has become the most exiting alternative to the gasoline car. Reformulated gasoline, ethanol, methanol, natural gas, and biogas seem to be able to offer only partial or short-sighted solutions to global energy-supply problem and environmental problems. The so called "technology forcing" strategy functioned well when catalytic converter technology was introduced, and the same strategy has hitherto functioned well during the state of California's attempt to force the development of electric vehicle technology. The most important thing is, however, to apply the technology forcing strategy and other strategies such as the use of economic instruments simultaneously.
 </P>
