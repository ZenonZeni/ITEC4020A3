<p><font size="3"><img src="pictures/indy.gif" vspace="30"><br>
<strong>Giovanni INDIVERI<br>
Doctoral student</strong></font></p>

<p><font size="3">Giovanni Indiveri received his degree in
Physics from the Department of Physics at Genoa University in
1995 with a thesis on computer simulation of 2-D cluster growth
with long-range interactions, supervised by Prof. Andrea C. Levi. The
main results of this work have been published in the journal
&quot;Thin Solid Films&quot;. From 1995 to 1996 he attended a Masters
course in Maritime Logistics at SOGEA, in Genoa, Italy. The
course, sponsored by the EC, was successfully completed in
January 1997.</font></p>

<p><font size="3">He is currently studying for a PhD at the
Department of Computer Science, Systems and Telecommunications
(DIST) at Genoa University and is supervised by Prof. Giuseppe
Casalino. The PhD grant is funded by the Italian Research Council
(CNR) and the study is mainly carried out at the Istituto
Automazione Navale. &nbsp;</font></p>

<p><font size="3">His research interests focus on hydrodynamic
modelling as well as simulation of underwater vehicles and
manipulation systems.</font></p>
