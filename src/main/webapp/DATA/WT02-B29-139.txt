<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>Transfers and Rollovers</STRONG></FONT><P>



If you currently have an IRA, now is a good time to ask yourself some questions:
<UL TYPE=DISC>
	<LI>Am I satisfied with the performance of my IRA?
	<LI>Does my IRA allow me flexibility to change investments?
	<LI>Is my entire IRA contribution invested?
	<LI>Do I receive personal service from my sales representative?
	<LI>Can I get immediate answers when I have questions about my IRA?
</UL>

<P>
If you answer "no" to any one of these questions, you should consider
transferring or rolling over
your IRA to MainStay. But first of all, what's the difference between an IRA
transfer and an IRA rollover?
<P>
An IRA transfer is the direct transfer of funds from one IRA to another. The
transfer is handled by the
current and future custodians of your IRA so no check is actually issued to you. 
With an IRA rollover, you
request a check from your current IRA custodian for the amount you wish to roll
over. You then have 60
days to reinvest these funds in another IRA. If you do not reinvest within 60 days,
your IRA will lose its
tax-deferred status, and you will be taxed on the distribution and could be
penalized for early
withdrawal.
<P>
Although you can transfer as often as you want, only one rollover is permitted
every 12 months.
<P>
Take a close look at your current IRA. If it's not offering the advantages you
want, now is the time to
transfer or roll it over to MainStay.
