<p><font size="3" face="Arial"><strong><img
src="images/mercosurlogo.gif" align="right" hspace="0"
width="200" height="155"></strong></font><font size="3"
face="Arial"><strong>FORTALEZA. (MP).- </strong>After long
discussions, a blue southern cross with a green under stroke on a
clear background was finally chosen as the seal of the Mercosur
trade block and officially inaugurated during the presidential
summit held in the north of Brazil on December 17th. Argentine
delegates objected the seal because the southern cross has
basically four stars and could be interpreted as a closed
organisation with only its four original members (Argentina,
Brazil, Uruguay and Paraguay), leaving aside new associate
members such as Chile and from next May, Bolivia. Uruguayan and
Brazilian delegates on the other hand argued that the southern
cross is a constellation of stars with the four brightest
representing the original country members. Finally it was agreed
that the seal, &quot;<em>if necessary could be modernised in the
future</em>&quot;, as more stars (countries) are definitively
incorporated to the group. The seal was chosen by a jury of eight
(two for each member country) among 1.412 presentations with a
10.000 US dollars prize for the winner, an Argentine artist. </font></p>
