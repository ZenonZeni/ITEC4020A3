<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>Morningstar's Rating System
 </STRONG></FONT><P>




<STRONG>What is Morningstar, and what is its star rating
system?</STRONG><BR>

Morningstar is an independent mutual fund rating and analysis service based in
Chicago.
<P>
Its star rating system is comparative: a fund earns stars based on its score versus
all the
other funds in
its investment category.
<P>
Morningstar has created a simple, catchy signature of its own - the star system for
indicating
value in a
mutual fund - that's very much like the signature "thumbs up" or "thumbs down"
ratings made
popular
by movie critics Siskel and Ebert.  Both ratings offer an endorsement, but neither
claims that
the choice
is right for you.  
<P>
For example, before deciding to invest, you may also want to know:
<P>

<TABLE>

<TR>

	<TH ALIGN=LEFT>
	The movie
	</TH>
	<TH ALIGN=LEFT>
	The mutual fund
	</TH>
</TR>
<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	Is it an action/adventure?
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	Will it take you on a rollercoaster price ride?
	
	</TD>
</TR>
<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	Does it star an actor you enjoy?
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	Is there a fund manager with a solid 	record?


	</TD>
</TR>
<TR>

	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	Are there dazzling special effects?
	</TD>
	<TD ALIGN=LEFT>
	Does it rely on exotic, risky investments?
	</TD>
</TR>

</TABLE>

<BR>
And so on. And so on.

<P>

You get the idea.  Just as "two thumbs up" doesn't mean you'll like the movie, a
five-star
Morningstar
rating doesn't mean the fund is for you.

<P>
<STRONG>What the stars say</STRONG><BR>
They describe what's already happened over the last 1-, 3-, 5-, and 10-year periods. 
(Of
course, past
performance is no guarantee of future results.)  They're designed to answer two
specific
questions:
<P>
<OL>
<LI>How did a fund perform based on the risks investors took?
<P>
<LI>How did that performance compare to all the other funds in the same investment
category,
including all
distributions reinvested and sales charges? 
</OL>
<P>
The star system is a ranking system.  That means that even if every fund in the
category
showed terrific
performance, the last of those terrific funds would still receive only one star. 
<P>
<STRONG>What the stars don't say</STRONG><BR>
They don't predict the future.  They won't tell you how the fund will perform
starting
tomorrow.  The stars
also don't summarize all the information you should analyze before selecting an
investment. 
They
don't tell you:
<P>
<UL TYPE=DISC>
	<LI>whether the fund is right for your specific goals,
	<LI>in what kinds of markets the fund performed well - and not so well,
	<LI>the strategies of the people who invest your money,
	<LI>the amount of price volatility over the years,
	<LI>and many other details about the fund.
</UL>
