<CENTER><H2>A guide to the billions of dollars worth of project opportunities
now opening up in Latin America</H2></CENTER>
<P></P>
<CENTER><IMG SRC="line.gif"></CENTER> 
<P>The<B><I> Lagniappe Monthly on Latin American Projects &amp; Finance</I></B>
is a timely and cost effective guide to the profitable but often difficult
business of large project development in Latin America.  It focuses on sectors
that are now absorbing very large investments - oil and gas, electric power,
mining, telecommunications, transportation and water treatment.  It trains its
sights on the entire Central and South American market from Mexico to Argentina.
 It is a dedicated source of reliable, focused intelligence for any firm that
has investment, supply or financial links to any one of these hot sectors in any
Latin American market.</P>
<CENTER><H3>Topics in PROJECTS &amp; FINANCE Newsletter</H3></CENTER>
<P>These are some of the topics covered in the <B><I>Lagniappe Monthly on
Latin American Projects &amp; Finance:</I></B></P>
<UL><LI>How companies are profiting from the deregulation and private
concessioning rules that are opening up the huge project market in Brazil;</LI>
<LI>Why companies are leery about stepping into Mexico's gas and petrochemical
opening;</LI>
<LI>How government plans for the multibillion dollar privatization of water
systems in Chile are proceeding;</LI>
<LI>What funding innovations the World Bank and IDB are adopting to advance
private sector projects;</LI>
<LI>How power developers are coping with contractual obstacles in Colombia;</LI>
<LI>Who is buying into the electricity sector in Peru and why;</LI>
<LI>The new investors coming to Bolivia as a result of capitalization and an
incipient mining boom;</LI>
<LI>How Mexican cement maker Apasco raised $100 million in a difficult
environment;</LI>
<LI>The advice of a Chase Manhattan banker offers for succeeding in Brazilian
Privatizations.</LI></UL>
<P> </P>
<CENTER><H3>Plus, An Exclusive Annual Project Guide</H3></CENTER>
<P>A subscription to <I>Lagniappe Monthly on Latin American Projects  &amp;
Finance</I> includes our annual &quot;Compendium of Major Projects Underway or
Planned in Latin America.&quot;  Published every January, provides the only
composite picture available in the market today of the array of infrastructure,
privatization and concession projects on the regions's agenda.  The survey
covers the gamut of projects from Mexico to Argentina in the categories of oil
and gas, electricity, mining, sanitation, roads and bridges, railways, ports,
airports, waterways and telecommunications.</P>
