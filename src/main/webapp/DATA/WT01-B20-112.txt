-- I wish to thank the staff of the Courier for the <a
href="helmers2.html">article in Wednesday's August 28th paper</a>
on <a href="helmers1.html">Allen Helmers</a>.&nbsp; It is very
important that the public be informed of the facts.&nbsp; We all
know someone who may have, or still could benefit from using
marijuana as medicine.<br>
<!--webbot bot="HTMLMarkup" startspan --><SPACER TYPE=HORIZONTAL SIZE=15><!--webbot
bot="HTMLMarkup" endspan -->We trust our doctors to prescribe the
best medicine for our ailments, but when the best medicine is
illegal and unavailable, we are forced to use medicine that can
cause severe side effects creating new problems that must be
treated.&nbsp; Where is the sense in all of this?&nbsp; If a
seriously ill patient has a legitimate medical necessity, that
patient should be certified and be exempted from criminal
prosecution by the state of Iowa.<br>
<!--webbot bot="HTMLMarkup" startspan --><SPACER TYPE=HORIZONTAL SIZE=15><!--webbot
bot="HTMLMarkup" endspan -->Again, thank you very much.&nbsp; I
hope that in the future the Courier will continue to publish the
truth on controversial issues that affect all of us, so that we
may be informed of the facts and make judgment accordingly. <br>
