<STRONG>FBI Director, Industry Leaders Testify at Senate Crypto Hearing; Hearing was Cybercast Live</STRONG> <FONT SIZE=-1>[July 25, 1996]</FONT>
<P>
The Senate Commerce Committee held a hearing today to consider S. 1726, the 'Pro-CODE' bill designed to relax export controls on encryption.  FBI Director Louis Freeh and other high-ranking Administration officials testified before the committee. A second panel of
computer industry leaders, including Netscape CEO Jim Barksdale and others,
also gave testimony.
<P>
The hearing was cybercast live on the Internet.  Audio and photo archives of the hearing are available at the <A HREF="http://www.crypto.com/events/072596/">Encryption Policy Resource Site (www.crypto.com).</A>
<p>
The cybercast was brought online by the <A HREF="http://www.cdt.org/">Center for Democracy and Technology</A>, the <A HREF="http://www.vtw.org/">Voters
Telecommunications Watch</A>, <A HREF="http://www.hotwired.com">HotWired</A>, and <A HREF="http://www.digex.net/">DIGEX</A> with the generous help and support of the Senate Commerce Committee.

<STRONG>Second Senate Encryption Hearing Held on June 26; Hearing was &quot;Cybercast&quot; Live on the Net</STRONG> <FONT SIZE=-1>[June 26, 1996]</FONT>
<P>
Sen. Conrad Burns (R-MT) presided over a second encryption policy hearing before the Senate Commerce Subcommittee on Science, Space and Technology.  In a historic first, the hearing was &quot;cybercast&quot; live on <A HREF="http://www.hotwired.com/wiredside/" TARGET="NEW">HotWired's WiredSide Chat</A> via <A HREF="http://www.realaudio.com/" TARGET="NEW">RealAudio</A>.  Netizens concerned about Internet privacy and security not only heard the hearing live, they also submitted questions to the witnesses and discussed cryptography policy with others online.
<P>
<CENTER><STRONG><A HREF="http://www.hotwired.com/wiredside/96/26/index3a.html" TARGET="NEW">Audio Transcript</A></STRONG><BR>
<FONT SIZE=-1>(Must have <A HREF="http://www.realaudio.com/" TARGET="NEW">RealAudio</A> properly configured on your browser to listen to the transcript.)</FONT></CENTER>
<P>
The hearing focused on the need to reform US encryption policies, proposals to relax export controls on encryption (specifically the Pro-CODE bill), and the privacy issues raised by the current policy.
<P>
Witnesses that testified at the hearing included:
<P>
<FONT SIZE=3>
<UL>
<LI>Phil Zimmermann, Inventor of <STRONG>P</STRONG>retty <STRONG>G</STRONG>ood <STRONG>P</STRONG>rivacy <A HREF="/crypto/current_legis/960626_Zimm_test.html">[Written Testimony]</A>
<LI>Whit Diffie, Father of Public-Key Cryptography
<LI>Phil Karn, Cryptographer
<LI>Marc Rotenberg, Director, Electronic Privacy Information Center
<P>
<LI>Jerry Berman, Executive Director, Center for Democracy and Technology <A HREF="/crypto/current_legis/960626_CDT_test.html">[Written Testimony]</A>
<LI>Matt Blaze, Cryptographer <A HREF="/crypto/current_legis/960626_Blaze_test.html">[Written Testimony]</A>
<LI>Barbara Simons, Chair of US Public Policy Committee, ACM
<LI>Robert Gargus, President and General Manager, Atalla Corporation
</UL>
</FONT>
The &quot;cybercast&quot; was organized by <A HREF="http://www.hotwired.com/" TARGET="NEW">HotWired</A>, <A HREF="http://www.digex.net/" TARGET="NEW">DIGEX</A>, <A HREF="http://www.vtw.org/" TARGET="NEW">Voters
Telecommunications Watch</A>, and the <A HREF="http://www.cdt.org/">Center for Democracy and Technology</A>.

<STRONG>First Hearing on Pro-CODE Encryption Bill held on June 12; Next Hearing Scheduled for June 26</STRONG> <FONT SIZE=-1>[June 13, 1996]</FONT>
<P>
On June 12, 1996, Sen. Conrad Burns (R-MT) presided over the first of two hearings on S. 1726, the "Pro-CODE" bill, before the Senate Subcommittee on Science, Space and Technology.  Witnesses testifying at the hearing included:
<FONT SIZE=3>
<UL>
<LI>Sen. Patrick Leahy (D-VT)
<LI>Sen. Patty Murray (D-WA)
<P>
<LI>Michael Zisman, President and CEO of Lotus
<LI>Jim Barkesdale, President and CEO of Netscape Communications
<LI>Jim Bidzos, president and CEO of RSA Data Security
<LI>Tim Krauskopf, V.P. and co-founder of Spyglass Inc.

<P>
