That is somewhere ahead of us of course, but we don't want to wait and be buying the "darlings," do we? We want to be <i>selling</i> the "darlings" to those who don't know any better.<p>Again, the TSE 100 Resource Index is giving a solid performance. Gradually, the various natural resources sectors are combining to provide a more equal share of market power. If you recall, at the end of August I told you about a technical breakout in the Oil and Gas sector, projecting a 28% move to 6800 pt. We are only a couple of percent short of this initial target and may see higher values yet. The TSE Metals index is almost at 5800 pt. On a breakout <i>above </i>5800, the Metals Index projects a move to 6600 or 15% gain. During the Spring, I marked The Paper and Forest Products chart with the message "cycle lows." Since then a slow but steady continuing investment recovery has helped natural resource fund portfolios. So what's left out from this happy little picture? Gold.<p>London Gold bullion prices have now sagged down through my Stop point and I have to rate bullion as a Sell. The TSE Gold Index has fared better. TSE Gold Index,  having made three intermediate term lows in the past year, has each low higher than the previous, indicating a modest uptrend. While there may be a number of reasons for this behaviour, the best one is the split personality of the Canadian gold sector. If you invest in a company engaged in <i>exploration,</i> you have probably made money. If your funds are in a gold <i>processing</i> company, lower bullion prices will have reduced profitability and share prices. </td><td> &nbsp</td><td background="../images/tbltile.gif" bgcolor=#c6d6c6
