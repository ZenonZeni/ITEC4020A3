<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" face=arial><U>Section II-B</U>:</FONT></B> 
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Acute Toxicity Data</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1</B></FONT><P></P>

<FONT face=arial><I><B>Oral Rat LD:</B></I> 5400 mg/kg</FONT><P></P>   

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Skin Rabbit LD50:</B></I> 5040 mg/kg</FONT><P></P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Inhalation mouse LC50: </B></I>48mg/m3 Irritation eye rabbit:4 mg </FONT><P></P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL">Severe microorganism mutation references and animal tumorigenic data cited.</FONT><P></P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Aquatic toxicity rating:</B></I> Tlm 96 1000-100 ppm</FONT><BR><P>

<B><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#9B004E"><U>Section III-A:</U> </FONT></FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Health Information</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 2</B></FONT><P></P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Inhalation:</B></I> Vapors have a mild narcotic effect and act as an upper respiratory  tract irritant. Symptoms may include irritation of the eyes, nose, and throat, drowsiness, headache, and loss of coordination.  Excessive exposure may lead to narcosis and central nervous system depression.</FONT><P></P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Eye Contact:</B></I> Vapors or splashes may cause irritation</FONT><P></P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Skin Contact:</B></I> May cause skin irritation. Skin absorption may occur with symptoms  paralleling those of inhalation exposure.</FONT><P></P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Ingestion:</B></I> May cause nausea, vomiting, drowsiness, gastrointestinal pain, cramps and diarrhea. Large doses may cause death.</FONT><P></P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Chronic Exposure:</B></I> Prolonged or repeated skin contact may cause dermatitis. No systematic chronic effects have been reported in humans.</FONT><P></P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Aggravation of Pre-existing Conditions:</B></I> <FONT FACE="ARIAL">Persons with pre-existing skin problems or impaired respiratory function may be more susceptible to the effects of this substance.</FONT></FONT><BR>
<P></P>
<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U><B>Section III-B:</B></U></FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>First Aid</B></FONT></I></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 2</B></FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Inhalation:</B></I> Remove to fresh air. If not breathing, give artificial
respiration. If breathing is difficult, give oxygen. Call a physician.</FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Eye/Skin Exposure:</B></I> In case of contact, immediately flush skin/eyes with plenty of water for at least 15 minutes while removing contaminated
clothing, shoes or eyewear. Wash articles before reuse. For eyes lifting lower and upper eyelids occasionally. Get medical attention
immediately.</FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Ingestion:</B></I>If swallowed, induce vomiting immediately by giving two glasses of water and sticking finger down throat. Never give anything by mouth to an unconscious person. Call a physician immediately.</FONT><BR><P></P>
<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U>Section IV</U></FONT></B>: 
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Precautionary and First Aid Procedures</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1</B></FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Precautions:</B></I> Warning! Flammable Liquid, Harmful If Swallowed,
Inhaled or Absorbed Through Skin. Affects Central Nervous System Caused Irritation.  Keep away from heat, sparks and flame. Avoid breathing mist.  Avoid contact with eyes, skin and clothing. Keep container closed. Use with adequate ventilation.
Wash thoroughly after handling.</FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>First Aid:</B></I> If swallowed, induce vomiting immediately by giving two glasses of water and sticking finger down throat. Never give anything by mouth to an unconscious person. If inhaled, remove to fresh air. If not breathing, give artificial respiration. If breathing is difficult give oxygen. In case of contact, immediately flush skin or eyes with plenty of water for at least 15 minutes. In all cases call a physician. (see section III)</FONT><BR><P></P>
<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U><B>Section V</B></U></FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Physical Data</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1 (P)</B></FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Appearance:</B></I> Clear, dark colored solution.
</FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Odor:</B></I>  Alcohol-like</FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Solubility:</B></I> Considerable solubility in water.</FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Boiling pt.:</B></I> &lt;100C  (&lt;212 F)</font><p>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B><I>Vapor density (Air = 1):</I></B> &lt;2.07</FONT><P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Melting pt.</B>:</I> &lt;-127 C (&lt; -195 F)</FONT><P></P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B><I>Vapor pressure(mmHg):</I></B> &lt;15 @ 20 C(68 F)</FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Specific Gravity:</B></I> &gt; 0.8</font><p>   

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B><I>Evaporation Rate:</I></B> &lt;1.3 (Butyl acetate=1)</FONT><BR><P></P>

<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U><B>Section VI</B></U> </FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Fire and Explosive Hazards</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1</B></FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Fire:</B></I> Flammable Liquid! Flash pt: 15 C (59 F) (closed cup)</FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Explosion:</B></I> None</FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Fire Extinguishing Media:</B></I> Water spray, dry chemical, alcohol foam, or carbon dioxide. Water spray may be used to keep fire exposed containers cool.</FONT><P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Autoignition temperature:</B></I> 440 C (824 F)  Flammable Limits in Air, % by Volume: lel: 2.1, uel: 13.5</FONT><P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Explosion:</B></I> Above flash point, vapor-air mixtures are explosive within flammable limits noted above. Vapors can flow along surfaces to distant ignition source and flash back.</FONT><P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Special Info:</B></I> In the event of a fire, wear full protective clothing and NIOSH-approved self-contained breathing apparatus with full facepiece operated in the pressure demand or other positive pressure mode. If a leak or spill has not ignited, use  water spray to disperse the vapors and to protect persons attempting to stop leak. Water may be used to flush spills away from exposures and to dilute spills non-flammable mixtures.</FONT><BR><P></P>

<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U><B>Section VII</B></U> </FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Reactivity</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1(P)</B></FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Stability:</B></I> Stable under ordinary conditions of use and storage.</FONT>
<P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Hazardous Decomposition Products:</B></I> Carbon dioxide and carbon monoxide may form when heated to decomposition.  Other minor amounts of toxic gases are acrolein, acids, ketones, and aldehydes.</FONT><P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Hazardous Polymerization:</B></I> Will not occur</FONT> <P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Incompatibility:</B>  </I> Reacts violently with potassium-tert-butoxide. Can react vigorously with oxidizing materials. May function as a   catalyst for reaction of selected organic compounds.</FONT><BR><P></P>

<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U><B>Section VIII</B></U> </FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Spill and Leak Procedures</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1 </B></FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Spill/Leak Procedures:</B></I> Ventilate area of leak or spill. Remove all sources of ignition. Clean-up personnel require protective clothing
and respiratory protection from vapors. Small spills may be absorbed on paper towels and evaporated in a fume hood. Allow enough time for fumes to clear hood, then ignite paper
in a suitable location away from combustible materials. Contain and recover liquid for reclamation when possible. Larger spills and lot sizes can be collected as hazardous waste and atomized in a suitable RCRA approved combustion chamber, or absorbed with vermiculite, dry sand, earth or similar material for disposal as hazardous waste in a RCRA approved facility. Do not flush to sewer! Water can be used to dilute to raise flashpoint and to flush away from possible sources of ignition.<p>
Ensure compliance with local, state, and federal regulations.</FONT><BR><P></P>

<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U><B>Section IX</B></U></FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Storage and Disposal</B></FONT></FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1</B> </FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Storage:</B></I> Material is to be kept away from high temperatures. Optimum storage temperature is between 10 and 30 degrees Centigrade.  Due to its flammable nature, large quantities (&gt; 1 liter quantities) of the material should be stored in a flame retardant metal cabinet.</FONT><P>

<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Disposal:</B></I> Small quantities of the material (up to .5 liter) can be put in glass dish and the solvents allowed to evaporate in a fume hood.  The resulting polymer film can then be washed with water to remove small trace amounts of  sulfuric acid and put into the ordinary trash.  Large quantities (&gt; .5 liter) of
the material should be incinerated. </FONT><BR><P></P>

<B><FONT COLOR="#9B004E" FACE="ARIAL"><U><B>Section X</B></U></FONT></B>
<CENTER><I><FONT FACE="ARIAL"><FONT COLOR="#008000"><B>Handling and Protective Equipment</B></FONT> </FONT></I></CENTER>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><B>No. 1</B></FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Handling:</B></I> Use with adequate ventilation to avoid breathing mist. Avoid contact with eyes, skin and clothing.  Material is harmful
if swallowed, inhaled or absorbed through the skin.</FONT><P>
<FONT FACE="ARIAL"><I><B>Protective Equipment:</B></I> Eye protection and rubber gloves</FONT></BLOCKQUOTE><P></P>
