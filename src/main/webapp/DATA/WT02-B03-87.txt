<H3>DATA SERVICES:</H3>
<LI><A HREF="http://www.esdim.noaa.gov/"><B>NOAA Environmental Info Services</B></A>
<UL>
<LI><A HREF="http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/ncdc.html"><B>National Climatic Data Center</B></A> archives & distributes global data in meteorology & climatology.
<LI><A HREF="http://web.ngdc.noaa.gov/"><B>National Geophysical Data Center</B></A> archives & distributes global data in marine geology and geophysics, paleoclimatology, solid earth geophysics, glaciology, & solar-terrestrial physics. Includes <A HREF="http://www.ngdc.noaa.gov/paleo/paleo.html">World Data Center-A for Paleoclimatology</A> 
<LI><A HREF="http://www.nodc.noaa.gov/"><B>National Oceanographic Data Center</B></A> archives & distributes global physical, chemical & biological oceanographic data.
</UL>

<LI><A HREF="http://gcmd.gsfc.nasa.gov/"><B>Global Change Master Directory</B></A>, NASA's comprehensive source of information about worldwide earth science data holdings.  Information on field experiments such as FIRE, FIFE, TOGA-COARE, GALE, ERICA, & OTTER as well as global projects such as ISCCP, COADS, GTE, JGOFS, ALE/GAGE, & FGGE/GARP.  Information on international satellite data holdings such as SPOT, METEOSAT, GMS, & MOS.  US data descriptions include NASA, NOAA, NCAR, USGS, DOE (CDIAC), EPA entries, along with universities & research centers.  Descriptions of non-US data through the International Directory Network. 

<LI><A HREF="ftp://cdiac.esd.ornl.gov/pub"><B>Carbon Dioxide Information Analysis Center</B></A> archives & distributes data on atmospheric CO2 & other radiative active trace gases.

<LI><A HREF="gopher://gopher.gcdis.usgcrp.gov/"><B>US Global Change Data & Information System</B></A> interconnects Federal agency data & information systems making global change data accessible to researchers, policy-makers, educators, industry, & the public. 
 
<LI><A HREF="http://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/gcps/gcps.html"><B>NOAA Global Climate Perspectives System</B></A> is a joint effort of CDC's Global Climate Laboratory, NOAA ERL's Climate Diagnostics Center, & NOAA's Climate Analysis Center offering global climatological datasets. 


<LI><B>EOSDIS DAACs:</B> (Earth Observing System Data & Information System Distributed Active Archive Centers)
<UL>
<LI><A HREF="http://eos.nasa.gov/">EOS</A> Program.
<LI><A HREF="http://seazar.jpl.nasa.gov/">JPL Physical Oceanography DAAC</A> archives & distributes data on ocean circulation & air-sea interactions including TOPEX/Poseidon & AVHRR Ocean Pathfinder.
<LI><A HREF="http://eosdis.larc.nasa.gov/">Langley Research Center DAAC</A> archives & distributes data on radiation budget, clouds, aerosols, & tropospheric chemistry including ERBE, SAGE, & SRB missions, ISCCP data & climate products, & FIRE project.
<LI><A HREF="http://daac.gsfc.nasa.gov/">Goddard Space Flight Center DAAC</A> archives & distributes data on upper atmosphere, atmospheric dynamics, global biosphere, and geophysics including AVHRR Land Pathfinder, CZCS, TOMS & UARS missions.
<LI><A HREF="http://eosims.asf.alaska.edu:12355/asf_homepage.html">Alaska SAR Facility DAAC</A> archives & distributes data relating to the role of the polar regions in global change/warming including ERS-1 & JERS-1 synthetic aperture radar (SAR) and derived products.
<LI><A HREF="http://wwwdaac.msfc.nasa.gov/">Marshall Space Flight Center DAAC</A> archives & distributes data on all aspects of hydrologic cycle including SSMI Pathfinder, SSMI, SMMR & MSU products 
<LI><A HREF="http://www-eosdis.ornl.gov/">Oak Ridge National Lab DAAC</A> archives & distributes data on earth's biogeochemical dynamics including RIFE, OTTER & BOREAS as well as DOE CDIAC. 
<LI><A HREF="http://eosims.colorado.edu:1733">National Snow & Ice Data Center DAAC</A> (NSIDC) archives & distributes data on snow cover, avalanches, glaciers, ice sheets, floating ice, ground ice, permafrost, atmospheric ice, extra-terrestrial ices, paleoglaciology & ice cores. 
<LI><A HREF="http://edcwww.cr.usgs.gov/landdaac/landdaac.html">USGS EROS Data Center DAAC</A> archives & distributes land imagery.
<LI><A HREF="http://www.ciesin.org/">CIESIN SEDAC</A> (Consortium for International Earth Science Information Network Socio-Economic Data and Applications Center) archives & distributes socio-economic data related to global change. 
<LI><B>AFFILIATED: <A HREF="http://ns.noaa.gov/saa/homepage.html">NOAA/NESDIS Satellite Active Archive</B></A> offering data from NOAA's satellites.

</UL>

<LI><A HREF="http://www1.whoi.edu/jgofs.html"><B>US JGOFS</B></A>: Joint Global Ocean Flux Study for carbon & associated biogenic elements.  Includes 
<a href="http://www1.whoi.edu/eqpac.html">Equatorial Pacific Process Study</a> and <a href="http://hahana.soest.hawaii.edu/">Hawaii Ocean Time-Series Program</a>. HOTS has been observing hydrography, chemistry & biology at a station north of Hawaii since October 1988 to comprehensively  describe the ocean at a site representative of the central North Pacific. 
<LI><A HREF="http://diu.cms.udel.edu/"><B>OCEANIC</B></A>: Ocean Information Center at Univ of Delaware.  OCEANIC provides data on WOCE (World Ocean Circulation Experiment) & TOGA-COARE (Tropical Ocean Global Atmosphere Coupled Ocean-Atmosphere Response Experiment).

<LI><A HREF="http://www.coare.ucar.edu/"><B>TOGA COARE International Project Office</B></A>: Tropical Ocean Global Atmosphere, Coupled Ocean-Atmosphere Response Experiment.  Also <A HREF="http://www.atmos.washington.edu/togacoare/summaries.html">summary images</A> & <A HREF="http://thunder.atms.purdue.edu:80/toga_atlas">Climatology of the TOGA-COARE and Adjacent Regions 1985-1990 Volume 1: Kinematic
Variables</A>
<LI><A HREF="http://www.pmel.noaa.gov/toga-tao/home.html"><B>TOGA-TAO buoys</B></A> provide oceanographic & meteorological data from approx 70 ATLAS & current meter moorings in tropical Pacific Ocean. Includes <A HREF="http://www.pmel.noaa.gov/toga-tao/realtime.html">realtime graphics</A> and <A HREF="http://www.pmel.noaa.gov/toga-tao/data-delivery.html">data delivery</A>.
 
<LI><A HREF="http://edcwww.cr.usgs.gov/eros-home.html"><B>USGS Earth Resources Observation System Data Center</B></A> (EROS).

<LI><A HREF="http://www.ucar.edu/dss/index.html"><B>NCAR's Data Support Section</B></A> offers atmospheric, geophysical & oceanographic data.

<LI><a href="http://www.ofps.ucar.edu/"><B>UCAR Office of Field Project Support</B></a> archives & distributes field project data. Includes WWW browsing & ordering.

<LI><A HREF="http://ferret.wrc.noaa.gov/ferret/main-menu.html"><B>Pacific Marine Environmental Laboratory Climate Data</B></A> provides browsing, visualization, & retrieval of climate data. 

<LI><A HREF="http://www.nohrsc.nws.gov/data.html"><B>National Operational Hydrologic Remote Sensing Center Data Services</B></A> (NOHRSC), a branch of the National Weather Service & NOAA, measures snow cover in US & Canada, using satellite data, ground observations, airborne sensors & hydrologic modeling.

<LI><A HREF="http://seawifs.gsfc.nasa.gov/SEAWIFS.html"><B>SeaWiFS Project</B></A> monitors global ocean color.

<LI><A HREF="http://iabp.apl.washington.edu/"><B>International Arctic Buoy Program</B></A> has a network of automatic data buoys for monitoring synoptic-scale fields of pressure, temperature, & ice motion throughout the Arctic Basin.


<LI><A HREF="http://dcz.gso.uri.edu/XBrowse/browse/browse.html"><B>Univ of Rhode Island AVHRR XBrowse</B></A> for browsing satellite AVHRR imagery.

<LI><A HREF="http://southport.jpl.nasa.gov/"><B>NASA/JPL Imaging Radar</B></A>: access data and info on capabilities, applications, & data characteristics of airborne & spaceborne imaging radar used for remote sensing of Earth.
 
<LI><A HREF="http://xtreme.gsfc.nasa.gov/"><B>NASA AVHRR Land Pathfinder</B></A> for browsing global multichannel land surface satellite data. 

<LI><A HREF="http://www.ssec.wisc.edu/"><B>Space Science & Engineering Center</B></A>, Univ of Wisconsin, Madison.  SSEC offers current satellite images & backfiles; current satellite images include <A HREF="gopher://gopher.ssec.wisc.edu:70/II/mcidas.d/other.d/Latest%20SST%20Map">global sea surface temperature</A>, <A HREF="gopher://gopher.ssec.wisc.edu:70/II/amrc.d/antarctica_images.d/%20LATEST">Antarctic infrared</A>, <A HREF="gopher://gopher.ssec.wisc.edu:70/II/gsdc.d/goes_images.d/%20LATEST%20VISIBLE">GOES visible</A>, <A HREF="gopher://gopher.ssec.wisc.edu:70/II/gsdc.d/goes_images.d/%20LATEST%20INFRARED">GOES infrared</A>, & <A HREF="gopher://gopher.ssec.wisc.edu:70/II/gsdc.d/global_images.d/%20LATEST">GOES-METEOSAT-GMS global composite</A>.  Also info on the McIDAS program.

<LI><A HREF="http://atm.geo.nsf.gov/index.html"><B>Unidata Integrated Earth Information Server</B></A>

<LI><A HREF="http://www.orstom.fr/hapex/"><B>HAPEX Sahel</B></A> (Hydrologic Atmospheric Pilot Experiment Sahel) Project studies the continent/atmosphere interactions (H2O, CO2 & energy fluxes) in the Sahel Area.  Files of ground data & quicklook Spot Images.

<LI><A HREF="http://sio.ucsd.edu/loc_services/"><B>Scripps Institution of Oceanography data servers</B></A> including <A HREF="http://nemo.ucsd.edu/nemo_front.html">CalCOFI</A> from 1949+, <A HREF="http://nemo.ucsd.edu/nemo_front.html">temperature & salinity</A> from US West Coast shore stations, <A HREF="ftp://aeolus.ucsd.edu/weather">weather</A>, & Center for Coastal Studies' <A HREF="http://www-ccs.ucsd.edu/ccs/about_datazoo.html">Data Zoo</A>.

<LI><A HREF="http://rainbow.ldgo.columbia.edu/datacatalog.html"><B>Lamont Doherty Climate Group Data Catalog</B></A>


<LI><A HREF="http://topex-www.jpl.nasa.gov/"><B>TOPEX/POSEIDON</B></A> the Ocean Topography Experiment observes earth's oceans by satellite.  Offers global ocean images & animations of wave height, wind speed, water vapor & dynamic ocean topography.  Related pages include <A HREF="http://oraac.gsfc.nasa.gov/~rienecke/topex.html">GSFC</A>, <A HREF="http://www.grdl.noaa.gov/SAT/SAT.html">NOAA</A>, <A HREF="http://shaman.colorado.edu/topex.html">Colorado Center for Astrodynamics Research</A>, <A HREF="http://dutlru8.lr.tudelft.nl/pages/ssrt.html">Delft Univ of Technology Altimetry Atlas</A> and <A HREF="http://ftp.csr.utexas.edu/sst.html">Univ of Texas Center for Space Research</A>.  UTCSR offers <A HREF="ftp://ftp.csr.utexas.edu/pub/sst/gifs">monthly gridded dynamic ocean topography heights</A> observed by TOPEX/POSEIDON altimeter & corresponding sea level anomalies.  These solutions appear in Climate Diagnostics Bulletin but may be revised due to additional data.

<LI><A HREF="http://biudc.nbi.ac.uk/psmsl/psmsl.info.html"><B>Permanent Service for Mean Sea Level</B></A> maintains monthly & annual mean sea level  measurements from over 1600 tide gauge stations around the world. 

<LI><A HREF="http://www.aodc.gov.au/AODC.html"><B>Australian Oceanographic Data Centre</B></A> 

<LI><A HREF="http://milkman.cac.psu.edu/~reh113/index.html"><B>Current Offshore Data</B></A>, updated hourly, for Eastern Pacific, Western Atlantic, Gulf of Mexico & Great Lakes (compiler: Bob Hart, Penn State Univ).

<LI><A HREF="http://www.unidata.ucar.edu/"><B>Unidata</B></A>, a national program governed by universities & funded by NSF Division of Atmospheric
Sciences. Provides computing & networking technologies that empower universities to acquire & use atmospheric & related data, much of it in real time. 

<LI><A HREF="http://www.geo.ed.ac.uk/quakes/quakes.html"><B>The World-Wide Earthquake Locator</B></A>  is an interface developed by Bruce Gittings of the Dept of
Geography at Univ of Edinburgh, using USGS National Earthquake Information Center data to provide information about earthquakes within hours of the events.  In reponse to a query, the data is dynamically accessed from a USGS server in Colorado, processed into an appropriate format in Edinburgh, and displayed using the Xerox PARC Map Viewer in California.
<LI><A HREF="http://www.epri.com/Strategic/VitalIssues/MECCA/MECCA.html"><B>Model Evaluation Consortium for Climate Assessment</B></A> (MECCA) quantifies plausible ranges of predicted climate change available from simulations with current climate models.

<LI><a href="gopher://gds.esrin.esa.it/1"><B>Earth Observation Guide & Directory Service</B></a> [ESA ESRIN]

<LI><A HREF="http://shark1.esrin.esa.it/"><B>Ionia 1km Net Browser</B></A> of ESA ESRIN allows browsing of data collected through the 1 km AVHRR Global Land Data Set project, submission of server queries, visualization of quick-look images of satellite passes, & ordering of SHARP data products to be processed. 

<LI><A HREF="http://nic.fb4.noaa.gov/products/analysis_monitoring/enso_advisory/index.html"><B>El Nino Southern Oscillation Diagnostic Advisory</B></A>: Climate Analysis Center & National Meteorological Center.

<LI><A HREF="http://www.cchem.berkeley.edu/Table/index.html"><B>Periodic Table of Elements</B></A>




<LI><B>DATA RELATED:</B>

<LI><A HREF="http://orpheus.ucsd.edu/sio/ocean/tides304.zip"><B>TIDES</B></A>: Edward Wallner's tide prediction software, v3.04, for Intel/DOS.  TIDES predicts tide height or tidal current strength at any time. The predictions' method & format are generally those used by the National Ocean Service (NOS) in its published tide & current tables.  Subordinate station constants are created by correcting reference station constants using NOS Tide Tables' Table 2 values.  TIDES may be used for non-commercial purposes for a $15 registration fee. <A HREF="http://orpheus.ucsd.edu/sio/ocean/tides.doc">Documentation</A> for TIDES.
