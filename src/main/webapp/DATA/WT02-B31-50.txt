If you are selected to join New York Life, you'll make a commitment to the Company, but the
Company
will also make a commitment to you.  With New York Life, you embark on a lifetime of learning and
professional development through comprehensive training in insurance and financial services. 
<P>Professional development occurs through New York Life Insurance Company (NYLIC) University.  It's a program designed to train and
develop agents throughout their careers.  The first phase is Career Development School, during which
you'll begin the process of getting your insurance licenses.  You'll receive a full introduction to products
and selling skills, including how to develop your client base.  <P>
	After an initial six months of training, you'll spend two weeks in the Associate Program, an intensive
experience that's focused on life insurance marketing, product selling, and technical knowledge.  Upon
completion, you'll be on your way to developing expertise in a selected area in the workplace or in the
personal market, addressing individual needs.<P>
	The next level of NYLIC University is the School of Professional Development, which offers
specialized courses combining class instruction with self-study.  New York Life strongly encourages its
agents to make a commitment to lifetime learning.  For this reason the Company provides tuition
reimbursement to Agents and Registered Representatives upon completion of certain industry
courses.<P>
	As your career develops, you may wish to move into management.  the Company is structured to
support you in reaching this goal.  Many of today's senior executives began their careers as agents in
the field.
