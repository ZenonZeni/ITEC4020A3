The move is a big step forward for the fund managers who will now be able to run their money market funds with high yielding chequing accounts, Visa cards and no fee bill payment. Through Citibank, AGF's services will also allow access to Interac and Cirrus ATM's-systems denied to all but the Canadian banking system. <b>Trimark Financial</b> have recently gone a similar route by buying little <b>Bayshore Trust </b>for $35 million. By buying Bayshore, Trimark will get membership in the Canadian Payments Association. This will allow Trimark to offer chequing and other banking privileges to Trimark's one million unitholders. Many observers see this as a direct attack on the banks, whose low interest savings and chequing accounts provides important bank revenue.<p><b>TD Greenline</b> is about to offer Internet trading to its customers. The trading system, known as "WebBroker" will be offered on a test basis to a group of high volume clients. Later "WebBroker" will be offered nationally by the end of September. Clients can expect to place trades at anytime, check account information and get a wide range of quotations.<p><b>C.I. The funds will be part of C.I.'s "Sector" group of funds that allow an investor to switch within the "sectors" without triggering capital gains. The resource fund will be managed 'in-house' by John Zechner, the international funds by BEA Assoc. And the Health fund by H.
