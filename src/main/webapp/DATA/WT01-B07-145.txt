<p><font color="#800000" face="Arial"><b><img
src="images/red.gif" width="15" height="15"> </b></font><font
color="#000080" face="Arial"><strong>ARGENTINE JOURNALISTS WILL
VISIT THE ISLANDS</strong></font></p>

<p><font face="Arial"><strong>STANLEY. (MP).- A small team of
journalists from Buenos Aires will be joining the two Argentine
groups of next of kin relatives when they travel to the islands
to visit the war cemetery in Darwin in mid January and February</strong>.
The announcement was made public by a member of the local
Legislative Council after &quot;<em>receiving a request</em>&quot;
from the Argentine government. The group of journalists will
include a news editor from the Buenos Aires Herald, a television
team from &quot;<em>Multimedia America</em>&quot; and a reporter
from TELAM, the official Argentine news agency. The first visit
of this kind to the war cemetery in Darwin where 234 Argentines
are buried took place in March 1991. On this occasion the
Argentines will be staying overnight at the Darwin lodge. </font></p>
