        <td valign="top">The CSL workbook is a critical element
        in our training program. This comprehensive guide to
        licensing explains all aspects of the regulatory process
        including the requirements of the Departments of State
        and Commerce, and the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and
        Firearms. The workbook is updated on a regular basis.
        <p>The workbook includes, among other things: </p>
        <ul>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">An explanation of the U.S.
                arms export system;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">The role of the Office of
                Defense Trade Controls;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">The </font><a
                href="itar.html">International Traffic in Arms
                Regulations (ITAR)</a><font color="#0000FF"> and
                the U.S. Munitions List;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">A comprehensive guide to
                the licensing process;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">Commodity jurisdiction
                determination and its critical role in defense
                trade;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">Key definitions and their
                impact on defense trade;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">License preparation
                guidelines, an agreement preparation manual, and
                sample licenses and agreements;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">The </font><a
                href="http://www.dtic.dla.mil/">Department of
                Defense</a><font color="#0000FF">&#146;s role in
                controlling proliferation of defense technology;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">A listing of other agencies
                and offices involved in the license review
                process;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">A profile of the Missile
                Technology Control Regime (MTCR), and other
                multilateral control regimes;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">Guidelines for employing
                foreign nationals;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">Congressional
                notifications;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">Denial Orders and
                anti-boycott controls on licensing;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">Summary of policy
                considerations impacting on licensing
                determinations;</font></li>
            <li><font color="#0000FF">An overview of the </font><a
                href="http://www.bxa.doc.gov/">Department of
                Commerce&#146;s Bureau of Export Administration</a><font
                color="#0000FF"> and the </font><a
                href="http://www.ustreas.gov/treasury/bureaus/atf/atf.html">Treasury
                Department&#146;s Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and
                Firearms</a><font color="#0000FF">.</font></li>
        </ul>
        <p>The CSL Workbook is comprehensive and timely.
        Exporters have found it an invaluable tool in
        understanding, implementing, and complying with the U.S.
        Government&#146;s regulatory requirements. </p>
