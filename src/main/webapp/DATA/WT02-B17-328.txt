<BLOCKQUOTE><CENTER>
<H2>The Rapid Assessment Program</H2>
</CENTER>

<P>
<IMG SRC="../../../RESOURCE/SLIDES/BIOLOGY/Louise.jpg" ALT="Louise.jpg - 6.3 K" WIDTH=131 HEIGHT=200 HSPACE=10 VSPACE=10 BORDER=0 ALIGN=right><STRONG>The Rapid Assessment Program (RAP)</STRONG> is a team of world-renowned field scientists brought together to work with host-country scientists on quick-but-thorough biological inventories of unstudied tropical habitats.
<P>
Rapid Assessment Program analyses and recommendations are published in <A HREF="../../CILIB/PUBLICAT/Pubs.htm">CI's RAP Working Papers</A>.  So far, RAP teams have surveyed habitats in Bolivia, Ecuador, Peru, Belize, Mexico, Guyana, and New Ireland (in Papua New Guinea)--and found so much previously unknown biodiversity that decision-makers in many of these countries are using the results to set new conservation priorities and redefine protected areas.  Future RAP trips will target further work on the eastern slopes of the Andes, and ecosystems in Madagascar, Papua New Guinea, and Indonesia.
<P>
To learn more about field team activities, <A HREF="rap.htm">click here</A>.</BLOCKQUOTE>
