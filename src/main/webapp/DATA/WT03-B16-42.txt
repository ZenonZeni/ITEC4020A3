<dt><h3><img src="pic/redball.gif"><a href="http://137.132.34.40/crisp_ql/spotgeo.html"> SPOT Catalogue Browse</h3></a>
<dt>A full resolution <a href="spot/spot.html">SPOT Image</a> has a coverage of 60km by 60km, with resolution of
20 metres for the 3-band multispectral mode images or 10 metres for the single band
panchromatic mode images.  The browse images are subsampled to resolution of 120
metres in both modes and compressed with JPEG.<p>  

<dt><h3><img src="pic/redball.gif"><a href="http://137.132.34.40/crisp_ql/ersgeo.html"> ERS Catalogue Browse</h3></a>
<dt>A full resolution <a href="ers/ers.html">ERS synthetic aperture radar (SAR) image</a> has a coverage of 
about 100km by 100km with resolution of about 20-25 metres.  The browse images are 
processed (with CRISP's ERS quicklook processor) to resolution of about 160 metres 
and compressed with JPEG.<p>

<dt><h3><img src="pic/at_work.gif"><a href="http://137.132.34.40/crisp_ql/rsatgeo.html"> RADARSAT Catalogue Browse</a></h3>
<dt>CRISP's <a href="rsat/rsat.html">RADARSAT</a> catalog browse is currently under testing.<br>   The data presently in the
catalog were acquired by CRISP for the RADARSAT Network Station Certification
exercise, which was successfully conducted from 1 to 4 December 1996.<br>  
CRISP will be undergoing a two week  <a href="rsat/rsat.html">RADARSAT</a>  Operational Evaluation exercise starting 16 December 1996.
