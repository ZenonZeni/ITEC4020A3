<p>
<b>Research Interests</b><p>

Experimental studies of plasma-materials interactions and boundary layer  
plasma physics. Present research topics include the erosion and deuterium 
retention behavior of beryllium when it is exposed to 
<a href="http://www.iterus.org">ITER</a> relevant plasma 
conditions in the PISCES-B linear plasma device. Plasma physics research 
includes the role of electric fields on transport in the boundary layer, 
investigation of magnetic sheath formation and RF ion heating.<p>

Other areas of interest include the design and optimization of plasma sources.
These efforts have culminated in the fabrication of the new, 800kW, 
<a href="http://pisces.ucsd.edu/~rdoerner/cusp.html">PISCES-Upgrade</a> plasma source, which is under construction in collaboration 
with <a href="http://www.mdc.com">McDonnell Douglas Aerospace</a>.<p>


For further information, contact <a href="mailto:rdoerner@ucsd.edu">Russ Doerner</a>.<p>
