<h1 align=center>Wednesday<br>
Topical Workshop<br>
School Principals, Administrators And Support Personnel</h1>
<blockquote>

<h3 align=center>Sponsored By The LDA Education Committee</h3>
<h3>
W4<br>
8:30 AM-3:45 PM</h3>

Realizing the important role school principals, administrators, and
support personnel play in the success of special education in the
classroom, the LDA Educational Services Committee enthusiastically
presents this workshop. 
<hr width="75%">

<h3 align=center>Principles of Special Education for Principals</h3>
<b>Laurence M. Lieberman, Ed.D.</b>, Special Education Consultant,
Boston, MA
<p>This segment provides an outline of issues related to defining
children in need of special services, providing services, and determining
the principal’s role in implementing services. The principal’s view of
special education is described within the broader context of the school
as a whole. 

<hr width="75%">

<h3 align=center>Creating a Positive School Climate:<br>Strategies for
Fostering Self-Esteem, Motivation, and Discipline</h3>

<b>Robert Brooks, Ph.D.</b>, Clinical Psychologist, Needham, MA

<p>Principals play a major role in creating a positive school climate, a
climate in which staff, students, and parents feel welcome and thrive.
Dr. Brooks will discuss a framework for understanding the components of
self-esteem and motivation, the importance of identifying and reinforcing
the "islands of competence" in ourselves, our staff, and our students,
and specific strategies for fostering self-esteem, motivation,
self-discipline, and resilience in all students, especially those with
learning disabilities. 

<hr width="75%">

<h3 align=center>Service Delivery Models:<br> There is More Than One
Way</h3>

<b>James M. Kauffman, Ph.D.</b>, Professor, University of Virginia, Charlottesville, VA 

<p>Dr. Kauffman, author and lecturer, will discuss classroom practices
that make up the discipline of special education. This presentation
recognizes the significant role of the general educator in dealing with
students with a variety of learning and behavioral problems in response
to the continuing trend to educate exceptional youngsters as close as
possible to the general education mainstream. 

<hr width="75%">

<h3 align=center>Ten Ways to Avoid Law Suits</h3>
<b>Reed Martin, J.D.</b>, Attorney-at-Law, Martin and Bishop, Houston, TX
<p>Mr. Martin will provide valuable information of particular interest to
school administrators regarding legal issues in schools when serving
students with learning disabilities. References will be made to pertinent
judicial decisions, Section 504, the Americans with Disabilities Act, and
the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act.
