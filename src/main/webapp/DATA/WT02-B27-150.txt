<h1>CONTEMPORARY ISSUES IN LAW, MEDICINE AND ETHICS</H1>
<H2>Edited by <strong>Sheila McLean,</strong> International Bar Association Professor of Law and Ethics in Medicine and Director, Institute of Law and Ethics in Medicine, Glasgow University, UK.</h2>
<HR>

February 1996 &nbsp 287 Pages &nbsp Hardback
<HR>
1 85521 586 1 &nbsp £42.50
<hr> 
</CENTER>
<BR><BR>
The rapidly changing face of modern medicine and the increasing involvement of public debate in its practice, are reflected in the wide range of contributions to this book, which takes a searching look at the issues which are currently at the forefront of modern debate in medical ethics and the law.
<BR><BR>
<HR>
<h3>CONTENTS:</h3>
<li>Introduction, <i>Sheila McLean</i>
<li>Professional Ethics and Business Ethics, <i>Robin Downie</i>
<li>Health Rights, Ethics and Justice: The Opportunity Costs of Rhetoric, <i>Derek Morgan</i>
<li>If You Know What's Good for You: Refusal of Consent to Medical Treatment by Children, <i>Sarah Elliston</i>
<li>Private Decisions and Public Scrutiny: Sterilisation and Minors in Australia and England,  <i>Kerry Peterson</i>
<li>Maternal/Foetal Conflict: Defined or Defused? <i>Jane Mair</i>
<li>Wrongful Life and Wrongful Birth, <i>Patricia Beaumont</i>
<li>Contemporary Issues in organ Transplantation, <i>JK Mason</i>
<li>Transplantation and the &quotNearly Dead&quot: The Case of Elective Ventilation, <i>Sheila McLean</i>
<li>Reason, Law and Medicine: Anencephalics as Organ Donors, <i>John Porter</i>
<li>Advance Directives / Living Wills, <i>Chris Docker</i>
<li>Confidentiality and the Human Genome Project: A Prophecy for Conflict? <i>Iona Brown and Philippa Gannon</i>
<li>Biotechnology and Intellectual Property: A Marriage of Inconvenience? <i>Graeme Laurie</i>
