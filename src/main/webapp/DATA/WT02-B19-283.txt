<CENTER><H3><B>HOUSE APPROPRIATIONS SUBCOMMITTEE <BR>
MOSTLY LEVEL-FUNDS IDEA FOR FY  97
</CENTER></H3></B> <P>


The House Labor, Health and Human Services, and Education Appropriations subcommittee
completed action on its FY 1997 appropriations bill early Friday morning, June 14.  Compared
to last year's House markup, which in effect originally zeroed out many of the support programs
under IDEA, the subcommittee level-funded all IDEA programs except for one, the Media and
Captioning Services program, which received a $900,000 boost over FY  96 levels.  Of course,
level funding does not take into account inflation or increased student enrollment under IDEA. 
The House also did not adopt President Clinton's recommendation that the 14 support programs
be consolidated into 5 programs. <P>

During the subcommittee markup, Rep. Steny Hoyer (D-MD) offered an amendment to
"Maintain Commitment to Local Schools," which would have added a total of $2.6 billion to
several programs.  Under this amendment, Part B of IDEA would have received an additional
$300 million.  Unfortunately, the amendment was defeated on a party line vote.   <P>

The Javits Gifted and Talented Students Program was also level-funded at $3 million, down two-thirds from its 1995 authorized level.  <P>
