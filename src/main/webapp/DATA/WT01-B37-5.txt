<h1>Organizing Books </h1>

<p> <b>From: Aaron D Wilson &lt;adw7@columbia.edu&gt;</b> </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/blueline.gif" align="BOTTOM"> </p>

<p> As promised here is a list of books I have in my collection
that deal with the subjects of political activism, organizing,
leadership development, and community organizing. Those I have
marked with an asterisk (*) have proven to be especially useful
to me. </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM">* <b>&quot;How
People Get Power&quot;</b>, by Si Kahn. 1994 (Latest edition) *
&quot;Organizing&quot;, by Si Kahn. 1991 ( &quot; &quot; ) </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>&quot;Political
Organizing in Grassroots Politics&quot;</b>, by Daniel Russell.
1990 ( Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now or
ACORN's organizing handbook). </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM">&quot;<b>Rules
for Radicals&quot;</b>, By Saul Alinsky. 1971 </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"> <b>&quot;War
Resister League--Organizer's Manual</b>. 1981 (You may have to
look for this in an academic library, as it is out of print). </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM">* <b>&quot;Resource
Manual for a Living Revolution&quot;</b>, by Virginia Coover, et
al. 1981 </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM">* <b>&quot;Organizing
for Power and Empowerment&quot;,</b> by Jacqueline B. Mondros and
Scott M. Wilson. 1994 (An amazing book, has a chapter called
Maintaining and Deepening Membership Participation...) </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>&quot;Organizing
for Community Action&quot;</b>, by Steve Burghardt. 1982 </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>&quot;NORML
Activist Packet&quot;</b>, Available for $15 from National NORML.
I recently purchased a copy and have found it to be an excellent
resource. A must for any marijuana activist. </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>&quot;Why
We Can't Wait&quot;</b>, by the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr..
1964 (This is a first hand account of the campaign to desegregate
Birmingham, Alabama. Great stuff on CD in America). </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>&quot;Conquest
of Violence&quot;</b>, by Joan Bondurant. 1965 (Analysis of five
Ghandhian campaigns). </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>&quot;Steal
This Book&quot;</b>, by Abbie Hoffman. 1971 (Learn why the power
structure hated the Yippies...) </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>&quot;Roots
to Power: A Manual for Grassroots Organizing&quot;</b>, by W.
Collette. 1984 </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>The Art
of Coalition Building:</b> A Guide for Community Leaders by Cheri
Brown, 1984, 45 pages, $3.50 This handbook takes the reader
through the step-by-step process of coalition building. Chapters
include: Getting Started; Choosing Realistic Positions and
Finding Allies; Developing Effective Leadership Skills; How a
Coalition Functions Internally; </p>

<p> <img src="../graphics/golddot1.gif" align="BOTTOM"><b>How to
Negotiate and Resolve Conflicts;</b> and Building Unity Across
Ethnic, Religious and Class Divisions. This generic
coalition-building guide highlights many of the difficulties that
are likely to emerge. Especially helpful is a six-page coalition
checklist to guide the reader through the process. Available from
the National Coalition Building Institute, 1835 K Street N.W.,
Suite 715, Washington, DC 20006, (202)785-9400. <br>
</p>
