<TD ALIGN=LEFT WIDTH=315><H2> DO'S AND DON'TS WHEN DEALING WITH
ELECTED OFFICIALS </H2> 
</TD> 
</TR> 
</TABLE> 
</center><P>

<center>
<TABLE WIDTH=500 TD VALIGN=TOP BORDER=0 CELLPADDING=0
CELLSPACING=0> 
<TR><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Make an appointment.
</b></td><td><b>Arrive
unexpected with the idea of seeing the official.</b></td></tr>
<tr> </tr>
<tr><TD>By letter or phone. <br> Confirm the
appointment.<br>  Appointments with legislative
aides are also available.  (Ask for the aide responsible for the issue(s) you wish to
discuss.)<P></td></tr><tr>
<TR><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Be on time for the visit.</b></td><td><b>Be
late for the
visit.</b><P></td></tr><tr>
<TR><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Be positive and friendly.</b></td><TD
WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Be
confrontational. </b><br> (Don't threaten or berate the
official.)<P></td></tr><tr>
<TR><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>State the reason for your visit. </b></td>
<td><b>Try to discuss several different issues.</b></td></tr><P>
<tr>
<TR><TD>Be concise and specific. <br> Introduce yourself
and identify the group you are representing. <br>
Briefly describe the issue(s).<br>  Refer to bills by name and number (e.g., budget
reform, "Budget Process Reform Act," H.R. 3732). <br> Limit the number of issues to
be discussed. <br> State your position and recommendation on the issue(s).<P>
</td></tr><tr>
<TR><TD><b>Provide reliable data/facts to support your
position.<P></b> </td><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Give incorrect information. </b></td></tr><P><tr>
<TR><TD><b>Personalize the issue(s).</b> <p> Tell how
the bill or
action will affect you, your relatives, friends, or neighbors.<P></td></tr><tr>
<TR><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Leave a written summary of your
position.<P></b></td>
<td><b>Leave issue papers or other lobbying documents.</b></td></tr><P><tr>
<TR><TD>Fact
sheets on issues
can be left.  Also leave your name,
address, and phone number.<P></td></tr><tr>
<TR><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Have a picture taken with official, if
possible.</b></td>
<td><b>Make a funny face or obscene gesture while your picture is
being taken with your Member of Congress.</b><P></td></tr><tr>
<tr><td> Use the picture in local newspapers to provide
publicity for the official, your
organization, and the issues discussed.  (If picture is not taken and meeting is a good
one, ask for a photo of the official and some background material.) <P></td></tr><tr>
<TR><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Write a thank-you letter.
</b></td><TD><b>Forget to
write
a thank-you letter.</b></td></tr><P>
<tr> 
<tr><td>Thank the official
for the visit. <br> Summarize the visit. <br> Identify follow-up steps committed by the
official and you. <br> Ask for the official's commitment. <br> Request a reply. <br>
Send a photo print (if taken) identifying persons in photo and date. (Enlargements have
a better chance of appearing on office wall/desk.)<P></td></tr><tr><TR><TD><b>Arrange for your official to visit students with
exceptionalities in various classrooms in his/her state or district.<P></b>
</td><TD WIDTH=245 VALIGN=top><b>Ignore the
member for the rest of the year.</b></td></tr><P>
<tr>
<TR><TD><b>Have others write letters.<P></b> Write about one issue
only.
<br>
If writing about a bill, refer to the title and number. <br> Be brief.  (One or two pages, if
possible.) <br> Be specific.  (Tell your position on the issue and why you hold that
position.) <br> Share personal experiences. <br> Ask for the official's commitment.
<br> Request a reply. </td></td></tr></table></center>
