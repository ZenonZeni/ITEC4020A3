<H3>Welcome to the website of The Scott Polar Research Institute (SPRI),  
the <a href="devel.htm">oldest</a> research centre in the world which 
covers both the Arctic and Antarctic regions. It is part of the Faculty of 
<A HREF="http://www.esc.cam.ac.uk/">Earth Sciences</A>
 and <A HREF="http://www.geog.cam.ac.uk">Geography</A> in the 
School of Physical Sciences of the <A HREF="http://www.cam.ac.uk/">
University of Cambridge</A>. Our <a href="#addr">address and 
phone number</a> is given below.</H3>

Also in Cambridge, and often confused with SPRI, is the 
<A HREF="http://www.nerc-bas.ac.uk/">British Antarctic
Survey</A> which is part of the <a href="http://www.nerc.ac.uk"> Natural
Environment Research Council</a>. Look at our usage 
<a href="stats.htm">statistics</a> for our webserver, which were created 
by <a href="http://www.statslab.cam.ac.uk/~sret1/analog/">Analog</a> 
software. 
<p>

To find out how to get to SPRI (and BAS) look at our 
<A HREF="map.htm">street map of Cambridge</A> <i>(87K)</i> or at our 
<A HREF="images/map2.jpg">road routes to Cambridge</A> <i>(226K)</i>.

<ul><b>Media enquiries</b>

Pam Davis</a>, Public Relations Coordinator.

Mills</a>, Librarian and Information Officer.</ul>

<h2>The Library, Archives and Museum</h2>

<ul>
<li>SPRI has the world's largest polar <a href="lib/libhome.htm">Library</a> 
and <a href="archmus.htm">Archives</a>, as well as a famous small polar 
<a href="archmus.htm#mus">Museum</a>.
<li>The Library/Archive have created a number of useful <a
