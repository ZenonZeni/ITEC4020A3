<h3>Scott Polar Research Institute</h3>

<h2>Summary of the Arctic archipelagos and islands, off Eurasia</h2>

</CENTER>
<hr>
<p>


Compiled by <b>R. K. Headland</b><p>

These lands in the Arctic Ocean are a diverse range of archipelagos and separate islands. 
The positions given are approximately the middle point for smaller islands and their limits 
for larger ones and groups. The other figures are from the best sources available to the 
author: not all are equally reliable. Names are given in the forms recommended by the 
Union G&eacuteographique International with, where the name has varied through double 
transliteration, the original form, and, in other cases, an English translation. Sightings, 
landings, and winterings are the first recorded, there may have been previous ones for 
some islands. Islands only a short distance off the continental mainland are not included. 
The <i>italic</i> number after the coordinates is the Universal Decimal Classification 
(for use in Polar Libraries) reference.<p>  

In easterly order the islands are:<br>

<dl><b>Svalbard Arkipelet ('Cold Coast')</b> 
<dd>76&#183;50-80&#183;80&#176;N : 
10-34&#176;E [85&#183;00-89&#183;78&#176;N : 11&#183;00-38&#183;00&#176;E] 
<i>(*32)</i>
<dd>Four main islands and about 150 lesser ones.
<dd>Area: 62 800 km2. Highest elevation: 1717 m. 60% glacierized.
<dd>Possibly sighted 1194; discovered 1596 by a Dutch whaling voyage (Jacob Heemskerck).
First wintering 1630; permanent mining settlement established 1906, currently three operate, four scientific stations operate.
<dd>Norwegian territory, Svalbard; under the Spitsbergen Treaty of 1920.</dl>

<dl><b>Bj&oslashrn&oslashya (Bear Island)</b> 
<dd>74&#183;60&#176;N : 19&#176;E 
[82&#183;89&#176;N : 21&#183;11&#176;E] <i>(*329)</i>
<dd>One isolated island.
<dd>Area: 178 km2. Highest elevation: 536 m. Unglacierized.
<dd>Discovered 1596 by a Dutch whaling voyage (Jacob Heemskerck).
First wintering 1823-24; meteorological station, established 1923, operates. 
<dd>Norwegian territory, Svalbard; under the Spitsbergen Treaty of 1920.</dl>

<dl><b>Zemlya Frantsa-Iosifa (Franz Joseph Land)</b> 
<dd>79&#183;73-81&#183;93&#176;N : 
37-65&#183;50&#176;E [88&#183;60-91&#183;00&#176;N : 
41&#183;11-72&#183;78&#176;E] <i>(*527)</i>
<dd>A group of 191 islands.
<dd>Area: 12 334 km2. Highest elevation: 606 m. 85% glacierized.
<dd>Possibly sighted 1865; discovered 1873 by an Austrian expedition (Julius Payer aboard Tegethoff). First wintering 1873-74; polar station established 1929, currently 2 operate.
<dd>Part of Arkangel'skaya Oblast, Russia.</dl>

<dl><b>Novaya Zemlya ('New Land')</b> 
<dd>70&#183;67&#176;-77&#176;N : 51-69&#176;E 
[78&#183;52-85&#183;55&#176;N : 56&#183;67-76&#183;67&#176;E] <i>(*526)</i>
<dd>Two main islands, several small ones.
<dd>Area: 81 280 km2. Highest elevation: 1547 m. 27% glacierized.
<dd>Possibly sighted 1032, known to Russian sealers from 13th century.
First wintering 1596-97; polar station established 1900, currently 4 operate.
<dd>Part of Arkangel'skaya Oblast, Russia.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Vize (Vize Island)</b> 
<dd>79&#183;50&#176;N : 76&#183;70&#176;E 
[88&#183;33&#176;N : 85&#183;22&#176;E] 
<dd>One isolated island.
<dd>Area: 289 km2. Highest elevation: 30 m. Unglacierized.
<dd>Discovered 1930 by a Soviet Union expedition (Vladimir Voronin aboard Sedov).
First wintering 1945-46; polar station, established 1945, operates.
<dd>Part of Taymyrskiy A.O., Russia.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Ushakova (Ushakov Island)</b> 
<dd>79&#183;42&#176;N : 80&#183;80&#176;E 
[88&#183;24&#176;N : 89&#183;78&#176;E] 
<dd>One isolated island.
<dd>Area: 328 km2. Highest elevation: 294 m. 45% glacierized.
<dd>Discovered 1935 by a Soviet Union expedition (Georgiy Ushakov aboard Sadko).
First wintering 1954-55; polar station, established 1954, operates.
<dd>Part of Taymyrskiy A.O., Russia.</dl>

<dl><b>Severnaya Zemlya ('Northern Land')</b> 
<dd>77&#183;83-81&#183;25&#176;N : 
90-108&#176;E [86&#183;48-90&#183;28&#176;N : 100&#183;00-120&#183;00&#176;E] 
<i>(*523)</i>
<dd>Four main islands and several smaller ones.
<dd>Area: 36 766 km2. Highest elevation: 965 m. 42% glacierized.
<dd>Discovered 1913 by a Russian expedition (Boris Vilkitskiy aboard Vaigach).
First wintering 1930-31; polar station established 1943, currently 2 operate.
<dd>Part of Taymyrskiy A.O., Russia.</dl>

<dl><b>Novosibirskiye Ostrova (New Siberian Islands)</b> 
<dd>73-77&#176;N : 
133-158&#183;50&#176;E [81&#183;11-85&#183;56&#176;N : 
147&#183;78-176&#183;11&#176;E] <i>(*522)</i>
<dd>Three groups of islands [Ostrova Lyakhovskiye (southern - 3x), Ostrova Anzhu 
(main - 3x), <a href="delong.htm">Ostrova De-Longa</a> (minor - 5x)], with some small offliers.
<dd>Area: 35 797 km2. Highest elevation: 426 m. Ostrova De-Longa 50% glacierized, 
remainder unglacierized.
<dd>Southern group discovered 1711 by a Russian expedition (Merkuriy Vagin), main 
group 1773 by Russian mercantile voyage (Ivan Lyakhov); remainder 1881 (United States 
expedition) and 1913 (Russian expedition). First wintering 1901-02; polar station 
established 1928, currently 2 operate.
<dd>Part of Yakutskaya-Sakha S.S.R., Russia. National Park since 1992.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Vrangelya (Wrangel Island)</b> 
<dd>71&#183;33&#176;N : 
178&#176;E-177&#176;W [79&#183;26&#176;N : 197&#183;78-203&#183;33&#176;E] 
<i>(*521)</i>
<dd>One isolated island.
<dd>Area: 7940 km2. Highest elevation: 1097 m. Unglacierized.
<dd>Existence surmised 1824 (Russian expedition), sighted 1849 (British expedition), 
probable first landing 1866 (German expedition). First wintering 1912-13; polar station 
established 1926, currently 3 operate.
<dd>Part of Chukotskiy A.O., Russia. National Park since 1976.</dl>

<dl><b>Ostrov Geral'd (Herald Island)</b> 
<dd>71&#183;38&#176;N : 
175&#183;67&#176;W [79&#183;3&#176;N : 204&#183;8&#176;E]
<dd>One small isolated island (65 km E of O. Vrangelya).
<dd>Area: 11 km2. Highest elevation: 372 m. Unglacierized.
<dd>Discovered 1849 by a British expedition (Henry Kellett aboard Herald). No wintering 
recorded, unoccupied.
<dd>Part of Chukotskiy A.O., Russia. National Park since 1976.</dl>
