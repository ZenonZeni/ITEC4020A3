<P>When investors talk about bonds, they are generally referring
to the generic class of fixed-income instruments, which include
not only bonds that technically are debt securities with maturities
of 10 years or more, but also short-term debt or money market
instruments, such as 90-day CDs and U.S. Treasury bills.  What
they all have in common is a fixed life or maturity, whether it's
30 days or 30 years.
<P>
Although bonds may not be as glamorous as stocks and make headlines
in your local newspaper, Americans have more money invested in
bonds than in stocks, mutual funds or other types of securities.
