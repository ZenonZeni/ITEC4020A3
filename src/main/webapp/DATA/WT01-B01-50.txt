<B>EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR<BR>
<!-- Horizontal Line Tag --><HR NOSHADE>
<!-- MailTo Tag --><A HREF="MAILTO:orme@cpj.org">William A. Orme, Jr.</A></B><BR>
Before joining CPJ in 1993, Orme covered Latin America as a journalist for 15 years. He reported from Mexico and Central America for <I>The Washington Post</I>, <I>The Economist</I>, <I>The Financial Times</I> and other publications. He was the founding editor of <I>Latin Finance</I>, a Miami-based regional business monthly. He has also taught post- graduate courses at the University of Southern California International Journalism program. Orme is a frequent radio and television commentator on press freedom issues and Latin American economic and political affairs. His book on Mexico and Nafta,<I> Continental Shift</I>, was published in 1993 by The Washington Post Company. Orme is fluent in Spanish. He has a bachelor&#146;s degree in international affairs from Friends World College.<BR>

<B>Africa</B><p>
<!-- MailTo Tag --><A HREF="MAILTO:africa@cpj.org">Kakuna Kerina</A><BR>
<I>Program Coordinator</I></B><BR>

Since joining CPJ as its Africa program coordinator in 1995, Kerina has launched two media campaigns to draw international attention to the plight of independent journalists in Nigeria and Zambia who are targets of harassment by government leaders intent on silencing opposition. She has also been leading efforts to free imprisoned Nigerian editor Nosa Igiebor and to aid Zambian editor Fred M&#146;membe , who faces up to 100 years in prison if convicted on charges leveled against him for his reporting. An editor, author and award-winning documentary filmmaker, Kerina has lived and studied in Ghana and Botswana and traveled throughout Africa. She speaks French and has a bachelor&#146;s degree in sociology and anthropology from Amherst College.<BR>

(212) 465-9344 x103<B><P>
<B>The Americas<p></b>
<!-- MailTo Tag --><A HREF="MAILTO:americas@cpj.org">Suzanne Bilello</A><BR>
<I>Program Coordinator</I></B><BR>
As CPJ&#146;s Americas program coordinator, Bilello is working to help foster a free press in Cuba and to call international attention to the Castro government&#146;s continued harassment of independent journalists. She is also focusing on press freedom issues in the rest of the region. Before joining CPJ in February 1996, Bilello had written about Latin America for several years, most recently as a business reporter for <I>Newsday</I>. In 1988, she received an Alicia Patterson fellowship to write about Mexico&#146;s economic and political crisis. She is fluent in Spanish and is a graduate of Barnard College and the Columbia Graduate School of Journalism.<BR>

(212) 465-9344 x104<BR>

<B>Asia</B><p>
<!-- MailTo Tag --><A HREF="MAILTO:asia@cpj.org"><B>Vikram Parekh</B></A><BR>
<I><B>Program Coordinator</I></B><BR>
Parekh joined CPJ as its Asia program coordinator in 1993. In recent months, he has  spearheaded a campaign to free imprisoned journalist Ahmad Taufik, the leader of Indonesia&#146;s only independent journalists union, and has conducted a fact-finding mission to the Indian-held state of Kashmir. Based on that trip, Parekh authored the report<I> On A Razor&#146;s Edge: Local Journalists Targeted by Warring Parties in Kashmir</I>. Before coming to CPJ, Parekh earned a J.D. from Rutgers University Law School where he specialized in international human rights law. He was also a 1992 Ford Foundation Fellow at the International Center for Law in Development in New York and held an internship with the International Human Rights Law Group in Washington, D.C. He speaks fluent Gujarati and has a bachelor&#146;s degree from the University of California at Santa Cruz.<BR>

<B>Central Europe and the Republics of the former Soviet Union<p>
<!-- MailTo Tag --><A HREF="MAILTO:europe@cpj.org">Catherine Fitzpatrick</A><BR>
<I>Program Coordinator</B></I><BR>
As CPJ&#146;s program coordinator for Central Europe and the republics of the former Soviet Union, Fitzpatrick is working with Russian journalists to develop independent press monitoring groups in Russia and is also focusing on press freedom issues in the Balkans and Central Asia. Fitzpatrick has a BA in Russian Language and Literature from the University of Toronto.Before coming to CPJ in 1996, Fitzpatrick worked as a consultant on the development of civil societies and human rights programs in the former Soviet Union with the Soros Open Society Foundation, the Soros International Science Foundation, the Ford Foundation, the Human Rights Project Group and the Mott Foundation. She also directed research for nine years at the former Soviet Union department of Helsinki Watch&#146;s New York office.  She has worked as a Russian interpreter and has translated several books into English, including ones by Boris Yeltsin, Eduard Shevardnadze and Yegor Ligachev. <BR>
