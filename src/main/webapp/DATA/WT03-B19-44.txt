<UL> <H1>News Briefs</H1>

<LI>Feburary 27, 1995<BR>
SeaSpace makes No. 15 on List of the Largest Telecommunications Manufacturers by the <I>San Diego Business Journal</I>.  Among the list were TV/COM International (#1) and Qualcomm, Inc. (#3).
<BR><BR>

<LI>March 1994<BR>
NASA AMES Research Center took both its TeraScan Polar Geostationary systems to Christchurch, New Zealand, 
to support a 6-month duration Antarctic ozone depletion experiment.  The experiment involves high-altitude ER-2 aircraft flights over the Antarctic.
<BR><BR>

<LI>The U.S. National Ice Center in Suitland, Maryland, is using its specialized TeraScan system to acquire and process full global coverage Defense Meteorological Satellite Program (DMSP) data.
<BR><BR>

<LI>July 1994<BR>
The U.S. Coast Guard ice breaker Polar Sea, under the command of Captain Lawson Brigham, embarked on a two-month expedition to the Arctic in mid-July with plans to reach the North Pole.
Aboard are two Scripps Insititution of Oceanography scientissts, Dan Lubin and Robert Whritner.  The vessel carries a TeraScan NOAA/DMSP acquisition and processing system.
<BR><BR>

</UL>
