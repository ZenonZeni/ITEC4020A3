<h2>Amorphous Silicon</h2>
<p><strong>Cell Design</strong>

<p><p>Because of amorphous silicon's unique properties, the cells are designed to have an ultrathin
(0.008-micron) <a href="ptype.html">p-type</a> top layer; a thicker (0.5- to 1-micron) intrinsic
(middle) layer; and a very thin (0.02-micron) n-type bottom layer.  The top layer is made so thin
and relatively transparent that most incident light passes right through it, to generate free
electrons in the intrinsic layer.  The p and n layers (produced by doping amorphous silicon)
create an <a href="ptype.html">electric field</a> across the entire intrinsic region to induce electron
movement in that layer.

<p><img border=0 align=left hspace=15 vspace=3 src="images/amorphous2.gif">
<font color="#F79D00">The typical amorphous silicon cell employs a p-i-n design, in
which an intrinsic layer is sandwiched between a p layer and an n layer.</font>
