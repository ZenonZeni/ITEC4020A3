<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<h4 align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Suicide</b></font></h4>
<hr>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Recognize the Signs </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - change in personality/sad, withdrawn, irritable, anxious, tired, indecisive, 
apathetic </p>
<p>2 - change in behavior/inability to concentrate </p>
<p>3 - change in sleep pattern/oversleeping, insomnia, early waking </p>
<p>4 - change in eating habits/loss of appetite &amp; weight, increased appetite &amp; 
weight </p>
<p>5 - loss of interest in friends, sex, hobbies, activities previously enjoyed </p>
<p>6 - worry over money, illness, etc... (either real or imagined) </p>
<p>7 - fear of losing control, going crazy, harming self or others </p>
<p>8 - feeling helpless, worthless, like nobody cares, like everyone would be better 
off without you </p>
<p>9 - feelings of overwhelming guilt, shame, self-hatred </p>
<p>10 - lack of hope for the future/feeling like things will always be bad </p>
<p>11 - drug or alcohol abuse </p>
<p>12 - recent loss/death, divorce, separation, broken relationship, employment, 
money, status, self-confidence, self-esteem, religious faith </p>
<p>13 - nightmares </p>
<p>14 - suicidal impulses, statements, plans, gestures </p>
<p>15 - giving away favorite things </p>
<p>16 - agitation, hyperactivity, restlessness </p>
<p>17 - casual statements/&quot;You'll be sorry when I'm dead.&quot;, &quot;I can't see any way 
out.&quot; </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Communication </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - the best way to know if someone is suicidal is to ask them </p>
<p>2 - do not fear asking if someone ever feels so bad that they contemplate suicide </p>
<p>3 - open communication, without showing shock or disapproval, may provide 
relief for the depressed person </p>
<p>4 - raising the question of suicide shows you are taking the person seriously &amp; 
responding to his/her distress </p>
<p>5 - if the person admits to contemplating suicide, ask more questions </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. how will you do it? </p>
<p>b. do you have the means? </p>
<p>c. when will you do it? </p>
<p>d. have you ever tried to do it before? </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Common misconceptions/Responses </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - people won't really commit suicide </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. this is not true </p>
<p>b. people who attempt or commit suicide have usually given some 
warning </p>
<p>c. do not ignore suicide threats no matter how casual they seem </p>
<p>2 - anyone who attempts suicide must be crazy </p>
<p>a. this is not true </p>
<p>b. most suicidal people are not psychotic or insane </p>
<p>c. suicidal people are upset, grief-stricken, depressed, or despairing </p>
<p>d. extreme distress &amp; emotional pain are not necessarily signs of 
mental illness </p>
</blockquote>
<p>3 - if someone is determined to commit suicide, he/she cannot be prevented </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. this is not true </p>
<p>b. even the most severely depressed person has mixed feelings about 
death </p>
<p>c. people waver until the vary last moment between wanting to live 
or die </p>
<p>d. most suicidal people do not want death/they only want their pain 
to stop </p>
<p>e. the impulse to die, however strong, does not last forever </p>
</blockquote>
<p>4 - people who commit suicide were unwilling to seek help </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. this is not true </p>
<p>b. studies show that more than half of suicide victims seek medical 
help within six months before their deaths </p>
</blockquote>
<p>5 - talking about suicide may give someone the idea </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. this is not true </p>
<p>b. however fleeting, most people contemplate suicide at some point </p>
<p>c. you can't instill morbid ideas merely by discussing the subject of 
suicide </p>
<p>d. the opposite is actually true/talking openly about suicide is very 
helpful </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Assessing the degree of risk </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - Low Risk of Suicide </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. mild anxiety </p>
<p>b. mild depression </p>
<p>c. no withdrawal </p>
<p>d. fairly good daily functioning/good at most activities </p>
<p>e. several resources </p>
<p>f. generally constructive coping strategies </p>
<p>g. several available significant others </p>
<p>h. no psychiatric help in past </p>
<p>I. positive attitude toward psychiatric help </p>
<p>j. stable lifestyle </p>
<p>k. infrequent excessive use of alcohol or drugs </p>
<p>l. no previous suicide attempts or low lethality suicide attempts in 
past </p>
<p>m. no disorientation or disorganization </p>
<p>n. little or no hostility </p>
<p>o. vague, fleeting thoughts of suicide/no actual plan </p>
</blockquote>
<p>2 - Moderate Risk of Suicide </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. moderate anxiety </p>
<p>b. moderate depression </p>
<p>c. some feelings of helplessness &amp; hopelessness </p>
<p>d. some withdrawal </p>
<p>e. moderately good daily functioning/good in some activities </p>
<p>f. some resources </p>
<p>g. some constructive coping strategies </p>
<p>h. few available significant others </p>
<p>I. moderately satisfied with psychiatric help in past </p>
<p>j. moderately stable or unstable lifestyle </p>
<p>k. frequent excessive use of alcohol or drugs </p>
<p>l. none or possibly one prior suicide attempt of moderate lethality </p>
<p>m. some disorientation or disorganization </p>
<p>n. some hostility </p>
<p>o. frequent thoughts of &amp; occasional planning of suicide </p>
</blockquote>
<p>3 - High Risk of Suicide </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. high anxiety or panic state </p>
<p>b. severe depression </p>
<p>c. feelings of hopelessness &amp; helplessness </p>
<p>d. withdrawn &amp; self-deprecating </p>
<p>e. poor daily functioning/not good in any activities </p>
<p>f. few or no resources </p>
<p>g. predominantly destructive coping strategies </p>
<p>h. one or no significant available others </p>
<p>I. negative view of psychiatric help received in past </p>
<p>j. unstable lifestyle </p>
<p>k. continual drug or alcohol abuse </p>
<p>l. none to multiple suicidal attempts of high lethality </p>
<p>m. marked disorientation or disorganization </p>
<p>n. marked hostility </p>
<p>o. frequent or constant thoughts of suicide </p>
<p>p. a specific suicide plan </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">How to find out if someone is seriously suicidal (Questions to ask in order) </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - Have you been feeling sad or unhappy? </p>
<p>2 - Do you ever feel hopeless, as if things can never get better? </p>
<p>3 - Do you have thoughts of death or ever think you'd be better off dead? (People 
may often feel this way, but that doesn't mean they'd kill themselves.) </p>
<p>4 - Do you ever have any actual suicidal impulses, urges to kill yourself? </p>
<p>5 - Do you have any actual plans to kill yourself? What methods have you 
chosen? Do you have the means to carry out your plan? How EXACTLY will 
you do it? </p>
<p>6 - When do you plan to commit suicide? </p>
<p>7 - Is there anything that would hold you back? Your family? Religion? Friends? </p>
<p>8 - Have you ever attempted suicide before? </p>
<p>9 - Would you be willing to talk to someone or to seek help if you feel 
desperate? Whom would you be willing to talk to?</p>
</blockquote>
