<H3>The 1997 J. E. Wallace Wallin Award</H3>


<STRONG>Deadline:</STRONG> Nominations must be postmarked by November 15, 1996.<P>

<STRONG>Background:</STRONG> This award was established in 1963 by the Board of Governors to honor a professional who has made outstanding contributions to the education of children and youth with exceptionalities. The award was named for and begun with the assistance of Dr. Wallin, a pioneer in the development of clinical psychology and special education in the United States.<P>

<STRONG>Candidate:</STRONG> All CEC members are eligible for the award as are Canadian and U.S. citizens who are not members of CEC. (Posthumous nominations are not accepted.)
<P>

The award honors an individual who has made a significant contribution to the education of children and youth with exceptionalities.  This person will have a prominent reputation nationally or internationally in one or more of the following areas:  publications; research; development of new concepts, approaches, or programs; new techniques for diagnosis or rehabilitation; improved psychological or educational evaluation procedures; improved administrative procedures; practical application of improved teaching devices; and/or dynamic leadership.
<P>
<STRONG>Nominator:</STRONG> Nominations can be accepted from individuals or CEC units.<P>

Print out nomination form below, or request forms and information sheets from CEC Headquarters: Office of the Executive Director, 1920 Association Drive, Reston, VA  20191-1589.
