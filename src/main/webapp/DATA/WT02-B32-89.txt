Dr Asenjo is currently working at the University of Chile.

<hr>
<p>
<b>Research Interests</b><br>
Aqueous two-phase systems for the separation and purification of proteins,
factors which enhance selectivity; reversed micelles; rational design of large
scale protein purification sequences; use of modern computer methods including
expert systems  and "hybrid" systems which use heuristics, data banks and
mathematical modelling; selective release of proteins from different cell
locations; use of lytic enzymes; modelling of biochemical
engineering/separation processes; regulation and synthesis of biopolymers and
enzymes in bacteria; fed batch culture for maximizing enzyme synthesis.<p>
<p>
<b>Teaching Acitivities</b><br>
Biochemical engineering: introductory and advanced topics; enzyme
engineering; kinetics and reactor design; mathematical modelling; separations
and downstream processing; process design in biotechnology. <p>
