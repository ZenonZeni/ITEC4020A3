<h3><font color="#800000" face="Arial">Joint Command For
Brazilian And Argentine Troops In A Near Future</font></h3>

<p><font face="Arial"><strong>&nbsp;BRASILIA. (MP).-</strong> In
not a too distant future Argentine and Brazilian troops could be
operating under an only joint command since both countries do not
consider each other &quot;<em>conflict hypothesis</em>&quot; and
now have common strategic targets.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">&quot;<em>Economic integration will
necessarily lead to a defence integration and the first steps are
self evident</em>&quot;, said Argentine Foreign UnderSecretary
Andrés Cisneros, following Brazil's official announcement of a
new defence policy which will be geared to the protection of the
Amazon, and to combat the drug trade.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">For the first time since the end of the
Triple Alliance war against Paraguay, in the second half of last
century, Argentine and Brazilian troops participated during
October in the &quot;<em>Southern Cross</em>&quot; joint military
exercises in the province of Corrientes to the north of
Argentina.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">For years now Argentine Navy jets take off
and land from the Brazilian aircraft carrier &quot;<em>Minas
Gerais</em>&quot;, since the &quot;<em>25 de Mayo</em>&quot; has
been practically out of action since 1982. </font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">&quot;<em>After a century and a half of
independence, our identity is not based on underlining
differences but common grounds; regional integration is above
nationalism</em>&quot;, said Andrés Cisneros.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">&quot;<em>There are practically no
differences between the Brazilian and Argentine defence policies</em>&quot;.</font></p>
