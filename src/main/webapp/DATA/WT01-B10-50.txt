<h2 align=center>Atlanta Reproductive Health Centre Photo Gallery<hr></h2>
<A HREF="ovind.jpg"><IMG ALIGN=center IMG SRC="ovind.gif"></A> <br>Injections of Lupron, Metrodin, Pergonal or Humegon allow the ovaries to develop many follicles as seen in this ultraound immediately prior to egg retrieval.<hr><p>
<A HREF="twopn.jpg"><IMG ALIGN=center IMG SRC="twopn.gif"></A> <br>Immediately after fertilization, the egg contains two pronuclei. Each contains half the chromosomal material from the one of the genetic parents.<hr><p>
<A HREF="four.jpg"><IMG ALIGN=center IMG SRC="four.gif"></A> <br>A four cell pre-embryo.  Each of the cells is called a blastomere. The embryo is surrounded by a protein matrix "shell" called the zona pellucida.<hr><p>
<A HREF="eight.jpg"><IMG ALIGN=center IMG SRC="eight.gif"></A> <br>On the third day after egg retrieval eight cell pre-embryos can be tranferred to the uterus.  On average, 15% of embryos such as this will implant after being transferred.<hr><p>
<A HREF="hatch.jpg"><IMG ALIGN=center IMG SRC="hatch.gif"></A> <br>Prior to implantation, the pre-embryo, now at the blastocyst stage, must hatch out of the zona pellucida prior to implantation.<hr><p>
