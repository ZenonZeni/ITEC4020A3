<H1><STRONG>Interesting Web Sites</STRONG></H1>
<IMG SRC="gif/line_blu.gif"><BR>
</CENTER>

<P>
To say that there is alot of information on the web on mental illness is an understatement! Here are a few selections to get you started. 
</P>
<UL>
<LH><H4>Sites from the Alliance for the Mentally Ill</H4></LH>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.nami.org">The National Alliance for the Mentally Ill Home Page</A> where our site is located, contains alot  of information on mental illness from the family and consumer perspective as well as news about NAMI activities. <BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.schizophrenia.com/ami/index.html">The AMI/FAMI HOME Page</A> created by D.J. Jaffe, NAMI board member  from the Manhattan affiliate, has great information on many topics of interest to NAMI families, including descriptions of mental illnesses, how to advocate to change the commitment laws, family access to information, and a summary of E. Fuller Torrey's speech given at the NAMI convention. <BR> 
</UL>



<UL>
<LH><H4>A Sampling of Interesting Web Sites</H4></LH>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]">The <A HREF="http://www.sonic.net/nilp/disabled.html">National Institute of Life Planning for Persons with Disabilitiies</A>
helps families obtain the latest information on transition, life and person centered planning, government benefits, advocacy, guardianship, aging, housing, supported employment, etc.  This site has the ability for you to get your questions answered by experts via E-mail. It also has lots of links to other programs.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]">The <A HREF="http://www.mentalhealth.org">National Mental Health Service Knowledge Exchange network, or KEN</A> is produced by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Substance Abuse & Mental Health Services,  Adminstration Center for Mental Health Services.  It contains factsheets on many aspects of mental illness, references to further reading material, and its own FTP site, <A HREF="ftp://ftp.health.org/pub/ken">ftp://ftp.health.org/pub/ken</A> for downloading documents. <BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]">The <A HREF="http://www.cityscape.co.uk/users/ad88/psych.htm">Psychiatry Online</A> is the site of the International Journal of Psychiatry from the United Kingdom.  This site is a reference point for psychiatrists and others interested in mental illness and mental health. It is an online journal with special sections on schizophrenia, children and adolescents and much more.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.nimh.nih.gov/">National Institute of Mental Health</A> is the place to read many of the publications on mental illness produced by NIMH .  Also includes information on clinical studies seeking patients.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]">The <A HREF="http://www.aacap.org/web/aacap/">American Academy of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry</A> has fact sheets for families on over 50 psychiatric disorders affecting children and adolescents.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.mhsource.com/narsad.html">NARSAD</A>, the research foundation formed by NAMI and three other organizations in 1986.  NARSAD raises and distributes funds for scientific research into the causes, cures, treatment and prevention of severe mental illness.  There are interesting articles on some of the researchers and their work, as well as information on new medications. <BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.mentalhealth.com/">Internet Mental Health</A> from Canada, has excellent information including descriptions of mental disorders, medications, treatment, research, pamphlets of information and articles. <BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.centerwatch.com">CenterWatch</A> lists clinical trials by disease categories. This site also contains a listing of newly approved drug therapies.<BR>
</UL>
<UL>
<LH><H4>Lists of Sites on Mental Health and Mental Illness</H4></LH> Some of the links from these sites are very useful, some are provocative.  Note that some reflect views on mental health issues that differ from the NAMI perspective.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.nami.org/other.htm">Other Sites of Interest from NAMI</A> contains a broad selection of mental health sites in alphabetical order.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.yale.edu/vayale/internet.html">Yale Schizophrenia Biological Research Center</A> contains a well organized list of links to other mental health sites.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://www.mentalhealth.com/">Internet Mental Health</A> from Canada has an extensive list of mental health links.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://earth.execpc.com/~corbeau/">Depression Resources List</A> points to newsgroups on depression as well as many sites on depression, medications and mental illness in general.<BR>

<IMG SRC="gif/bul_bl_r.gif" ALT="[*]"><A HREF="http://uhs.bsd.uchicago.edu/~bhsiung/mental.html/">Univ. of Chicago's Dr. Bob</A> web page is oriented to professionals. It has links organized by institutions involved with mental health and mental illness, journals, and links to other resources on the web.<BR>



</UL>
