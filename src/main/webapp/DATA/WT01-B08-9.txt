<p><font size="3" face="Arial"><strong><img src="images/ombu.gif"
align="right" hspace="0" width="166" height="218"></strong></font><font
size="3" face="Arial"><strong>MONTEVIDEO. (MP).- The &quot;</strong><em><strong>ombú</strong></em><strong>&quot;,
the magnificent tree which grows and outstands solitarily in the
pampas and Southamerican grasslands, has been selected as the
symbol of cultural integration for Mercosur. </strong>The
Education and Culture Ministers of the four founding countries
agreed on the ombú sketch during a meeting in Uruguay and
decided it will be used to identify all cultural assets,
facilitating their circulation through Customs and Post Offices
of the different country members. The cultural logotype includes
two &quot;<em>ombú</em>&quot; outlines, one blue and red,
another green and yellow, which overlap, on a white background
and with the inscription Mercosur Cultural in black. The novelty
of the symbol is that when the two outlines are overlapped into a
single figure the inscription can become Mercosul, which is the
Portuguese translation of Mercosur. So the new cultural symbol
includes several integration factors such as the colour of the
banners and the two languages, Spanish and Portuguese. Ministers
also decided on an agenda of activities for the next semester
which will begin with a show of pictures from the world famous
Jesuit Missions and the first of a series of stamp editions
dedicated to outstanding artists of Mercosur. </font></p>
