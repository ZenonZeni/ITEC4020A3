&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;&#160;Madagascar, the fourth largest island in the world and a mini-continent in its own right, was christened "the naturalists' promised land" by French naturalist Philibert Commerson.  Madagascar separated from the coast of Africa over 65 million years ago and in its isolation has become a laboratory for evolution, giving birth to wildly strange creatures and plants.  Among the endless surprises we will encounter on this 19-day adventure is the most ancient of all living primates, the exotic lemur.  Madagascar is also home to over half of the world's chameleon species and boasts one of the richest floras in the world.  Two full days of the trip will be devoted entirely to exploring Madagascar's most celebrated reserve, the Berenty Reserve.  Here we can expect to find ring-tailed lemurs, famous for their complete lack of fear and gibbon-like sifakas only an arm's length from the camera lens.  Our Madagascar expedition is ideal for participants who possess a true spirit of adventure akin to that of the early explorers--it is the essence of adventure travel and the destination of a lifetime.
