<h3>  Background </h3>
My basic research in aqueous humor outflow and glaucoma was the subject
of my S.M. and Ph.D. thesis, and has been continued at the University of
Toronto.  We have a very effective collaboration underway with ophthalmologists
and bioengineers in the U.S.A.<p>
<h3> Synopsis </h3>
Glaucoma is an ocular disease in which the normal outflow of fluid from
the eye is impeded, causing the pressure within the eye to become abnormally
high.  We are studying the tissues responsible for this process, in an 
attempt to relate tissue structure and function to hydrodynamic resistance.<p>
