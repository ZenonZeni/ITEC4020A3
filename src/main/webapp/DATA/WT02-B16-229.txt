<h2>From Cells to Arrays</h2>
<p><strong>A Closer Look</strong>
<p><img border=0 align=left hspace=15 vspace=3 src="images/singcell.gif">
The <em>solar cell</em> is the basic building block of a PV system.  Individual
cells can vary in size from about 1 cm (1/2 in.) to about 10 cm (4 in.) across.  However, one cell
only produces 1-2 W, which isn't enough power for most applications. (Photo: Siemens Solar Industries).

<br clear=all> <br>

<p><img border=0 align=left hspace=15 vspace=3 src="images/01654.gif">
A <em>module</em> typically consists of several solar cells, although thin-film materials like
amorphous silicon and cadmium telluride can be made directly into modules, effectively
bypassing the solar cell.  These two silicon modules are rated at approximately 50 W each and
provide power for a street light using 12 V battery storage.  Modules such as these can also be
connected into arrays for more power.  (Photo: W. Gretz, NREL)

<br clear=all>
<p><img border=0 align=left hspace=15 vspace=3 src="images/01026.gif">  
<em>Arrays</em> are the large power producers and consist of several modules.  They can be
used to provide up to several megawatts of electric power. (Photo: Sacramento Municipal Utility
District).
