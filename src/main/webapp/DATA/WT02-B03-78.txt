<H1>The North Atlantic Current Project: Observational Studies</H1>

<P>
<HR></P>

<P>The North Atlantic Current is being studied by a group of observationalists
and modellers here at URI/GSO, with <A HREF="tom.html">Dr. Tom Rossby </A>and
<A HREF="http://seip.gso.uri.edu/LRot.html">Dr. Lew Rothstein </A>as co-principal
investigators. Among the observational studies are a <B><A HREF="floats.html">multi-year
RAFOS float study</A> </B>and a regional climatology of the Newfoundland
Basin. 
<HR></P>

<H2>A regional climatology of the Newfoundland Basin on neutral surfaces</H2>

<P>A climatological description of the North Alantic Current (NAC) region
has been developed from the historical hydrographic record as provided
by the National Oceanographic Data Center. The climatology is described
on specific volume anomaly surfaces which provide approximations to the
neutral surfaces upon which water parcels are free to move and mix laterally.
The median depth, temperature, salinity, potential density, potential vorticity,
and Montgomery potential (an exact stream function on specific volume anomaly
surfaces) are described on a 0.5 degree grid for the Newfoundland Basin.
This regional climatology presents a fine scale neutral surface view of
the waters of the Newfoundland Basin which allows a more detailed study
of the system than is afforded by other existing climatologies for the
North Atlantic. </P>

<H3><A HREF="/nac_clim.html">This climatology is now available on-line!</A>
</H3>

<P>Through the <A HREF="/nac_clim.html">NAC climatology page</A>, users
may gain access to the median climatological fields, their errors, streamfunctions,
decadal and seasonal subsamples, and more. The data are available in netCDF
and ASCII format as well as GIF89a images. </P>

<P>Questions or problems concerning this climatology should be addressed
to Dr. Ed Kearns <A HREF="mailto:edk@rafos.gso.uri.edu">at URI</A> or <A HREF="mailto:ekearns@tsc.ndbc.noaa.gov">at
the National Data Buoy Center</A>.
