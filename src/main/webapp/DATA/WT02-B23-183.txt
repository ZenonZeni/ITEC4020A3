<h1 align=center>
Friday<br>Table Talk for Parents</h1>

<h2 align=center>An Opportunity for Informal Discussion<br>on Topics of Interest to Parents</h2>

<h3>F31<br>
1:30 PM - 2:50 PM</h3>

<b>Coordinator: Ann Fleming</b>, LDA Past President, Lowesville, VA


<p><b>STOP the Obsession<br>
Imy F. Wax</b>, Deerfield, IL<br>
Methods of encouraging your children to become independent and let go. 

<p><b>Post High School Options: What Happens Next for the Student with a Learning Disability?<br>
Lois Meyer</b>, The Lab School, Washington, DC<br>
<b>Gail Goldman</b>, The Lab School, Washington, DC<br>
This discussion will focus on available college services and making career choices. 

<p><b>The ABC’s of IEPs<br>
Candace Cortiella</b>, Parent Advocate, Burke, VA<br>
The role and responsibility of parents in the IEP process. How to be an equal partner and negotiate your child’s needs. 

<p><b>LDA of Illinois: Where Parents Can Go for Help<br>
Mary Cotter</b>, Former LDA Board Member, Lincolnwood, IL<br>
Where to find help for individuals with learning disabilities. 

<p><b>The Doctor’s Corner<br>
Warren Weinberg, M.D.</b>, UTSW Medical Center, Dallas, TX<br>
An opportunity to chat with a doctor who understands learning disabilities. 

<p><b>Home Schooling the Child with Learning Disabilities<br>
Nancy Musich</b>, LDA Lake County, Waukegan, IL

<p><b>Ann Kornblet</b>, Past President of LDA, St. Louis, MO<br>
Home and school working together for a consistent program for managing discipline. 

<p><b>Living with Learning Disabilities<br>
Harry Sylvester</b>, LDA Board Member, Albion, ME<br>
Harry Sylvester, an adult with severe learning disabilities, will share his life experiences from failure starting in the first grade to acceptance and success as an adult. Practical information will be given to help those without learning disabilities to
 understand what it means to have these hidden disabilities. 

<p><b>Homework<br>
Margaret Carr</b>, LDA Board Member, Ft. Worth, TX<br>
Helpful hints for alleviating those evening nightmares. 

<p><b>Mediation<br>
J. Gerald Minskoff</b>, James Madison University,
Harrisonburg, VA<br>
Mediation may be an effective alternative to Due Process, saving time and money, and expediting help for the student. 

<p><b>Interpreting Test Results<br>
Barbara Cordoni</b>, Southern Illinois University, Carbondale, IL<br>
Age level, grade level, percentile, stanine, standard score, standard deviation: what do they all mean and how do they relate to a student with learning disabilities?
