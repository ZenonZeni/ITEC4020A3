<h2 align=center>Asperger Syndrome and Nonverbal Learning Disabilities:
Imaging, Brain Diagnostic and Medical Findings</h2>

<p><b>Welcome:<br>
Audrey McMahon</b>, PAB, LDA Research Committee, Exeter, NH
<p><b>
Moderator:<br>
Kenneth Bonnet, Ph.D.</b>, New York University School of Medicine, New York, NY

<p><b>Presenters:<br>
Ami Klin, Ph.D.</b>, Yale University School of Medicine, New Haven, CT<br>
<b>Marie Bristol, Ph.D.</b>, NICHD, Bethesda, MD<br>
<b>Christopher Gillberg, M.D.</b>, University of Goteborg, Goteborg, Sweden<br>
<b>Mohammed Ghazziudin, M.D.</b>, University of Michigan School of Medicine, Ann Arbor, Ml<br>

<p>The rapidly developing work in the Yale-LDA collaborative project on
Asperger Syndrome will be described.
Asperger syndrome and high functioning autism have begun to be studied
with large scale, intensive programmatic efforts in neuropsychology,
genetics and brain imaging. The genetics, neuropsychology and functional
imaging results of Asperger syndrome are newly appearing in the medical
literature and have instructive implications for teaching, parenting and
professionally managing the growth, development and optimization of
Asperger syndrome and high function autism individuals.
