        <H2 ALIGN="CENTER"><CENTER>POTASSIUM AND SODIUM RATIOS
<BR>AND THEIR CONNECTIONS TO
<BR>YOUR GOOD HEALTH</CENTER></H2>

        We all know we're dealing with a food environment that is very different from the
one for which our body was designed.  Almost all whole foods start with potassium
outweighing sodium by a far larger ratio.  But after the processors, packagers, and
cooks have finished their work (not to mention what may be added at the table), this
relationship has been drastically altered, and in many cases reversed.


<P>     Without an ample supply of potassium in your plasma between your cells, your cell
membranes are restricted in their ability to remove sodium from the cell's interior. 
If sodium can't be removed, many processes don't run at full power, and the sodium
can back up in the cell like cars on a freeway on a rainy day.  It takes potassium
molecules on the outside to remove that excess sodium from the inside.

<P>See the problem?  Even haddock, a salt water fish, naturally 
     contains nearly two-anda-half times as much potassium as sodium.  
     But when turned into an easily-preserved tasty treat, the ratio 
     of the two minerals becomes almost four to one in the wrong direction.
