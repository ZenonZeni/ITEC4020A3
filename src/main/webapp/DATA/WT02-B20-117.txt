<H3>The 1997 CEC Research Award</H3>


<STRONG>Deadline:</STRONG> Nominations must be postmarked by November 15, 1996.<P>

<STRONG>Background:</STRONG>This award was established in 1989 by the Board of Governors to recognize an individual, group of individuals, institution, or agency whose research has contributed to the body of knowledge about the education of exceptional children and youth leading to the improvement of their education.<P>

<STRONG>Candidate(s):</STRONG> May be an individual, group of individuals, institution, or agency.

<P>


<STRONG>Nominator:</STRONG> Nominations can be accepted from individuals or CEC units.<P>

<STRONG>Award:</STRONG> The Professional Standards and Practice Committee has the authority to give one award each year or to deny granting the award in any given year.<P>


Print out nomination form below, or request forms and information sheets from CEC Headquarters: Office of the Executive Director, 1920 Association Drive, Reston, VA  20191-1589.

<H4>Information Needed to Complete Nomination</H4>

Please submit six copies of each of the following:<P>

1.	All information requested on the nomination form.<P>

2.	Vita of each nominee or description of the institution or agency nominated, including its purpose and history.
<P>
3.	Description of the program in research, including copies of the published works, relevant reviews, and articles.  Because this material cannot be returned, submit no more than five exemplars.
<P>
4.	Evidence of how the program of research has contributed to the education of exceptional children and youth, including:
<DL><DT>
	<DD>(a)	The extent to which the research has had a direct impact on practice, programs, or policy and a statement of how the research is impacting on the quality of education for exceptional children and youth.

	<DD>(b)	Three to five (maximum) letters of recommendation describing the importance and impact of the research on the delivery of programs and services to exceptional children and youth.

	<DD>(c)	Three to five (maximum) letters, each from an official at a different field-site, describing how the research has been used to positively enhance the education and/or development of exceptional children and youth, improve programs and/or services, enhance assessment practices, develop other research programs which have grown from the original research, impact policy, and/or affect the preparation of personnel.

	<DD>(d)	A listing of literature citations of the research (publications, technical reports, conference citations, presentations, media productions, etc.) to demonstrate the usage of the research findings by others in the field.
</DT></DL>

<STRONG>Please submit six copies of all requested materials in one complete package</STRONG>.<P>
