<H2 ALIGN=CENTER>Successful Research on the<BR>
Efficacy of Cooperative Learning</H2>
<FONT SIZE=-1>Developed by <A HREF="../Students#LR">Luz Rodriguez</A></FONT><BR>
<HR> 
<A HREF="CoopRef#Hauserman">Hauserman (1992)</A> states that numerous studies have illustrated the academic and affective benefits of cooperative learning techniques, elevating levels of student achievement, providing the greatest gains for low and middle achievers, <A HREF="../Inclusion/Inc.home">minority groups, and handicapped students</A>.  Likewise, high achieving students have performed equally well or better in cooperative learning situations in comparison to students in control groups that have used individualzed methods.  Overall, research has indicated that there is significant group-to-individual transfer, allowing for greater levels of achievement in science, computer classes, mathematics, reading comprehension, and higher quality cognitive reasoning strategies.<BR>
<BR>
The following are some of the effective research-based cooperative learning techniques:<BR>
<UL>
<LI> Student teams-achievement divisions, or STAD
<LI> Teams-games-tournment, or TGT
<LI> Team assisted individualization, or TAI
<LI> Learning together and along: Cooperation, competition, and individualization
<LI> Group Investigation Method
<LI> Jigsaw II
</UL>
