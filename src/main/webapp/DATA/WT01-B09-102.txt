<h3>Electricore</h3><p>

<blockquote>
<B>Project Title:  5­Phase Synchronous Reluctance Motor <p>

Performing Organization:  Delphi Energy & Engine Management Systems, Delco Propulsion Systems</B><p>

<P>Technical POC: Ahmed El­Antably, Delphi­E, (317) 579­3696 </P>

<P><B>Project Summary:</B></P>

<P>The 5­phase synchronous reluctance motor­system is an out­growth of an on­going
synchronous reluctance motor system development program. It will have improved performance over the 3­phase
synchronous reluctance motor system in all six categories listed below. It is predicted that this machine will have
wide applications for many military and commercial applications under 40,000 pounds GVW.</P>

<P><UL>
<LI>     higher efficiency and high power density (10%­20%) 
<LI>     lower rotor losses, permitting simpler cooling system 
<LI>     better utilization of silicon, allowing lower rated devices 
<LI>     simpler to control, can use a cheaper controller than the induction motor 
<LI>     can be operated without sensors, this increases the reliability and reduces cost 
<LI>     provides higher reliability than 3­phase induction or synchronous reluctance machines 
</UL></P>
<P>This new design will achieve superior efficiency due to better iron and copper utilization and enhanced magnetic
characteristics of the rotor by maximizing the rotor saliency ratio.</P>

<P><B>Project Deliverables: </B></P><P>Final report based on development and test results of 5­phase synchronous reluctance
motor on a dynamometer.
<P></P>
<B>Project Schedule:</B> </P>

<P><UL>
<LI>Two year project beginning February 1, 1996.</UL></P> 


</blockquote>
