<BODY  background="recipe.jpg"><p><FONT COLOR="#8A6CF5"><CENTER><BIG><B>Tabbouli Stuffed Mushrooms</B></BIG></CENTER></FONT><hr>
<p> 1 dozen large stuffing mushrooms<br>1 cup Italian seasoned breadcrumbs<br>2 tbs. olive oil<br>1 large onion<br>3 tbs. fresh basil<br>1/2 cup prepared tabbouli<br>6 cloves garlic<br>1/2 cup white wine<br>1 vegetarian vegetable boullion cube<br>grated locatelli<p>Remove stems from mushrooms & finely chop<br>Chop onion<br>Heat pan<br>Sautee garlic and onions over medium until translucent (about 10 minutes)<br>Add mushroom stems, boullion and fresh basil continue to saute until the mushrooms have cooked through about 5 minutes<br>If mushrooms and onions stick, deglaze the pan with the white wine<br>Lower heat to low<br>Add remaining wine<br>Add prepared tabouli<br>Mix well<br>Add breadcrumbs slowly until the mixture begins to stiffen and resembles Thanksgiving stuffing (you may not need to add a full cup)<br>Stuff the mushroom caps with the filling, sprinkle with locatelli cheese and bake at 350 degrees for 15-20 minutes.<p>Portabello mushrooms work wonderfully with this recipe and can be served as a main course with a salad. My measurements are never exact, so play with the recipe a bit!  Enjoy.
