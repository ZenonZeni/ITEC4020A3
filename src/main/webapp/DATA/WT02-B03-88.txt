<H1>North Atlantic Current Project: RAFOS Float Studies</H1>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<P>The objective of the RAFOS float program, jointly supported by the <A HREF="http://www.onr.navy.mil/">Office
of Naval Research </A>and the <A HREF="http://www.nsf.gov/">National Science
Foundation</A>, was to study the structure of the currents in the North
Atlantic Currrent (NAC) region and the exchange of waters between the subtropical
and subpolar gyres in the Newfoundland Basin. One hundred <A HREF="Gifs/bigtom_2.gif">RAFOS
floats </A>were deployed on two density surfaces corresponding to sigma-theta
= 27.2 and 27.5, respectively. All floats were designed to cycle once or
twice a day to density surfaces 0.1 sigma-t units above and below their
nominal level to determine changes in stratification and temperature along
the trajectories. Three separate float deployments took place: July-August
1993, November-December 1993 and October-November 1994. Most float missions
had a duration of ten months, with the final floats surfacing in the summer
of 1995. At the end of its mission, each float releases its ballast weight,
returns to the surface, and telemeters data to <A HREF="http://www.argosinc.com/">Argos</A>,
a French satellite-based data collection and platform location system.
Once all the data are downloaded and processed, the trajectory of the float
can be reconstructed from the time series of acoustic travel times. The
pressure and temperature time series are also included in the telemetered
data. The floats were tracked using <A HREF="Gifs/sources.gif">four moored
sound sources </A>developed by <A HREF="http://www.polarint.com/sprtnfcn.htm">Sparton
of Canada</A>, energized by power modules from Webb Research Corp. of East
Falmouth, Massachusetts.</P>

<P>We present hear a summary of the float trajectories on each density
surface. For additional information or a copy of our data report, please
contact<A HREF="mailto:tom@rafos.gso.uri.edu"> Dr. Tom Rossby</A> or <A HREF="mailto:sandy@yvette.gso.uri.edu">Ms.
Sandra Anderson-Fontana </A>at the <A HREF="http://hollywood/">Graduate
School of Oceanography</A>.</P>
