<h3 align="center"><a name="sites"><font color="#0000FF">Demolition</font></a><font
color="#0000FF"> Landfilling</font></h3>
<p><img src="images/LFSM.jpg" alt="landfill compaction"
align="right" hspace="10" width="300" height="99"><font
color="#0000FF">Minnesota's state-of-the-art landfill for
demolition and construction debris is <b>SKB Environmental</b>'s<b> </b>Rich
Valley Demolition Disposal Facility. Opened in 1989, the facility
is designed with a clay liner and leachate collection system to
protect the environment now and in the future, providing
environmental safeguards that ensure peace-of-mind for waste
generators. </font></p>
<p align="left"><font color="#0000FF"><strong>SKB Environmental</strong>
processes demolition and construction debris yearly from large
and small projects. Material deposited in the landfill is
visually inspected to ensure environmental integrity, and a
computerized data system tracks the origin of all debris. At our
landfill facility, C &amp; D waste is first sorted for
recyclables, and then the waste to be disposed of is placed and
compacted to proper density. The landfill is manned and equipped
to provide acceptable turnaround times which translates to cost
savings for the customer. </font></p>
<p align="center">&#160; </p>
<h4 align="center"><font color="#0000FF"><font size="4">State-of-the-Art
Construction:</font></font></h4>
<dir>
    <p align="center">
<li><font color="#0000FF">Two-foot thick
        clay liner </font></p>
    </li>
   <p align="center">
<li><font color="#0000FF">Leachate
        collection system </font></p>
    </li>
    <p align="center">
<li><font color="#0000FF">Upgradient and
        Downgradient ground water monitoring network </font></p>
    </li>
</dir>
<p align="left"><font color="#0000FF">The schematic below
illustrates the design for the liner and leachate collection
system installed at the Rich Valley Landfill. At the landfill
base, the compacted clay liner has a minimum thickness of two
feet and meets a permeability specification of 1 x 10<sup><font
size="1">-7</font></sup> cm/sec. The leachate collection system
is constructed of six inch PVC collection pipes (Schedule 80).
Above the liner and leachate collection pipes there is a three
foot thick protective cushion/drainge layer, whichconsists of
native sandy soils with minimum permeability of 5 x 10<sup><font
size="1">-3</font></sup> cm/sec. The clay liner intercepts
leachate as it percolates from the base of the waste deposit and
drainage layer. The upper surface of the clay liner is sloped so
that leachate flows by gravity to the collection pipes and then
to a lift station. The lift station is a central collection point
from which leachate is pumped to tanker trucks for discharge to a
local wastewater treatment facility. Because of the comparatively
uncontaminated nature of the leachate, SKB environmental is not
assessed a strength charge for leachate disposal. There are six
monitoring wells, screened in the unconsolidated glacial drift
aquifer underlying the facility, which are used to monitor
upgradient and downgradient ground water quality.</font></p>

        <h3 align="center"><a name="service"><font
        color="#0000FF">Transfer</font></a><font color="#0000FF">
        Station &amp; Recycling Center</font> </h3>
        <p><img src="images/TIRESSM.jpg"
        alt="tires for recycling" align="left" hspace="10"
        width="140" height="200"><font color="#0000FF">The Rich
        Valley Landfill Recycling Center accepts &quot;white
        goods&quot; and demolition and construction debris from a
        variety of sources, including businesses and homeowners
        through a transfer station. The station transfers
        construction waste to environmentally sound and cost
        effective disposal at the Rich Valley Landfill. Wood
        products, car tires, and white goods such as
        refrigerators, stoves, hot water heaters and washing
        machines are recycled.</font> </p>
        <p><font color="#0000FF">An innovative program to recycle
        shingle waste into asphalt roadbase is also conducted at
        the Recycling Center. <strong>SKB Environmental</strong>
        is working with a large-scale shingle manufacturer,
        Dakota County, and the State of Minnesota to evaluate
        optimal amounts of this waste material that can be used
        in state-certified roadbase mixes.</font> </p>
