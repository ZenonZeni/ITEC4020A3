Look closely at the action of past bond selloffs on the Government of Canada bonds of 2011. You can see that when the bond market corrects it will break below the trend-smoothing 30 week moving average. The bonds then start a strong recovery. That should be the way things go this time. We will use that as our Stop Out point in case I am being a little too quick off the mark.<p>In the US, the bond markets have been mixed in the past few weeks. The Fed. stance seems to be to avoid changing interest rates going into the Presidential Elections. This accommodates Mr. Clinton, who is also trying to ensure smooth sailing by pushing hard to get Democrat Fed. nominee's approved quickly. Some Fed. officials are getting a little concerned that rising wage demands may become an inflationary factor and that rising commodities prices will be more than a short term phenomenon. Overall economic factors remain positive. Productivity for US workers grew by 2.6% for the first quarter. America claims the most productive workers in the world based on these figures, and worker efficiency is an essential component to generating fast growth without increases in inflation. Unemployment is down to 5.5% and retail sales are slightly lower.<p>The CRB Index, our harbinger of consumer inflation got as high as 264.00 and has gone into a minor correction phase.
