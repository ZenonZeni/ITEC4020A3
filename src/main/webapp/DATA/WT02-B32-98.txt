<hr><p>
Dr. Rastall gained his BSc in Applied Biology at Thames Polytechnic (now
University of Greenwich) in 1984 and remained at that institute to research for
a PhD on the cell surface biochemistry of <i>Erwinia amylovora</i>. After
postdoctoral work on the use of glycosidases in the synthesis of
oligosaccharides at the University of Westminster, he joined the University of
Reading as a lecturer in biotechnology in 1993.<p>
<b>Research Interests</b><br>
Dr. Rastall's research interests are principally in the area of carbohydrate
bioengineering and bioremediation. He is the leader of the <A HREF="cbgindex.htm">Enzyme and Molecular Biotechnology Group</A> within the <A HREF="http://www.fst.reading.ac.uk/research/biotech/biores.htm">Biotechnology and Biochemical Engineering Group</A> in the department. 
<p>
<b>Teaching Acitivities</b><br>
Dr. Rastall is responsible for teaching protein structure, enzyme technology
and protein engineering at undergraduate level and enzyme technology at
postgraduate level. He has a keen interest in the development of
<a href="/courses/index.htm">computer-assisted learning</a> approaches as a means of enhancing teaching
quality.
