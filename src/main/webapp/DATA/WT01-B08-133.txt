<H2>Tips for Preparing New Lawns</H2>
<P>Whether using seed or sod, always begin by thoroughly preparing the soil.  For best results, incorporate 2-3 inches of compost or other organic matter into the soil.  Till to a depth of 6-8 inches.  Level area with rake or roller.</P>

Under any new lawn it is important to establish a basis of soil fertility.  To give the turf a gentle start, add <P><A HREF="product.htm">Whitney Farms Organic Lawn Food</A>  at a rate of 25 lbs. per 1000 feet.  Rake in lightly to a depth of 1-2 inches.</P>

<P>Keep the new lawn area moist throughout its first 3 weeks.  For newly seeded lawns, keep it moist until the grass is at least 2 inches tall.  For newly sodded lawns, ... keep turf area moist for at least 3 weeks.

Apply <P><A HREF="product.htm">Whitney Farms Organic Lawn Food</A> again 4-6 weeks after lawn is established.

<H2>Tips for a Healthy Lawn</H2>
<LI>Aerate lawn in spring and fall to help reduce thatch problems.
<LI>Add a 1/4 to 1/2 inch of finely screened organic matter annually to enhance soil life under turf.
<LI>Mow regularly.  To recycle nutrients (particularly nitrogen), leave lawn clippings whenever possible.
<LI>When you water, water deeply.  Most lawns require about an inch of water per week.  Avoid watering daily to prevent shallow root development.

<LI>In the spring, mow the lawn short to help stimulate the grass.  In the summer and
fall, allow your grass to grow higher.  This will help, shade the lawn and crowd out potential weeds.  In the late fall, lower the height again.
