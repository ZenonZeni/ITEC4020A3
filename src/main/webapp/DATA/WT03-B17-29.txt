<TITLE>Programs</title><center><B><H1>Rhodes College<BR>
Counseling and Student Development Center<BR>
List of Programs</H1></B></center>
<P>
<hr>
<p>
The Counseling and Student Development Center can provide programs on many topics related to wellness, student development, and mental health.  If you have a special interest, or would like to request a special program for your group, contact a staff member at 726-3849.  You can also e-mail a staff member using the link listed below.
<P>
Here is a list of programs being sponsored or co-sponsored by the Counseling and Student Development Center during the Spring 1996 semester.  Check back frequently; we think up new and exciting programs all of the time!<br> 

<P>
<hr>
<P>
<H3>August</H3><p>
<ul>
<li>23 - <b>New Student Orientation</b> - The Counseling Center Staff will assist with new student orientation.
</ul>
<p>
<h3>September</H3><p>
<ul>
<li>6 - <B>Friday at 3</B> - A student support group.  For more information and location of the group meetings, call ext. 3849.
<li>9 - <b>Activities Fair</b> - Come by the Counseling Center's table, sign up for <i>E-motion</i> (our electronic newsletter), and get a free prize!  Also, check out all of the cool information we will have. <b>4 p.m. in the Bellingrath Quad.</b>
<li>19 - <B>Study Skills Workshop:  Part 1</B>  Signup: In person or call the Counseling & Student Development Center (Room 310 Briggs; Ext. 3849; 8:30am-5pm, M-F) before Sept 12. The two-part workshop will cover:<br>
	- managing time at the college level,<br>
	- improving concentration,<br>
	- preventing procrastination,<br>
	- note taking and listening skills,<br>
	- test preparation, and/or<br>
	- test anxiety.<br>
	For students unable to attend one or both sessions, we offer individual
assistance by appointment with one of our counselors.  We also have numerous
types of self-help handouts students may look through and take with them.
<b>4-5pm, 201 Palmer Hall</b>
<li>20 - <b>Friday at 3</b> - See description for Sept. 6.
<li>21 - <b>Becoming a Confident Student</b> - This is the first in a series of student development workshops.  Students are welcome to come to one or all of the workshops!  This session will focus on college adjustment, study skills, social skills, College policies, and having fun while becoming academically successful. <b>1 p.m. in Clough 102.</b>  Registration is encouraged by calling ext. 3030.
<li>25 - <b>Booze News!</b> - Join members of the Rhodes student body, administration, and staff in a discussion about Rhodes' alcohol policy and drug policy. <B>8 p.m. in Buckman 108.</B>
<li>26 - <b>Study Skills Workshop:  Part 2</b> See description for part one.
</ul>
<p>
<h3>October</h3><p>
<ul>
<li>10 - <b>National Depression Screening Day</b> -  Self-administered assessments will be available in the refectory, the library, and in the Student Center.  Call the Counseling Center at ext. 3849 for more information.
<li>12 - <b>The Buzz about Substance Abuse</b> - A student development workshop focusing on attitudes toward and behaviors surrounding alcohol and other substance use.  Get the facts; test your knowledge and beliefs! <b>1 p.m. in Clough 102.</b>  Registration is required! Calling ext. 3030.
<li>14-18 - <b>Keeping the Good Times Alive!</b> - a celebration of Alcohol Awareness Week.  All week check out the cool info tables and bulletin boards in the refectory, Briggs lobby, and the library.
<li>14 - <b>Secret Parking Lot Campaign</b> - be watching for your ticket to alcohol facts and bonus stuff from the Bookstore!
<li>15 - <b>The Reality of Acquaintance Rape</B> - National speaker and sexual assault survivor Katie Koestner shares her experiences. 7:30 p.m. in Hardie.  Co-sponsored by the Counseling and Student Development Center, the Rhodes Activities Board, Pan, IFC, and the Dean of Student Affairs Office.
<li>16 - <b>D.U.I.:  Demonstrating and Understanding Intoxication</b> - Can Charlie Landreth, Assistant Dean of Student Affairs; Cindy Pennington, Director of Student Activities; Matt Dean, Director of the Campus Life Center; and Kari Duncan, Coordinator of Recreational Services pass a field sobriety test after having a few drinks?  Find out when the Memphis Police Department calls on these administrators after a happy hour!  Guest spots by students from SafeRides and other cool stuff, too.  A Whine (yes, whine) and cheese reception to follow so you can chat with your D.U.I. administrators!  Co-sponsored by the Counseling and Student Development Center, Residence Life, and Group.
<li>17 - <b>Make it a Safe Ride Coffee House</b> sponsored by Catalyst! - Come jam to the music, practice your safe driving unimpaired habits, and hang out with your friends. Place and time TBA.
</ul>
<p>
<h3>November</h3><p>
<ul>
<li>5 - <b>Study Skills Workshop</b> - Having troubles at mid-term? Need to enhance your memory retention, test-taking skills, note-taking skills or more?  This is the place for you!  <B>3:30 to 5 p.m., Palmer 205.</B>  Registration encouraged by calling ext. 3030.
<li>21 - <B>Friendship in the Age of AIDS</B> - Two fraternity men...they never thought that HIV and AIDS happened to people like them, until one became HIV positive.  Join national speakers T. J. Sullivan and Joel Goldman in an honest discussion about HIV and AIDS.  <b>8 p.m. in Blount Lecture Hall</B> Co-sponsored by the Counseling and Student Development Center, Moore Moore Student Health Center, the Rhodes Activities Board, Pan, and IFC.
<li>23 - <B>Beating the Blues:  Understanding Suicide and Depression</B> - A student development workshop designed to help students identify symptoms of depression and suicidal tendencies in friends, determine courses of action to help, and how to support themselves when friends are dealing with emotional problems. <B>1 p.m. in Clough 102.</B>
</ul>
