<h4 align="center">Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>

<p align="center"><font color="#000080"><b>Homeless &amp; Missing
Service</b></font></p>

<hr>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="3" face="Arial"><em><b>How to
report a missing person</b></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - call or fax the emergency hotline which is monitored 24
    hours a day, 7 days a week (912-328-3555)</p>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
    <p>2 - when you contact the Service, you will be asked to
    attach a photograph of the missing person to a completed and
    notarized application </p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="3" face="Arial"><em><strong>What
is the Homeless &amp; Missing Service for Persons with Mental
Illness?</strong></em></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - a NAMI program</p>
    <p>2 - a program designed to help locate mentally ill persons
    who are reported missing by families, loved ones, or friends</p>
    <p>3 - a program intended to provide assistance &amp; support
    to families &amp; consumers</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial"><b><i>How the Service works </i></b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - there is no charge for help, but donations are
    accepted</p>
    <p>2 - verifies the name, address, &amp; telephone number of
    the person requesting help</p>
    <p>3 - verifies the name, sex, &amp; general description of
    the missing person</p>
    <p>4 - determines where the person disappeared from &amp; how
    long s/he has been gone</p>
    <p>5 - asks the person requesting help to send copies of
    guardianship papers, court orders, or doctors&#146; letters
    (if any exist) attesting to the mental &amp; physical
    condition of the missing person</p>
    <p>6 - asks permission to obtain information about the
    missing person, to prepare &amp; distribute missing person
    flyers, &amp; to provide information for radio &amp;
    television broadcasts</p>
    <p>7 - either the national or state office will keep you
    informed of its progress</p>
    <p>8 - if it finds a missing person who is a danger to
    him/herself or to others, the Service will notify you immediately
    of the person&#146;s location &amp; condition, notify the
    local law enforcement office, or notify the mental health
    state agency.</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial"><b><i>What the Service
cannot do</i></b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - return the missing person home (it can only help to
    locate the person)</p>
    <p>2 - refer the missing person to a hospital</p>
    <p>3 - tell you where the missing person is if the person is
    at least 21 &amp; objects to notice of his/her whereabouts
    (it can inform you of the person&#146;s general condition)</p>
    <p>4 - commit a person to a medical facility</p>
    <p>5 - make any decisions about legal transactions</p>
</blockquote>
