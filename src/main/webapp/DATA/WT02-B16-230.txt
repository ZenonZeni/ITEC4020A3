<h2>Balance of System</h2>

<p><strong>Mounting Structures</strong>

<p>Photovoltaic arrays have to be mounted on some sort of stable, durable structure that can support
the array and withstand wind, rain, hail, and other adverse conditions.  Sometimes, the mounting
structure is designed to track the sun. 

<p>Stationary structures are usually used with flat-plate systems and generally tilt the PV array at a
fixed angle that is determined by the latitude of the site, the requirements of the load, and the
availability of the <a href="lightsun.html">sunshine</a>.  

<p><img border=0 align=left hspace=15 vspace=3 src="images/tracking.gif">
<font color="#F79D00">Among the choices for stationary mounting structures, rack
mounting may be the most versatile. It requires relatively simple construction
and hardware and can be installed easily on the ground or on flat or slanted
roofs.</font>
