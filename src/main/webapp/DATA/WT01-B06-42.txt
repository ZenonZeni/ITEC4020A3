<H2>AAII Product Listing</H2>
<P>
<STRONG>The AAII Journal</STRONG>
</P>
<P>
This 40-page publication is the primary benefit of AAII membership. It is sent
to all members 10 times a year. Topics covered include stocks, bonds, market
structure, portfolio strategies, and stock selection strategies. The Journal
also includes quarterly columns on financial planning, life insurance products,
mutual funds, investment newsletters, technical analysis and taxes. The focus is
on providing information and how-to articles on investing for individuals.
</P>
<P>
Members receive &quot;The Individual Investor's Guide to Low-Load Mutual
Funds&quot;
in March (see below for description), instead of the Journal, and in December
members are sent the annual Tax and Financial Planning Guide. AAII membership is
$49 a year.
</P>
<HR>
<P>
<STRONG>Computerized Investing</STRONG>
</P>
<P>To help you make the best use of your computer equipment for your finances
and investing, AAII publishes a 28-page bimonthly newsletter. Regular features
include:
</P>
<UL>
<LI>How-to articles (complete with programs) on using computers to
assist in investment analysis and valuation
</LI>
<LI>Instructions for setting up spreadsheet programs based on
investing
techniques described in AAII Journal articles
</LI>
<LI>Reviews of investment software and information services</LI>
<LI>Comparisons of similar investment-related products
</LI>
<LI>Summaries of new software packages, books, and databases</LI>
<LI>Information on AAII&#146;s on-line forum and descriptions of
investing-related Internet sites</LI>
<LI>Meeting notices for area AAII computer groups</LI>
</UL>
<P>AAII members can subscribe to Computerized Investing for $30/year or
non-members pay $40/year. Subscriptions include six issues and The Individual
Investor&#146;s Guide to Computerized Investing.
</P>
<HR>
<P>
<STRONG>Quarterly Low-Load Mutual Fund Update</STRONG> </P>
<P>
Every fund investor needs timely mutual fund data to compare fund performance.
Knowing how well your mutual funds are doing relative to funds with similar
investment objectives enables you to make well-informed decisions on your mutual
fund investments.
</P>
<P>
For only $24.00 a year, the Quarterly Low-Load Mutual Fund Update reports fund
performance by quarter over the last year and over the most recent three-year
and five-year periods. The difference between a fund's performance and that of
an average of funds of the same objective is given along with a risk index,
yield, and expense data. Coverage includes over 800 no-load and low-load mutual
funds. Lists of top-performing funds and the performance of major indexes make
your fund's performance easy to monitor and evaluate. The Quarterly Update will
also keep you abreast of fund developments such as a change in a portfolio
manager.
</P>
<P>
The Quarterly Update will be mailed at the beginning of each quarter (January,
April, July and October). The Quarterly Update is also available as a
computerized menu-driven program on diskette containing an expanded data set for
$39.00 a year (IBM, with at least 530K free RAM and 2MB hard drive storage
space, or Mac, requiring Mac Plus or higher with 2MB hard disk storage), making
screening and sorting fast and easy. The non-member price of the newsletter is
$30.00; $50.00 for the non-member computer version.</P>
<HR>
<P>
<STRONG>The Individual Investor's Guide to Low-Load Mutual Funds
</STRONG>
</P>
<P>
This widely acclaimed book annually examines over 800 no-load and low-load
mutual funds.
</P>
<P>
The Guide provides important investment information for all funds, including:
One-, three-, five-, and 10-year annual return; risk measures; bull and bear
market performance; beta for stock funds; average maturity for bond funds;
portfolio turnover; expense ratio; distributions and net asset value.
</P>
<P>
A primary feature of the Guide is its full-page, detailed reports on each fund.
In addition to 10 years of investment data, the Guide includes: Investment
objective; portfolio manager; major portfolio holdings; services; distribution
dates; exchange options; ticker; and minimum investments.
</P>
<P>
The Guide is mailed in March free to AAII members. Additional copies for AAII
members are $19 each. (The non-member price is $24.95.)</P>

software, the guide includes and overview of investing, an overview or

hardware.</P>
<P>The guide will be mailed free to Computerized Investing subscribers.
Additional copies are available for $19 (The non-member price is $24.95.)</P>

<P>
<STRONG>Investment Home Study Kit</STRONG>
</P>
<P>
Investment Home Study is a comprehensive course on investing designed for those
individuals who already understand the basics. It explores in depth the
concepts, strategies and analytical methods useful for successful investing and
portfolio management. The 10 lessons include:
</P>
<UL>
<LI>Lesson I: Concepts of Investment Risk and Return--Measures of risk and
return; the impact of diversification.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson II: Investment Alternatives--Risk considerations and analysis;
market factors.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson III: Stocks--Risk and return; valuation; investment strategies.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson IV: Options--Valuation models; strategies.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson V: Debt Investment--Analyzing different types of bonds; investment
approaches.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson VI: Mutual Funds--How they work; tax implications; risk and return;
guidelines for selecting funds.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson VII: International Investments--Risks, diversification benefits,
ways to invest internationally.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson VIII: Futures Markets--Trading mechanics; speculation; hedging.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson IX: Real Estate--Risk and return; the market; tax considerations.
</LI>
<LI>Lesson X: Other Investments--Collectibles--analysis and investment
guidelines.
</LI>
</UL>
<P>
The entire curriculum is shipped in a three-ring binder for easy reference and
storage. Members receive free updates and revisions as they are developed, which
are available to non-members for a nominal charge. The complete course is
available to AAII members for $55.00 and to non-members for $75.00.</P>

<P>
<STRONG>Investing Basics Videocourse</STRONG>
</P>
<P>
AAII's Investing Basics Videocourse teaches the fundamentals of investing to
beginners. In three and a half hours, you will learn the techniques and
strategies you should know to be a successful investor. The accompanying
workbook reproduces the graphics and tables used in the video; the videotape
format allows you to stop at any point during the course to review the section
you just covered in the workbook. In addition, the workbook includes a
bibliography of information sources at the end of each section. The videocourse
includes:
</P>
<P>
Part I: Basic Financial Concepts--This section covers the broad concepts you
need to think about when studying any kind of investment, including risk,
return, the risk/return trade-off, the time value of money, and how to calculate
future values.
</P>
<P>
Part II: Bonds and Other Fixed-Income Investments--This section provides you
with the basics of investing in bonds including risk, return and the risk/return
trade-off for bonds; characteristics of bonds; how to read bond quotes in the
newspaper; other fixed-income investment alternatives; and how to purchase
bonds.
</P>
<P>
Part III: Investing in Common Stocks--This section provides the basics of
investing in stocks, including risk and return of common stocks; stock valuation
methods; how to read stock quotations; stock indexes and indicators; how to
purchase common stocks; and stock mutual funds.
</P>
<P>
Part IV: Building a Portfolio of Investments--This section explains how to frame
your investments into a personal portfolio. Topics covered include
diversification, asset allocation, modifying portfolio risk, calculating
portfolio return, how to build a portfolio reflecting your personal investment
considerations, and how to monitor and rebalance your portfolio.
</P>
<P>
The complete videocourse with accompanying workbook is available to AAII members
for $98 and to non-members for $129.</P>

<P>
<STRONG>Stock Investing Basics Booklet</STRONG>
</P>
<P>
Stock Investing Basics Booklet Stock Investing Basics, a new booklet from AAII,
teaches you the basics of fundamental stock analysis without miring you in
intricate details. Using a simple worksheet, various valuation methods are
described and illustrated that help you judge how fairly the market is pricing a
stock of interest. Appendixes show you how to perform stock screening on a
computerized database, using each method to develop a short list of stocks to
investigate. Some of the valuation methods discussed include:</P>
<UL><LI>Growth Stock Investing.
</LI>
<LI>Investing in Utilities for Income and Growth.</LI>
<LI>Investing in Low P/E Stocks.
</LI>
<LI>Finding Stocks With Winning Characteristics.
</LI>
<LI>Stock Screening &agrave; la Peter Lynch.
</LI></UL>

<STRONG>Mutual Funds Videocourse</STRONG>
</P>
<P>
This four-hour videotape with an accompanying workbook offers comprehensive
