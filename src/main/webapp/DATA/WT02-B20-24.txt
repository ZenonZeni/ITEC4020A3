<b><i>SOCIAL EMOTIONAL NEEDS OF GIFTED CHILDREN-SELECTED READINGS
FOR EDUCATORS (E527bib)</b></i><P>

September 1993<P>

Adderholt-Elliot, M.(1987).  Perfectionism: What's bad about being too good? 
Minneapolis:  Free Spirit Publishing.<p>

Amabile, T. M. (1989).  Growing up creative: Nurturing a lifetime of creativity. 
New York: Crown.<p>

Austin, A. B., & Draper, D. C. (1981).  Peer relationships of the academically
gifted: A review.  Gifted Child Quarterly, 25.  129-133.<p>

Baum, S. M., Owen, S. V., Dixon, J. P. (1991).  To be gifted and learning
disabled: From identification to practical intervention strategies.  Mansfield Center, CT:
Creative learning Press.<p>

Betts, G. T., & Neihart, M. (1985).  Eight effective activities to enhance the
emotional and social development of the gifted and talented.  Roeper Review, 8,
18-23.<p>

Colangelo, N., & David, G. A. (1991).  A handbook of gifted education. 
Needham Heights, MA: Allyn & Bacon.<p>

Colangelo, N. (1985).  Counseling needs of culturally diverse gifted students. 
Roeper Review, 8, 33-35.<p>

Cummings, R., & Fisher, G. (1991).  The school survival guide for kids with LD
(learning differences).  Minneapolis: Free Spirit Publishing.<p>

Delisle, J. R. (1992).  Guiding the social and emotional development of the gifted
and talented.  Englewood Cliffs, NJ: Prentice-Hall, Inc.<p>

Frasier, M. (1981).  Minority gifted children.  In B. S. Miller & M. Price, (Eds.). 
The gifted child, the family and the community.  New York: American Association for
Gifted Children.<p>

Gardner, H. (1983).  Frames of mind: The theory of multiple intelligences.  New
York: Basic Books.<p>

Halsted, J. W. (1988).  Guiding gifted readers from preschool to high school: A
guide for parents , teachers, librarians and counselors.  Dayton, OH: Ohio Psychology
Publishing.<p>

Hipp, E. (1987).  Fighting invisible tigers: A student guide to life in "the jungle". 
Minneapolis: Free Spirit Publishing.<p>

Kerr, B. (1991).  A handbook for counseling the gifted and talented.  Alexandria,
VA: American Association for Counseling and Development.<p>

Kerr, B. A. (1985).  Smart girls, gifted women.  Columbus, OH: Ohio Psychology
Publishing.<p>

Reilly, J. M. (1992).  Mentorship: The Essential Guide for Schools and Business. 
Dayton, OH: Ohio Psychology Press.<p>

Rimm, S. (1985, November-December).  Identifying underachievement: The
characteristics approach.  Gifted Child Today.  2-5.<p>

Schmitz, C. C., & Galbraith, J. (1985).  Managing the social and emotional needs
of the gifted.  Minneapolis: Free Spirit Publishing.<p>

Silverman, L. (Ed.) (1993).  Counseling the gifted and talented.  A
comprehensive resource for counselors and teachers of gifted learners and graduate
students in counseling psychology.  Denver, CO: Love.<p>

Sternberg, R. (1986).  Intelligence applied: Understanding and increasing your
intellectual skills.  Fort Worth, TX: Harcourt, Brace, Jovanovich College
Publishers.<p>

VanTassel-Baska, J., (Ed.).(1989).  A practical guide to counseling the gifted in a
school setting.  Reston, VA: Council for Exceptional Children.<p>

VanTassel-Baska, J. L., & Olszewski-Kubilius, P. (1989).  Patterns of influence
on gifted learners: The home, the self, the school.  New York: Teachers College
Press.<p>

Watts, J. (1990).  In search of perspective.  Dayton, OH: Ohio Psychology
Publishing.<p>

Webb, J. T., Meckstroth, E. A., & Tolan, S. S. (1982).  Guiding the gifted child. 
Columbus, OH: Ohio Psychology Publishing.<p>

Whitmore, J. R., & Maker, C. J. (1985).  Intellectual giftedness in disabled
persons. Rockville, MD: Aspen Publications.<p>
