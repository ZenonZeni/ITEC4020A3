<h4 align="center">Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>

<h4 align="center"><font color="#000080"><b>Dissociative
Disorders</b></font></h4>

<p align="center"><font color="#000080"><b></b></font>&nbsp;</p>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><b>Definition</b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - a dissociation from or interruption of a person&#146;s
    fundamental aspects of waking consciousness</p>
    <p>2 - triggered as a response to trauma or abuse</p>
    <p>3 - a coping mechanism/a dissociation from a situation or
    experience too traumatic to integrate with the conscious self</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Dissociative Amnesia</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - blocking out critical personal information, usually
    traumatic or stressful </p>
    <p>2 - Localized amnesia</p>
    <blockquote>
        <ul>
            <li>no memory of specific events</li>
            <li>memory loss is localized within a specific window
                of time</li>
            <li>usually temporary</li>
        </ul>
    </blockquote>
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
    <p>3 - Selective amnesia/recall only small parts of events
    from a defined period of time</p>
    <p>4 - Generalized amnesia/when one&#146;s amnesia
    encompasses one&#146;s entire life</p>
    <p>5 - Continuous amnesia/no memory of events beginning at a
    certain point in the past up until the present</p>
    <p>6 - Systematized amnesia/loss of memory for a specific
    category of information</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Treatment</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - psychotherapy</p>
    <p>2 - a combination of psychopharmacological &amp;
    psychosocial treatments</p>
    <p>3 - antidepressants</p>
    <p>4 - antianxiety medication</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Dissociative Fugue</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - a rare disorder</p>
    <p>2 - sudden &amp; unexpected taking of physical leave on a
    journey that lasts several days or months</p>
    <p>3 - results in traveling over thousands of miles</p>
    <p>4 - unaware of or confused about one&#146;s identity</p>
    <p>5 - causes some to assume a new identity</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Dissociative Identity
Disorder/Multiple Personality Disorder</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - the most famous of the dissociative disorders</p>
    <p>2 - leads one to have more than 1 distinct identity </p>
    <p>3 - different identities surface at different times but on
    a recurring basis</p>
    <p>4 - marked by memory gaps that vary with one&#146;s
    personalities</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000"><strong>Depersonalization Disorder</strong></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - recurrent feelings of depersonalization/detachment or
    distance from one&#146;s own  body, self, experiences</p>
    <p>2 - the most easily identified dissociative disorder</p>
    <p>3 - feeling of being in a dream or being &quot;spaced
    out&quot;</p>
    <p>4 - feeling out of control of one&#146;s actions &amp;
    movements </p>
    <p>5 - interrupts normal functioning</p>
    <p>6 - causes some to believe the external world is unreal or
    distorted</p>
</blockquote>
