<P><HR WIDTH="90%" align=left>Key

<UL>
   <LI>AI--artificial insemination
   
   <LI>IVF/ET--in vitro fertilization/embryo transfer
   
   <LI>ED--egg donor
   
   <LI>AID--artificial insemination by donor (where both H and W are
   infertile)
   
   <LI>H--husband; W--wife; S--surrogate
</UL>

<P><HR WIDTH="90%" align=left><TABLE BORDER=0 CELLPADDING=1
WIDTH=475>
   <TR>
      <TD VALIGN=top>
         <P ALIGN=RIGHT><A NAME="*">*</A>
      </TD><TD COLSPAN=10>
         <P>These figures represent an average total cost, including
         all expenses, but assume two things: 1) that your surrogate
         is charging $13,000 for her fee and has insurance which
         covers the pregnancy, and 2) that your surrogate conceives
         within the first 3 AI attempts or on the first IVF/ET
         attempt. Total actual fees depend, of course, on the
         location of your surrogate, how many tries it takes before
         she conceives, her fee, etc. At whatever point you select a
         surrogate, you make an initial $15,000 into SMI's escrow
         account. Additional deposits, if necessary, are made when
         you receive your monthly accounting statement.
      </TD></TR>
   <TR>
      <TD VALIGN=top>
         <P ALIGN=RIGHT><A NAME="**">**</A>
      </TD><TD COLSPAN=10>
         <P>The legal fees, as explained in the accompanying
         information, are divided into 2 parts. The first part is the
         initial withdrawal; the second is the withdrawal that occurs
         only after a pregnancy has been achieved.
      </TD></TR>
   <TR>
      <TD VALIGN=top>
         <P ALIGN=RIGHT><A NAME="***">***</A>
      </TD><TD COLSPAN=10>
         <P>Most surrogates ask for around $13,000, although we are
         beginning to see somewhat higher fees. If you select a
         surrogate who is charging more than $13,000, you would add
         the difference to the total cost of the program. You will
         know this information prior to selecting her. Your
         surrogate's fee is deposited with SMI within 5 days after
         she conceives.
      </TD></TR>
   <TR>
      <TD VALIGN=top>
         <P ALIGN=RIGHT><A NAME="****">****</A>
      </TD><TD COLSPAN=10>
         <P>AID surrogacy is generally difficult to do unless you and
         your surrogate are from the same state. If you are, then the
         procedures are treated the same as any other traditional
         adoption.
      </TD></TR>
</TABLE>
