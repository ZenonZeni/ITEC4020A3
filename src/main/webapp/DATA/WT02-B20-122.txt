<H3>1996-1997 Student CEC Graduation Awards</H3>
</TD></TR>
<TD WIDTH=475 ALIGN=LEFT>



During the 1994 annual convention in Denver, the Student CEC Board of Governors created two graduation awards to be awarded annually, one to an undergraduate Student CEC member and the other to a Student CEC member in a graduate or doctoral program.  The winners of the award will receive $350 upon graduation with part of the money going towards professional CEC membership.<P>

<H4>Purpose of the Award</H4>

To better increase the retention rate of Student CEC members transitioning to CEC membership as well as provide support for expenses incurred while in school, two awards of $350 will be offered annually: 1) to an undergraduate Student CEC member and 2) to a graduate (doctoral or master's candidate) Student CEC member upon completion of their education at their university or college.<P>

<H4>Eligibility Criteria to Apply for the Award</H4>

The following is a list of criteria that must be met in order to apply for the award:<P>
<DL><DT><DD>
1.	An undergraduate and/or graduate (doctoral or master's candidate) student enrolled at an accredited college or university who will be graduating in the academic year the award is given (i.e. in order to receive the award in 1997, each applicant must be graduating sometime in 1997).
<DD>2.	Overall grade point average of 3.0 or better or marks equal to a B or higher.
<DD>3.	Member of Student CEC.
</DT></DL>

<H4>Application Process</H4>

To be considered for the award, the following information must be submitted to the Student CEC Honors Committee by December 2, 1996 (postmarked):<P>
<DL><DT><DD>
1.	Application Cover Sheet
<DD>2.	Documentation of your GPA or marks
<DD>3.	A list of college, community, Student CEC and other extracurricular activities
<DD>4.	A two page essay (double spaced) explaining:<BR>
<UL>
<LI>your experience with persons with exceptionalities;
<LI>your desire to work with persons with exceptionalities;
<LI>your view of the exceptional person in the classroom; and
<LI>what you hope to accomplish as a professional.
</UL>

<DD>5.	Two letters of recommendation addressing your commitment to special education and Student CEC from:<BR>
<UL>
<LI>your Student CEC advisor (in collaboration with your chapter's executive committee); and
<LI>an academic advisor, employer or professor.
</UL>
</DT></DL>

<H4>Selection Process and Criteria</H4>

The winners of the award will be chosen by the Student CEC Honors Committee.  The Honors Committee must receive all applications postmarked by December 2, 1996.  They will review the applications and make their selections based on:<P>
<DL><DT><DD>
	 1)	the responses in the essay, namely experience with persons with exceptionalities, the desire to work with persons with exceptionalities and future goals as a professional; 
	<DD> 2)	the letters of recommendation;
	<DD> 3)	the grades/marks and other extracurricular activities; and
	<DD> 4)	Student CEC activities.  
</DT></DL>
<H4>
Awards Process</H4>

The undergraduate and graduate (doctoral and master's candidate) members winning the award each receive $350 with part of this money going to renewal of professional membership.  The monetary award will be given upon receipt of a final transcript indicating the degree conferred which must be sent to the Coordinator of Student Activities within two months of graduation.  The winners will be recognized at the Student CEC awards presentation during the annual convention but do not need to be present to win.<BR><CENTER>------------------------------------------------------------------------------</CENTER>
