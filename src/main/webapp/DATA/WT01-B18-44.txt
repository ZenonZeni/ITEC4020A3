<I><B>E. William Behrens</B></I>
<P>
Associate Professor, Department of Marine Science. Ph.D., Rice
University (1963), M.S. University of Michigan (1958), B.A., Cornell
University (1956).
<P>
<I><B>Marine Geology</B></I>
<P>
High-resolution and multichannel seismic reflection data acquisition,
processing, and interpretation; continental slope, sedimentology.
<P>
<I><B>Research Interest</B></I>
<P>
The history of the development of continental margins is contained
in their sediments. The study of continental slope sedimentary
records includes direct sampling and observation of water column
physical and chemical parameters, sediment mineralogy, texture,
and structures, micropaleontological content, geochemical and
isotopic composition, and acoustic characteristics. Remote sensing
(marine geophysics) is used to observe sediment thicknesses, stratigraphic
relationships of sedimentary units, structural context and style,
and acoustic characteristics related to diagenetic phenomena.
From a combination of remote sensing and ground truth data (cores),
research projects are designed to interpret the history of depositional,
diagenetic, tectonic, and environmental conditions and processes
that have created the existing features of continental margins.
The same tools of sediment sampling and analysis, and seismic
reflection profiling apply to the study of the history of deeper
oceanic as well as shallower coastal environments.
