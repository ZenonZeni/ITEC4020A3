<ul>
<li>17,630 people died in Canada in alcohol related crashes from
1983 to 1991.

<li>1.1 million people were injured from 1983 to 1991

<li>1,414 people killed in 1992

<li>5.2 million days lost activity and employment in 1987
</ul>

<hr noshade><p>

<blockquote>
"Every year, drunk driving leaves a terrible trail of death, 
injury, heartbreak and destruction... <br>
In terms of deaths and serious injuries resulting in 
hospitalization, drunk driving is clearly the crime which 
causes the most significant social loss to the country."<p>	
</blockquote>

<b>Source: Justice Peter Cory, Supreme court of Canada, 1995.</b><p>

<hr noshade><p>

<blockquote>
Since 1980, the Canadian Centre for Justice Statistics has 
reported in excess of 100,000 charges for impaired driving
in Canada each year.<p>
</blockquote>

<b>Source: Canadian Centre for Justice Statistics.</b><p>

<hr noshade><p>

<ul>
<li> approximately 1,680 people killed in 1993 in alcohol 
related crashes
<li> 4.6 deaths per day (average)
<li>1 death every 6 hours<p>
</ul>

<b>Source: Doug Beirness, Traffic injury Research Foundation 
of Canada</b><p>

<hr noshade><p>

<ul>
<li> 113,731 injuries (approx.) in 1993 in alcohol related crashes
<li> 311 people injured per day (approx.)
<li> 12 people injured per hour <approx.)
<li> 1 injury every 5 minutes (approx.)
</ul>

<b>Source: Transport Canada</b><p>

<h3>The following statistics are for the year 1993</h3>

<img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><img src=images/dash.gif><p>

<b>DEATHS</b>

<ul>
<li>565 people were killed in Ontario in alcohol-related crashes<p>
</ul>

<hr noshade><p>

<b>CRASHES</b>

<ul>
<li>13,616 impaired drivers were involved in collisions<p>

<li>There were 37.3 alcohol-related crashes per day<p>

<li>1.5 alcohol related crashes per hour<p>
</ul>

<hr noshade><p>

<b>TYPES OF VEHICLE</b>

<ul>
<li>73% of snowmobile and all terrain vehicle fatalities involved alcohol<p>

<li>77% of marine vehicle fatalities invoved alcohol<p>

<li>57% of motor cycle fatalities involved alcohol<p>

<li>43% of motor vehicle fatalities involved alcohol<p>
</ul>

<hr noshade><p>

<b>CHARGES</b>

<ul>
<li>26,177 drivers were charged with impaired driving<p>

<li>63% of all suspensions issued for impaired driving were 
issued for a second offence<p>
</ul>

<hr noshade><p>

<b>DEMOGRAPHICS</b>

<UL>
<li>80% of alcohol fatalities are male<p>

<LI>58% of all traffic fatalities in the 25-34 year old range
involve alcohol<p>
</UL>

<hr noshade><p>

<b>SCOPE and COST</b>

<ul>
<li>Road side surveys show that one in five drivers on the road
at night, during weekends, has been drinking<p>

<li>The social cost of impaired driving, including personal injury, 
property damage, lost wages and productivity for 1993 was
nine billion dollars. (150,000 days of hospital care, 38,000 
ambulance calls and 800,000 hours of police time)<p>
</ul>
