<h1 align=center>Learning Disabilities Association</h1>

<h2 align=center>FACT SHEET</h2>

<h3 align=center>TESTS OF GENERAL EDUCATIONAL DEVELOPMENT (GED TESTS)</h3>

<p><b>Educational Services Committee
<br>November 1993</b> </p>

<p><b>What is the GED?
<br></b>For students who have not graduated from high school, GED is a
means of obtaining a diploma that is equivalent to a high school diploma.
Most secondary programs will accept the GED as equivalent to graduation
from a regular high school program. The GED tests are designed to measure
the important knowledge and skills, usually learned during four years of
high school, that individuals may have obtained in a different manner.
</p>

<p><b>Who is eligible?
<br></b>Individuals who have not completed high school are eligible to
take the GED with the following stipulations: </p>

<ul>
<li>The individual must be a resident of the state in which (s)he is taking
the test. </li>

<li>The individual must <i>not</i> be enrolled in a public school. </li>

<li>Applicants under the age of eighteen must apply to the State Department
of Education, Adult Services, for permission to take the test. </li>

<li>Individuals with Learning Disabilities must fill out an &quot;Application
for Special Testing&quot; and sign a release of information to have certain
medical and psychological records sent to GED for review. </li>
</ul>

<p><b>Which learning disability conditions may justify special accommodations?
<br></b>Learning Disability conditions which are eligible for special accommodations
include, but are not limited to, dyslexia, dysgraphia, dyscalculia, dyslogia,
attention deficit disorder, receptive aphasia, distractibility, and hyperactivity.
Documentation of the learning disability may be required. </p>

<p><b>What modifications are available to individuals with learning disabilities?
<br></b>Possible modifications which can be obtained include: </p>

<ul>
<li>Audiocassette edition (with printed reference copy) </li>

<li>Large print edition </li>

<li>Use of a scribe </li>

<li>Extended time </li>

<li>Use of a calculator </li>

<li>Frequent breaks </li>

<li>Use of a private room for testing </li>
</ul>

<p><b>Is there a fee for taking the test?
<br></b>Generally, there is a modest fee for taking the test for the first
time. An additional minimal fee is required for each retest. </p>

<p><b>Are preparation classes and/or practice materials available?
<br></b>Free classes and materials are provided to prepare and individual
to take the GED. Classes are usually held at local colleges or technical
schools and instruction is individualized to allow students to progress
at their own rate. Individual practice materials are also available form
Prentice Hall Regents Cambridge Adult Education Division of Prentice Hall
Publishers. </p>

<p><b>What is the format of the GED tests?
<br></b>The GED tests consist of five tests in the areas of writing skills,
social studies, science, literature and the arts, and mathematics. The
questions in each of these tests require a general knowledge of the subjects
and the ability to think. There are few questions asking about facts, details,
or definitions. </p>

<p><b>What are the contents of the tests?</b> </p>
<dl>
<dd><b>Writing skills</b>
    <dl>
    <dd>Part I (55 questions)
    <dd>Grading: 35% sentence structure, 35% usage, 30% for spelling,
        punctuation, capitalization
    <br>
    <dd>Part II (essay)<br>
</dl>

<dd><b>Social Studies (64 questions)</b>
<dd>Grading: 50% life science, 50% physical science
<br>
<dd><b>Literature and the arts (45 questions)</b>
<dd>Grading based on interpretation of: 50% popular literature, 25% classical
literature, 25% commentary on literature and the arts
<br>
<dd><b>Mathematics (56 questions)</b>
<dd>Grading: 50% arithmetic, 30% algebra, 20% geometry
</dl>

<p><b>What is passing?
<br></b>An individual must achieve a minimum total score of 225 points
on the five tests with no single score below 35. If a test is failed, there
is a waiting period before retesting. The waiting period depends on the
total score that the individual has achieved. Retests are given after
the five tests have been completed. Individuals may take as many tests
at one session as desired. There is no time limit in completing the five
tests. The writing skills test requires the individual to complete an essay
on an assigned topic. </p>

<p><b>Who issues the diploma?
<br></b>The State Department of Education issues diplomas. </p>
