</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"> <B><I> BXA Annual Report and Foreign Policy Reports to Congress:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>   On January 16, BXA delivered to the Congress its <a href="theannua.htm"><B>Annual Report and report on Foreign Policy Contorls</A></B>. <P>


</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"> <B><I> Ohio Firm Receives $160,000 Civil Penalty for Illegal Exports:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>   On January 8, Assistant Secretary John Despres ordered <a href="rmi.htm"><B>RMI Titanium</B></A> of Niles, Ohio, to pay a $160,000 civil penalty to settle allegations that had exported six shipments of titanium alloy to France and Israel without the required export licenses.<P>


</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"> <B><I> Encryption Items Transferred from the U.S. Munitions List to the Commerce Control List:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>   On December 30, the Federal Register (61 Fed. Reg. 68572) published an interim rule that amends the EAR by exercising jurisdiction over, and imposing new combined national security and foreign policy controls on certain encryption items previously listed on the United States Munitions List. This rule is consistent with both the Executive Order and the Presidential Memorandum issued on November 15, 1996.  Included in the rule are export licensing policies for different categories of encryption items, and criteria for key escrow or key recovery products, agents, and development plans.  You may download this Federal Register notice from the <a href="encstart.htm"><B>BXA Encryption Page</B></A>.  You may also refer to the <A href="encrypt.html"><b>press release</B></A> located on this website.<P>




</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"><B><I> Contacting BXA for Export Licensing Advice:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>  With the new Export Administration Regulations becoming fully effective on December 31, BXA has been experiencing a high volume of calls seeking export control advice.   Many of the inquiries are about obtaining a correct export control number for a commodity, determining if a license is required, or identifying license exceptions.  If you experience a busy signal, please be patient.  We are doing all we can to assist everyone.  The best times to call are before 10 and after 3:30 EST.  You may also try to reach our Western Regional Office located in Newport Beach, CA at (714) 660-0144 between the hours of 8:30 and 5:00 PST.<P>



</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"><B><I> Texas Firm Receives Criminal Fine for Illegal Exports to China:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>  On December 20, New World Transtechnology (NWT), Galveston, Texas, pled guilty to charges stemming from its 1992 illegal exports of computers valued at $97,585 to a nuclear equipment factory in the People's Republic of China.  A district judge in Galveston fined NWT $10,000 and levied a special assessment of $600.  Office of Export Enforcement special agents seized one of the computers prior to its shipment to China.<P>


</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"> <B><I> Illinois Firm Receives Civil Penalty for Antiboycott Violations:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>   On December 30, Acting Assistant Secretary Frank Deliberti ordered Aurora Pump, an Illinois manufacturer and exporter of industrial and commercial pumps, to pay a civil penalty of $18,000 to settle allegations that it agreed to furnish information about another person's blacklist status and failed to report its receipt of boycott-related requests from Kuwait in violation of the antiboycott provisions of the Export Administration Regulations.<P>


</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"><B><I> German Firm and its General Manager Denied Export Privileges and Given $500,000 Criminal Fine for Illegal Shipments to Libya:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>  On December 18, Acting Assistant Secretary Frank Deliberti denied the export privileges of Doornbos GmbH of Solingen, Germany, and its general manager, Helmut Korelski for four years in settlement of allegations that they violated the Export Administration Regulations by their involvement in shipments of U.S.-made machine parts and construction equipment to Libya.  Korelski, acting for Doornbos, acquired the goods in the United States claiming that their ultimate destination was Germany, when in fact he sold the goods to the Dong Ah Consortium for use in the Great Man-made River Project in Libya.  In a separate proceeding, Doornbos and Korelski pled guilty to one conspiracy count in district court in Ohio and Doornbos received a criminal fine of $500,000.  An order denying export privileges prohibits the person or entity from participating in any export transaction involving items subject to the Export Administration Regulations.<P>


</font><font size=4 color=blue><img src="rediam.gif"><B><I> New Jersey Firm Receives Civil Penalties for Exports a Chemical used in Ballistic Missiles:</I></B></font><font size=3 color=navy>   On December 16, Assistant Secretary John Despres ordered May National Associates, Inc. of Clifton, New Jersey, to pay a $25,000 civil penalty to resolve allegations that it exported hydroxyl-terminated polybutadiene to France without the required export license.  Hydroxyl-terminated polybutadiene is commonly used in rocket engines and its exportation is controlled to prevent the proliferation of ballistic missile systems.<P>
