<STRONG><A NAME="hmo">Health Maintenance
Organization (HMO)</A></STRONG><BR>
A type of plan that controls health care expenses by making
cost-saving arrangements with
physicians, hospitals and other health care providers.  HMOs
usually offer reduced
out-of-pocket costs to members who have their care provided or
coordinated by a
primary care
physician participating in the HMO network.<P>


<STRONG><A NAME="mi">Managed Indemnity</A></STRONG><BR>
A plan in which the covered person has the flexibility to obtain
care from any licensed provider
but
must pay a greater portion of health care costs than with a
network-based plan.  Utilization
review and case management programs apply managed care principals
to indemnity plans
to help
control costs.<P>

<STRONG><A NAME="pos">Point-of-Service (POS)</A> </STRONG><BR>
A plan that offers greater flexibility than an HMO-only product. 
The POS allows members to
select one of two options each time  care is needed: either the
HMO plan as described
in this glossary,
or a traditional indemnity plan which enables the covered person
to choose any non-network licensed
physician or hospital for care.  However, this requires higher
out-of-pocket costs than
network care.<P>

<STRONG><A NAME="poe">Multi-Option (also known as
Point-of-Enrollment or POE)</A></STRONG><BR>
Similar to a POS plan in that there is a choice of two, and
sometimes more, different
options.  However, a multi-option plan is less flexible than a
POS plan because the
member
must select his/her option at the time of enrollment and is
typically locked-in to that
choice for a year.<P>



<STRONG><A NAME="ppo">Preferred Provider
Organization (PPO)</A></STRONG><BR>
Similar to an HMO plan in that reduced costs are available to
members who use providers
that
participate in the PPO network.  The typical difference is that
the
member may self-refer to a
preferred provider and does not need to have any care coordinated
by a primary care
physician.<P>


<STRONG><A NAME="pcp">Primary Care Physician (PCP)
</A></STRONG><BR>

A Primary Care Physician, or PCP, is a provider who coordinates a
member's covered health care in an HMO.  In many ways, Primary
Care Physicians (or PCPs) are a return to something that was very
good about medicine in the old days-- a doctor who knows you
well.  <P>
