<h4 align="center">Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>

<p align="center"><font color="#000080"><b>Multiple Personality
Disorder</b></font></p>

<hr>

<p align="center"><font color="#FF0000"><em>This information
about MPD extracted from the DSM-IV&quot;</em></font></p>

<hr>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="4" face="Arial"><b>Symptoms</b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - episodes of amnesia or time loss/inability to remember
    events in all or part of a preceding time period </p>
    <p>2 - unaware of the problem/condition</p>
    <p>3 - repeated encounters with people they don&#146;t
    recognize but who claim to know them</p>
    <p>4 - failure to know how they got somewhere</p>
    <p>5 - discovery of items they don&#146;t remember purchasing</p>
    <p>6 - depressed/suicidal</p>
    <p>7 - self-mutilation</p>
    <p>8 - auditory or visual hallucinations/hearing voices</p>
    <p>9 - symptoms in children</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. depressive tendencies</p>
        <p>b. anxiety</p>
        <p>c. conduct problems</p>
        <p>d. episodes of amnesia</p>
        <p>e. difficulty paying attention in school</p>
        <p>f. hallucinations</p>
    </blockquote>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="4" face="Arial"><b>Treatment</b></font>
- psychotherapy with hypnosis where the goal is to enable the
patient to achieve breakdown of the separate personalities and
unification of them into a single personality. </p>

<p>&nbsp;<font color="#FF0000" size="4" face="Arial"><b>Definition/Symptoms</b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>- 2 or more separate &amp; distinct personalities control
    one at different times</p>
    <p>- often misdiagnosed in children as schizophrenia</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="4" face="Arial"><b>Causes</b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - physical or sexual abuse in childhood/ MPD usually
    emerges at age 5 or 6</p>
    <p>2 - dissociation </p>
    <p>b. used as a defense against trauma </p>
    <p>c. employed in an attempt to make it appear as though
    abuse is being  inflicted against someone else instead of
    against oneself</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="4" face="Arial"><b>The
&quot;personalities&quot; or &quot;alters&quot;</b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - when under the control of one personality, the person
    can&#146;t remember events that occurred while other
    personalities were in control</p>
    <p>2 - different personalities exhibit differences in speech,
    mannerism, attitude, thought, &amp; gender</p>
    <p>3 - personalities may differ in physical properties (i.e.
    allergies, handedness, or eyeglass prescription)</p>
    <p>4 - usually stable over time</p>
</blockquote>
