An overwhelming quantity of free information is available for the average investor to make
their investment decisions. Sounds excellent, if you have the time and means to sort it all
out.<P>

Most people don't have the time, and will gladly pay a sales charge to utilize the knowledge
and direction of a professional investment advisor.  Almost two-thirds of all mutual fund
assets have been sold through investment advisors.<A
HREF="#1"><SUP>1</SUP></A><P> 

Unfortunately, media "financial experts" portray smart investors as only those who buy no-load,
"no-help" funds.  So are the majority of mutual fund investors inept? No, they simply realize the
value of the advice provided by a professional investment advisor.<P>

Here are some reasons you should consider using the services of a financial advisor:<P>

<UL>
<LI>Daily monitoring of investment markets
<LI>In-depth analysis of your investment needs and strategy to obtain them
<LI>Retirement planning
<LI>College planning
<LI>Specialized investment research
<LI>Familiar voice to express your interests and concerns regarding your investments
</UL>
<P>
What source would you rather consult for critical investment decisions which could have an impact on 
both your present and future financial positions?<P>
<UL>
<LI>Large stacks of nice glossy brochures, charts, and graphs.
<LI>A seasoned investment professional dedicated to servicing your investment needs.
</UL>
