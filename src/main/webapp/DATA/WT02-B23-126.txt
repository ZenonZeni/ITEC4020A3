<p><em><strong>Title</strong></em><strong>. </strong>"The Work of Engineering Schools: An Organization Shift for 21st Century Education and Research"</p>
<p><em><strong>Abstract</strong></em><strong>. </strong>
Engineering education is in the midst of a transforming shift that began 
at the beginning of the last quarter of the present century.  While the 
curriculum retains the structure and pedagogy of the mid-20th Century, 
concern for its long-term viability and utility has risen in the face of 
an altered over arching external framework.  The seminar will discuss the 
following key elements for the future of engineering education:
</p>

<p>
<ul>
<li>Major trends in technology and engineering since the 19th Century</li>
<li>The paradox of the 1990s in engineering education</li>
<li>Key questions for the curriculum and the organization to support it</li>
<li>What needs to be done in the schools and for the faculty</li>
</ul>
</p>
