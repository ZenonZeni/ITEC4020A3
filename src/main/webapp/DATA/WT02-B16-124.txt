<p><strong><A href="/office"> Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy</a> Benefits</strong>
</center>
<p>Efficiency and renewable energy technologies are critical to building a clean, strong economy:  
Benefits include:

<ul>
<li>Affordable and comfortable housing for American families.  Our programs will save $17 billion in 
residential energy costs by 2005.
<li>Higher profits and more available capital for American businesses.  Our programs will save $12.5 
billion in commercial energy costs by 2005.
<li>Pollution prevention and greater productivity for American industries.  Our programs will save 
$11.8 billion in industrial energy costs by 2010.
<li>A lower foreign trade deficit and reduced dependence on foreign oil (under current trends, imports 
will rise to 60 percent by 2015).
<li>New entrepreneurial opportunities and jobs (a total of 19,000 jobs in the year 2000, and 186,000 
jobs in the year 2010).
<li>An increased share of the $425 billion global market for energy and environmental 
technologies.
<li>Reduced federal energy costs.  Our programs will save federal taxpayers $380 million by 
2000.
</ul>

<p>DOE's <a href="/office">efficiency and renewable energy</a> programs:
<ul>
<li>Embody a new approach to government voluntary partnerships, with shared investment a major 
alternative to tax subsidies and "command-and-control."
<li>Help the marketplace work better.
<li>Deliver one of the government's highest rates of return on the taxpayer dollar.
</ul>
