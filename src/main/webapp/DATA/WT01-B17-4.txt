TWO POLITICIANS SPEAK OUT
  <!-- --[ End title ]-- -->


</b></font>
<hr color="#ff0000" size="1" width="80%" align="left">
<p>

<blockquote>
<P>



  <!-- --[ Insert the article text here ]-- -->
Public outrage has infiltrated political camps as well. Representative Bill
Richardson (D-New Mexico) and Senator Orrin Hatch (R-Utah) addressed an audience
of health and supplement manufacturers and health store retailers at their annual
convention in Anaheim in March 1994.

<p>
Among their comments:

<p>
<BLOCKQUOTE>HATCH:"Everyone in the world knows that antioxidants such as E and C work. 
        But the FDA is unwilling to allow the claim. Many of their own staff gobble these
things like popcorn, and eight out of ten doctors take vitamin E to prevent heart
disease."
<P>RICHARDSON:  "It's a fight between people and bureaucracy We don't want the
government to tell us what vitamins we are going to take!  "
        <P>HATCH: "The American people want the freedom to buy safe dietary supplements."
        <P>RICHARDSON:  "This is a consumer movement against the regulators."
        <P>HATCH: "One hundred million Americans don't need to change. The FDA needs to
        change."
        <P>RICHARDSON:  "This is an issue of freedom of choice."
        <P>HATCH: "A tiny but powerful agency is engaged in an extensive lobbying campaign
        to subvert your freedom."
        <P>RICHARDSON: This is an issue of telling the FDA that they have ludicrous
        regulations."
<P>HATCH: "The FDA knows I am mad at the way you're being treated."
        <P>RICHARDSON:  "The government is SAYING:  ÎWe run your lives.  You as the
        consumer don't have the right to know and you are too stupid to know."'
        <P>HATCH:"When it comes to false and misleading advertising and information, the
        FDA has the corner on the market."
        We are grateful to have these men in our corner, helping to fight for American
        health freedom.
</BLOCKQUOTE>
<p>
