My, what a difference  a couple of years makes, eh?<p>Canadian bond prices are up about 12% since I recommended a bond buy in late June. Canadian bond prices are now moving to new all time highs. Many GIC customers are suffering from the nastiest case of "Rate Shock" in a generation,  since interest rates are now the lowest in 30 years. Of course, on the other side of the fence, consumers are getting the cheapest long term mortgage rates in 30 years too. I'd have to say that we are <i>probably </i>heading into the bottoming phase of the 50 to 60 year Kondratieff Wave cycle. Looking back the (Kondratieff) economic peak was in 1980. You might remember those days: $800 per oz. for gold. I remember borrowing some house fixup money at 21.5%. Canada Savings Bonds were 19.5% that year and many investors expected even higher rates. Why am I rambling? Well, in 1980, no one had a clue where the economic peak was. Now it's as clear as day. Have rates hit the bottom? Or can we go even lower? Ask me in a few year's time. One clue might be that the Federal Reserve has done the job yet again and accommodated the Incumbent President-a strategy that we've been playing for many months. Unfortunately, the honeymoon may be over within a few months.
