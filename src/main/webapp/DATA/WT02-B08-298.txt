<H1>Why is Ocean Circulation Important?</H1>
<p>
<p>
<h3>
Ocean circulation the key factor that controls global climate change.  Thus, TOPEX/Poseidon's <a href=sci_goals.html>primary science goals</a> include monitoring ocean circulation, studying <a href=dynamics.html>ocean dynamics,</a> and calculating heat transport.    
<p>
"The Global Conveyer Belt for Heat" represents in a simple way how ocean currents carry warm surface waters from the equator toward the poles and moderate global climate.  This global circuit takes up to 1,000 years to complete.     
<p>
<center><img src="images/con_belt.gif" alt="The Global Conveyer Belt for Heat"></center>
<p>
A fundamental tool used to study global surface ocean circulation is the Ocean Dynamic Topography map.  In the figure below, ocean topography highs or "hills" are shown as red and yellow, and lows or "valleys" are blue and purple.  This map reveals large features such as <a href=gyre_def.html>ocean gyres</a> that are fairly stable over time.  From these maps, scientists calculate the speed and direction of ocean currents.
