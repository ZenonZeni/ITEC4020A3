<h1 align="center">The Register's Editorials</h1>

<p align="center"><font size="6">Wise use of marijuana backed</font></p>

<p align="left"><spacer type="horizontal" size="50"><font size="4">Curt Smith (&quot;</font><a
href="smithc.html"><font size="4">Marijuana Is Hardly a Cure</font></a><font
size="4">,&quot; Oct. 24) denies that marijuana has valid medical
uses and criticizes state Senator Elaine Szymoniak for voicing support
of its medically-supervised use. He is wrong on both counts.</font> <br>
<spacer type="horizontal" size="50"><font size="4">Actually, marijuana has been used as a safe and effective
medicine for nearly every culture on Earth for thousands of
years. Modern research, to the extent that is has been allowed,
has continued to support its safety and efficacy for a variety of
conditions. </font><br>
<spacer type="horizontal" size="50"><font size="4">Recently that </font><a href="../POT/ana.html"><font
size="4">American Nurses Association</font></a><font size="4">
approved a resolution calling for nurses' education regarding the therapeutic
uses of marijuana and supporting continued investigation in
controlled trials. The ANA joins the </font><a
href="../apha.html"><font size="4">American Public Health
Association</font></a><font size="4">, the </font><a
href="../POT/nnsaposi.html"><font size="4">National Nurses
Society on Addictions</font></a><font size="4"> and several state
nursing associations supporting patients' rights to use marijuana as medicine
under medical supervision. </font><br>
<spacer type="horizontal" size="50"><font size="4">Szymoniak and other Iowa legislators who support responsible
use of medical marijuana are to be praised for their compassion
and courage.</font> </p>
