<CENTER><U><B><FONT SIZE=4 FACE="Arial">World's Largest Online
Library of Drug Policy</FONT></B></U></CENTER>
<P>
<B>The Drug War obviously isn't working. It is time we took another
look at the evidence on drugs. Now you can do it through the Internet.</B>
The DRCNet Online Library contains thousands of documents covering
all aspects of drugs and drug policy, from ancient history to
the latest developments from Europe. You can find this library
on the World Wide Web at the following location:
<P>
<B><FONT SIZE=5>http://www.druglibrary.org</FONT></B>
<P>
<U><B>What These Libraries Contain:</B></U>
<UL>
<LI><B><FONT SIZE=2>Major Studies of Drugs and Drug Policy over
the last 100 years</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=2>, including summaries
and full text of many studies. These studies include the <U>New
York City Bar Association Report</U> (1994), the <U>National Commission
on Marihuana and Drug Abuse</U> (1972), the <U>La Guardia Committee
Report</U> (1944), and many more.</FONT>
<LI><B><FONT SIZE=2>Historical Information</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=2>,
including the shocking origins of the drug laws. Included in the
collection are the <U>actual transcripts of the hearings for the
Marihuana Tax Act</U> and many other historical works, including
texts dating back to 550 BC. Why were these laws passed in the
first place? You will find it hard to believe - it had nothing
to do with what you have assumed.</FONT>
<LI><B><FONT SIZE=2>Latest News from Europe</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=2>
- including up-to-date information on drug policies in the <U>Netherlands</U>
as well as other developments, such as the <U>heroin maintenance
clinics</U> currently being run in Switzerland.</FONT>
<LI><B><FONT SIZE=2>DEA Judge Young's ruling on medical marijuana</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=2>.
In 1989, the DEA's Chief Administrative Law Judge finished hearing
two years of evidence on medical marijuana and ruled that the
DEA's actions in restricting the medical use of marijuana were
&quot;unconscionable.&quot; Our online library has the full text
of Judge Young's decision.</FONT>
<LI><B><FONT SIZE=2>The Drug Legalization Debate </FONT></B><FONT SIZE=2>-
Extensive materials covering all of the arguments in the &quot;drug
legalization&quot; debate from all perspectives, including the
full text of documents from all points of view. <U>See all the
arguments and all the evidence from both sides</U>.</FONT>
<LI><B><FONT SIZE=2>Government Documents </FONT></B><FONT SIZE=2>-
We maintain an extensive collection of Federal and State Government
documents related to drug policy including many reports which
have never been formally published or are so rare that they are
not available in most libraries.</FONT>
<LI><B><FONT SIZE=2>Much, much more</FONT></B><FONT SIZE=2> -
literally <U>thousands of documents</U> on a huge range of topics!</FONT>

</UL>
