<DT><A HREF="http://instruct.ride.ri.net/fig4.html">instruct.ride.ri.net/fig4.html</A><BR>
<BR>
<DD>This page discusses metacognition in reference to one of the NCTM standards, giving examples of four levels of metacognition for each of three sections.<BR>
<BR>
<DT><A HREF="http://www.ncrel.org/sdrs/areas/issues/students/learning/lr1metn.htm">www.ncrel.org/sdrs/areas/issues/students/learning/lr1metn.htm</A><BR>
<BR>
<DD>This page gives some guidelines one can use for facilitating metacognition.  Basically, there are questions students can ask themselves while doing assignments that are reflective of metacognition.<BR>
<BR>
<DT> <A HREF="http://www.ncrel.org/sdrs/areas/issues/students/learning/lr1metin.htm">www.ncrel.org/sdrs/areas/issues/students/learning/lr1metin.htm</A><BR>
<BR>
<DD>This page presents an example of a Metacognitive Interview Form for younger students.  It's a great example of how simple things can help even young students become more metacognitive.
