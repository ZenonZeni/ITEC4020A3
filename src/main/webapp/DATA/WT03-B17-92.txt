<H2>What is NITROX ?</H2>
<UL>Recreational Divers typically dive with air, which itself is actually a NITROX mix of 79% Nitrogen and 21% Oxygen.  NOAA (National Oceanographic Atmospheric Administration) scientific divers and commercial divers have known for years that nature's "mix" is not the most suitable for depths ranging from 40 to 130 ft.  The nitrogen causes narcotic and decompression disadvantages at these depths.  Therefore air mixes have been developed that use a higher percentage of oxygen to offset the undesirable side effects of high percentages of Nitrogen in the breathing mixture.  NITROX mixtures with percentages of Oxygen greater than 21% are called Enriched Air Nitrox (EANx)  The commonly used EANx mixtures are EAN 32 (32% Oxygen / 68% Nitrogen) and EAN 36 (36% Oxygen / 64% Nitrogen)</UL>
