<h3><font color="#800000" face="Arial">Future Of Agriculture </font></h3>

<p><font face="Arial"><strong><img src="images/ovejas.gif"
alt="Sheep" align="right" hspace="0" width="250" height="218"></strong></font><font
face="Arial"><strong>PORT STANLEY. (MP).-</strong> A 30 pages
paper titled &quot;<em>Future of agriculture in the Falklands</em>&quot;,
which is a summary on the many reports on farming, a most
controversial issue in the islands, was finally released by the
Legislative Council.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">The paper written by CEO Andrew Gurr
underlines the idea that agriculture is the &quot;<em>long term
prize for the islands</em>&quot; since once fishing and oil are
over, farming will be the only resource available.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">From this basic idea the report describes a
significant list of recommendations to develop an alternative
agriculture, taking advantage of new technologies, such as
improvement of pastures, crops, trees, legumes, cattle breeding,
wool classification, fertilizers, genetic engineering, etc..</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">But also taking into account living
conditions in the Camp to ensure a balanced development in all
the islands, and that consumer prices, for example, are not
higher than in Port Stanley like at present.</font></p>

<p><font face="Arial">Falklands Landholdings is another important
issue included in the report with all its impact on land
subdivision and community farming.</font></p>
