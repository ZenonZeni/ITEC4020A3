<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<h4 align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Depression In Elderly</b></font></h4>
<p><font color="#FF0000">What is clinical depression? </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - a biologically based brain disorder </p>
<p>2 - affects thoughts, feelings, behavior, &amp; physical health </p>
<p>3 - an &quot;affective disorder&quot; (causes changes in mood) </p>
<p>4 - may affect the immune system &amp; make one more susceptible to other 
illnesses </p>
<p>5 - sadness that does not just go away by itself </p>
<p>6 - it can develop in anyone at any age/it's not an inevitable sign of aging </p>
<p>7 - affects more than 11.6 million Americans </p>
<p>8 - less than 1/3 of those affected actually seek treatment </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Causes of depression </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - no single, definitive cause </p>
<p>2 - psychological, biological, environmental, &amp; genetic factors </p>
<p>3 - inherited biological make-up makes one more prone/depression runs in 
families </p>
<p>4 - certain brain chemicals are involved </p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>a. norepinephrine </p>
<p>b. serotonin </p>
<p>c. dopamine </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>5 - environmental factors </p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>a. prolonged stress </p>
<p>b. loss (i.e. of a spouse or friend) </p>
<p>c. a major life change (i.e. retirement/moving) </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Who is at risk? </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - older women </p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>a. twice as likely as men to become seriously depressed </p>
<p>b. biological factors (i.e. hormonal changes) make them more 
vulnerable </p>
<p>c. stress often falls more heavily upon them </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>1. maintaining relationships </p>
<p>2. caring for an ill loved one </p>
<p>3. caring for children </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>2 - unmarried &amp; widowed individuals </p>
<p>3 - those who lack a supportive social network </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Symptoms </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - different from symptoms in younger populations </p>
<p>2 - memory problems </p>
<p>3 - confusion </p>
<p>4 - social withdrawal </p>
<p>5 - loss of appetite </p>
<p>6 - inability to sleep </p>
<p>7 - irritability </p>
<p>8 - delusions/hallucinations </p>
<p>9 - severe sadness (often not openly acknowledged or shown) </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">Treatment </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - depression in the elderly often goes untreated </p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>a. mistake feelings/symptoms for dementia or normal aging </p>
<p>b. don't recognize symptoms or know how to deal with them </p>
<p>c. think it is a character flaw &amp; don't want to be stigmatized </p>
<p>d. blame themselves for their symptoms &amp; are too ashamed to seek 
help </p>
<p>e. worry that treatment is too costly </p>
<p>f. a side effect of some medications often prescribed to older 
people </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>2 - 80% of depressed individuals can be effectively treated </p>
<p>3 - medications/antidepressants </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. tricyclics </p>
<p>b. monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) </p>
<p>c. selective serotonin re-uptake inhibitors (SSRIs) </p>
<p>d. norepinephrine &amp; serotonin reuptake inhibitors (NSRIs) </p>
</blockquote>
<p>4 - psychotherapy </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. used with patients who have significant life crises, lack social 
support, or lack coping skills to deal with life situations </p>
<p>b. especially useful with the elderly because they often live alone, 
have inadequate support systems, or have no contact with a primary 
care physician </p>
</blockquote>
<p>5 - electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. important because 70% of elderly patients fail to take 25 to 50% 
of their medication </p>
<p>b. commonly given to patients over 61 years old </p>
<p>c. generally underused, unavailable, or burdened with myth &amp; 
misinformation</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
