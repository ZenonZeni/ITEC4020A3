<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>The Executive Bonus Plan: Insurance Benefits for Valued Executives</STRONG></FONT><P>


<STRONG>Executive Bonus Plan: What Is It?</STRONG><BR>
Every successful business has at least one executive and, often, more than one
individual whose
contribution to
the business is invaluable. Naturally, you value their contribution and want to
provide these employees
with an
added incentive package that can help tie them to your business. One such incentive
is an Executive
Bonus Plan.
An Executive Bonus Plan, also known as a Section 162 plan, is a tax deductible way
for you, the
employer, to
selectively provide life insurance and supplemental retirement benefits to your
valued executives. <BR>
<P>
<IMG SRC="/nyl/images/pscpbp.gif" ALT="Graph" ALIGN=LEFT BORDER="0">

<STRONG>Benefits to the Employer </STRONG><BR>
An Executive Bonus Plan offers an employer the following features and benefits:
<P>
<UL>
<LI>Tax deductible.
<LI>Covers select executives.
<LI>Does not require IRS approval.
<LI>Simple to create.
<LI>By funding with life insurance, the executive has an added incentive to stay
with the company, often
referred to
as "Golden Handcuffs."
<LI>The amount of life insurance coverage on select executives can vary.
</UL>
<P>

<STRONG>Benefits to the Owner/Employee</STRONG><BR>
As owner, you also have the right to apply for and own a life insurance policy on
your life.
<P>
<UL>
<LI>You own the policy.
<LI>If the corporation's tax bracket is higher than the owner/employee, more
after-tax dollars will be
available to
pay the premium.
<LI>Death benefit proceeds provide a source of income to your family upon death.
</UL>
<P>

<STRONG>Benefits to the Employee</STRONG><BR>
The select executive will receive many valuable benefits from this type of plan:
<P>

<UL>
<LI>Executive owns the life insurance and any cash values.
<LI>Personal insurance needs are met with business dollars. 
<LI>The policy's cash value can be used to supplement retirement income.
<LI>Death benefit proceeds may be income tax free.
<LI>Death benefit proceeds can be used for estate settlement costs or as a source
of funds for a buy-sell
agreement.
</UL>
<P>

<STRONG>How Does an Executive Bonus Plan Work?</STRONG><BR>
In this arrangement, an executive applies for and owns a personal life insurance
policy. The employer
pays the key
executive's life insurance premiums, either directly to the insurance company or as
a bonus to the
executive. In
most cases, the premiums are deductible to the company as a business expense.




<P>
<FONT SIZE="-1">Note: Although the premiums are included in the executive's current
income, the death
benefit
may be income tax
free.
</FONT>
