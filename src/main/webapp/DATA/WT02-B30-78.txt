<FONT SIZE=+1><STRONG>Option 4</STRONG></FONT><BR>
<STRONG>Leave Your Money in Your Employer's Plan (If Allowed by Plan)</STRONG><P>
Some employers' plans allow exiting employees to leave their money in the plan.
That
enables
you to put off until some future date your decision on how to take your
distribution. This
has some of the same advantages as a rollover IRA, such as continued tax deferral
and
protection from current taxes. You'll also still have the right to use the special
tax
treatment at a later date.<P>
This option has certain disadvantages, though. Your investment choices are limited
to
whatever is available within your employer's plan. These investment choices may not
be
right
for you now. Also, access to your money may be restricted, according to the plan
provisions.<P>
If the plan allows, you may be able to arrange for periodic payments
to provide you with income. The amount you receive becomes taxable as income, but
the
remainder can still be at work for you, accumulating tax deferred. However,
periodic
payments
may
cause you to become ineligible for moving your money into another employer's plan.
You'll
need to check with your plan to determine what limitations may apply.
