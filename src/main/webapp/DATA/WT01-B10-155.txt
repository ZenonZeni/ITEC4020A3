<h3><font color="#0000FF">Compost's Agricultural Value</font></h3>
<p><font color="#0000FF">At <strong>SKB Environmental</strong>'s </font><a
href="ABOUTNEW.HTM#Dakota"><font color="#0000FF">new facility</font></a><font
color="#0000FF"> for yard waste and other source separated
compostable materials, we are engaged in comparative research on
end uses for yard waste compost and source separated organics
compost. focused on the viability of using compost or land
applied yard waste as soil amendments for agricultural
production. Current research includes identification of baseline
soil characteristics and testing of compost applications to
determine whether it is possible to obtain an NPK
(nitrogen/phosphorous/potassium) benefit comparable to
application of commercial fertilizers. Research entails analysis
of release rates throughout the growing cycle and tracking crop
yields, testing after crops are removed to check residual
nutrients as well as their availability and mobility compared to
commercial fertilizers. Testing is conducted at different soil
depths to also measure nutrient migration. Analysis also measures
changes in soil characteristics such as moisture holding capacity
resulting from compost or yard waste applications.</font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF">Rick O'Gara, President of <strong>SKB
Environmental</strong>: &quot;We are happy to share information
about our program with persons who have an interest in composting
and agricultural end uses. Waste professionals, agricultural
researchers, and the public are welcome to schedule a tour of the
facility. As research results become available, they'll be made
available in our <i>What's New</i> section.&quot; </font></p>
<h3><font color="#0000FF">Landfill Operator Training </font></h3>
<p><font color="#0000FF">When the Minnesota Pollution Control
Agency developed a separate certification program for
construction/demolition landfill operators five years ago, the
MPCA turned to <strong>SKB Environmental</strong> for help
teaching the course.</font></p>
<p><font color="#0000FF">Six times each year, our staff provide
training in facility operations, management, equipment and
maintenance, and regulatory reporting. &quot;Making our knowledge
and experience available to improve our industry is what <strong>SKB
Environmental</strong> is all about,&quot; John Domke, Vice
President for Operations.</font></p>
