<h3 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h3>
<h3 align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) </b></font></h3>
<table cellpadding=15 cellspacing=15 width=100%>
<tr><td valign=top width=50%><h4><font color="#FF0000">Basic Facts</font></h4>
<p>1 - legislation intended to prohibit discrimination 
against people with disabilities in employment, 
state &amp; local government activities, public 
accommodations, public transportation, 
telecommunications, &amp; public services </p>
<p>2 - definition of disability includes people with 
mental illness </p>
<p>3 - &quot;disability&quot; defined by the ADA </p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>a. a physical or mental 
impairment that substantially 
limits one or more life activities </em></p>
<p><em>b. having a record of impairment 
of one or more life activities </em></p>
<p><em>c. being regarded as having an 
impairment of one or more life 
activities </em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>4 - &quot;mental impairment&quot; defined by the ADA </p>
<blockquote>
<p><em>any mental or psychological 
disorder, such as mental 
retardation, organic brain 
syndrome, emotional or mental 
illness, &amp; specific learning 
disabilities </em></p>
</blockquote>
<hr>
<h4><font color="#FF0000">Who to Call about an ADA Violation </font></h4>
<p>1 - a private attorney </p>
<p>2 - Equal Employment Opportunities Commission 
(EEOC):1-800-669-4000 </p>
<p>3 - U.S. Department of Justice: 1-800-514-0301 
(Titles II &amp; III) </p>
<p>4 - The President's Committee on Employment of 
People with Disabilities: 202-376-6200 </p>
<p>5 - Job Accommodation Network: 
1-800-526-7234 </p>
<p>6 - Bazelon Center for Mental Health Law: 
202-467-5730 </p>
<p>7 - Disability Rights Education &amp; Defense Fund: 
202-986-0375 </p>
<p>8 - The designated protection &amp; advocacy agency 
in your state </p>
<p>9 - legal services (legal aid) in your area </p>
<hr>

<p>2 - <u>The ADA and People with Mental Illness: A

<p>3 - <u>A Technical Assistance Manual on the

Center for Mental Health Law. 202-467-5730 </p>
</td><td valign=top width=50%><h4><font color="#FF0000">Title I (Employment) </font></h4>
<h6>
<p>1 - applies to private employers with 15 or more 
employees, state &amp; local governments, 
employment agencies, labor organizations, &amp; 
management committees </p>
<p>2 - does not apply to the federal government </p>
<p>3 - protects qualified individuals who don't need 
more than reasonable accommodation </p>
<p>4 - you must disclose your disability in order to 
be protected by the ADA </p>
<p>a. employers are obligated to make reasonable 
accommodation only if they are aware of one's 
disability </p>
<p>b. it is the responsibility of the employee to 
inform the employer that accommodation is 
needed </p>
<p>5 - the Hiring Process </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. the ADA forbids blanket 
inquiries into your 
medical/psychiatric history </p>
<p>b. an employer may only ask 
objective questions about the 
duties of the job </p>
</blockquote>
<p>6 - employers are required to provide reasonable 
accommodations for qualified individuals with 
disabilities </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. self-paced workloads </p>
<p>b. flexible hours </p>
<p>c. modified job responsibilities </p>
<p>d. paid or unpaid leave for 
hospitalization or incapacity </p>
<p>e. a supportive &amp; understanding 
supervisor </p>
<p>f. easy access to supervision &amp; 
supports </p>
<p>g. frequent guidance &amp; feedback </p>
</blockquote>
<p>7 - covers recruitment, hiring, job assignment, 
pay, lay-off, firing, training, promotion, benefits, 
&amp; leave </p>
<p>8 - Two Exceptions </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. an employer is not required to 
provide an accommodation if it 
will impose an undue hardship on 
the operation of the business (ie. 
too costly, extensive, substantial, 
or disruptive) or will 
fundamentally alter the nature or 
operation of the business </p>
<p>b. an employer may refuse to 
employ or accommodate an 
individual who poses a direct 
threat to the health or safety of 
others in the workplace </p>
</blockquote>
<p>9 - Filing Complaints </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. file a charge with the Equal 
Employment Opportunity 
Commission (EEOC) within 180 
days of the alleged act </p>
<p>b. the EEOC may mediate &amp; 
negotiate a settlement </p>
<p>c. the EEOC may either file a 
lawsuit or issue a &quot;right to sue&quot; 
letter </p>
<p>d. you may file a lawsuit in a 
federal district court </p>
</blockquote>
<h4><font color="#FF0000">Title II (State &amp; Local Governments)</font> </h4>
<p>1 - prohibits discrimination against qualified 
individuals in all programs, activities, &amp; services </p>
<p>2 - eliminate discriminatory eligibility criteria 
unless necessary for the provision of the service </p>
<p>3 - Filing a Complaint </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. file a complaint with the U.S. 
Department of Justice/call 
1-800-514-0301 </p>
<p>b. file a lawsuit in a federal 
district court </p>
<p>c. compensatory damages for pain 
&amp; suffering may be awarded </p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>d. reasonable attorney's fees may 
be awarded </p>
</blockquote>
<h4><font color="#FF0000">Title III (Public Accommodations) </font></h4>
<p>1 - applies to any place of public accommodation </p>
<p>2 - applies to any person who owns, leases, or 
operates a place of public accommodation </p>
<p>3 - includes a wide range of entities such as 
restaurants, hotels, theaters, medical offices, 
pharmacies, retail stores, or museums </p>
<p>4 - Filing a Complaint </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. file a complaint with the U.S. 
Department of Justice/call 
1-800-514-0301 </p>
<p>b. file a private lawsuit in a 
federal district court </p></h6>
</blockquote>
