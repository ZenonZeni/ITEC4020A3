This 12-page paper gives a series of concrete proposals for treatment

<p><b>Accardo, Pasquale J. <i>The Invisible Disability: Understanding Learning
Disabilities in the Context of Health and Education.</i></b> National Health
&amp; Education Consortium in partnership with LDA, 1996. ($9.00, including
postage and handling.)

<blockquote>
<p>This 50-page paper is oriented towards education, health care professionals,
parents, and policy-makers. It dispels <a
href="articles/id/myths.html">myths and misconceptions
about learning disabilities</a> and identifies 
<a href="articles/id/causes.html">select
causes of school failure</a>. It explains: 

<ul>
<li>how learning disabilities are defined; 
<li>how students with learning disabilities and their families are affected;
    and 
<li>how learning disabilities can be addressed by interdisciplinary teams
of health and educational professionals.
</ul>

<p>Dr. Pasquale is the editor of <i>Developmental Milestones</i>, a newsletter
of the Society for Developmental Pediatrics. He has published 20 books
and over 50 articles on learning disabilities and other developmental concerns.
</blockquote>

<p><b>Hagin, Rosa A. and Waterhouse, Lynn <i>Teaching
Reading: Definitions of Commonly Used Terms</i></b>
<blockquote>
This booklet defines the terms used by educators to describe
aspects of teaching reading. Reading is  the  most difficult and the
most important skill  required of young children to learn, and there have
been thousands of studies of the reading process, and hundreds of programs
developed for teaching reading. Thus it is not surprising that there
are many terms associated with the teaching of reading.
<p> The definitions included in this booklet are organized by five
topics:
<ol>
<li>components of the teaching process
<li>approaches to teaching reading
<li>reading material
<li>reading classroom organization
<li>measuring reading skill
</ol>
<p>(17-page booklet. $4.00, including shipping and handling fees.)
</blockquote>

<h2><a name="Toll_free"></a>Toll-Free Resources for Adults with Learning
Disabilities</h2>

<p>There seem to be countless toll-free numbers offering bits of information,
guidance and suggestions. Despite the useful services that these toll-free
numbers provide, many people are confused about exactly what information
they can expect to receive. The following resource list, compiled by the
National Adult Literacy and Learning Disabilities Center, is designed to
give an overview of what information the following organizations give to
their callers. Information specific to learning disabilities is printed
in italics. </p>

<blockquote>
<p><b>American Association for Vocational and Instructional Materials</b>
1-800-228-4689 
<br>American Association for Vocational Instructional Materials has an
operator, available from 8:00am-5:00pm Eastern Time (ET) Monday-Friday,
who provides information on educational materials (software, videos, manuals),
including a <i>listing of Category L Modules that deal with teaching students
with special needs.</i> Products are available for purchase. </p>

<p><b>Equal Employment Opportunity Commission</b> 1-800-669-3362 
<br>Equal Employment Opportunity Commisssion (EEOC) voice mail directs
calls from 7am-5:30pm ET (Monday-Friday). Operators accept orders for publications,
fact sheets, posters, and a resource directory for people with disabilities,
<i>including learning disabilities.</i> They do not answer questions relating
to employment but can give referrals to local EEOC offices. </p>

<p><b>ERIC Clearinghouse on Adult, Career, and Vocational Education at
the Center for Employment, Education &amp; Training at Ohio State University</b>
1-800-848-4815 
<br>ERIC Clearinghouse on Adult, Career, and Vocational Education has a
24-hour voice mail service that provides information on ERIC Digests, annotated
bibliographies, and assorted publications, <i>some with information on
learning disabilities.</i> ERIC does not answer specific questions on disabilities,
local programs, or jobs. </p>

<p><b>Federal Student Aid Information Center</b> 1-800-433-3243 
<br>Federal Financial Aid Hot Line accepts calls from 9am-8pm ET(Monday-Friday)
and provides information on eligibility, benefits, applications and other
questions about Student Aid. Voice mail directs calls during business hours.
</p>

<p><b>GED Hotline</b> 1-800-629-9433 
<br>General Education Development (GED) Hotline has a 24-hour operator
service that provides information on local GED classes and testing services.
<i>They have an accomodations guide for people taking the GED who have
a learning disability</i> </p>

<p><b>HEATH Resource Center</b> 1-800-544-3284 
<br>National Clearinghouse on Postsecondary Eduction for Individuals with
Disabilities (HEATH Resource Center) has information specialists available
from 9am-5pm ET(Monday-Friday) who provide resource papers, directories,
information on national organizations, and <i>a resource directory for
people with learning disabilities.</i> </p>

<p><b>Job Accommodation Network</b> 1-800-526-7324 
<br>Job Accommodation Network (JAN) has a free consulting service from
8am-8pm ET(Monday-Thursday) and 8am-5pm ET(Friday) that provides information
on: equipment, methods and modifications for persons with disabilities
to improve their work environment. All information is specific to the disability,
<i>including learning disabilities.</i> </p>

<p><b>Learning Resources Network</b> 1-800-678-5376 
<br>Learning Resources Network (LERN) has an operator service from 8am-5pm
ET(Monday-Friday) that provides informaton to practitioners of adult continuing
education. The also give consulting information, take orders for publications,
and provide phone numbers of assocations and organizations that deal with
<i>learning disabilities</i>. </p>

<p><b>National Center for Research in Vocational Education</b> 1-800-762-4093

<br>National Center for Research in Vocational Education has an operator
available from 8am-5pm Pacific Time(PT)(Monday-Friday) who provides information
on products, electronic services, and vocational education. A catalog and
newsletter are also available. <i>The Office of Student Services produces
materials on learning disabilites and a sub-catalog for special populations</i>
</p>

<p><b>National Library Services for the Blind and Physically Handicapped</b>
1-800-424-8567 
<br>National Library Services for the Blind and Physically Handicapped
has a voice mail system that directs calls from 8am-4:30pm ET (Monday-Friday).
Operators provide information on audiocasette, large print, and Braille
books and magazines for recreational reading. <i>Callers with learning
disabilities must meet certain guidelines to use these services.</i> </p>

<p><b>National Literacy Hotline</b> 1-800-228-8813 
<br>Literacy Hotline has a 24-hour bilingual (Spanish/English) operator
service that provides information on: literacy/education classes, GED testing
services, volunteer organizations, and a <i>learning disabilities brochure.</i>
</p>

<p><b>Orton Dyslexia Society</b> 1-800-222-3123 
<br>Orton Dyslexia Society (ODS) has a 24-hour voice mail service that
receives information requests. From 8:30am-4:30pm ET (Monday-Friday) at
(410)296-0232, ODS staff direct people to appropriate materials about issues
of dyslexia. Information is given on publications about dyslexia, referrals
for testing and tutors, <i>branches of ODS, and workshops and conferences</i>.
</p>

<p><b>Recording for the Blind and Dyslexic, Inc.</b> 1-800-221-4792 
<br>Recording for the Blind and Dyslexic, Inc. has operators available
from 8:30am-7:pm (Monday-Friday) who provide information on over 80,000
recorded textbooks (in 4-track cassette or computer disk) and other classroom
materials, from 4th grade through post-graduate levels, available on loan;
the application process and one time fee ($37.50); and the certification
process. <i>Callers with learning disabilities are eligible to participate
but must complete the certification requirements.</i> </p>

<p><b>Social Security Administration</b> 1-800-772-1313, 1-800-325-0778(TT)

<br>Representatives answer calls from 7am-7pm ET (Monday-Friday) and provide
information on a wide range of Social Security and Supplemental Security
Income matters. A limited number of automated services are available 24
hours a day. Bilingual (Spanish/English) services are also available. </p>

<p><b>National Library of Education at the US Office of Educational Research
and Improvement</b> 1-800-424-1616 
<br>US Office of Educational Research and Imrprovement has an operator
available from 9am-4pm ET (Monday-Friday) and provides information on statistics
on education and schools, publications, references to other agencies, and
<i>references to a specialist on learning disabilities.</i> </p>
</blockquote>
