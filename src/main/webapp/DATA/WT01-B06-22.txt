<B><FONT SIZE=5 COLOR="#AF0000">Options Available</FONT></B>

<P ALIGN=CENTER><TABLE BORDER=0 CELLPADDING=1 WIDTH=485>
   <TR>
      <TD>
         <P>There are actually four options available for couples
         looking to select a surrogate, and for women considering
         being in our program. They are as follows:
         
         <OL>
            <LI>Artificial insemination (AI): The surrogate is
            artificially inseminated with the sperm from the husband
            of the couple. The child is genetically related to the
            surrogate. The wife of the couple adopts the child via a
            step-parent adoption.
            <BR>
<BR>

            <LI>In vitro fertilization/Embryo transfer (IVF/ET): An
            egg (or eggs) is retrieved from the wife of the couple,
            combined with her husband's sperm, and the resulting
            embryo(s) is implanted in the surrogate. The child is not
            genetically related to the surrogate, and the couple's
            names go on the original birth certificate.
            <BR>
<BR>

            <LI>Egg Donor (ED): The "surrogate" donates an egg (or
            eggs) which is fertilized with the husband's sperm, and
            the embryo is then transferred to his wife. The child is
            genetically related to the surrogate, but because the
            wife delivers the child, she does not need to adopt it.
            This is sort of a reverse surrogacy.
            <BR>
<BR>

            <LI>Artificial Insemination by Donor (AID): The surrogate
            is artificially inseminated with donor sperm, because
            both the husband and the wife of the couple are
            infertile. The child is genetically related to the
            surrogate, and both the husband and wife must adopt it.
         </OL>
