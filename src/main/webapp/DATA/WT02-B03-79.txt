<H1 ALIGN=CENTER><FONT COLOR="#191970">The Oleander Project</FONT></H1>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<P><A HREF="Gifs/bigole.gif"><IMG SRC="Gifs/bigole.gif" BORDER=0 HEIGHT=96 WIDTH=141 ALIGN=LEFT></A>The
Oleander project has been a cooperative effort involving <A HREF="tom.html">Dr.
Tom Rossby</A>, <A HREF="http://satori.gso.uri.edu/docs/cornillon.html">Dr.
Peter Cornillon</A>, <A HREF="erik.html">Dr. Erik Gottlieb</A>, <A HREF="http://www.oasd.bnl.gov/rosters/flagg.html">Dr.
Charlie Flagg</A> (<A HREF="http://www.bnl.gov">Brookhaven National Laboratory</A>),
<A HREF="george.html">George Schwartze</A> and <A HREF="sandy.html">Sandra
Anderson-Fontana</A>, and the <A HREF="Gifs/bigole.gif">M/V <I>Oleander</I></A><I>
</I>operators (<A HREF="http://www.bermuda.bm/bcl">Bermuda Container Lines</A>)
and crew. </P>

<P><BR CLEAR=BOTH>
</P>

<P>Since August 1992 the container vessel M/V <I>Oleander</I>, which transits
weekly between New Jersey and Bermuda, has been collecting upper ocean
current velocity and surface temperature data using an acoustic Doppler
current profiler (ADCP) automated by special software (AutoADCP). Along
its transit the ship crosses the Slope Water, Gulf Stream, and part of
the Sargasso Sea, and thus offers the unprecedented opportunity to measure
the local flow of these waters on a repeated long-term basis. The project's
goal is to establish a multi-year dataset that will permit detailed studies
of seasonal and interannual variability of the upper ocean velocity and
temperature fields. </P>

<P>The difficulties of operating an ADCP on a container vessel, such as
bubble attenuation, electrical problems, and navigational errors, are described
in Flagg et al. (<I>J. Atmos. Oceanic Tech.</I>, 1996 submitted). Combining
data from attitudinal GPS (AGPS) and ADCP `bottom tracking' mode has yielded
transducer misalignment angle and speed scaling factor to within 0.07 deg
and 0.3%, respectively. The velocities collected using AGPS have most navigational
errors removed, and have produced information on the magnitude and extent
of gyrocompass errors in the pre-AGPS (pre-June 1995) data. </P>

<P>The data are being processed using the publicly available CODAS (Common
Oceanographic Data Access System) data processing software developed at
the <A HREF="http://www.soest.hawaii.edu">University of Hawaii</A> by <A HREF="http://www.soest.hawaii.edu/~efiring/">Dr.
Eric Firing,</A> Julie Ranada, Ramon Cabrera and others.</P>

<P>An example of velocity vector plots, from an outbound cruise during
the first week of January 1997, can be seen by clicking <A HREF="Gifs/vectors.gif">here</A>.
Vectors are plotted at depths of 25m, 50m, 100m and 200m. The location
of the Gulf Stream is obvious from the strong vectors, up to nearly 200
cm/sec to the northeast.</P>
