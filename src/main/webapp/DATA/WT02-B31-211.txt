<FONT SIZE=5>Michael Collins</FONT><BR></A>
An epic drama with Liam Neeson fighting for Irish Independance and winning an acting award in the process.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>The Mirror Has Two Faces</FONT><BR></A>
...and Barbra Streisand wears four hats as star, director, producer and songwriter for her new comedy romance.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5><A HREF="../promo/daylight.html">Daylight</A></FONT><BR>
A tunnel below the Hudson River is sealed by an explosion and it's up to Sylvester Stallone to lead the trapped commuters out of the dark and the rising damp.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5><A HREF="evita.html">Evita</A></FONT><BR></A>
Madonna finally gets her chance to sing <I> Don't Cry For Me Argentina </I> from the Balcony - and the crowd love it.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>The Portrait of a Lady</FONT><BR>
The long-awaited union of director Jane Campion and Nicole Kidman in an elegantly realised, superbly performed work.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>Extreme Measures</FONT><BR>
Hugh Grant is a doctor in trouble, but it's not for laughs in this deadly serious, high suspense thriller.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>Chain Reaction</FONT><BR>
Paranoia runs rampant and Keanu Reeve cannot be sure whom to trust (even when it's Morgan Freeman) after he is charged with murder and sabotage. <P>

<FONT SIZE=5>A Very Brady Sequel</FONT><BR>
Living in the '70s and loving it.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>Children of the Revolution</FONT><BR>
Judy Davis is sensational in a diabolically funny retelling of an Australian history that never was.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>The Ghost and the Darkness</FONT><BR>
Michael Douglas and Val Kilmer hunt two killer kings of the jungle in a true and extraordinary adventure thriller.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5><A HREF="../promo/romeo.html">William Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet</A></FONT><BR>
...Or, more correctly, Buz Luhrmann's strictly stylish new approach to the classic.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>Grace of My Heart</FONT><BR>
The life and loves of a singer/songwriter played out against the musical styles of the '50s, '60s and '70s.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>That Thing You Do!</FONT><BR>
A rock band's brief moment of fame in 1964 as told by Tom Hanks in his directional debut.<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>The Associate</FONT><BR>
Whoopi Goldberg wows Wall Street... as a man!<P>

<FONT SIZE=5>Looking For Richard</FONT><BR>
Al Pacino dissects Shakespeare with facinating results.<P>
