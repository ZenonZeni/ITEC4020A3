<p><strong>Why <a href="/office/utility">Utility</a> Energy Use Matters</strong>
<ul>
<lh></lh>
<li>The utility sector is a big influence on the U.S. economy.  Electric and gas utilities represent a national capital investment portfolio of $790 billion.

<li>Utilities are significant energy consumers, accounting for 55 percent of total U.S. 
energy consumption.

<li>Utilities provide roughly $260 billion each year in energy services to homes, 
businesses and industries.  This is 5.2 percent of the gross national product, an average 
expenditure of $235 per month for each household in America.

<li>The production and consumption of energy by utilities has a significant impact on the 
environment.  For example, carbon dioxide emissions attributed to primary energy use by 
utilities constitute 33 percent of the U.S. total.

<li><a href="http://www.nrel.gov/documents/profiles.html">Renewable energy technologies</a> will help 
utilities assure clean and ample energy 
supplies to meet America's growing demand, while making our economy less vulnerable to 
swings in fossil fuel prices.

<li>Because they are cleaner than conventional power plants, 
renewable energy systems can 
help utilities and industries meet environmental goals.

<li>Renewable energy systems are reliable, versatile and flexible.

<li>The need for renewable energy worldwide is creating dynamic business opportunities for
the U.S. renewable energy industry.
</ul>

<p><strong><a href="/office/utility">DOE's Programs</a> . . . </strong>
<ul>
<lh></lh>
<li>Help develop new sources of clean energy, including energy efficiency and renewable 
energy technologies.

<li>Assist utilities in long-range planning.

<li>Will save $14.5 billion in utility energy by the year 2000.

<li>Help U.S. industries develop technologies to capture part of the $1 trillion global 
market for electric generation systems.
</ul>
