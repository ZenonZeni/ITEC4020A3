<p><b>What are the Diagnostic Criteria for Anorexia Nervosa?</b> </p>
<p>The following are required for the diagnosis of anorexia nervosa: </p>
<ul>
<li>being underweight (less than 85% of <a href="metropol.htm">expected body weight</a>)</li>
<li>loss of menstrual periods, or menses present only following hormone administration 
(e.g., estrogen or birth control pills) </li>
<li>intense fear of gaining weight or becoming fat, even though underweight </li>
<li>distorted body image, undue influence of body weight or shape on self-evaluation, or 
denial of the seriousness of the current low body weight </li>
</ul>
<p>These criteria are established by the American Psychiatric Association and published in 
the <i>Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fourth Edition</i>. </p>
