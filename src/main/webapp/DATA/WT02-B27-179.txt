<HR>You have been browsing home pages, including the <a href="index.html">VESS homepage</a>. It contains many underlined phrases, including the one that you just clicked on to get here.(At least, if you are using a WWW browser such as <i>Netscape</i> or <i>Mosaic</i> the phrases are underlined.).<p>If you click on an underlined phrase, you will be taken to a place that will give you information about that phrase. You will be taken to:<ul><li>another place in the page you are viewing, or<li>another page in the same WWW site, or<li>another WWW site entirely</ul>

<a name="move">
<hr><h3>MOVING WITHIN A HOME PAGE:</h3>You can move around as follows:<ul><li>Long pages often have a table of contents at the top. Clicking on one of them will take you down the page to a specific topic.<li>Entering a <b>Ctrl Home</b> (holding the Ctrl key down and pressing the home key) moves you to the top of the page.<li>Entering a <b>Ctrl End</b> moves you to the bottom of the page.<li>Pressing the <b>PgUp key</b> moves up one screen full<li>Pressing the <b>PgDn key</b> moves down one screen full<li>If you click/hold on the little <b>square box</b> at the right of the screen, you can move the box up and down in order to scan through the page.<li>Clicking on the <b>browser's Back key</b> at the top of the screen movesyou back to the previous page that you previously viewed <li>Clicking on the <b>browser's Forward key</b> at the top of the screen moves you forward to the next page that you previously viewed</ul><hr>

<a name="save">
<h3>SAVING A HOME PAGE IN A FILE:</h3><ul><li>In <i>Netscape</i>:<ul> <li>Click on <b>File</b> at the upper left of the screen.

<li>Click on <b>Save as...</b> <li>Select the drive and directory where you want to store the page <li>Click on <b>Save File as Type:</b>. You can select <ul>  <li><b>Source</b> which will save the home page in HTML format with all of its imbedded control codes. This is great for later viewing on your browser, but terrible for printing out.  <li><b>Plain Text</b> which removes the codes and saves the pageas easily readable form for later editing or printing.</ul> <li>Select the file name and click on <b>OK</b></ul><li>In <i>Mosaic</i>:<ul> <li>Click on <b>File</b> at the upper left of the screen.  <li>Click on <b>Document Source</b> <li>Select the drive, directory and file name where you want to store the page. <i>Mosaic</i> will save the home page in HTML format with imbedded control codes.</ul></ul>

<a name="wp">
<h3>Producing a wordprocesser document containing the material you need from the web.</h3>


<br><BR>1.If necessary, print this page first.

<BR><BR>2.On your keyboard, hold down <cite>Alt</cite> and press <cite>Esc</cite> repeatedly until <strong>Program Manager</strong> appears (Or Alt+Tab until the logo appears).

<BR><BR>3.Open <strong>Write</strong> or another Windows-based Wordprocessor.

<BR><BR>4.Hold down <cite>Alt</cite> and press <cite>Esc</cite> until <strong>Netscape</strong> reappears.

<BR><BR>5.Open the page you need (use a <a href="wwwmarks.html#comp">Search engine</a>) to find the info if necessary).

<BR><BR>6.Highlight all the relevant text by dragging the mouse left button or by using shift and the cursers.

<BR><BR>7.On your keyboard, hold down the <cite>Control</cite> key and then press "c" (copies text to the clipboard).

<BR><BR>8.Hold down <cite>Alt</cite> and press <cite>Esc</cite> until your <strong>wordprocessor</strong> appears.

<BR><BR>9.Click on where you want text to begin, then hold down the <cite>Control</cite> key and then press "v" (pastes from the clipboard).

<BR><BR>10.Use <cite>Alt</cite> and <cite>Esc</cite> to return to <strong>Netscape</strong>.

<BR><BR>11.Travel in Netscape until you find the next piece of text you want to use.

<BR><BR>12.Repeat steps 6 to 11 until you have pasted all the text of interest to your <strong>wordprocessor</strong>.

<BR><BR>13.Edit, save or print your wordprocessor document.

<a name="home">
<h3>Making VESS FastAccess your homepage</h3>

<li><b>Netscape 2.0</b>
<ol>
<li> Click "Options" on menu bar
<li> Click "General Preferences..."
<li> Select "Appearance" tab
<li> Type "www.euthanasia.org/fastaccs.html" as your "Start With: Home Page Location"
<li> In the OPTIONS Menu:
<li> Click SAVE OPTIONS

</ol><br>
<li><b>Netscape 1.2 and previous versions</b>
<ol>
<li> Click menu bar "Options"
<li> Click "Preferences..."
<li> Select pull-down menu "Styles"
<li> Type "www.euthanasia.org/fastaccs.html" as your "Start With: Home Page Location"
<li> Add FastAccess to your bookmarks by clicking on menu bar "Bookmarks," then click "Add Bookmark."
</ol><br>
<li><b>Microsoft Internet Explorer</b>
<ol>
<li> Go to <A HREF="http://www.euthanasia.org/fastaccs.html">Fast Access</A> start page
<li> Click "View" on menu bar
<li> Click "Options"
<li> Select "Start and Search Pages" tab
<li> Click button "Use Current" then click "Apply"
<li> You may do this for both Start and Search Pages
</ol><br>
<li><b><a Name="AOL">AOL Web Browser:</b>
<ol>
<li>Add Fast Access as your start-up page by clicking "Prefs"
<li>Change Home Page location to "www.euthanasia.org/fastaccs.html"
</ol><br>
<li><b><a Name="WEBEXP">WebExplorer (OS2 Warp)</b>
<ol>
<li> Click menu bar "Configure"
<li> Click "Servers"
<li> Type "http://www.euthanasia.org/fastaccs.html" as your "Home Document URL"
<li> Click "Load at startup?" checkbox
</ol><br>
<li><b><a Name="MOSAIC">NCSA Mosaic</b>
<ol>
<li> Click menu bar "Options"
<li> Click "Preferences..."
<li> Select "Document" tab
<li> Type "http://www.euthanasia.org/fastaccs.html" as your "Home Page"
</ol><br>
<li><b>Spry Mosaic</b>
<ol>
<li> Click menu bar "Options"
<li> Click "Configuration"
<li> Type "http://www.euthanasia.org/fastaccs.html" at the "URL:" prompt
</ol><br>
</ul>
