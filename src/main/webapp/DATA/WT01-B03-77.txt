<dd><h3 align=center>Benjamin M. Rosen<br>
Chairman </h3>
</dd>
</dl>
<p>Benjamin M. Rosen is chairman of Rosen Motors and a director of 
Capstone Turbine. He is also chairman of Compaq Computer Corporation, 
the world&#146;s largest personal computer company. <br>
<br>
In addition, Mr. Rosen is chairman and partner of Sevin Rosen Funds, a 
venture capital firm managing a multi-million dollar portfolio. Sevin Rosen 
has invested in more than 80 technology start-up companies, including 
Borland International, Citrix, Compaq Computer, Convex Computer, 
Cypress Semiconductor, Cyrix, Electronic Arts, Landmark Graphics, Lotus 
Development, Proteon, Quarterdeck Office Systems and Silicon Graphics. <br>
<br>
Previously, Mr. Rosen was a vice president and senior electronics analyst at 
Morgan Stanley &amp; Co. He started his career as an electronics engineer at Raytheon and Sperry Gyroscope. <br>
<br>
In 1992, <i>Computerworld</i> magazine chose Mr. Rosen as one of 25 people in the computer industry who &#147;changed the world.&#148; 
Also in 1992, <i>Computer Reseller News</i> selected him as the No. 1 executive in the personal computer industry and featured 
him as one of the 10 &#147;Legends of the PC Industry.&#148;<br>
<br>
Mr. Rosen is vice chairman of the Board of Trustees of the California Institute of Technology, a member of the Board of 
Managers of Memorial Sloan-Kettering Cancer Center and a member of the Board of Overseers of Columbia Business 
School.<br>
<br>
Born in New Orleans, Mr. Rosen holds a B.S. in electrical engineering from the California Institute of Technology, an M.S. in 
electrical engineering from Stanford University and an M.B.A. degree from Columbia Business School.</p>
