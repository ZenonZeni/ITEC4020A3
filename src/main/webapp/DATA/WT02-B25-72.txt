<h4 align=center>Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>
<h4 align=center><font color="#000080"><b>Veterans</b></font></h4>
<hr>
<p><font color="#FF0000">NAMI's Advocacy Activities </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - 1990, helped reverse the congressional decision to cut $800,000,000 in 
psychiatric care for dependents of active-duty military </p>
<p>2 - 1991, supported the Disabled American Veterans (DAV) class-action lawsuit 
to restore $125,000,000 for 13,5000 &quot;incompetent&quot; veterans </p>
<p>3 - since 1994, has had representatives on the Undersecretary for Health's 
Special Committee for the Seriously Mentally Ill Veteran </p>
<p>4 - since 1994, has sent presenters to the Veterans Affairs' National Training 
Program continuing education workshops </p>
<p>5 - every year, provides expert testimony at congressional committee hearings 
regarding veterans with serious mental illness </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">NAMI Offers Support </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - provides expertise in developing family support groups </p>
<p>2 - provides the latest data about serious mental illness </p>
<p>3 - serves as an information clearinghouse </p>
<p>4 - assists with finding the 200,000 homeless veterans </p>
</blockquote>
<p><font color="#FF0000">How Veterans &amp; their Families Can get Help </font></p>
<blockquote>
<p>1 - how to get medical care </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. contact your local Department of Veterans Affairs </p>
<p>b. supply information that you were honorably discharged (Form 
DD-214) </p>
<p>c. complete a financial statement for the past year </p>
<p>d. you must have completed at least 2 years if you enlisted on or 
after October 17, 1981 </p>
</blockquote>
<p>2 - financial compensation </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. if you are disabled by injury or disease incurred in or aggravated 
by active service in the line of duty </p>
<p>b. payments are based on the degree of disability </p>
<p>c. additional money for your dependents and/or spouse is available 
if your service-connected disabilities are evaluated as a 30% or 
more disabling </p>
</blockquote>
<p>3 - the Department of Veterans Affairs will help you file your claim for benefits </p>
<blockquote>
<p>a. build your case from the ground up </p>
<p>b. review all pertinent laws, regulations, precedents, &amp; medical 
histories </p>
<p>c. prepare claim forms &amp; briefs </p>
<p>d. assemble the required paperwork </p>
<p>e. represent you before government boards </p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
