<H2><I>D. </I>Conclusion<I></H2>

</I><BR>
The best industrial efficiency programs start with employee suggestions, 
which subsequently must be supported by determined management and by 
federal, local, or utility financing and technical support. Communicating 
to plant staff members that energy efficiency is a priority and 
encouraging them to identify energy-saving opportunities taps the know-how 
of those closest to the manufacturing process - which can have 
extraordinary results.<BR>
<p>     A key lesson from these industrial case studies is that process 
manufacturing efficiency improvements require a pervasive ethic of 
constant feedback from all members of a business. Efficiency improvements 
are rarely one-shot investments. Continuous improvement in energy 
efficiency and waste reduction can boost productivity and 
competitiveness - while empowering employees. It takes people - not 
just technology - to save energy, expand a business, and improve the 
environment at the same time.
