<h4 align="center">Helpline Fact Sheet </h4>

<h4 align="center"><font color="#000080"><b>Clozapine--Treatment
for </b></font><a href="schizo.htm"><font color="#000080"><b>Schizophrenia</b></font></a></h4>

<hr>

<p><font color="#FF0000" size="4" face="Arial"><b>Risk or
possible Side Effects involved with Clozapine</b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - Agranulocytosis</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. developed by 1 to 2 percent of Clozapine consumers</p>
        <p>b. white blood cell count drops dramatically</p>
        <p>c. creates a vulnerability to &amp; inability to fight
        off infections</p>
        <p>d. dangerous &amp; potentially fatal</p>
        <p>e. usually successfully treated by stopping Clozapine</p>
        <p>f. may be treated through hospitalization &amp; a drug
        that increases white blood cell production</p>
        <p>g. reversible if caught early enough</p>
        <p>h. how to minimize the risk of contracting
        Agranulocytosis</p>
    </blockquote>
    <blockquote>
        <blockquote>
            <p>1. check the white blood cell count each week</p>
            <p>2. have weekly blood tests sent to the pharmacy
            before weekly prescriptions can be picked up</p>
            <p>3. avoid taking Clozapine with other drugs
            (alka-seltzer, antibiotics, anticonvulsants, antidepressants,
            cold medicines)</p>
        </blockquote>
    </blockquote>
    <p>2 - seizures in 1 to 5% of consumers</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. the higher the dose, the greater the risk of
        seizures</p>
    </blockquote>
    <p>3 - cardiovascular &amp; respiratory side effects are
    possible, but very rare</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. lowered blood pressure</p>
        <p>b. increased heart rate</p>
        <p>c. usually managed easily by a gradual increase of
        Clozapine dosage</p>
        <p>d. weight gain, drooling, initial lethargy are not
        uncommon, but are easily managed by dose adjustment or
        other interventions </p>
    </blockquote>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial"><b><i>Response rate</i></b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - effective for about 60% of those who try it</p>
    <p>2 - relieves some symptoms (hallucinations, anxiety,
    paranoia) within 4 to 6 weeks </p>
    <p>3 - some symptoms must be treated for 6 to 12 months</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial"><b><i>What is Clozapine?</i></b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - a relatively new medication for patients with
    treatment-resistant schizophrenia</p>
    <p>2 - approved by the FDA in 1990</p>
    <p>3 - given to those who have not responded well to standard
    antipsychotic drugs</p>
    <p>4 - manufactured &amp; marketed by Sandoz Pharmaceuticals</p>
    <p>5 - the product name is Clozaril</p>
</blockquote>

<p><font color="#FF0000" face="Arial"><b><i>Benefits of Clozapine</i></b></font></p>

<blockquote>
    <p>1 - unique effectiveness</p>
    <p>2 - often succeeds where all other medications have failed</p>
    <p>3 - has resulted in numerous instances of
    deinstitutionalization</p>
    <p>4 - treats the &quot;positive symptoms&quot; of
    schizophrenia</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. hallucinations</p>
        <p>b. delusions</p>
        <p>c. bizarre behavior</p>
        <p>d. hostility</p>
    </blockquote>
    <p>5 - treats the &quot;negative symptoms&quot; of
    schizophrenia</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. withdrawal</p>
        <p>b. blunted emotions</p>
        <p>c. lack of motivation</p>
        <p>d. inability to experience pleasure</p>
    </blockquote>
    <p>6 - lack of side effects</p>
    <blockquote>
        <p>a. no muscle spasms, cramps, or posturing movements
        which are common reactions to antipsychotic drugs</p>
        <p>b. minimal incidence of restlessness, muscle rigidity,
        or tremor</p>
        <p>c. does not cause the common disfiguring side effect
        of Tardive Dyskinesia (involuntary movements like sucking
        &amp; smacking lips or grimacing)</p>
    </blockquote>
</blockquote>
