<h2>Homicide Rates 1910-1944 </h2>

<p>The following are Homicide rates for the years prior to,
during, and following Prohibition of alcohol in the United States
(according to &quot;Murder Statistics from Statistical Abstract
of the United States&quot;, U.S. Dept. of Commerce). </p>

<p><img src="../graphics/blueline.gif" width="562" height="19"> </p>

<pre>
<font size="2"> The ten years      Prohibition         Prohibition
 preceeding         begins 1920         ends 1933
 Prohibition
 
 1910 - 4.6         1920 - 6.8          1933 - 9.7      
 1911 - 5.5         1921 - 8.1          1934 - 9.5    
 1912 - 5.4         1922 - 8.0          1935 - 8.3
 1913 - 6.1         1923 - 7.8          1936 - 8.0
 1914 - 6.2         1924 - 8.1          1937 - 7.6
 1915 - 5.9         1925 - 8.3          1938 - 6.8
 1916 - 6.3         1926 - 8.4          1939 - 6.4
 1917 - 6.9         1927 - 8.4          1940 - 6.3
 1918 - 6.5         1928 - 8.6          1941 - 6.0
 1919 - 7.2         1929 - 8.4          1942 - 5.9
                    1930 - 8.8          1943 - 5.1
                    1931 - 9.2          1944 - 5.0
                    1932 - 9.0

</font>
</pre>

<hr>

<p>Note that at it's height, the homicide rate under Prohibition
was not disimilar to modern homicide and non-negligent
manslaughter rates (as recorded in the FBI Uniform Crime
Reports). These have hovered around the 9.2 +/- 1.0 per 100,000
mark for the past twenty years. <br>
</p>
