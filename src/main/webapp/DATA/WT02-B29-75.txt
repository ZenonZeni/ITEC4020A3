<FONT SIZE="+1"><STRONG>Defined Benefit Pension Plan Questions and
Answers</STRONG></FONT><P>



<STRONG>Q:	Who can establish Defined Benefit plans?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Corporations, sub-chapter S corporations, self-employed
individuals,
sole
proprietorships, partnerships, and non-profit organizations.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	What is the limit on contributions to the plan?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	An annual contribution necessary to provide maximum annual
benefit to
an
employee up to the lesser of 100% of their compensation or $120,000. (Subject to
cost of living
increases.)
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	When must the plan be established?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	By fiscal year-end (12/31 for calendar year plans).
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	When must contributions be made?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	By tax time plus extensions.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	Is the employer a fiduciary?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Yes.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	Is plan administration required (e.g., annual reports to the
IRS)?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Yes, including filing certain reports with the IRS and the
DOL.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	What are the eligibility requirements for participants?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:	</STRONG>Maximum requirements are 2 years of service at 1,000 hours a
year and 21
years of age.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	What are the vesting requirements?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	If the eligibility requirement is greater than 1 year of
service,
participants
must be 100% vested upon entry into plan. If eligibility requirement is one year or
less, and
the plan is
"top heavy," a 6-year graduated vesting schedule may be used.  More liberal vesting
schedules are
available for non "top heavy" plans.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	When can money be withdrawn from the plan?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:	</STRONG>Generally, at retirement, at termination of employment, in
case
of death, in
case of disability
</UL>
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	How are distributions taxed?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Lump sum distributions may be eligible for favorable tax
treatment.
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	Are loans available?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Yes. If the plan allows them, employees can borrow half of
their vested
interest in the plan up to $50,000, which usually must be repaid within 5 years. 
Plan loans to
owner-employees in unincorporated businesses, or shareholder employees in
sub-chapter S
corporations, are prohibited transactions.
<P>
<STRONG>Q: 	Are annual contributions required?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Yes, as long as the plan is not "overfunded" as determined by
actuarial
calculations.  
<P>
<STRONG>Q:	How are contributions/benefits determined?</STRONG><BR>
<STRONG>A:</STRONG>	Benefits are determined by a written formula that takes age,
years of
service,
compensation, and other employee demographics into consideration. Contributions are
based on
actuarial calculations to assure funds are available to pay benefits.
