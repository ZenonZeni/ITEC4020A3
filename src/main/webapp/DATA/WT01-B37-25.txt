<h2>My Question: </h2>

<p>In all my study and review of the information regarding this
issue, one question keeps coming back to me. Let's assume - for
the sake of argument - that marijuana has no medical value
whatsoever, despite the fact that it has a several thousand year
history of medical use and that a prescription drug is made from
its primary active ingredient. Let's assume - for the sake of
argument - that all these medical marijuana patients are just
fooling themselves. </p>

<p>Even in that case, what would we stand to gain as a society by
punishing sick people and putting them through an already
overloaded criminal justice system? Even if they are deluding
themselves -- <b>what benefit is there to prosecuting sick
people?</b> </p>
