<H2><I>E. </I>Conclusion</H2>
<BR>
<BR>
Renewables will be a key component of any national strategy to cut carbon 
dioxide emissions and reduce the threat of global climate change. 
Renewables costs have fallen steeply and are expected to drop further. 
Wind energy is already nearly cost-competitive with fossil 
generation - even when the high social and environmental costs of 
fossil fuels are not added to their prices. Photovoltaics are already 
cost-effective for certain transmission and distribution expansion 
applications, and are often the best option for off-grid generation. Solar 
thermal technologies continue to improve thanks to prudent federal 
government assistance, and offer promise as large-scale, centralized 
generation facilities. Geothermal is a proven technology yet to tap its 
vast geographic potential.<BR>
