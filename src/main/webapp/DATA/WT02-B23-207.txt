<H4>T14<BR>
THURSDAY 10:15 AM-11:15 AM </H4>

<H3>A Beginners Guide to the Internet: <BR>
How to Put the Internet in Your Classroom and Integrate it in the Curriculum
</H3>

<P><B>Marc Young</B>, Kennedy Krieger School, Baltimore, MD </P>

<P>This session is for the teacher or educator who would like to get started
using the internet in the classroom but doesn't know where to begin or
how to get started. Participants will be exposed to the Internet, WWW,
e-mail, modems and a list of educational sites for both students and teachers.
</P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>T26<BR>
THURSDAY 11:30 AM-12:30 PM </H4>

<H3>Internet Telecommunication Services, Helper Applications, and Search
Tools for Macintosh and IBM Computers </H3>

<P><B>Greg Church</B>, Kennedy Krieger School, Baltimore, MD <BR>
<B>Marc Young,</B> Kennedy Krieger School, Baltimore, MD </P>

<P>The major focus of this presentation will be to explore the rapid development
of telecommunications technology, interactive Internet multimedia, and
"real-time" applications as well as their potential to transform the
curriculum
and redefine schools. Participants will learn what types and quality of
interactive Internet applications are possible using various commercial
telecommunication services. Educators will experience Internet helper applications
that bring, for example, live audio, communications, graphics, and video
into the classroom. Educators will also be exposed to the most popular
information search engines on the world wide web. Participants will compare
different options available through these services. These information search
services include INFOSEEK, ALTA VISTA, YAHOO, LYCOS, WEB CRAWLER, and SEARCH.COM.
</P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>T39<BR>
THURSDAY 1:30 PM-2:50 PM</H4>

<H3>101 Ways to do a Book Report </H3>

<P><B>Sarah Calhoun</B>, Kennedy Krieger School, Baltimore, MD </P>

<P>This presentation will demonstrate the various technological tools that
can be used by both teachers and students for the purpose of presenting
information and developing student authorship Participants will learn the
"how to" aspect of producing projects by engaging in hands-on activities
using a variety of multi-media equipment. </P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>T63<BR>
THURSDAY 4:15 PM-5:15 PM</H4>

<H3>Phonics + Whole Language Boost Reading Scores: Red Wagons Deliver Laptops
and New Software to Primary Students </H3>

<P><B>Jeannine Herron, Ph.D.</B>, Dominican College, San Rafael, CA </P>

<P>To boost reading skills, this project provided 30 laptops and a new
software curriculum that integrates phonics, whole language, and typing!
Presentation will include research results, software demonstration and
a video of kids in action! </P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>F13<BR>
FRIDAY 10:15 AM-11:15 AM</H4>

<H3>How Can Technology Improve Learning and Work Skills? </H3>

<P><B>Brian Bryant, Ph.D.</B>, University of Texas, Austin, TX </P>

<P>Both computers and assistive technology can help students and adults
with learning disabilities develop needed skills. This session will provide
current information, ideas, and a demonstration of computer hardware, software,
and other technology tools that can be used to increase learning, communication,
and vocational success. Information about specific practical resources
will be provided. </P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>F22<BR>
 FRIDAY 11:30 AM-12:30 PM</H4>

<H3>Motivating the Reluctant Writer Through Technology </H3>

<P><B>Amy Best</B>, Kennedy Krieger Institute, Baltimore, MD <BR>
<B>Kathryn Bruno</B>, Kennedy Krieger Institute, Baltimore, MD </P>

<P>Teachers will experience many ways to work on writing skills with the
reluctant writer using technology. Many different programs will be demonstrated
in this audience participation workshop aimed at getting the reluctant
writer to learn and practice writing skills and even become excited about
writing. </P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>F36<BR>
FRIDAY 1:30 PM-2:50 PM</H4>

<H3>Technology and Inclusion: Using Technology to Increase Accessibility
to Mainstream Activities for Children with Mild to Moderate Disabilities
</H3>

<P><B>Diana H. Malkin</B>, Teacher, Sherman, TX <BR>
<B>Nancy Foster</B>, Teacher, Sherman, TX </P>

<P>This session will demonstrate the use of technology to increase the
integration of students with disabilities into inclusive classrooms. The
presenters will look at the use of talking word processing programs, an
alternative keyboard and overlays as a way to increase the productivity
of students with disabilities in inclusive classrooms. </P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>F48<BR>
FRIDAY 3:00 PM-4:00 PM</H4>

<H3>Compuplay: Facilitating Inclusion Through Technology </H3>

<P><B>Joyce A. Morimoti, M.S.Ed.</B>, National Lekotek Center, Evanston,
IL </P>

<P>Compuplay Centers are play and resource centers that support families
with children who have disabilities. Using developmentally appropriate
software and assistive technology, Compuplay classes help to strengthen
children’s achievements and build confidence through interactive play both
during individual sessions as well as group sessions that foster inclusion.
</P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>F61<BR>
FRIDAY 4:15 PM-5:15 PM</H4>

<H3>Impacts of Multimedia Anchored Instruction on Classroom Interactions</H3>

<P><B>Candyce Williams Glaser</B>, Vanderbilt University, Nashville, TN
</P>

<P>The audience will be provided with an overview of an instructional technique
known as Anchored Instruction. Specific ways anchored instruction can be
used to increase academic outcomes for students with learning disabilities
in inclusive settings will be discussed. Ways anchored instruction can
be used to increase social skills for students with learning disabilities
in inclusive settings will be demonstrated. </P>

<H3>S7<BR>
SATURDAY 8:30 AM-10:00 AM<BR>
<BR>
Computer-Assisted Writing: Using Word Processing to Improve the Writing
Skills of Individuals with Learning Disabilities </H3>

<P><B>Rena B. Lewis,</B> San Diego State University, San Diego, CA <BR>
<B>Tamarah Ashton</B>, San Diego State University, San Diego, CA <BR>
<B>Candy Kieley</B>, San Diego State University, San Diego, CA</P>

<P>What word processing tools provide the most benefit for individuals
with learning disabilities? Spelling checkers, grammar checkers, synthesized
speech, word prediction, alternative keyboards? This session will explore
these approaches and suggest strategies for matching word processing tools
to the needs of individual writers. </P>

<P>
<HR WIDTH="100%"></P>

<H4>S12<BR>
SATURDAY 10:15 AM-11:45 AM</H4>

<H3>Preparing Special Education Teachers with the World Wide Web </H3>

<P><B>Sean Joseph Smith,</B> The University of Virginia, Charlottesville,
VA</P>

<P>The national mandate to integrate technology within the classroom setting
is clear to all educators. This presentation provides examples of helping
teachers not only use the world wide web but also to acquire practical
information relevant to individuals with learning disabilities through
the case study method. </P>

Problem-Solving by Engineering Bridges </H4>

<P><B>Mark Trexler</B>, Kennedy Krieger Middle School, Baltimore, MD</P>
