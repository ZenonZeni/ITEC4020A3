<H2><CENTER>Some Considerations your Egg Donation Contract Should
Cover</CENTER>
</H2><H3><CENTER>Prepared by the Law Offices of Vorzimer, Garber, Masserman and Ecoff </CENTER>
</H3><P><BR>
<OL><LI>Establish financial responsibility on the part of the Recipient Couple
for all expenses incurred pursuant to this contract.<BR>
<LI>Declare that the Recipient Couple are financially and legally responsible
for the child no matter what. Additionally, Recipient Couple have full custody
and parental rights regarding the Child.<BR>
<LI>All parties must have legal and medical informed consent (all parties
must be represented by legal counsel).<BR>
<LI>Social disease testing (including AIDS) to be completed by all parties.
Additionally, Egg Donor must be medically examined and declared medically
appropriate for the program.<BR>
<LI>Provide ownership of embryos, disposition of embryos, and legal definition
of parentage.<BR>
<LI>Establish specific responsibilities of each party so as to minimize
misunderstandings later on.<BR>
<LI>Records should be kept by the Center on all parties in the event information
on a party is later needed for legal or medical reasons.<BR>
<LI>Details legal and psychological framework so all aspects of this process
are thought out, considered and pondered by everyone prior to entering into
this agreement.<BR>
<LI>Outline confidentiality concerns and provides for privacy for all
parties.<BR>
<LI>All parties intend contract to be governed by the laws of California.
</OL><P>
